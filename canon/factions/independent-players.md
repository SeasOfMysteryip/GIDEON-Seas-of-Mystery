# Independent Players

## Core Identity

Independent Players are opportunists: relic scavengers, pirate brokers, black-market navigators, rogue documentarians, private analysts, mercenary protectors, and people who survive by selling access to the impossible.

They are not one faction. They are the pressure that proves the Spectral Sea has an economy.

## Goals

- Crack mythic truth for personal gain.
- Sell relic paths, maps, safe passage, evidence, or silence.
- Avenge old blood feuds.
- Stay alive by staying useful.

## Economic Function

- Move rumors between powers.
- Sell access to fracture zones or Mirror Harbors.
- Launder salvage and relic fragments.
- Turn civilians into customers and witnesses into liabilities.
- Let Darak-style figures plausibly enter any faction's orbit without belonging.

## Typical Members

- Black-market relic traders.
- Former Protectors turned mercenary.
- Private-sector anomaly analysts.
- Pirate brokers.
- Bleed-tourism operators.
- Civilians with unwanted sensitivity.

## Gideon Failure Test

If Independent Players behave like a unified faction with one ideology, Gideon should flag it. Their rule is leverage, not loyalty.
