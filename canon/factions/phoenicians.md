# The Phoenicians

## Core Identity

The Phoenicians are the rival inheritance line to Atlantean/Watcher stewardship: relic counterfeiters, ley saboteurs, mapwrights, imperial traders, and control-seekers who treat knowledge as possession.

They are not simply pirates. In the current Seas framework, pirate power often carries Phoenician pressure because the old bloodline logic learned to survive through routes, ledgers, secrecy, and violence.

## Doctrine

- Knowledge should be controlled before it is shared.
- Relics are tools, keys, projections, and leverage.
- The ley grid is not sacred; it is infrastructure to seize or counterfeit.
- Bloodline is claim, not responsibility.

## Current Pressure

- Blackbeard draws on Phoenician-aligned power and old oath structures.
- Murakami expresses Phoenician logic through tactical mastery and relic utilitarianism.
- Ching Shih may align with Phoenician resources without surrendering sovereignty.
- Sophia's dual inheritance makes her dangerous to both Atlantean and Phoenician claims.

## Failure Test

If Phoenician pressure becomes generic villain lore or simple ethnicity/history reference, Gideon should flag it. The story use is doctrine: control, counterfeit, route, ledger, and command.
