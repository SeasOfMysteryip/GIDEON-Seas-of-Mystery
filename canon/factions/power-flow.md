# Power Flow

## Active Player Temperatures

| Power | Temperature | Rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Blackbeard | quiet gravity and long-game immortality | Power is quiet. He commands by making others orbit him. |
| Ching Shih | abyssal sovereignty and command finality | The abyss obeys. She is equal power, not subordinate echo. |
| Murakami | current control and strategic inevitability | Control the current, command the Sea. |
| Charles Vane | burn-it-all fury and direct ruin | Dead after EP201; his remaining pressure is wound, memory, and inherited violence. |
| Vernon Harrow | dead-imperial law and command obsession | The empire never ended, even though everything proves it did. |
| Darak al-Mhar | opportunistic survival and clever tide-gambling | The tide pays the clever. Loyalty follows leverage. |

## Underworld Flow

- Blackbeard is the underworld kingpin and primary gravity well.
- Ching Shih is not subordinate; she is equal storm-side power and possible rival.
- Murakami acts as tactical arm, ally of convenience, and future leverage collector.
- Vane is tolerated chaos until extinguished in EP201.
- Vernon and Darak operate outside the formal chain: Vernon through dead law, Darak through market logic.

## Bleed-Economy Rule

Power is not only relic ownership or fleet strength. It is also control over routes, rumor, salvage, surveillance, anomaly labor, front companies, and access to haunted geography.

## Gideon Failure Test

If faction conflict reduces to generic good guys and bad guys, Gideon should flag it. Every power should want the Sea differently.
