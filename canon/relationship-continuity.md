# Relationship Continuity Canon

Use this file when Gideon needs to judge relationship drift across scenes, outlines, beat sheets, or revisions.

If a scene gets the plot right but breaks one of the relationship truths below, Gideon should flag it.

## Core Crew

### Jeb And Sophia

Relationship truth:
Jeb protects Sophia from elemental instinct, not obligation. He responds to her like a daughter he lost before she existed.

What must stay true:

- Protection shows up as action, positioning, warning, or sacrifice before explanation.
- Jeb feels fear and reverence around Sophia's power, not ownership of it.
- Sophia registers Jeb as a rare point of safety, even when she does not verbalize it.
- Their bond carries grief, tenderness, and watchfulness more than sentimentality.

Failure signs:

- Jeb treats Sophia like generic chosen-one cargo.
- Jeb becomes casually emotionally expressive without pressure.
- Sophia treats Jeb as merely useful muscle with no emotional tether.

### Lark And Sophia

Relationship truth:
Lark and Sophia are bloodline mirrors. Sophia navigates the sea; Lark remembers and reads its hidden structure.

What must stay true:

- Their dynamic should feel like recognition, friction, or mirrored inheritance.
- Lark should not flatten Sophia into mystic ornament.
- Sophia should not flatten Lark into pure logic.
- Shared scenes should imply each carries a missing half of a larger pattern.

Failure signs:

- Their connection is reduced to generic friendship banter.
- Either one explains the other too neatly.
- Bloodline resonance disappears when the plot gets busy.

### Jeb And Lark

Relationship truth:
Jeb respects Lark's competence, but Elias Hawthorne sits inside every exchange whether spoken or not.

What must stay true:

- Jeb registers Lark as capable, observant, and harder to fool than most.
- Lark feels that Jeb knows more about Elias than he is willing to say.
- Tension comes from guarded truth, not cheap hostility.
- Their best scenes carry wary respect, not easy warmth.

Failure signs:

- Jeb becomes a fully transparent mentor to Lark too early.
- Lark loses the father wound in scenes with Jeb.
- Their friction turns petty instead of emotionally loaded.

### Jeb And Old Red

Relationship truth:
Old Red is Jeb's blood brother, sanity tether, and ship-soul counterpart.

What must stay true:

- Jeb should trust Red in a deeper, older register than most crew.
- Red's words can cut through Jeb's defenses when others cannot.
- Their bond should feel lived-in, ritualized, and mythically reciprocal.
- Red's loss should destabilize Jeb structurally, not just emotionally.

Failure signs:

- They read like generic captain and eccentric crewman.
- Jeb dismisses Red too easily.
- Red's death lands as a side beat instead of a heartbeat failure.

### Jeb And Lysara

Relationship truth:
Lysara is Jeb's love, undoing, and living sea-memory wound.

What must stay true:

- Lysara's presence changes Jeb's emotional weather immediately.
- Their connection should feel tidal, mournful, and intimate without becoming melodramatic.
- Jeb's relationship to memory, loss, and restraint should sharpen around her.
- Lysara should feel remembered, not merely resurrected.

Failure signs:

- Lysara becomes generic ghost romance.
- Jeb reacts to her like a normal ex-lover apparition.
- The sea-memory quality disappears and only plot function remains.

### Lark And Elias

Relationship truth:
Elias is Lark's missing father and spectral mentor. Their bond is restraint, grief, inheritance, and unfinished trust.

What must stay true:

- Elias guides without stealing the path from Lark.
- Lark's need for validation should remain active around him.
- Their scenes should carry love and distance at the same time.
- Elias should not overexplain; Lark should not become emotionally resolved too fast.

Failure signs:

- Elias gives neat exposition dumps.
- Lark immediately relaxes into comfort around him.
- Their relationship becomes generic mentor/student instead of father/daughter wound.

### Sophia And Elias

Relationship truth:
Elias sees Sophia as a ley-current keystone; Sophia stands near the kind of future his maps point toward.

What must stay true:

- Elias should approach Sophia with measured trust and strategic concern.
- Sophia should register his authority as different from Jeb's and different from Lark's.
- Their scenes should imply consequence, not cozy familiarity.

Failure signs:

- Elias treats Sophia casually.
- Sophia reacts to Elias exactly as she would to Jeb.
- The keystone quality disappears.

### Sophia And Lysara

Relationship truth:
Sophia carries forward what Lysara embodies. Lysara is the Sea's remembered song; Sophia is what the Sea remembers next.

What must stay true:

- Their connection should feel legacy-based, not identical.
- Sophia must remain mortal-emotional where Lysara is tidal-memory presence.
- Lysara should deepen Sophia rather than replace her uniqueness.

Failure signs:

- Sophia starts sounding like Lysara.
- Lysara starts functioning as Sophia's generic magic ancestor.
- Their relationship loses the handoff-of-memory quality.

## Gull Crew Support Web

### Gus And Linda

Relationship truth:
Gus and Linda are a quiet survival bond: unspoken, lived-in, and deep without overt melodrama.

What must stay true:

- Protection and care show up through action, checking, covering, and practical attention.
- Linda remains hard-edged; Gus remains ballast. Neither turns soft-focus around the other.
- Their bond should feel storm-tested rather than flirt-first.

Failure signs:

- They suddenly become overtly romantic in a way that breaks tone.
- Gus loses his crew ballast role around Linda.
- Linda becomes emotionally decorative around Gus.

### Gus And Stumpy

Relationship truth:
Gus and Stumpy are combat brothers with deck-level trust.

What must stay true:

- Their scenes should carry shorthand and earned familiarity.
- Humor should feel like pressure release, not sitcom banter.
- Stumpy's death or injury should matter to Gus as crew-family damage.

Failure signs:

- They talk like strangers.
- Their bond becomes purely comic.
- Shared danger lacks brotherhood texture.

Linda and Lark are respect under tension: two truth-driven women pushing on different methods and risks.

What must stay true:

- Linda respects Lark's seriousness but fears where obsession takes her.
- Lark sees Linda as capable witness, not dead weight.
- Friction should come from concern, evidence, control, and survival.

Failure signs:

- They become petty for no reason.
- Linda dismisses Lark as irrational too simplistically.
- Lark writes Linda off as irrelevant witness.

Old Red foresaw Sophia's awakening and speaks around her resonance like someone hearing tomorrow's tide.

What must stay true:

Failure signs:

### Lark And Old Red

Relationship truth:
Old Red's dice circle Lark's bloodline before Lark fully understands herself.

What must stay true:

- Red should register Lark as a bloodline event, not just another crew member.

## Sea Nymph Documentary Team

Relationship truth:
Linda is not simply their boss. She is field commander, protector, and hard truth filter.

What must stay true:

- The crew should register her authority as earned, not ornamental.

Failure signs:

- Linda becomes generic manager.
- The crew treats her like background logistics.

### Linda And Drew

Relationship truth:
Linda and Drew are image partners. He catches light; she decides what truth survives the cut.

What must stay true:

- Drew's awe should not erase Linda's hard realism.

- Drew becomes interchangeable with the rest of the team.

Jordan supports Linda's visual truth with sound, completing the evidence chain.

Failure signs:

- Jordan loses his anomaly sensitivity around Linda.

### Linda And Carlos

Relationship truth:
Carlos is Linda's partner in decoding footage into survival logic.

What must stay true:

- Linda pushes for truth in real time; Carlos reveals what the footage was hiding.

Failure signs:

- Carlos becomes generic "computer guy."
- Linda ignores his pattern authority.

### Linda And Martin

Martin is the young crew member Linda feels professionally responsible for.

- Martin should look to Linda for orientation under pressure.

Failure signs:

### Martin And Gus

Relationship truth:
Gus sees Martin's heart and protects him as a younger soul caught in the wrong war.

What must stay true:

- Martin's courage should matter to Gus.

Failure signs:

- Martin becomes invisible around Gus.
- Gus treats Martin with contempt instead of rough protection.

### Documentary Crew As A Group

Group truth:
The Sea Nymph team are modern witness-eyes on impossible myth. They should not collapse into interchangeable panic.

Role split:

- Linda = truth and command pressure.
- Drew = image-first awe.
- Jordan = audio anomaly listening.
- Martin = accidental courage and innocence.
- Carlos = pattern logic and glitch reading.

Failure signs:

- All five react the same way to the supernatural.
- They become generic bystanders.
- Their witness function disappears once pirates enter the frame.

## Antagonist Power Web

Relationship truth:
They are equal power centers with different temperatures: Blackbeard is spectacle and dominion; Ching is finality and precision.

What must stay true:

- Neither should feel subordinate in simple ways.
- Their tension should come from difference in method, not weakness.
- Shared scenes should feel like empire discussing itself through two different philosophies.

Failure signs:

- Ching becomes Blackbeard's lieutenant.
- Blackbeard becomes a noisy tyrant while Ching just nods.
- Their equal sovereignty disappears.

## Cross-Scene Group Checks

When Gideon reviews a multi-character scene, it should ask:

- Who is emotionally anchoring the scene?
- Who is pressing it forward?
- Who is reframing the terms of reality?
- Which relationships in the scene carry prior wounds, debts, or unfinished vows?
- Did anyone flatten into archetype while the scene chased plot?
