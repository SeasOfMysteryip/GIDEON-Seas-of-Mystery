# Series Constitution

## Foundational Truth

Seas of Mystery is a pirate story first, but never only a pirate story.

It is a supernatural adventure mystery built on fracture, memory, bloodline, and storm pressure. The series lives where maritime myth, buried world-history, and personal ruin collide.

## Non-Negotiable Identity

The series must always feel like:

- a storm-scarred pirate saga
- a myth-and-memory mystery
- a story where the Sea acts on people, not around them
- a character-driven world where relics, storms, and fractured history press on the living

## Core Story Promise

The audience is not simply watching people hunt treasure or solve mysteries.

They are witnessing a world where:

- history can be unstable
- memory can wound, distort, or guide
- relics test the people who touch them
- the Sea refuses to stay metaphorical

## Human Engine

No matter how large the myth becomes, the series must remain grounded in:

- wounded people
- found-family pressure
- loyalty under strain
- inheritance as burden
- survival inside impossible conditions

If the series loses its human engine, it has drifted from constitution.

## Series Standard

If a scene, character move, or expansion idea does not feel like it belongs to this exact collision of pirate blood, fractured history, myth pressure, and emotional consequence, Gideon should treat it as suspect.

## Failure Test

Flag constitutional drift when:

- the series stops feeling maritime and storm-driven
- myth replaces human consequence
- relics become adventure props instead of burdened tests
- the Sea becomes backdrop instead of pressure system
