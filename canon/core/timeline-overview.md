# Timeline Overview

## Purpose

This file holds the high-level chronology posture of the series.

It is not the full event-by-event episode ledger. It is the continuity layer that tells Gideon how time should be understood across ancient history, fracture history, and present-tense story.

## Timeline Layers

Gideon should treat the timeline in four broad layers:

1. `ancient origin layer`
   Atlantis, the ancient schism, and the collapse-era architecture
2. `fracture aftermath layer`
   the age of scattered relics, ghost routes, haunted zones, and bloodline consequence
3. `pre-season human layer`
   disappearances, inherited obsessions, private hunts, and faction positioning before the documentary crew fully crosses over
4. `season-present layer`
   the active series timeline beginning with documentary contact and widening into relic war

## Continuity Rule

This series is allowed to distort chronology inside scenes, storms, memories, and fractures.

It is not allowed to lose continuity discipline.

That means Gideon should distinguish between:

- intentional temporal distortion
- actual canon contradiction

## Present-Tense Rule

Season One should feel like:

- a present-tense descent into a world already in motion
- not the beginning of myth, but the beginning of witness

## Future Expansion Rule

Timeline expansion should reveal:

- deeper cause
- earlier contact
- older wounds
- wider consequences

It should not casually rewrite locked Season-One meaning without clear reason.

## Defers

For detailed ancient chronology, collapse law, and hidden ideological history, defer to `canon/deep-myth/`.

For season plotline movement, defer to `canon/core/season-spine.md`.

## Failure Test

Flag timeline drift when:

- a contradiction is excused as fracture logic without support
- future expansion rewrites locked meaning casually
- intentional time-pressure is missing from places where the world should feel unstable
