# Season Spine

## Logline

When a rogue documentary crew is pulled into the fractured tides of the Spectral Sea, they bind their fate to a dying captain, a haunted ship, and relics of an ancient civilization. As timelines fracture and myths bleed real, survival opens a war buried beneath history, memory, and the sea itself.

## Series Overview

The series follows the collision between the documentary crew, Jeb's storm-scarred world, the relic circuit, and the awakening of a larger hidden system.

The show should move like a mystery, but feel like a haunted maritime convergence event.

## Season One Spine

Season One is a spiral of:

- relic awakening
- bloodline recognition
- documentary witness becoming participation
- crew formation through violence and loyalty
- growing awareness that history is unstable

Season One ends in awakening rather than closure. As of the final Season One restructure, `EP108` is the finale: Old Red dies, the Bloody Gull falls into the Abyss, and the mechanics of escape move to the opening of Season Two.

## Locked Season-One Arc Threads

### Lark And Elias

Lark follows Elias's unfinished path through fracture logic, inherited mapping, and deepening bloodline consequence.

### Jeb / Vane / Blackbeard Chain

Jeb's false understanding of the past drives a major emotional and mythic chain whose fuller truth lies buried beneath Blackbeard's orchestration.

### Linda And Gus

Their relationship forms an emotional anchor inside the storm and keeps the series human under rising myth pressure.

### Sophia

Sophia moves from vulnerable captive pressure toward relic-harmonic centrality and living-map significance.

### History Is Unstable

The doc crew's footage and witness position become proof that history in this world is not a stable record.

## Season-One End State

By the end of Season One:

- the Gull falls into the Abyss, wounded and changed
- the crew survives the season, but is trapped in consequence rather than released from it
- the relic system is more awake than before
- Atlantis and deeper world architecture move closer to the surface
- the story graduates from discovery into larger war

## Failure Test

Flag season-spine drift when:

- the doc crew remain passive too long
- Sophia, Lark, and Jeb stop feeling like convergence points
- Season One feels like answer-delivery instead of awakening
- the end state does not open the world wider
