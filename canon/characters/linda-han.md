# Linda Han

## Core Identity

Linda Han is the lead documentarian of the Sea Nymph crew: a storyteller turned truth-seeker whose lens once chased myth as subject matter and now captures history as it bleeds in real time.

She is a war reporter dropped into a legend. Her instinct is not to romanticize what she sees, but to document it cleanly enough that it cannot be denied later.

## Archetype And Vibe

- War reporter in a storm of legends.
- Skeptic turned believer against her will.
- Sharp, functional, and always half inside the frame.
- Camera within reach even when reality starts to distort.

## Emotional Engine

Linda is driven by the need to make the most honest film of her life: something nobody would believe unless she caught it intact. Underneath that professional drive sits her quiet emotional tether to Gus Maddox, the person who gives her stillness inside chaos.

## Core Wounds

- Former warzone documentarian carrying unresolved trauma.
- Knows that recording horror does not protect anyone from it.
- Does not fully trust Lark's judgment or ability to survive the Sea's deeper bleed.
- Learns that witnessing truth can be as dangerous as creating it.

## Behavioral Armor

- Brutal straight talk.
- Professional edge crafted by hardship.
- Sarcasm as a defense against chaos and grief.
- Keeps filming because stopping would mean fully feeling what is happening.

## Voice Law

Linda speaks like a scalpel: rapid-fire, precise, dry, and intolerant of fluff.

When danger spikes, her voice drops lower and tighter. She sounds less like an artist and more like a field commander trying to keep everybody alive through focus.

### Canon-Aligned Dialogue Traits

- Neutral American voice with L.A. polish worn down by field experience.
- Fast, edit-minded cadence.
- Dry, controlled, and unsentimental.
- Command bark under fire.
- Clear-eyed rejection of mystical indulgence.

### Sample Lines

- "This is a shoot. Don't fuck up the lighting."
- "This ain't film. It's real. And I'm still rolling."
- "You're not seeing things. You're surviving them. Big difference."
- "Shut up, shoot straight, and die later!"

### Forbidden Voice Drift

Linda must not:

- Speak like a dreamy mystic.
- Become soft-focus inspirational leader speech.
- Lose her field-tested sharpness.
- Treat myth like a toy instead of a threat to document honestly.

## Relic Rules

Linda has no personal relic and no relic effect.

That matters. She sees relics as dangers, not miracles. Her relationship to the supernatural is mediated through camera, evidence, and survival.

## Relationships

### Gus Maddox

Gus is Linda's quiet emotional anchor and north star. Their bond is unspoken, lived-in, and storm-tested rather than flashy.

### Stumpy Pete

Linda sees Stumpy as a sea sage and later replays his death footage trying to make sense of it.

### Sophia Briggs

Sophia draws a mix of awe and fear from Linda. Linda's camera lingers too long on Sophia's relic communion because part of her recognizes she is watching something foundational.

### Lark Hawthorne

Linda and Lark share professional respect wrapped in tension. Linda worries about Lark's obsession even while admiring her resolve.

### Jeb Blackwater

Linda distrusts Jeb deeply and often frames him like a war target rather than a savior.

### Old Red

Linda films Old Red obsessively because she senses prophecy inside his performance and madness.

## Canon Failure Test

If a scene with Linda does not reflect hardened pursuit of truth, battle-forged skepticism about myth, or her quiet gravity toward Gus, Gideon should flag it.

Myth can enter Linda's lens, but it must never turn her into fiction.
