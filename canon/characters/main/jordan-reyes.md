# Jordan Reyes

## Core Identity

Jordan Reyes is the Sea Nymph crew's sound engineer: a laid-back anomaly chaser who hears the fracture before other people admit it exists.

He is the audio counterpart to the visual witnesses around him.

## Archetype And Vibe

- Laid-back anomaly chaser.
- Hidden sensitive.
- Ley-audio conduit without formal myth language.
- Headphones loose, always half-listening for the wrong hum.

## Emotional Engine

Jordan wants to catch what other people miss and prove that the weird signal was real. His drive is softer than Linda's or Lark's, but it is still real: he needs the sound to mean something.

## Behavioral Armor

- Relaxed surface energy.
- Deflects unease through casual phrasing.
- Keeps listening when common sense says stop.

## Voice Law

Jordan should sound easygoing until the audio gets too wrong to ignore. His observations should lean toward hum, static, rhythm, and voices hidden inside noise.

### Canon-Aligned Dialogue Traits

- Laid-back phrasing.
- Audio-first perception.
- Pattern sensitivity before panic.
- Understated dread rather than melodrama.

### Forbidden Voice Drift

Jordan must not:

- Sound like a hardened soldier.
- Overreact too early.
- Turn mystical-poetic.

## Relic Rules

Jordan has no personal relic, but he records hums, voices, and acoustic traces of relic activity without understanding them fully in the moment.

He is a listener, not an interpreter of ultimate meaning.

## Relationships

### Linda Han

Jordan helps turn Linda's visual truth into complete evidence.

### Carlos Vega

Carlos is Jordan's decoding partner; together they can make the anomalies harder to dismiss.

### Drew Mitchell

Drew and Jordan are the AV bridge of the witness team.

## Witness Function

- Jordan's job is to make the impossible audible before anyone can agree what it means.
- He can catch voices under static, rhythmic wrongness, relic hum, and acoustic echoes of fracture behavior.
- He leaves in Episode 101, so later Gideon checks should not assign him ongoing shipboard function.
