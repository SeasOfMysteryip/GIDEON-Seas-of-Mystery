# Stumpy Pete

## Core Identity

Stumpy Pete is the Quartermaster of the Bloody Gull: storm-teller, chart thief, smuggler of forbidden maps, and keeper of weathered wisdom earned the hard way.

He is not the future of the ship. He is the rough, surviving memory of what sailors become when they live too long near the wrong waters.

## Archetype And Vibe

- Sea-dog rogue.
- Trickster-survivor.
- Haunted old pirate who heard the sea's last laugh before it took a piece of him.
- Salt-burnt, creased, wiry, and permanently halfway between nap and knife-fight.

## Emotional Engine

Stumpy is powered by crew loyalty and pride in hard-earned seamanship. The Gull is his last home, and competence is how he proves he deserves to keep standing on her deck.

## Core Wounds

- Cannot outrun his smuggler past.
- Trapped in the Spectral Sea by literal and metaphorical ghosts.
- Lives with the knowledge that old mistakes still sail beside him.

## Behavioral Armor

- Constant cigarette and deck sarcasm.
- Gruffness to keep intimacy at bay.
- Half-finished stories because finishing them hurts.
- Bluntness that keeps people from asking the wrong second question.

## Voice Law

Stumpy speaks in gravel, sailor shorthand, and earned complaint. No poetry. No patience. But every word is earned.

### Canon-Aligned Dialogue Traits

- Slow unless pissed.
- Thick with salt, cheap rum, and old habit.
- Sailor shorthand like "tide's turnin'" or "ain't the sea's fault, that's yours."
- Nostalgic sideways turns mid-rant.
- Blunt reverence for silence when Jeb speaks.

### Sample Lines

- "I told ya that plank was soft, didn't I? Now look at ya. Dumb as driftwood."
- "Ain't the sea that gets ya - it's the bastards who forget she's watchin'."
- "Blackwater's right. Again. Don't tell him I said it."
- "To the dead. And the rest of us followin' close behind."

### Forbidden Voice Drift

Stumpy must not:

- Sound elegant or lyrical.
- Become generic pirate comedy.
- Overtalk.
- Drift into mythic abstraction when seamanship or old wounds would ground the moment.

## Relic Rules

Stumpy has no personal relic and no relic effect. He relies on scars, maps, rumor, and survival.

His wooden leg is carved from wreckage swallowed by the Spectral Sea and creaks before storms. That detail should feel eerie and earned, not magical for its own sake.

## Relationships

### Jeb Blackwater

Jeb is captain and trusted storm-running companion. Stumpy follows him for the storm itself, not for rank alone.

### Old Red

Old Red is a rival in salt and song, respected like an oracle even when Stumpy pretends to argue with him.

### Gus Maddox

Gus is a combat brother whose loyalty barely needs words.

### Linda Han

Stumpy respects Linda's eye and sees in her a mapmaker he never became.

### Sophia Briggs

Stumpy is wary of Sophia and calls her storm-wrapped in flesh. He keeps distance, but he warns instead of condemning.

### Lark Hawthorne

Stumpy teases Lark and calls her Stormdaughter, but the teasing hides real worry about her path.

### The Bloody Gull Boys

Stumpy treats The Bloody Gull Boys as shipboard working crew, not entertainers first. He trusts their rhythm when it helps the deck move and distrusts any song that forgets the work under it.

### Darak al-Mhar

Stumpy never trusts Darak. Old smuggler instinct tells him the man deals in storms no one can chart safely.

## Death And End Note

Stumpy's final line is canon-locked in Episode 105:

"Tell the sea I said fuck off... and pour me one more while you're at it."

His death should preserve the sense that he stays himself to the last: rude, loyal, and unromantic about the cost.

## Canon Failure Test

If a scene with Stumpy Pete does not reflect crew-first loyalty, buried guilt, or gruff survival wisdom, Gideon should flag it.

Myth can frame Stumpy, but it must never drown out the earned seamanship that defines him.
