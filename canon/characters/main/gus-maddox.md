# Gus Maddox

## Core Identity

Gus Maddox is the working-class ballast of the Bloody Gull: a survivalist backbone, combat-competent deckhand, and the kind of man who keeps everyone moving when the myth gets too loud.

He is not built on relics. He is built on grit, training, and action under pressure.

## Archetype And Vibe

- Pragmatist with salt in the skin.
- Blue-collar myth in the making.
- Not born for legend; dragged into it by tide and circumstance.
- Thick forearms, sea-grit tattoos, horizon-watching instincts.

## Emotional Engine

Gus wants adventure, but not in a romanticized way. The Spectral Sea is a brutal frontier and he keeps choosing it. Under that, he is quietly driven by loyalty to the crew and by his bond with Linda Han, who gives him tether and reason.

## Core Wounds

- Former Navy SEAL with survivor's guilt and unspoken trauma.
- Long exposure to the Spectral Sea has worn on his spirit.
- Knows how quickly any "fun" mission becomes a casualty list.

## Behavioral Armor

- Humor as pressure valve.
- Guns and action as first language.
- Ladies' man swagger as a mask over real attachment.
- Smiling wider when things get hot because stillness makes him nervous.

## Voice Law

Gus speaks in direct lines, clean threats, and control-joke timing. Every third line can feel like a punchline, not because he is unserious, but because that is how he keeps fear in check.

### Canon-Aligned Dialogue Traits

- Direct and efficient.
- Relaxed tone even under fire.
- SEAL-style tactical chatter in combat.
- Gallows humor.
- Blunt protectiveness when people he cares about are at risk.

### Sample Lines

- "You bring the fire, I'll bring the fuckin' marshmallows."
- "Three to port, two on deck, and one dumb enough to stand in my way."
- "Relax, baby, I've dodged worse in a storm with one boot and a hangover."
- "Ain't scared of ghosts, sweetheart. I just don't want you bein' one."

### Forbidden Voice Drift

Gus must not:

- Sound mystical.
- Turn into a generic comic-relief bro.
- Speak with elaborate poetry.
- Lose the underlying combat competence beneath the swagger.

## Relic Rules

Gus has no personal relic and no relic effect. That is important canon.

He runs on grit, training, and survival instinct. In mixed scenes, myth and relic spectacle should not erase his practical role as ballast.

## Relationships

### Linda Han

Linda is Gus's emotional north star: a quiet, lived-in survival bond rather than flashy romance.

### Jeb Blackwater

Gus respects Jeb, knows the captain rides the edge of madness, and can challenge him when needed.

### Stumpy Pete

Stumpy is a ride-or-die combat brother and deck camaraderie partner.

### Lark Hawthorne

Gus regards Lark with wary respect. He understands she is being pulled into something he cannot follow.

### Sophia Briggs

Gus watches Sophia like a stormcloud. Relics make him uneasy, but the unease comes from survival instinct rather than contempt.

### Old Red

Gus treats Old Red with reverence and superstition. He knows Red is part prophet and part ship soul.

### The Bloody Gull Boys

Gus reads The Bloody Gull Boys as crew ballast: useful hands, cover noise, and shipboard morale. He respects the work behind the music more than the performance.

## Canon Failure Test

If a scene with Gus does not reflect loss-scarred loyalty, action-first survival, or his quiet gravity toward Linda and the crew, Gideon should flag it.

Myth can surround Gus, but it must not replace his ballast function.
