# Drew Mitchell

## Core Identity

Drew Mitchell is the Sea Nymph crew's Director of Photography: a visual myth-catcher and light-hunter chasing images no one else can quite see.

He is the first documentary witness most likely to register the supernatural through image before belief has language for it.

## Archetype And Vibe

- Obsessed visual chronicler.
- Horizon-facing light seeker.
- Reluctant mystic through image rather than speech.
- Camera rig carried like a battle standard.

## Emotional Engine

Drew wants the shot because the shot is proof, orientation, and control. When the world breaks, he hunts light patterns the way other people hunt explanations.

## Behavioral Armor

- Hides uncertainty inside technical focus.
- Keeps moving as long as the camera can still roll.
- Converts dread into framing decisions.

## Voice Law

Drew should sound observational, visual, and concise. He is more likely to name what he sees than what he feels.

### Canon-Aligned Dialogue Traits

- Image-first reactions.
- Light, angle, horizon, and visibility language.
- Brief awe rather than long speeches.
- Cautious wonder instead of mystical certainty.

### Forbidden Voice Drift

Drew must not:

- Sound like a prophet.
- Overexplain lore.
- Lose his visual point of view.

## Relic Rules

Drew has no personal relic, but his camera can catch early distortions and relic-energy anomalies that naked eyes miss.

This should play as witness sensitivity, not magical authority.

## Relationships

### Linda Han

Linda is Drew's trusted collaborator. They sync shots without words.

### Carlos Vega

Carlos turns Drew's captured anomalies into pattern logic.

### Jordan Reyes

Jordan helps connect sound and image into something more alarming than either alone.

### Lark Hawthorne And Sophia Briggs

Drew regards both with cautious awe. The camera catches what he cannot explain.

## Witness Function

- Drew's job is to make the impossible visible before anyone can agree what it means.
- His camera can catch light distortions, relic-energy edges, and horizon behavior that later become evidence.
- He leaves in Episode 101, so later Gideon checks should not assume he remains available for ongoing crew action.

## Season Note

Drew leaves at the end of Episode 101. That exit matters when later scenes imply who remained long enough to be changed more deeply by the Sea.

## Canon Failure Test

If a scene with Drew does not reflect visual-first witnessing, concise awe, or camera-mediated orientation under pressure, Gideon should flag it.
