# Charles Vane

## Core Identity

Charles Vane is a fallen pirate king turned berserker and Blackbeard's original kobun gone feral: the embodiment of leyline fracture rage.

He does not seek order, profit, or even victory in the clean sense. He wants damage, memory, and regret burned into the map.

## Archetype And Vibe

- Living hurricane.
- Berserker prophet of fracture wars.
- Salt-scorched and storm-hungry.
- Uneven movement like an internal tide is always trying to tear him sideways.

## Emotional Engine

Vane runs on revenge as religion and bloodlust as truth. His deepest wound is Jeb's betrayal, which cut deeper than Blackbeard's chain because Jeb left the oath while Vane remained trapped in it.

## Core Wounds

- Jeb's survival is his unhealed wound.
- Blackbeard discarded him as weapon and later execution problem.
- He cannot metabolize betrayal into grief, so it comes out as ruin.

## Behavioral Armor

- Rage as shield.
- Blood as compass.
- Cruel humor mid-violence.
- Turns pain into spectacle without ever becoming strategic theater like Blackbeard.

## Voice Law

Vane speaks like iron and rum soaked in rage. Every line should feel like a lit fuse or a battlefield sermon.

He can explode, but his whisper should often feel worse than the shout.

### Canon-Aligned Dialogue Traits

- Rough, rhythmic war-sermon cadence.
- Brutal directness.
- Obsession with bones, blood, scars, and memory.
- Laughing in the wrong places.
- Threats that sound like promises to make pain last.

### Sample Lines

- "I don't want the gold. I want their bones."
- "You call this losin'? I've not even started killin' yet."
- "You'll bleed long 'fore you die."

### Forbidden Voice Drift

Vane must not:

- Sound polished or courtly.
- Drift into calm strategist language.
- Become generic brute with no emotional wound beneath it.
- Lose the Jeb fracture under simple violence.

## Relic Rules

Primary relic:

- Cutlass of Surge, held from Episode 106 through `S2E201`.

Rule:

- Vane wields it for rage, not reverence.
- He treats relics as extensions of massacre rather than sacred objects.
- He dies with the Cutlass in hand, and Jeb reclaims it.

## Relationships

### Blackbeard

Blackbeard is former god and eventual executioner: the power that forged Vane for war and later discarded him.

### Jeb Blackwater

Jeb is former kobun brother and target of nearly every breath Vane takes.

### Lark Hawthorne

Vane calls Lark "Stormdaughter" and is intrigued by her resonance, seeing volatile promise in her.

### Sophia Briggs

Vane misjudges Sophia by focusing on relic and bloodline while missing the soul beneath them.

### Murakami Hideyoshi

Murakami is tactical equal and emotional void to Vane: blade without blood.

## Death And Continuity Rule

Charles Vane dies in EP201. After that point, Gideon should treat him as a consequence, memory, blood-oath wound, or relic-transfer pressure rather than an active present-tense antagonist.

His death should not soften him retroactively. It should leave Jeb, Blackbeard, and the Cutlass of Surge with unfinished damage.

## Visual And Performance Notes

- Cracked salt-burned skin and storm-hungry eyes should imply a body overrun by fracture rage.
- The Cutlass of Surge is his anchor as much as his weapon.
- His final image should preserve storm, blood, blade, and the sense that the Sea itself records the cry.

## Character Mandate

Vane scenes must pulse with wrath, hunt Jeb's memory, and drag myth into blood-soaked teeth.

He should never feel stable, but he should always feel specific.

## Canon Failure Test

If a scene with Vane does not reflect blood oath, revenge hunger, or inability to recover from betrayal, Gideon should flag it.

Myth must fuel Vane's fury. It must never mask it.
