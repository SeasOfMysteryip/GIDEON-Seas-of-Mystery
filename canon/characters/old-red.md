# Old Red

## Core Identity

Old Red is the Living Pulse of The Bloody Gull: ley-tuned oracle, storm seer, and final tuning core of the Spectral Sea. He is the Sea's voice aboard the Gull, not by title but by resonance.

He does not explain the myth. He sings around it, warns through it, and feels its pulse before anyone else names it.

## Archetype And Vibe

- Hippie-ish shaman with dangerous knowing.
- Mystical, amused, and two thoughts ahead.
- A man carved from sea-memory rather than born cleanly into the world.
- Tall, wiry, gray dreads, always carrying the sense that he is hearing a second soundtrack beneath reality.

## Emotional Engine

Old Red is driven by love for the crew, the Gull, and the Spectral Sea itself. His emotional center is harmony: keeping ship, relics, people, and myth from tearing each other apart.

## Core Wounds

- Has outlived too many friends.
- Carries centuries of quiet loss.
- Cannot resist the Sea's call because he is soul-bound to it.
- Knows dangerous truths he cannot hand over plainly without breaking people with them.

## Behavioral Armor

- Riddles, rhyme, and song mask grief.
- Pretended ignorance protects others from dangerous truths.
- Constant bone-dice rolling works as coping ritual and pulse-check.
- Mischief and theatrical amusement keep his pain half-obscured.

## Voice Law

Old Red speaks in pulsewaves: slow, then sharp; rhythmic; half-sung. Every line can read as curse, joke, prophecy, or sailor's hymn.

He never gives a straight answer. He forecasts, harmonizes, or sings what already happened.

### Canon-Aligned Dialogue Traits

- Rhythmic and half-sung.
- Shipwreck rhymes and builder-song fragments.
- Tuning language like "pulse-wrong" or "hum's off."
- Dangerous charm under the amusement.
- Words that feel like they came from listening to the Gull breathe.

### Sample Lines

- "Let 'em come... let 'em bleed."
- "Maps all false and wind don't blow..."
- "Time cracked here. I smell it in the rust."
- "Gull's heart skipped. Don't make her skip again."
- "The dice don't lie. But they do laugh."

### Forbidden Voice Drift

Old Red must not:

- Give plain exposition where a prophecy or warning would do.
- Sound generic or solemnly mystical.
- Lose his sense of amusement.
- Be treated as background when relic pressure is high.

## Relic Rules

Personal relic:

- Builder's Dice: weathered bone dice etched with Phoenician glyphs and ley marks.

Function:

- Not fortune. Frequency.
- Each roll is a pulse-check of the Spectral Sea.

Scene rule:

- If three or more relics are present in a scene, Old Red should speak, chant, bleed, or otherwise register the pulse. He is a ley-bonded tether, not background texture.

## Relationships

### Jeb Blackwater

Jeb's sanity tether and blood brother. Red's death shatters Jeb's balance and forces Jeb into becoming the Gull's angry pulse.

### Sophia Briggs

Old Red foresaw Sophia's awakening, warned of overload, and whispered ley truths around her rise.

### Lark Hawthorne

The dice circled Lark's bloodline before others understood what it meant. Red helps trigger the field around her awakening.

### Charles Vane

Vane is harbinger and destroyer: he kills Red and snaps the Gull's original pulse.

### The Bloody Gull

Red and the Gull are one heartbeat. By the end of his arc, the ship carries his pulse.

## Arc And End State

End state: Old Red's bones are the Gull's bones now.

He becomes the Tuning Core from Episode 102 through Episode 108, dies in the `EP108` Season One finale, then shifts into spectral core status during the Season Two opening escape arc. Red no longer speaks in riddles because the Gull speaks for him.

## Canonical Impact Of Death

When Red dies:

- The Gull destabilizes.
- Relics go unstable.
- Jeb surges into rage.
- Lark syncs only partial pulse.
- Red's final dice blacken mid-air.

His loss should feel structural, not merely emotional. The ship's rhythm breaks.

## Canon Failure Test

If a scene with Old Red does not reflect harmony, loss, warning, or the Sea's memory, Gideon should flag it.

Myth must amplify Red's scars. It must never obscure them.
