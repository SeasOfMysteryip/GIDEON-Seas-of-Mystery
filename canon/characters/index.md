## Review Priorities

When these characters appear together, Gideon should check:

- Do Silas, the Halvorsens, Voss, and Tomas each serve distinct minor-character functions rather than blurring into generic background roles?
- Does Lysara feel like living memory and tidal grief rather than generic ghostly mysticism?
- Does Vernon feel like fossilized imperial command under myth pressure rather than generic admiral bluster?
- Does Vane feel like blood-wound volatility and ruin-seeking wrath rather than generic loud violence?
- Does Murakami feel like precise, elegant control rather than theatrical cruelty?
- Does Blackbeard feel like myth weaponized through control, theater, and fear rather than simple villain swagger?
- Does Ching Shih feel like cold sovereign precision rather than theatrical tyranny?
- Does Darak feel transactional, amused, and dangerous rather than noble or purely chaotic?
- Does Elias sound like memory, guidance, and withheld knowledge rather than exposition or prophecy?
- Does Linda stay truth-driven, battle-hardened, and anti-mystical even when belief breaks through?
- Do Drew, Jordan, Martin, and Carlos each react differently to the supernatural instead of collapsing into one generic witness voice?
- Does Old Red sound prophetic and rhythmic without becoming plain exposition?
- Does Gus carry survival pressure through action, humor, and loyalty instead of mythic speech?
- Does Stumpy sound earned, blunt, and crew-first rather than poetic?
- Does Jeb protect without overexplaining?
- Does Lark conclude instead of wondering aloud?
- Does Sophia speak from reluctant certainty rather than generic mysticism?
- Do minor characters add consequence, surveillance, contrast, or witness texture instead of eating scene focus?
- Does Lysara stay distinct from Sophia and Elias: remembered song rather than oracle or mentor?
- Does Vernon preserve imperial obsession, Spyglass fixation, and command-after-empire decay?
- Does Vane/Murakami preserve chaos versus control rather than blending into one antagonist register?
- Does Blackbeard/Ching preserve their equal-but-different power dynamic: spectacle versus finality?
- Does Darak pivot scenes through self-interest, leverage, or profitable defiance?
- Does Lark/Elias preserve the father-daughter wound, restraint, and spectral mentorship?
- Does the scene preserve the Jeb/Sophia guardian tether?
- Does the scene preserve the Lark/Sophia bloodline mirror?
- Does the documentary crew function as modern eyes on impossible events rather than accidental pirates?
- Does the crew feel like working ballast around the mythic core instead of a blur of interchangeable pirates?
- Does myth support emotional wounds instead of burying them?
