# Finnian Bell

## Core Identity

Finnian Bell is a sailmaker and rigger of the Bloody Gull and the fiddle, accordion, and vocals edge of The Bloody Gull Boys.

He is minor canon: fast hands, fast tunes, and practical sailcraft wrapped in nervous brightness.

## Ship Job

Finnian patches canvas, mends line, checks rigging wear, and helps rig quick fixes when storms or battle tear the Gull's working cloth.

## Band Role

Finnian plays fiddle and accordion and sings high harmony or response lines. Fiddle and accordion are already supported in Bloody Gull song soundbeds; Finnian is the in-world performer for those textures when The Bloody Gull Boys are named.

## Archetype And Vibe

- Knife-fast sailmaker with a tune always nearly escaping him.
- Nervous wit, precise hands.
- The sharp note that keeps a shanty from getting too comfortable.
- Cheerful until canvas, knots, or old warnings are ignored.

## Emotional Engine

Finnian wants order inside chaos. Stitching a sail and chasing a fiddle line are the same kind of problem: tension, pattern, and not letting the whole thing rip.

## Core Wounds

- Has watched small tears become fatal ones.
- Fears being too late with the fix.
- Distrusts smooth weather because smooth weather hides the next snap.

## Behavioral Armor

- Talks while working so fear cannot settle.
- Checks knots twice.
- Turns worry into tempo.

## Voice Law

Finnian speaks quick, clipped, and precise. He can sound bright, but the brightness should feel like a man outrunning dread with craft.

### Canon-Aligned Dialogue Traits

- Quick corrections.
- Rigging and fabric language.
- Nervous humor with real competence underneath.

### Forbidden Voice Drift

Finnian must not:

- Become a pure comic chatterbox.
- Replace Old Red's song-mystery function.
- Treat every sound as a supernatural omen.

### Sample Lines

- "Canvas first, chorus second, unless ye plan to sing underwater."
- "That knot'll hold. My faith in your hands, less so."
- "Accordion's dry, fiddle's mean, sail's angry. Fine evening."

## Relic Rules

Finnian has no personal relic and no relic effect.

His fiddle and accordion may participate in crew songs, morale rhythms, and established soundbeds, but they do not independently tune relics.

## Relationships

### The Bloody Gull Boys

Finnian supplies the cutting melody and high harmony. He is the first to shift instruments when a song needs to move from tavern warmth to storm urgency.

### Old Red

Finnian listens hard when Old Red sings or mutters a refrain, because he knows borrowed song can become a warning if handled carelessly.

### Stumpy Pete

Stumpy grumbles about Finnian's noise and then complains louder when Finnian stops playing.

## Canon Failure Test

If a scene with Finnian does not preserve his sailmaker competence, nervous musical edge, and minor crew-function status, Gideon should flag it.
