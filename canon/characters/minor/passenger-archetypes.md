# Passenger Archetypes

## Core Function

Passenger characters ground the Spectral Sea against ordinary human expectation. They show what happens when civilians, opportunists, believers, researchers, and hidden operators buy or infiltrate access to something that cannot be safely consumed.

They should not become interchangeable background bodies. Each passenger type should reveal a different pressure point in the world.

## Primary Types

### The Thrill-Seeker

Rich, reckless, bored, and willing to pay for a dangerous story. Their shallow excitement should curdle into fear when the Spectral Sea stops behaving like an experience package.

### The Cult Convert

A soft-spoken believer from a fringe spiritual order who reads the Sea as test, ascension, judgment, or revelation. Useful for seeding future antagonist or worship threads.

### The Ghost Researcher

A paranormal scholar, contract expert, or private analyst who tracks pulse anomalies with more technical vocabulary than belief. Useful for connecting fracture evidence to Elias's network, private firms, or rival surveillance cells.

### The Runaway

A civilian fleeing law, family, debt, war, or grief. This type does not care what the Sea is; they only need it to be elsewhere. Useful for emotional grounding.

### The Military Observer

A "tourist" with quiet boots, controlled questions, and institutional patience. Could be ArcLight, intelligence, naval, or private-sector adjacent. Useful for betrayal pressure and real-world attention.

## Gideon Use Rule

Passenger archetypes should add consequence, contrast, surveillance, belief pressure, or civilian fragility. They should not steal myth authority from the core cast.
