# Bram Pike

## Core Identity

Bram Pike is the Bloody Gull's carpenter's mate and the upright-bass-and-vocals anchor of The Bloody Gull Boys.

He is minor canon: ballast, repair muscle, rhythm floor, and shipboard color.

## Ship Job

Bram patches planks, braces damaged bulkheads, secures cargo, repairs benches and railings, and knows which groans in the ship mean strain, rot, impact, or something stranger.

## Band Role

Bram plays upright bass and sings low harmony for The Bloody Gull Boys. His bass is battered, reinforced, and usually lashed near the galley or performance space when not in use.

## Archetype And Vibe

- Big-handed repairman with sawdust in his cuffs.
- The low note under the crew's panic.
- Patient until a fool damages good wood.
- More comfortable holding rhythm than attention.

## Emotional Engine

Bram wants the ship to hold. Every repair, every bass note, and every low harmony comes from the same instinct: keep the deck beneath them from giving way.

## Core Wounds

- Survived a wreck where the ship broke faster than the crew could understand.
- Trusts wood, rope, and rhythm more than speeches.
- Carries quiet fear that no repair is ever final in the Spectral Sea.

## Behavioral Armor

- Slow answers.
- Physical steadiness.
- Dry understatement when everyone else gets loud.

## Voice Law

Bram speaks sparingly, with low, practical certainty. When he jokes, it lands heavy and late.

### Canon-Aligned Dialogue Traits

- Short repair-minded observations.
- Bass-register calm under pressure.
- Deadpan warnings about structural danger.

### Forbidden Voice Drift

Bram must not:

- Become generic gentle-giant comic relief.
- Speak in florid pirate lyricism.
- Treat the bass as a magical object.

### Sample Lines

- "Deck's holding. Your nerve might not."
- "That crack weren't there before the chorus."
- "Sing lower. Ship likes low."

## Relic Rules

Bram has no personal relic and no relic effect.

His sensitivity to ship sounds is craft and long exposure, not supernatural diagnosis.

## Relationships

### The Bloody Gull Boys

Bram is the band's bottom end and practical stop-signal. If he quits playing to listen to the hull, the others pay attention.

### Stumpy Pete

Stumpy trusts Bram's read on damaged wood and hates admitting it.

### Gus Maddox

Bram and Gus share action-first respect: no ceremony, just hands where the work is.

## Canon Failure Test

If a scene with Bram does not preserve his repair function, low-harmony steadiness, and minor crew ballast role, Gideon should flag it.
