# Callum Thorne

## Core Identity

Callum Thorne is a topman of the Bloody Gull and the guitar-and-vocals lead of The Bloody Gull Boys, the ship's rough working band.

He is minor canon: a visible piece of shipboard life, morale, and oral tradition, not a hidden relic bearer or major plot engine.

## Ship Job

Callum works the upper rigging, trims sail under pressure, watches weather changes from the yards, and helps relay deck calls when wind or battle noise makes normal orders useless.

## Band Role

Callum plays guitar and sings lead or call lines for The Bloody Gull Boys. His playing should feel percussive, salt-stiff, and useful on deck: a rhythm the crew can haul, stomp, or brace to.

## Archetype And Vibe

- Crow's-nest charmer with rope burns on both hands.
- Working sailor first, performer second.
- Flashy enough to lead a chorus, practical enough to stop mid-song for a sail tear.
- Young enough to grin at danger, old enough not to mistake it for romance.

## Emotional Engine

Callum wants the crew to keep moving together. Music is how he turns fear into timing, timing into work, and work into survival.

## Core Wounds

- Has seen panic kill faster than cannon fire.
- Carries guilt over one bad rigging call that cost another sailor a fall.
- Hates silence after battle because silence makes him count who is missing.

## Behavioral Armor

- Grins before he thinks.
- Keeps his hands busy with strings, knots, or sail ties.
- Uses charm to lighten pressure without pretending danger is small.

## Voice Law

Callum speaks quick, bright, and deck-practical. He can be musical without becoming poetic.

### Canon-Aligned Dialogue Traits

- Short call-and-response phrasing.
- Sailor timing and work-song cadence.
- Light teasing that turns serious when rigging, weather, or crew safety is at stake.

### Forbidden Voice Drift

Callum must not:

- Become a mystic singer or prophecy voice.
- Outshine Jeb, Old Red, or the main crew in myth scenes.
- Sound like a modern rock frontman.

### Sample Lines

- "Hands on rope, eyes on Cal. Pull when I holler."
- "If the mast starts singing back, that's my cue to stop."
- "One more chorus and the fear'll remember who's captain."

## Relic Rules

Callum has no personal relic and no relic effect.

Songs he performs may carry Bloody Gull oral culture, but his music does not create standalone magic unless a separate canon rule or relic scene establishes it.

## Relationships

### The Bloody Gull Boys

Callum often leads the count-in and first verse, but the band works as crew before ego. If the deck needs hands, the song stops.

### Old Red

Callum respects Old Red's older, stranger relationship to song and knows not to mimic prophecy.

### Jeb Blackwater

Callum follows Jeb's commands without performance flourish when the captain's voice cuts through.

## Canon Failure Test

If a scene with Callum does not preserve his function as working topman, shipboard morale lead, and minor crew texture, Gideon should flag it.
