# Storm Profile

## Core Identity

The Storm is a dimensional collision event.

It is not normal weather and not a poetic metaphor. It is the Spectral Sea failing to stay separate from the world cleanly.

## In-World Names

Recognized in-world names include:

- The Storm
- Fracture Surge
- Riftfront
- the Sea's Pulse

Use `Echo Tempest` only with caution, since older terminology in the source module overused `echo` language.

## Core Law

The Storm is Atlantis bleeding through time.

It appears when fracture pressure, relic surge, bloodline ignition, or system collapse reach a threshold the world cannot absorb quietly.

## Primary Components

The storm can include:

- fracture pulse
- leyline surge
- physical-law failure
- temporal static
- entity distortion
- relic recoil
- shared-memory feedback
- glyph-bearing lightning
- mirror-harbor geography folds

These do not need to appear all at once in every scene, but a true major Storm event should feel like more than wind and rain.

## Phenomenological Effects

Storm events may cause:

- ships to emerge too early or too late
- personal memories to overwrite or cross-contaminate
- the Gull to feel incoming pressure before visible onset
- relics to activate, scream, destabilize, or resonate without clean user control
- Watcher or guardian manifestations during peak events

## Character Experience Rule

The Storm should feel different to different characters:

- Sophia feels memory and recognition pressure
- Lark feels time and self-pressure
- Jeb feels blood-heat, warning, and old scar return

If everyone experiences the Storm in exactly the same generic way, Gideon should flag it.

## Origin Hot Zones

Storms cluster most strongly around:

- Bermuda Triangle
- Devil's Sea
- Sargasso Sea
- Point Nemo
- Arctic memory-freeze zones

These sites should preserve distinct pressure profiles, not just “bad weather here.”

## Faction Reading Rule

Different factions read Storm events differently:

- Protectors see trial and filtering pressure
- flame or rebel sects may read divine punishment
- Blackbeard's forces may treat storms as tactical advantage
- Murakami's forces may treat them as tests or systems to be gamed

Gideon should preserve those different readings in strategic scenes.

## Myth Tone

The Storm is the Sea's scream for recognition.

It calls the blood-bound, remembers what history tried to drown, and pulls rather than merely passes.

## Failure Test

Flag storm drift when:

- the Storm behaves like ordinary weather with cosmetic weirdness
- no time, memory, or identity pressure accompanies it
- relics remain unaffected during major surge
- it creates spectacle without metaphysical consequence
