# Mirror Harbors

## Core Identity

Mirror Harbors are fixed threshold ports where real-world ports and the Spectral Sea overlap through permanent, emotionally distorted access points.

They are not illusions, hallucinations, or drifting bleed events. They are conscious echoes born from trade, trauma, piracy, grief, myth, and the ley grid beneath.

## Core Law

You do not sail to a Mirror Harbor. You bleed into one.

Each Mirror Harbor:

- is anchored to a historical seaport of trade, piracy, or myth
- emerges through a permanent portal such as mist rupture, tide fracture, sky crack, or songline drift
- reflects what the port was, will be, or refused to become
- carries a local toll
- warps time, identity, memory, or motive

## Behavior Rules

- Fixed Fracture Anchors: Mirror Harbors do not move.
- Reflections, Not Replicas: The Spectral version carries emotional distortion rather than literal duplication.
- Leyline-Locked: Harbors sit on ancient convergence lines or pulse scars from Atlantean collapse.
- Cost of Entry: Voice, name, time, memory, orientation, or emotional certainty may be taken.
- Faction Use: Watchers, Ghostfleet, Revisionists, cults, smugglers, and independent operators use certain harbors as safe havens, traps, routes, or myth banks.

## Transition Law

Mirror Harbors are infrastructural thresholds in the Sea's bleed system. They are ancient Atlantean in origin and were later adapted by Watchers, Ghostfleet navigators, Revisionist couriers, and the modern bleed economy.

They do not usually warp the surface world. They filter crossing through emotional, biological, or temporal toll.

From a canon standpoint:

- a Mirror Harbor functions like an underground checkpoint
- portals allow access to Spectral drift lanes
- every crossing carries emotional, biological, or temporal cost
- real-world consequence is localized, not apocalyptic
- the real port remains usable while the Spectral twin remembers what was traded or taken

## Harbor Logic

- These harbors do not forget.
- Some show you who you were.
- Others ask you to give it up.
- Every ship that enters bleeds.
- Every ship that exits carries something it did not have before: weight, whisper, drift, loss, signal, debt, or route.

## Usage Scenarios

Use Mirror Harbors for:

- full episode settings with local emotional law
- relic malfunction or amplification
- civilian, military, cult, or passenger drift arcs
- Ghostfleet bases such as Hollow Bastion or Saltcrow Haven
- Revisionist traps such as Tally Rift or Jade Maw
- grounded real-port scenes that open into Spectral consequence without breaking the whole world

## Failure Test

Flag Mirror Harbor drift when:

- a harbor is treated like a generic spooky port
- no toll or local law exists
- the crossing feels consequence-free
- the real port and Spectral twin are not distinguished
- the harbor's emotional logic is missing
