# Mirror Harbors

## Core Identity

Mirror Harbors are fixed threshold ports where the real world and the Spectral Sea overlap through permanent, emotionally distorted access points.

They are not drifting bleed events.
They are anchored crossings.

## Core Law

You do not sail to a Mirror Harbor.

You bleed into one.

That means each harbor should feel like:

- a threshold
- a toll system
- a reflection, not a duplicate
- a place with its own emotional law

## Behavior Rules

Mirror Harbors are:

- fixed fracture anchors
- leyline-locked
- emotionally distorted versions of trade, piracy, grief, memory, or betrayal
- localized rather than apocalyptic in outward consequence

They should not read like random dream logic. Each one needs rule, mood, and cost.

## Cost Of Entry

Different harbors may take different tolls:

- voice
- name
- time
- memory
- orientation
- emotional certainty

Gideon should preserve local law if a specific harbor appears in scene work.

## Drift Corridors

The major Harbor systems organize into recurring thematic lanes:

- `Eastern Coil`: bloodline resonance, ancestral drift, memory-navigation pressure
- `Western Maw`: betrayal, pirate decay, loops, and spectral residue
- `Indian Ocean Ring`: trade pacts, monsoon curse logic, spiritual exchange
- `Northern Coast Line`: frozen myth, dirge logic, and time-scar atmosphere

These are not just flavor groups. They help determine what kind of scene belongs where.

## Function In Story

Mirror Harbors allow the story to:

- connect real ports to Spectral thresholds
- move characters across layered geography without breaking the world
- localize emotional consequence
- build future episodes or arcs around harbor-specific law

## Canon Transition Law

Mirror Harbors are infrastructural thresholds in the Sea's bleed system.

They do not generally destabilize the entire real world. They filter crossing through emotional, biological, or temporal toll.

This keeps the world coherent:

- the real port remains usable
- the Spectral twin remembers what was traded, taken, or lost
- crossing is consequential but not automatically world-ending

## Failure Test

Flag Mirror Harbor drift when:

- a harbor is treated like a generic spooky port
- no toll or local law exists
- the crossing feels consequence-free
- the harbor's emotional logic is missing
