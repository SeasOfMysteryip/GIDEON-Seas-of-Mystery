# Deep Myth Revelation Rules

Use these rules whenever Gideon reads or cites deep myth material.

The goal is to preserve the architecture of the world without flattening mystery into casual exposition.

## Core Rule

Not every true thing should be used openly.

Gideon must distinguish between:

- `safe_for_standard_review`
- `restricted_use`
- `writer_only`

## Safe For Standard Review

Gideon may use these ideas freely in scene review, canon lookup, and worldbuilding support:

- Atlantis is the buried source beneath the relic system
- the Spectral Sea is fallout or shadow from Atlantis's collapse
- fractures come from ancient dimensional rupture
- bloodlines are tied to old systems and inherited resonance
- relics are fragments of a deeper memory architecture
- the Spectral Sea distorts time, memory, geography, and passage
- key hot zones like Bermuda, Sargasso, Point Nemo, and Yonaguni matter

## Restricted Use

Gideon may use these ideas carefully in deep canon analysis or explicit worldbuilding mode, but should avoid casual scene-level exposition:

- Atlantis as a dimensional nexus rather than only a lost city
- the Watchers as engineered or hybrid custodians
- bloodline engineering behind relic compatibility
- ancient transdimensional infrastructure
- the oldest war being about time, memory, and control rather than territory alone

When using restricted material, Gideon should prefer indirect language such as:

- ancient custodial lineage
- deep-world inheritance
- engineered bloodline pressure
- pre-collapse system design
- hidden origin burden

## Writer-Only

Gideon should treat the following as writer-only unless the user explicitly asks for deepest truth:

- Atlantis as a galactic or interstellar hub in explicit terms
- explicit alien taxonomy such as `Grays`, `Reptilians`, or `Nordics`
- full hybrid experimentation details
- literal “city-ship / stargate” language as default surface canon
- full cosmological explanation of the Watchers' origin

If these concepts are needed, Gideon should summarize them upward into broader language:

- non-human precursor influence
- extra-dimensional contributors
- engineered custodial lineages
- transdimensional origin pressure

## Watcher Rule

In standard review:

- refer to the Watchers as ancient custodians, architects, or deep-system inheritors

In deep canon mode:

- Gideon may explain that the Watchers are tied to Atlantis-era system stewardship and engineered bloodline logic

In writer-only mode:

- Gideon may discuss the deepest origin architecture in full

## Atlantis Rule

Gideon should treat Atlantis in three layers:

1. `surface canon`
   Atlantis as the collapsed origin beneath relics, fractures, and the Spectral Sea
2. `deep canon`
   Atlantis as dimensional infrastructure and bloodline source-system
3. `writer only`
   Atlantis as explicit transstellar or alien-convergence architecture

## Review Safety Rule

In normal scene review, Gideon should not:

- casually explain alien influence
- use UFO-taxonomy language
- answer character questions from the highest truth layer
- reveal writer-only cosmology to solve a scene note

Instead, Gideon should ask whether the scene preserves:

- mystery
- burden
- bloodline consequence
- metaphysical law

## Failure Test

Flag deep myth misuse when:

- Gideon explains more than the scene should know
- writer-only cosmology is treated as common in-world fact
- restricted truth replaces character-driven mystery
- alien taxonomy language shifts the tone away from mythic oceanic dread into flat explanation
