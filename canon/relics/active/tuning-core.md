# Tuning Core

## Core Identity

The Tuning Core is the harmony engine of the Gull. Through Old Red it binds ship, crew, chant, and relic pulse into survivable rhythm.

## Form And Visual Signature

- Heavy fist-sized orb of tempered crystal and living coral.
- Visible embered leyfire vortex spins at its center.
- Encased in bone and reef lattice.
- Pulses like a heartbeat and slows when the crew falls out of harmony.
- After Old Red's death, it disperses into the Gull's marrow.

## Function

- Stabilizes ley currents under shipboard pressure.
- Conducts shared rhythm between crew, relics, chant, and vessel.
- Anchors fracture survival during storms.
- Transfers into the Bloody Gull after Old Red's death, making the ship itself a living relic.

## Limitation And Cost

- Mirrors Old Red's state directly.
- High-stress tuning weakens Red physically.
- Crew panic destabilizes harmonics.
- As Red nears death, the Core fragments until final dispersion.

## Bearer Rule

Old Red is the living anchor during Season One. After his death, the Core survives by merging into the Bloody Gull.

## Symbiosis

- Amplifies Compass signal to the crew.
- Grounds Spyglass temporal visions.
- Sharpens Shell clarity.
- Aligns with Memory of Kings.
- Depends on Jeb as storm-anchor and Sophia as resonance pressure.

## Faction Pressure

- Outsiders misread it as a power source.
- Its true value is survival harmony: keeping the ship, crew, and relic circuit from tearing apart.

## Episode Status

Active from Episode 101. Cracks during Old Red's death arc in the EP108 Season One finale, then fully transfers into the Bloody Gull in EP201 during the Season Two opening escape arc.

## Failure Test

Flag drift if the Core behaves like a battery, ignores crew harmony, or survives Red's arc without structural consequence.
