# Compass of Ley Currents

## Core Identity

The Compass of Ley Currents is a bloodline-bound navigation relic that points toward older truth, not ordinary geography. It remembers routes the world has already lost.

## Form And Visual Signature

- Atlantean brass, etched with forgotten ley glyphs.
- Needle moves with memory, not magnetism.
- Cracked face like old glass smoothed by centuries of lost hands.
- Glyphs glow like bioluminescent tidepools when active.
- In Sophia's grip, it pulses hot with bloodline resonance.

## Function

- Charts ancient ley currents across worlds and timelines as they once flowed.
- Guides navigation through fractured realms and liminal bleed zones.
- Detects temporal drift and ghost-route pressure.
- Aligns with existing ley signatures; it does not summon ships or create routes from nothing.

## Limitation And Cost

- Shows what was, not always what is.
- Aggravates Sophia's mixed bloodline pressure as Atlantean pull intensifies.
- Scorches skin through ley feedback and pulse fatigue.
- Past navigators bleed into the present, corrupting Sophia's sense of self.
- Overuse can leave permanent tidal or ley-mark scarring.

## Bearer Rule

Sophia Briggs is the canon-locked bearer. The Compass should never behave like neutral equipment or a clean GPS device.

## Symbiosis

- Shell of Memory decodes Compass flow into interpretable signal.
- Spyglass of Storms visually confirms the routes the Compass pulls toward.
- Memory of Kings layers historical and moral pressure over the route.
- Abyssal Chronometer can cross-flare with it, but that flare is unstable and dangerous.

## Faction Pressure

- Atlantean legacy systems treat it as sacred navigation core.
- Phoenician imitators envy and attempt to counterfeit its function.
- Ghost fleets are drawn to its pulse because they follow memory, not orders.

## Episode Status

Season One active relic and one of the main pressure points in Sophia's awakening arc.

## Failure Test

Flag drift if the Compass behaves like GPS, opens routes without cost, works cleanly for non-bloodline users, or creates routes that were not already latent in the system.
