# Lysara's Locket

## Core Identity

Lysara's Locket is a siren-memory relic built on longing, persuasion, and emotional entrapment. It is not an Atlantean circuit piece and should feel different from the main relic system.

## Form And Visual Signature

- Twin-spiral seashell fused to a silver filigree chain.
- Delicate but unnaturally strong.
- When opened, it plays a hypnotic melody across water, storm, and fracture tide.
- Melody manifests as air-ripples bending light.
- Shell ridges emit soft bioluminescence.

## Function

- Influences listeners through mesmeric song.
- Amplifies longing, regret, and emotional vulnerability.
- Carries fragments of Lysara's memory.
- Can stabilize or worsen storm and fracture states through song resonance.

## Limitation And Cost

- Haunts bearer and listener with lingering melody.
- Loops unresolved desire and emotional memory.
- Erodes emotional stability under repeated use.
- Is especially dangerous to those tied to Lysara's memory.

## Bearer Rule

Jeb is the original emotional bearer. Darak becomes the active political bearer after the trade for Sophia. Those relationships should read differently.

## Symbiosis

- Pulses near Sophia through emotional-frequency cross-resonance.
- Serves as a counterpoint to war-pulse relics like the Beacon.
- Can influence council moods and political manipulation.

## Faction Pressure

- Political manipulators and council players value it as leverage.
- It should read as a high-risk emotional weapon, not a battlefield power relic.

## Episode Status

Gifted to Jeb in Season One, haunts his arc, and is traded to Darak for Sophia's freedom.

## Failure Test

Flag drift if the locket acts like neutral music magic, loses its Lysara tether, or stops exacting emotional cost.
