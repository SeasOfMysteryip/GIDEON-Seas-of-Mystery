# Blackbeard's Ring

## Core Identity

Blackbeard's Ring is a sovereign tether relic: command, ambition, ghost-fleet control, and life extended through spectral hunger.

## Form And Visual Signature

- Forged iron signet ring carved with Blackbeard's flaming-crown sigil.
- Ember-blue ghost fire flickers inside the seal.
- Metal is unnaturally cold and thrums at leyline intersections.
- Spectral silhouettes swirl inside the ring's shadow.
- During fleet command, fire cracks along the wearer's knuckles.

## Function

- Commands dead fleets with exact authority.
- Raises shipwrecks and binds drowned souls into naval service.
- Channels ley surge during storms.
- Tethers the bearer's continued existence to spectral dominion.

## Limitation And Cost

- Feeds on ambition and ley hunger.
- Cannot be safely removed after full bond.
- Punishes hesitation and diminishing will.
- Acts more like a shackle than a crown.

## Bearer Rule

Blackbeard is the canon bearer. The Ring should feel inseparable from his command identity.

## Symbiosis

- Flares near Crown of the Abyss.
- Responds to Compass presence.
- Intensifies at fracture crossings and fleet convergence.

## Faction Pressure

- Dead-command artifact tied to Blackbeard's long game.
- Others may covet it, but its law is bonded ambition.

## Episode Status

Active through Season One council, convergence, and manipulation beats, with future imbalance seeded through its clash with the Crown.

## Failure Test

Flag drift if the Ring feels removable, morally neutral, or disconnected from Blackbeard's sovereign will and spectral debt.
