# Relic Circuit

The relic circuit is an Atlantean mnemonic engine built to test whether the world has earned the right to remember.

It is not a treasure map and not a random collection of artifacts. It is a sequence of linked nodes whose activation reshapes navigation, perception, memory, balance, force, and life across the Spectral Sea.

## Core Law

When the circuit completes, the sea awakens.

This does not mean every node must be physically present in every scene. It means relic behavior should be understood as part of a larger system rather than isolated magic items.

## Node Map

### Navigation Node
- Relic: `Compass of Ley Currents`
- Symbol: The Wayfinder
- Function: guides through veils, fracture routes, and older ley paths
- Rule: without navigation, the circuit cannot orient

### Perception Node
- Relic: `Spyglass of Storms`
- Symbol: The Seer
- Function: confirms hidden routes, time fractures, and unstable currents
- Rule: perception follows direction, but does not guarantee truth

### Language Node
- Relic: `Shell of Memory`
- Symbol: The Voice
- Function: translates pulses, buried systems, memory signals, and Sea-language
- Rule: revelation is partial and emotionally charged

### Memory Node
- Relic: `Memory of Kings`
- Symbol: The Chronicle
- Function: records, projects, and tempts authorship over fate
- Rule: memory stabilizes direction but also burdens the bearer with choice

### Consciousness Node
- Relic: `Pearl of Lucidity`
- Symbol: The Dreamer
- Function: bridges minds, dreams, and non-physical thresholds
- Rule: expands circuit pressure beyond the material world

### Harmony Node
- Relic: `Tuning Core / Heart Stone`
- Symbol: The Pulsekeeper
- Function: binds the circuit into coherent rhythm
- Rule: without harmony, the circuit splinters

### Balance Node
- Relic: `Atlantean Astrolabe`
- Symbol: The Equilibria
- Function: maintains equilibrium across systems, worlds, and timelines
- Rule: prevents ley stress from collapsing the network

### Power Node
- Relic: `Cutlass of Surge`
- Symbol: The Stormblade
- Function: directs raw ley force into defense, rupture, or amplification
- Rule: power without harmony or balance invites destructive overload

### Environment Node
- Relic: `Stormglass`
- Symbol: The Stormcaller
- Function: stabilizes or controls external storm conditions
- Rule: environmental mastery should never feel disconnected from circuit stress

### Life Node
- Relic: `Coral Fragment`
- Symbol: The Lifegiver
- Function: restores life, ecology, and post-war balance
- Rule: without life, the circuit becomes conquest without future

## Circuit Flow

The source sequence is:

`Compass -> Spyglass -> Shell -> Memory -> Pearl -> Heart Stone -> Astrolabe -> Cutlass -> Stormglass -> Coral -> Loop`

Working canon meaning:

- direction begins the process
- perception confirms it
- language interprets it
- memory stabilizes and deepens it
- consciousness expands it
- harmony binds it
- balance keeps it from tearing apart
- power weaponizes or defends it
- environment externalizes it
- life determines whether the system sustains or devours itself

## Node Loss Rule

Loss or corruption of a node destabilizes adjacent pulses.

That means Gideon should track not only which relic is present, but what part of the larger chain is missing, damaged, counterfeit, dormant, or being forced into false service.

## Active Season-One Pressure

Season One primarily concentrates pressure around:

- `Compass of Ley Currents`
- `Shell of Memory`
- `Spyglass of Storms`
- `Memory of Kings`
- `Tuning Core`
- `Cutlass of Surge`
- `Abyssal Chronometer` as unstable Phoenician imitation pressure
- `Spectral Beacon` as convergence catalyst rather than balanced node

These should feel like fragments of a system under assembly, not a fully understood circuit.

## Emergent Season-One Circuit

In Season One, the active relics aboard and around the Bloody Gull should be treated as an emergent circuit even when the characters do not yet understand it in those terms.

Working pressure centers include:

- `Compass of Ley Currents`
- `Shell of Memory`
- `Spyglass of Storms`
- `Memory of Kings`
- `Tuning Core`

The important rule is that the circuit forms through:

- emotional fracture
- bloodline resonance
- proximity under stress
- harmonic pressure

Not through simple possession or inventory completion.

Characters may believe they are surviving one relic at a time. Blackbeard and other higher-order players may recognize that a larger circuit is assembling beneath them.

## Faction Desire Map

The source module frames faction pressure around the circuit like this:

- `Compass`: Sophia and navigator-aligned seekers
- `Spyglass`: Lark and navigator bloodline pressure
- `Shell`: library-facing interpreters, Murakami, and memory-seeking powers
- `Memory of Kings`: Lark, Elias-linked pressure, and factions afraid of authored history
- `Pearl`: espionage or dream-bridge interest
- `Heart Stone / Tuning Core`: crew survival, ship harmony, and Old Red's legacy
- `Astrolabe`: ley architects and systems thinkers
- `Cutlass`: Jeb, Vane, and war-driven claimants
- `Stormglass`: fleet-scale captains and environmental controllers
- `Coral Fragment`: healers, restorers, and ecological custodians

Gideon should use this map to judge motive. Different factions want different nodes for different reasons.

## Strategic Circuit Pressure

The relic circuit also creates a mythic treasure-hunt economy under fracture pressure.

That means factions do not simply hunt artifacts as collectibles. They track:

- which nodes are waking
- which bloodlines are syncing
- which combinations could sever, stabilize, or weaponize the Spectral tether
- who is close to assembling a meaningful chain

This strategic pressure should shape pursuit, alliance, betrayal, and surveillance behavior.

## Blackbeard Rule

Blackbeard should be treated as one of the clearest high-order circuit readers in the setting.

His long-game motive around the circuit is not simple ownership. It is control over:

- assembly
- access
- tether-breaking potential
- memory leverage
- who gets to survive system awakening with autonomy

If Blackbeard is present in a relic strategy scene, Gideon should assume he is evaluating the circuit beneath the visible action.

## Counterfeit And Corruption Rule

Not every relic adjacent device belongs cleanly to the circuit.

Some artifacts, especially Phoenician imitations and corrupted mirrors, should be treated as:

- counterfeit nodes
- unstable amplifiers
- domination hacks
- predatory lures

The `Abyssal Chronometer` is the clearest current example: it mimics navigation and timing pressure without true harmonic integrity.

## Story Use Rule

In scene review, Gideon should ask:

1. Which node is active here?
2. What part of the circuit function is being pressured?
3. Is this activation harmonizing the system, distorting it, or forcing it?
4. Does the scene understand the relic as part of the circuit, or is it using it like a disconnected trick?

## Failure Test

Flag circuit drift when:

- relics are treated as unrelated collectibles
- counterfeit devices are treated like true nodes without consequence
- the circuit appears to grant power with no systemic stress
- a scene forgets that balance, harmony, and life are part of the same system as force and navigation
- convergence events happen without broader faction, fleet, or emotional consequences
