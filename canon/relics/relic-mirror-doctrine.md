# Relic Mirror Doctrine

The relics of the Spectral Sea are not prizes, power-ups, or generic ancient weapons.

They are memory under pressure.
They are survival made material.
They reflect what humanity needs in order to endure the storm without forgetting itself.

## Primary Law

Relics are mirrors before they are instruments.

A relic does not simply give power. It reveals hunger, legacy, grief, desire, loyalty, fear, or control in whoever reaches for it.

If a scene treats a relic like a convenient solution instead of a mirror of consequence, Gideon should flag it.

## Human Meaning Layer

At doctrine level, the core relic system reflects survival forces:

| Relic | Human Force |
| --- | --- |
| Compass of Ley Currents | Purpose and direction against the drift. |
| Shell of Memory | Communication and the bridge between selves. |
| Spyglass of Storms | Perception, longing, and the danger of seeing too much. |
| Memory of Kings | History, authorship, and the burden of choice. |
| Spectral Beacon | Hope distorted into summons and convergence pressure. |
| Tuning Core | Love, harmony, and the pulse that keeps a ship from tearing itself apart. |

These are not abstract symbols only. Their symbolic meaning should shape how they activate, whom they burden, and what they cost.

## Commandment

The relics are not prizes. They are oaths.

To take hold of one is to enter a relationship with memory, consequence, and the Sea's older laws.

## Relic Behavior Rules

- Relics do not solve problems cleanly.
- Relics expose what was buried under the problem.
- Activation should come through pressure, not convenience.
- Cost should track with intimacy, obsession, bloodline strain, or emotional overload.
- A relic's symbolic function should match its practical function.
- Symbiosis matters: relics strengthen, distort, or destabilize one another.

## Motive Engine

No one chases relics only for power.

Characters and factions chase validation, salvation, identity, control, revenge, belonging, or survival. The relic becomes the pressure point where that desire shows its true shape.

Ask in every relic scene:

1. What does the character think this relic will give them?
2. What truth does the relic actually expose instead?

## Failure Test

Flag relic drift when:

- a relic behaves like generic magic
- activation has no emotional or symbolic cost
- a bearer uses a relic outside its relationship law
- a scene uses relics only to move plot and not reveal character
- multiple relics are present but show no resonance, distortion, or pressure on one another
