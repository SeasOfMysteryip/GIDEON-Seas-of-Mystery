# Spectral Naval Combat Codex

This file contains the core named maneuvers Gideon should treat as locked tactical doctrine. These are not visual labels. Each maneuver has a battlefield purpose, typical users, cost, and lawful spectral twist.

## Tactical Laws

- Tactics are not interchangeable spectacle. Each named maneuver carries a tactical purpose, typical users, and a spectral twist.
- Spectral twists should intensify the maneuver, not replace its naval logic.
- Named maneuvers should preserve faction identity. Ching Shih should not fight like Vane, Murakami should not fight like Jeb, and Blackbeard should not fight like Darak.
- Relic effects in battle should create cost, disorientation, exposure, or tactical consequence. They should not become free superweapons.
- If a tactic is named on-page, the scene should show why that name matters.

## Named Maneuvers

### The Bladebreaker Maneuver

- Inspiration: Battle of Trafalgar, divide-and-break line action.
- Used by: Jeb Blackwater, Old Red, Captain Lysara in legend.
- Core tactic: ram or cut through the enemy line at a perpendicular angle to shatter formation and disrupt the enemy's ley circuit.
- Spectral twist: the Gull times the impact to a relic pulse; enemy relics panic-flare on contact.
- Cost/risk: requires precise ship-rhythm alignment. Mistimed use can damage the attacking vessel's own pulse integrity.
- Gideon check: Bladebreaker should feel like decisive line rupture, not generic ramming.

### The Driftburn Gambit

- Inspiration: historical fire-ship attacks.
- Used by: Revisionist cults, dead-ship operators, desperate faction cells.
- Core tactic: sacrifice a hollowed, relic-dead, cursed, or burning ship and drive it into the enemy at the moment of highest formation density.
- Spectral twist: the dead ship carries crew echoes that sing or plead across the water seconds before ignition.
- Cost/risk: the gambit contaminates nearby waters with memory residue and can burn allies if the fracture current turns.
- Gideon check: Driftburn should feel like sacrificial horror, not a normal explosive.

### The Pulse Choke

- Inspiration: Battle of Salamis, using narrow water to erase enemy advantage.
- Used by: Ching Shih, Ghostfleet harbormasters, bottleneck commanders.
- Core tactic: lure an enemy into a ley-throat, fracture corridor, harbor mouth, or narrow current, then seal the exit.
- Spectral twist: false bearings, ghost maps, and corrupted navigation make escape routes look open after they have already closed.
- Cost/risk: requires control of the local channel or a superior map of the fracture field.
- Gideon check: Pulse Choke should create compression and helplessness before impact.

### The Fog Rattle

- Inspiration: Battle of the Nile, strike under cover and confusion.
- Used by: Murakami Hideyoshi, mist predators, precision ambush ships.
- Core tactic: attack during ley fog or pulse confusion, especially against anchored, sleeping, or disoriented ships.
- Spectral twist: time-sense fractures before the crew realizes the battle has begun.
- Cost/risk: the attacker must know the field better than the fog does. Poor execution turns the ambush into self-blindness.
- Gideon check: Fog Rattle should feel surgical, quiet, and out-of-sync.

### The Ghostlash Feint

- Inspiration: Battle of the River Plate, harass-and-misdirect.
- Used by: Lark Hawthorne, Revisionist saboteurs, projection-capable ships.
- Core tactic: harass with illusions, fast movement, or false safe zones until the enemy commits to the wrong vector.
- Spectral twist: phantom fleets, projection assaults, and false hull signatures make the rear strike feel impossible.
- Cost/risk: requires a credible false image and can collapse if the target has better pulse reading.
- Gideon check: Ghostlash should misdirect before it wounds.

### The Marrowline Cut

- Inspiration: predator ambush and command-target strike.
- Used by: Vernon, ley corsairs, relic extraction hunters.
- Core tactic: strike at relic-bearers, helms, command centers, or pulse anchors rather than trying to destroy the whole ship.
- Spectral twist: mist camouflage and pulse masking leave crews intact while the thing that mattered disappears.
- Cost/risk: failure exposes the attacker as a surgical predator and can trigger retaliation from the whole fleet.
- Gideon check: Marrowline Cut should remove a core function, not just kill bodies.

### The Shatterwake Coil

- Inspiration: submarine pack hunt and encirclement pressure.
- Used by: Blackbeard's ringbearers, deep-hunt ships, dread fleets.
- Core tactic: spiral around prey, compress its pulse field, and collapse interface control from within.
- Spectral twist: ship-image multiplication makes the prey see hundreds where there are few; hulls fracture from ley feedback before cannon fire.
- Cost/risk: requires enough presence or false presence to make the prey's own fear feed the coil.
- Gideon check: Shatterwake should feel like domination through pressure and dread.

### The Leviathan Pulse

- Inspiration: directed-energy strike and line rupture.
- Used by: The Bloody Gull from late Season One forward only under rare alignment.
- Core tactic: charge a relic triad or ship pulse into a precise ley rupture fired in a narrow vector.
- Spectral twist: Shell, Spyglass, ship pulse, or related anchors can create temporal rupture: memory burn, time displacement, or moment-unwriting.
- Cost/risk: dangerous to relic bearers and ship stability. It should never be casual artillery.
- Gideon check: Leviathan Pulse needs visible consequence and should not solve a battle for free.

### The False Flag Spiral

- Inspiration: deceptive surrender and signal warfare.
- Used by: Mirage fleets, signal forgers, Darak-style opportunists, some Revisionist cells.
- Core tactic: use known relic frequencies, flags, voices, or distress signals to drop enemy defenses.
- Spectral twist: counterfeit relic frequencies project familiar voices or trusted signals across the tide.
- Cost/risk: once detected, it poisons trust in all future signals.
- Gideon check: False Flag Spiral should be betrayal by signal, not mere disguise.

### The Crown's Reckoning

- Inspiration: psychological domination and sovereign collapse.
- Used by: Ching Shih as a final protocol.
- Core tactic: do almost nothing. Let command presence, relic pressure, and fleet discipline convince the enemy the fight has already ended.
- Spectral twist: the Crown of the Abyss darkens enemy relics and black water rises without contact.
- Cost/risk: only works if her authority already owns the emotional field.
- Gideon check: Crown's Reckoning should end through finality and surrender pressure, not spectacle.
