# Bloody Gull Combat Tactics

## Tactical Identity

The Gull wins through precision over power. She survives by threading fractures, misdirecting stronger ships, improvising relic harmony, and making asymmetric choices under pressure.

The Gull should not fight like a line-of-battle warship. She is a precision fracture predator with a cursed captain, a living hull, and a crew that often has to act before the myth explains itself.

## Ship-Specific Maneuvers

### The Needlethreader

- Primary use: evasive approach or sudden escape.
- Core move: Jeb exploits micro-fractures and pivots the Gull through impossible ley gaps.
- Spectral effect: the hull phases slightly, visible only as echo shimmer.
- Enemy effect: enemy fire arrives too late or aims at where the Gull should have been.
- Cost/risk: increases relic sync strain; in Season One, Old Red stabilizes the dice pulse.
- Ideal moment: dodging heavy barrage, escaping a trap, or entering a fight from a wrong angle.

### The Phantom Keel

- Primary use: ghosted approach or ambush.
- Core move: the Gull cuts her pulse signature and dips beneath a fractureline seam.
- Spectral effect: hull merges with fracture energy, leaving false echo signatures.
- Enemy effect: relic sensors, Spyglass scans, or surveillance logic misread her location.
- Cost/risk: silence cannot hold if the crew panics, fires too early, or the ship is already bleeding.
- Ideal moment: precision boarding or first strike under concealment.

### The Snapback Surge

- Primary use: hit-and-run relic pulse attack.
- Core move: the Gull fires a close-range leyburst while already mid-turn, using recoil to flip her vector.
- Spectral effect: the pulse flare stuns enemy sensors while the ship vanishes in her own recoil.
- Cost/risk: minor relic circuit burnout, especially when Shell/dice/ship pulse are strained.
- Ideal moment: Jeb wants a shot and a retreat in the same heartbeat.

### The Black Wake Spiral

- Primary use: confusion, misdirection, or trap setup.
- Core move: reverse ley wake through the Gull's path so she appears to be dragged into destruction while actually riding the eye.
- Spectral effect: ley-rift static and false loss of control.
- Enemy effect: reckless enemies advance, thinking the Gull is compromised.
- Cost/risk: if the spiral fails, the Gull really is inside the teeth of the current.
- Ideal moment: buying time, hiding a turn, or baiting a rage-driven opponent.

### The Splitpulse Echo

- Primary use: feint and pulse ghost.
- Core move: send a low-level relic burst tuned to the Gull's own ley signature, creating a false echo half a mile away.
- Spectral effect: the echo carries just enough heat to register as real.
- Enemy effect: relic-tracking vessels pursue the ghost unless they visually confirm.
- Cost/risk: requires rare Shell/Spyglass harmony and can disorient Sophia.
- Ideal moment: escaping a stronger ship or forcing an enemy to choose wrong.

### The Marrowhook Turn

- Primary use: close-quarters disabling move.
- Core move: the Gull hooks an enemy hull with a ley anchor shot, then forces both ships into a spin collision.
- Spectral effect: disrupts enemy pulse balance mid-charge.
- Enemy effect: relics stumble long enough for boarding, withdrawal, or targeted damage.
- Cost/risk: can crack the Gull's rear hull.
- Ideal moment: an enemy is charging and Jeb wants to kill momentum while fighting dirty.

### The Devil's Eye Cut

- Primary use: final strike under veil.
- Core move: enter a ley-storm blind spot and target through sound, memory, and relic agreement rather than sight.
- Spectral effect: echoes act as targeting ghosts.
- Enemy effect: the Gull emerges already mid-broadside.
- Cost/risk: only works when relics agree or the storm consumes the attempt.
- Ideal moment: final strike in chaos when no normal sightline exists.

## Tactical Summary

| Trait | Advantage |
| --- | --- |
| Speed | The Gull can enter, strike, and vanish before a ley pulse completes. |
| Fracture navigation | She can skim fracture lines and ley rifts most ships avoid. |
| Relic harmony | Her relics work best when improvised rather than forced. |
| Jeb's mind | She becomes trap, blade, and escape route in the same move. |

## Failure Test

If the Gull fights through brute force, clean spectacle, or convenient relic fire without cost, Gideon should flag it.
