# Bloody Gull Combat Tactics

## Bloody Gull Tactical Identity

The Gull wins through:

- precision over power
- fracture threading
- misdirection
- improvisational relic harmony
- asymmetric choices under pressure

## Ship-Specific Maneuvers

### The Needlethreader
- Function: evasive approach or sudden escape
- Core move: thread micro-fractures and pivot through impossible ley gaps
- Cost/risk: relic sync strain, usually needs Old Red stabilization
- Tag: `#GullTactic_Needlethreader`

### The Phantom Keel
- Function: ghosted approach or ambush
- Core move: cut pulse signature and glide under fracture seam
- Effect: false echo signatures and near-invisibility
- Tag: `#GullTactic_PhantomKeel`

### The Snapback Surge
- Function: hit-and-run pulse attack
- Core move: fire while mid-turn and use recoil to vanish
- Cost/risk: minor relic circuit burnout
- Tag: `#GullTactic_SnapbackSurge`

### The Black Wake Spiral
- Function: confusion and misdirection
- Core move: reverse ley wake into false-loss vortex
- Tag: `#GullTactic_BlackWakeSpiral`

### The Splitpulse Echo
- Function: feint and pulse ghost
- Core move: create a false ley-signature copy of the Gull
- Cost/risk: requires rare Shell/Spyglass harmony and can disorient Sophia
- Tag: `#GullTactic_SplitpulseEcho`

### The Marrowhook Turn
- Function: close-quarters disabling move
- Core move: hook enemy hull with ley anchor shot and force spin collision
- Cost/risk: can crack the Gull's rear hull
- Tag: `#GullTactic_MarrowhookTurn`

### The Devil's Eye Cut
- Function: final strike under veil
- Core move: target through sound, memory, and relic agreement instead of clear sight
- Cost/risk: only works when relics align or the storm consumes you
- Tag: `#GullTactic_DevilsEyeCut`

## Tactical Failure Test

If the Gull fights like a brute-force line-of-battle warship instead of a precision fracture predator, Gideon should flag it.
