# Historical Battle References

Reference-only. Use for scene inspiration, not hard in-world law.

## Battle Patterns

### Battle of Trafalgar

- Pattern: divide and break.
- Story use: a captain commits to a dangerous perpendicular cut through the line.
- Spectral adaptation: Bladebreaker-style line rupture and ley circuit panic.

### Battle of the Nile

- Pattern: anchor trap and night ambush.
- Story use: the attacker understands enemy position better than the enemy understands its own safety.
- Spectral adaptation: Fog Rattle, ley fog, moored vulnerability, time-sense damage.

### Battle of Copenhagen

- Pattern: feint, blind, negotiate.
- Story use: false confidence, interrupted command logic, and tactical refusal.
- Spectral adaptation: signal deception, command confusion, false-safe maneuvering.

### Battle of Salamis

- Pattern: use the narrow.
- Story use: smaller or more precise force erases enemy size by choosing the channel.
- Spectral adaptation: Pulse Choke in ley throats, harbor mouths, or fracture corridors.

### Battle of Santiago de Cuba

- Pattern: predict and box them in.
- Story use: force escape into a pre-read vector.
- Spectral adaptation: signal decoys and fracture exit ambush.

### Battle of the River Plate

- Pattern: feint and harass.
- Story use: weaker force pressures a stronger one into wrong assumptions.
- Spectral adaptation: Ghostlash Feint, phantom fleet, false safe zone.

### Fire-Ship Tactics

- Pattern: psychological war and sacrificial vector attack.
- Story use: dread arrives before impact.
- Spectral adaptation: Driftburn Gambit, dead ship song, memory fire.

## Failure Test

Historical references should add tactical shape. They should not become exposition or force the scene to copy a real battle beat-for-beat.
