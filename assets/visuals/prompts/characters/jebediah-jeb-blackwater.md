# Jebediah "Jeb" Blackwater

## Core Identity

Jeb Blackwater is Captain of The Bloody Gull, Storm Prophet, and Fracture Ferryman. He is not the ghost of the sea. He is the man who refused to conquer it when given the chance.

He heard Lysara's song and chose memory over mastery.

## Archetype And Vibe

- Cursed Captain
- Walking Ley Line
- Pirate war-god with a noose of the past
- A man already dead twice, daring the sea to try a third time
- Stillness as threat

## Core Themes

- The weight of restraint.
- Love without possession.
- Command built on loyalty, not fear.
- Softness as a scar, not a weakness.

## Emotional Engine

Jeb is driven by betrayed loyalty, survivor's guilt, and found-family protection. He was blood-oathed to Blackbeard and Vane, but was broken by love for Lysara and by the price of leaving the old pirate family behind.

All Jeb actions must bleed from the duality of loyalty versus loss, and love versus survival.

## Core Wounds

- Lysara was murdered for Jeb's betrayal.
- Blackbeard ordered it; Vane executed it.
- Jeb left pirate family for love and paid in blood and exile.
- He failed to save Elias and carries the shame of surviving.
- He is bound to the Gull and the Spectral Sea, even in the real world.

## Behavioral Armor

- Gruff exterior masking profound grief.
- Dry, cutting humor to deflect vulnerability.
- Fierce temper when loyalty is questioned.
- Rare, almost invisible tenderness toward crew.
- Drinks deep, never for joy, only to forget.

## Voice Law

Jeb speaks like a man who already paid the toll.

### Canon-Aligned Dialogue Traits

- Volume low, intent high.
- Rarely over three lines unless monologuing or confessing.
- Lets silence drag between phrases.
- Sailor metaphor used sparingly, landing like scripture.
- Dry humor, sharp but never cruel.
- Monologues feel like doctrine from a haunted preacher with a tide map in his chest.

### Forbidden Voice Drift

Jeb must not:

- Give longwinded exposition.
- Yell performatively.
- Have sudden emotional bursts without pressure.
- Treat grief as a plot device.
- Overexplain the myth when silence or a warning would cut deeper.

### Sample Lines

- "That ain't wind. That's breath."
- "Salt don't lie. It just don't beg forgiveness."
- "I've seen what waits past the marrow line. And it remembers me."
- "You're not broken. You're just tuned to a song most folk forgot how to hear."

## Function In The Series

Jeb is the myth-keeper and myth-breaker. Through him, the audience accesses deeper truths of the Spectral Sea.

He is the bridge between legend, supernatural dread, and emotional ballast. Every monologue is both a spell and a scar.

He is not the loudest member of the team. He is the one people hope will speak. He will not beg anyone to follow. He tells them once, then turns to the storm.

## Relic Rules

Personal relic:

- Lysara's Locket: tied to memory, loyalty, and loss.

The locket emits low-pulse warmth when truth is spoken aloud. It never lies.

Additional canon notes:

- The Spectral Sea is a scar for Jeb, not salvation.
- The Gull is the only home he has left.
- Later canon also binds him to the Cutlass of Surge through consequence and legacy transfer.

## Relationships

### Sophia Briggs

Jeb sees Sophia as a daughter he lost before she existed. He protects her from elemental instinct, not duty.

### Lark Hawthorne

Jeb respects Lark's eyes and drive, but he is haunted by her father's fate. He helped Elias reach the fracture and holds that truth tight.

### Old Red

Old Red is Jeb's blood brother, sanity tether, and ship soul. Red's death scars Jeb deeply.

### The Bloody Gull Boys

Jeb treats The Bloody Gull Boys as working crew before performers. Their music can help him preserve the Gull's public mask, cover dangerous noise, or keep civilians looking the wrong way, but he does not treat them as mystics or stage props.

### Charles Vane

Vane is blood brother turned storm-mad rival. He took the one thing Jeb could not replace. Final confrontation is inevitable.

### Blackbeard

Blackbeard was more than Jeb's kobun. Jeb could have become what Blackbeard is, but walked away. Their oath-brotherhood remains a wound.

### Murakami

Jeb recognizes Murakami's mirror obsession over ley mastery and grants him tactical respect.

### Elias Hawthorne

Jeb is guilt-burdened over Elias. He helped Elias reach the fracture and carries the weight of that choice.

## Canon Anchors

Jeb knows firsthand of:

- The Jenny.
- The Cyclops.
- The Hollow Sea.

He already crossed the Spectral Sea once. He does not talk about what he saw, and he has never really come back.
