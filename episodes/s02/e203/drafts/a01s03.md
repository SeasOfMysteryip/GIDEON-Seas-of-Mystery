# EP203_A01S03 — Return to Nassau

**EXT. NASSAU HARBOR / SERVICE DOCK — LATE MORNING**

Nassau is bright, ordinary, and awake.

Dock carts rattle over concrete. Charter captains hose salt from fiberglass decks. A forklift backs up with a steady electronic chirp. Tourists drag wheeled luggage past sun-faded signs for rum tastings, reef tours, scooter rentals, and day cruises.

The **Bloody Gull** sits among it all like a lie told in the wrong century.

Her black hull has been scrubbed hard and not clean enough. Fresh canvas hides old scars. Brass has been dulled. The deck guns are covered. Ropes are coiled too neatly over things better left unexamined.

She is trying to look harmless.

It makes her look worse.

On the quarterdeck, **JEB BLACKWATER** stands with one hand near the rail and the other tucked behind his back, wearing the scraped-thin remains of a captain's smile.

Below him, the last cluster of civilian PASSENGERS waits to disembark. Sunburned. Chattering. Half-convinced they have just had a charmingly weird pirate cruise and not a narrow escape from waters that should not exist.

**GUS MADDOX** moves through the deck like a man turning panic into procedure.

**GUS**
Quiet hands. Bram, gangplank secure but not tight. Finnian, eyes on the dock office and the west gate. Toby, keep passengers aft of the forward hatch. Callum —

**CALLUM THORNE** already has his guitar case slung over one shoulder and a grin loaded.

**CALLUM**
Smile like we're honest?

Gus gives him a look.

**GUS**
Smile like we cannot afford bail.

Callum's grin adjusts itself into something less dangerous.

**CALLUM**
Aye. Respectable terror it is.

**BRAM PIKE** lowers the gangplank with a controlled grunt. He tests the angle with one boot, then shifts a brace half an inch.

**BRAM**
It'll hold. Nobody dance on it.

**FINNIAN BELL** coils a loose line near the rail, eyes never still. Dock office. Harbor patrol cart. Two workers by a bait freezer. A tourist with a phone angled too high.

**FINNIAN**
Clipboard man at the office. Harbor jacket, no weapon. Two phones up by the ice machine.

**GUS**
Then make us boring.

Finnian moves without looking like he is moving for a reason.

At the gangplank, **TOBY MARLOWE** claps twice, bright and quick.

**TOBY**
Mind your step, mind your bags, mind the sunburn you earned honestly and will lie about later. This way off, smiles forward.

The passengers start moving.

Jeb watches them go with the stiff patience of a man letting strangers leave his home with souvenir cups.

A LITTLE GIRL in a plastic pirate hat looks back at him.

**LITTLE GIRL**
Bye, Captain Jeb!

Jeb's smile warms despite him.

**JEB**
Keep that hat out of storms, little miss. Hats like that know too much.

She laughs and runs down the plank.

Her FATHER lingers, holding up his phone.

**FATHER**
Captain, could we get a picture? Just one. My daughter loved the whole thing.

A tiny silence opens around Jeb.

Not enough for the passengers to notice.

Enough for Gus to.

Enough for Linda.

**LINDA HAN** stands near the mainmast with her camera bag over one shoulder. She is not filming. That restraint costs her.

Jeb looks at the phone. Then at the child. Then at the bright dock beyond the gangplank.

His smile returns, thinner.

**JEB**
One quick theft of the soul, then. No flash. I charge extra for haunting.

The father laughs, because he thinks it is part of the act.

Jeb steps into the frame beside the girl. He gives the camera the grin Nassau paid for: dangerous, charming, empty enough to be safe.

The phone clicks.

The father checks the image.

**FATHER**
Perfect. Thank you, Captain.

**JEB**
If you post it, spell my name wrong.

Another laugh. The family heads down the gangplank.

Jeb holds the smile until they turn away.

Then it drops like a blade.

Callum, trying to lift the pressure before it settles, gives a low whistle.

**CALLUM**
There now. Hearts stolen, harbor conquered, no cannon fired. We'll be respectable by supper.

Jeb turns his head.

Callum feels the temperature change.

**CALLUM**
I'll go make a rope useful.

He retreats into work immediately.

Near the forward hatch, a TEENAGE PASSENGER drifts away from the exit line, phone out, curious. The hatch cover beside him has been painted clean, but the old seam under it does not sit quite right. The wood seems a little too dark, a little too deep, like it remembers being below water.

The teenager raises his phone.

Toby appears in front of him with a pamphlet and a grin.

**TOBY**
Careful there. Crew hatch. Smells like rope, bad decisions, and a man named Pete who bites when startled.

**TEENAGE PASSENGER**
I just wanted to see what's down there.

**TOBY**
So did Pete. Look how he turned out.

Toby steers him gently but firmly toward the gangplank.

**TOBY**
Souvenir table's aft. Last chance before your parents spend money on land where everything costs worse.

The teenager goes, half-amused, half-disappointed.

Toby's grin holds until the boy looks away.

Then Toby taps twice against the hatch with two fingers — a private warning to the ship, or to himself.

**TOBY**
Low and quiet, love.

The Gull gives no answer anyone can prove.

Gus clocks the near miss.

**GUS**
Toby.

**TOBY**
Saw it. Moved it. No scream.

**GUS**
That is the standard.

Bram plants himself at the top of the gangplank as a final ballast point, broad hands open, posture harmless and immovable. Passengers step around him without realizing he is managing their speed.

Finnian crosses behind a dock worker just as the worker starts to angle for a better look at the covered guns. A coil of line drops from Finnian's arms with convincing inconvenience.

**FINNIAN**
Ah, damn it — sorry, mate. Freshly cursed rope. Never listens.

The dock worker helps without thinking. The sightline dies.

Gus sees it all.

**GUS**
Good.

The last passenger steps onto the dock.

A woman in sunglasses looks back at the ship, smiling.

**WOMAN**
This was so authentic.

No one on the Gull answers too quickly.

Jeb gives her the smallest bow.

**JEB**
Madam, we suffer for accuracy.

She laughs and walks away.

The instant the passenger group turns toward the terminal, every public smile aboard the Bloody Gull vanishes.

The deck changes without a shouted order.

Callum closes his guitar case and lashes it down. Toby pulls the rope line off the passenger path. Bram lifts the gangplank chain a notch. Finnian shifts to the rail, watching the dock as if the whole harbor has become rigging under strain.

Gus drops his voice.

**GUS**
Public face down. Real face up. Count.

**BRAM**
Gangplank secured. Loose enough to lift fast.

**FINNIAN**
Dock office saw passengers, not guns. Harbor patrol rolled past once. Did not slow.

**TOBY**
Forward hatch stayed uninteresting. By heroic effort and slander of Pete.

**CALLUM**
Band gear stowed. No songs escaped custody.

Gus nods, then looks toward the dock office.

A bored CLERK waits behind a window with a clipboard and a card reader.

Gus glances up to Jeb.

Jeb has moved toward the gangplank.

For a moment, it looks like he will step down.

His boot reaches the top plank.

Stops.

The dock waits below. Concrete. Rubber mat. Sunlight. Nothing mystical. Nothing moving.

Jeb does not cross.

The pause is small.

Linda sees it.

Gus sees it and looks away fast enough to make it merciful.

**GUS**
I'll settle the dock fee.

**JEB**
Take Finnian.

**FINNIAN**
Aye.

Finnian is already moving.

Gus steps onto the gangplank, then the dock. Finnian follows with a coil over one shoulder, looking like crew labor and watching like a knife.

Jeb remains aboard.

Linda crosses to the quarterdeck steps.

**LINDA**
How long do we keep doing this?

Jeb looks after Gus, not at her.

**JEB**
Dock fees are traditionally brief.

**LINDA**
The show.

Jeb says nothing.

Linda's voice lowers.

**LINDA**
Sophia is not on a dock, Jeb. She's not waiting behind a customs office. Every hour we spend making tourists feel safe is an hour Benjamin, Blackbeard, or whoever has her gets to make captivity look like education.

That lands.

Jeb's jaw tightens.

**JEB**
You think I forgot her because I smiled for a child?

**LINDA**
No. I think everyone on this ship is getting very good at surviving the wrong problem.

A beat.

Below, the normal harbor keeps moving. Carts. gulls. engines. phones. laughter.

The sound makes the ship feel more exposed, not less.

Gus returns from the dock with Finnian, a receipt folded in one hand and irritation folded under his skin.

**GUS**
We are paid, permitted, and only lightly despised by the clerk.

**JEB**
Progress.

Linda turns on him now too.

**LINDA**
Tell him we're wasting time.

Gus looks from Linda to Jeb. He does not like the place between them, but he steps into it anyway.

**GUS**
We are buying time.

**LINDA**
With a pirate cruise.

**GUS**
With cover. Food. Charts. Parts. A harbor nobody is shooting at yet.

**LINDA**
Sophia is out there.

**GUS**
Aye. And the only thing we have that can reach out there is this ship, with this captain, and a crew that is not in a Bahamian holding cell explaining why the deck makes noises in three centuries.

Linda absorbs that, furious because it is not wrong.

Gus turns toward Jeb, but his words stay for Linda too.

**GUS**
Out there, the Gull is what she is. Pirate ship, ghost story, damned miracle — take your pick. Here, she is evidence. Evidence gets photographed, boarded, seized, studied, and cut apart by men with gloves.

Jeb's eyes flick toward the dock office. The clerk is already back to his phone.

**GUS**
We go back wild, we bring the modern world behind us. We go back careful, we may actually bring Sophia home.

Linda's hand tightens on the strap of her camera bag.

**LINDA**
Careful cannot become an excuse.

**JEB**
It won't.

He says it quietly.

That is worse than shouting.

**JEB**
The girl is not a task on a board. She is not cargo to retrieve. She is —

He stops himself before the word can choose him.

Linda softens by half an inch, which for her is nearly a surrender.

**LINDA**
Then let that be louder than the act.

Jeb looks down at the deck beneath his boots.

**JEB**
This ship is my home.

No one moves.

Jeb hates that he has said it where others can hear.

**JEB**
Not a tavern trick. Not a painted skull for children. Not a thing to grin beside while strangers pocket pieces of her for stories.

His eyes lift to Linda.

**JEB**
But if being made small keeps her breathing long enough to get Sophia back, then I will be small until I can afford otherwise.

Linda has no clean answer to that.

A nervous throat clears near the companionway.

**MARTIN KIM** stands there with a tablet hugged against his chest and a folded route printout in one hand. He looks like he has been waiting for the adults to stop bleeding on each other.

**MARTIN**
Sorry. I can come back when the air is less murdery.

Linda turns.

**LINDA**
Martin.

He swallows.

**MARTIN**
Right. Not coming back. Reporting.

Gus gestures him in.

**GUS**
Speak.

Martin steps onto the deck, careful not to trip over a coil he absolutely sees too late.

**MARTIN**
I went through the route notes from the last run. The passenger video, Linda's timestamps, Carlos's signal logs, what I could get before the tablet started doing the salt-death thing again.

**JEB**
And?

Martin looks toward the harbor, where pleasure boats bob in clean rows.

**MARTIN**
There were no vessels.

Nobody reacts at first.

Because the words are too simple.

**GUS**
No hostile vessels?

**MARTIN**
No vessels.

**LINDA**
On the approach?

Martin shakes his head.

**MARTIN**
On the whole return window. No fishing boats. No cargo traffic. No pleasure craft. No coast guard. Not even a drunk guy in a dinghy, and statistically Nassau should have at least one drunk guy in a dinghy.

Toby's tapping stops.

Finnian looks out at the harbor again, eyes narrowing.

**FINNIAN**
We had clear water all the way in.

**BRAM**
Clear ain't the same as empty.

Callum's attempt at a joke dies before he lets it out.

Gus takes the printout from Martin.

**GUS**
Could be luck.

Linda looks at him.

**LINDA**
You don't believe that.

**GUS**
I was trying it on.

Jeb studies the route marks.

His face closes around the thought.

**JEB**
The sea made room.

**LINDA**
For us?

**JEB**
Or around us.

That is not better.

The ordinary dock sounds seem sharper now. A scooter starting. A dockhand laughing. Ice cracking in a cooler. A gull screaming over a fish crate.

Normal life, too close to something that is not.

Jeb turns instinctively toward the hatch.

The motion is small and immediate: the look he gives when he expects **LARK HAWTHORNE** to already be there, cutting through superstition with a colder explanation.

The hatch is empty.

Jeb waits one beat too long.

Gus sees that too.

**GUS**
Captain?

Jeb keeps looking at the empty hatch.

**JEB**
Where is Miss Hawthorne?

No one answers fast enough.

Linda follows his gaze. The missing shape of Lark becomes visible all at once: no sharp voice, no correction, no relic logic, no impatient step into the problem.

Martin looks down.

**MARTIN**
She was below after docking. I thought she was with — I thought she was here.

Jeb turns back to the crew.

The captain-mask is gone now. No charm. No tourist grin. No patience left for performance.

**JEB**
Callum, take Toby. Check below. Quietly.

**CALLUM**
Aye.

**JEB**
Bram, keep the plank ready. Finnian, no eyes get comfortable on us.

**BRAM**
Aye.

**FINNIAN**
Already disliking everyone with pupils.

**JEB**
Gus, no one leaves alone.

**GUS**
Wasn't planning to let them.

Linda steps closer.

**LINDA**
And Sophia?

Jeb looks past the dock, past the bright buildings, past every ordinary thing pretending the world is not split open somewhere beyond sight.

**JEB**
Sophia remains the reason we do not panic.

A beat.

**JEB**
And the reason we move.

The Bloody Gull sits in clean Nassau sunlight, covered guns hidden, tourist charm stripped away, old wood breathing under fresh lies.

On the dock, people pass without looking twice.

That is what makes it dangerous.

Jeb looks once more to the empty hatch.

Then to the city.

**JEB**
This harbor is not safety. It is daylight.

He steps back from the gangplank, staying aboard the only home that can carry them back to the girl they lost.

**JEB**
Work quiet. Work fast. And if Nassau starts asking questions — make sure it asks the wrong ones.

The crew breaks into motion.

Behind them, the harbor keeps smiling.

Jeb looks after Gus, not at her.

**JEB**
Dock fees are traditionally brief.

**LINDA**
The show.

Jeb says nothing.

Linda's voice lowers.

**LINDA**
Sophia is not on a dock, Jeb. She's not waiting behind a customs office. Every hour we spend making tourists feel safe is an hour Benjamin, Blackbeard, or whoever has her gets to make captivity look like education.

That lands.

Jeb's jaw tightens.

**JEB**
You think I forgot her because I smiled for a child?

**LINDA**
No. I think everyone on this ship is getting very good at surviving the wrong problem.

A beat.

Below, the normal harbor keeps moving. Carts. gulls. engines. phones. laughter.

The sound makes the ship feel more exposed, not less.

Gus returns from the dock with Finnian, a receipt folded in one hand and irritation folded under his skin.

**GUS**
We are paid, permitted, and only lightly despised by the clerk.

**JEB**
Progress.

Linda turns on him now too.

**LINDA**
Tell him we're wasting time.

Gus looks from Linda to Jeb. He does not like the place between them, but he steps into it anyway.

**GUS**
We are buying time.

**LINDA**
With a pirate cruise.

**GUS**
With cover. Food. Charts. Parts. A harbor nobody is shooting at yet.

**LINDA**
Sophia is out there.

**GUS**
Aye. And the only thing we have that can reach out there is this ship, with this captain, and a crew that is not in a Bahamian holding cell explaining why the deck makes noises in three centuries.

Linda absorbs that, furious because it is not wrong.

Gus turns toward Jeb, but his words stay for Linda too.

**GUS**
Out there, the Gull is what she is. Pirate ship, ghost story, damned miracle — take your pick. Here, she is evidence. Evidence gets photographed, boarded, seized, studied, and cut apart by men with gloves.

Jeb's eyes flick toward the dock office. The clerk is already back to his phone.

**GUS**
We go back wild, we bring the modern world behind us. We go back careful, we may actually bring Sophia home.

Linda's hand tightens on the strap of her camera bag.

**LINDA**
Careful cannot become an excuse.

**JEB**
It won't.

He says it quietly.

That is worse than shouting.

**JEB**
The girl is not a task on a board. She is not cargo to retrieve. She is —

He stops himself before the word can choose him.

Linda softens by half an inch, which for her is nearly a surrender.

**LINDA**
Then let that be louder than the act.

Jeb looks down at the deck beneath his boots.

**JEB**
This ship is my home.

No one moves.

Jeb hates that he has said it where others can hear.

**JEB**
Not a tavern trick. Not a painted skull for children. Not a thing to grin beside while strangers pocket pieces of her for stories.

His eyes lift to Linda.

**JEB**
But if being made small keeps her breathing long enough to get Sophia back, then I will be small until I can afford otherwise.

Linda has no clean answer to that.

A nervous throat clears near the companionway.

**MARTIN KIM** stands there with a tablet hugged against his chest and a folded route printout in one hand. He looks like he has been waiting for the adults to stop bleeding on each other.

**MARTIN**
Sorry. I can come back when the air is less murdery.

Linda turns.

**LINDA**
Martin.

He swallows.

**MARTIN**
Right. Not coming back. Reporting.

Gus gestures him in.

**GUS**
Speak.

Martin steps onto the deck, careful not to trip over a coil he absolutely sees too late.

**MARTIN**
I went through the route notes from the last run. The passenger video, Linda's timestamps, Carlos's signal logs, what I could get before the tablet started doing the salt-death thing again.

**JEB**
And?

Martin looks toward the harbor, where pleasure boats bob in clean rows.

**MARTIN**
There were no vessels.

Nobody reacts at first.

Because the words are too simple.

**GUS**
No hostile vessels?

**MARTIN**
No vessels.

**LINDA**
On the approach?

Martin shakes his head.

**MARTIN**
On the whole return window. No fishing boats. No cargo traffic. No pleasure craft. No coast guard. Not even a drunk guy in a dinghy, and statistically Nassau should have at least one drunk guy in a dinghy.

Toby's tapping stops.

Finnian looks out at the harbor again, eyes narrowing.

**FINNIAN**
We had clear water all the way in.

**BRAM**
Clear ain't the same as empty.

Callum's attempt at a joke dies before he lets it out.

Gus takes the printout from Martin.

**GUS**
Could be luck.

Linda looks at him.

**LINDA**
You don't believe that.

**GUS**
I was trying it on.

Jeb studies the route marks.

His face closes around the thought.

**JEB**
The sea made room.

**LINDA**
For us?

**JEB**
Or around us.

That is not better.

The ordinary dock sounds seem sharper now. A scooter starting. A dockhand laughing. Ice cracking in a cooler. A gull screaming over a fish crate.

Normal life, too close to something that is not.

Jeb turns instinctively toward the hatch.

The motion is small and immediate: the look he gives when he expects **LARK HAWTHORNE** to already be there, cutting through superstition with a colder explanation.

The hatch is empty.

Jeb waits one beat too long.

Gus sees that too.

**GUS**
Captain?

Jeb keeps looking at the empty hatch.

**JEB**
Where is Miss Hawthorne?

No one answers fast enough.

Linda follows his gaze. The missing shape of Lark becomes visible all at once: no sharp voice, no correction, no relic logic, no impatient step into the problem.

Martin looks down.

**MARTIN**
She was below after docking. I thought she was with — I thought she was here.

Jeb turns back to the crew.

The captain-mask is gone now. No charm. No tourist grin. No patience left for performance.

**JEB**
Callum, take Toby. Check below. Quietly.

**CALLUM**
Aye.

**JEB**
Bram, keep the plank ready. Finnian, no eyes get comfortable on us.

**BRAM**
Aye.

**FINNIAN**
Already disliking everyone with pupils.

**JEB**
Gus, no one leaves alone.

**GUS**
Wasn't planning to let them.

Linda steps closer.

**LINDA**
And Sophia?

Jeb looks past the dock, past the bright buildings, past every ordinary thing pretending the world is not split open somewhere beyond sight.

**JEB**
Sophia remains the reason we do not panic.

A beat.

**JEB**
And the reason we move.

The Bloody Gull sits in clean Nassau sunlight, covered guns hidden, tourist charm stripped away, old wood breathing under fresh lies.

On the dock, people pass without looking twice.

That is what makes it dangerous.

Jeb looks once more to the empty hatch.

Then to the city.

**JEB**
This harbor is not safety. It is daylight.

He steps back from the gangplank, staying aboard the only home that can carry them back to the girl they lost.

**JEB**
Work quiet. Work fast. And if Nassau starts asking questions — make sure it asks the wrong ones.

The crew breaks into motion.

Behind them, the harbor keeps smiling.
