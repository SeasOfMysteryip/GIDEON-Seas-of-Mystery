## EP203_A02S01 — Benjamin’s Roof / The Cyclops Walk

**T2 Working Draft — provisional page mass**

### INT. USS CYCLOPS — BENJAMIN’S PRIVATE QUARTERS — UNKNOWN

Sophia Briggs stands very still in a room that should not exist.

Not a cell.

That is the first thing wrong with it.

No iron. No chains. No wet boards under her bare feet. No Blackbeard’s men laughing in the passage. No stink of bilge or blood or old smoke.

Only polished wood. Warm brass. Clean linen. A carved ceiling dark with age and oil, patterned in a language she does not know but feels arranged to recognize her.

Phoenician shapes repeat in the walls, the doorframes, the small bronze lamps fixed in alcoves. Not museum pieces. Not trophies. Lived-in things. Useful things. A dead world still being used by people who never agreed it was dead.

Sophia looks down at herself.

Clean clothes.

Finer than anything she has worn in weeks. Maybe longer. A deep blue jacket cut to fit her shoulders. A white shirt soft enough to feel insulting. Boots that are not new, exactly, but chosen. Broken in. Her size.

A table has been set near the windows.

Fruit. Bread. Hot tea. A bowl of something fragrant, still steaming. No one has told her to eat it.

That is the second thing wrong.

The windows look out over open water. Too much sky. Too much air. The sea moves beyond the glass with a calm that feels staged.

Behind her, a voice says her name.

BENJAMIN BRIGGS stands near the far wall, one hand resting lightly on the back of a chair.

He looks older than a ghost should.

Alive enough to hurt.

BENJAMIN
Sophia.

Sophia does not move.

SOPHIA
No.

Benjamin absorbs it without flinching.

BENJAMIN
That is fair.

SOPHIA
No, it is not fair. It is the first sensible thing anyone has said in this room.

A faint warmth crosses his face. Not amusement. Recognition.

BENJAMIN
You have your mother’s refusal to be managed.

Sophia’s jaw tightens.

SOPHIA
Do not do that.

BENJAMIN
All right.

He lets the answer stand. No correction. No command.

That restraint lands worse than anger would have.

Sophia looks around the quarters again, searching for the trick, the bars hidden under the polish.

SOPHIA
Where am I?

BENJAMIN
Aboard the Cyclops.

Sophia looks back to him.

SOPHIA
The USS Cyclops disappeared.

BENJAMIN
Yes.

SOPHIA
That was not an answer.

BENJAMIN
No. But it is the answer this room can survive for now.

A beat.

The ship creaks softly around them. Not like wreckage. Like something vast adjusting in its sleep.

Sophia hears voices outside the door. Low. Disciplined. Waiting.

SOPHIA
And you are alive.

BENJAMIN
I am here.

SOPHIA
That was also not an answer.

BENJAMIN
No.

He does not apologize. He does not defend himself. He watches her with the terrible patience of someone who has rehearsed this meeting and still cannot control it.

Sophia steps toward the table, not to eat. To put something between them.

SOPHIA
Did you pick these?

BENJAMIN
The clothes?

SOPHIA
The clothes. The food. The view. The room. The amount of distance between you and the door.

Benjamin’s eyes follow the geometry she names.

BENJAMIN
I asked that you not be made afraid if it could be helped.

SOPHIA
You failed.

BENJAMIN
Yes.

That stops her for half a breath.

He gestures toward the tray, not quite an invitation.

BENJAMIN
You are not required to eat. You are not required to forgive me. You are not required to believe anything I say today.

SOPHIA
That sounds generous until I notice all the things you left off the list.

BENJAMIN
You may walk the ship.

SOPHIA
May I leave it?

Benjamin is quiet.

There it is.

Sophia gives a small nod, almost to herself.

SOPHIA
There are the walls.

Benjamin looks pained, but not surprised.

BENJAMIN
There are dangers outside this hull that would turn kindness into negligence.

SOPHIA
And inside?

BENJAMIN
Inside, there are people who have waited a long time to meet you.

SOPHIA
That is not comforting.

BENJAMIN
No. I imagine it is not.

A knock at the door.

Not sharp. Not servant-like. A careful sound.

Benjamin does not answer immediately. He watches Sophia, letting her see that the room is waiting on him.

Then:

BENJAMIN
Come.

The door opens.

A REVERENT ATTENDANT enters with a folded shawl over one arm and a small brass key on a cord. She is composed, formal, and visibly moved by Sophia’s presence.

Not afraid.

Honored.

That is worse.

The Attendant lowers her head.

ATTENDANT
Miss Briggs. If anything is unsuitable, we can correct it.

Sophia’s eyes flick to Benjamin.

SOPHIA
Correct it.

The Attendant hears the edge and lowers her gaze further.

ATTENDANT
Yes, miss.

Sophia looks at the jacket sleeve again.

SOPHIA
How long have you known my size?

The Attendant hesitates.

Benjamin does not rescue her.

ATTENDANT
Long enough to avoid guessing.

Sophia lets out one short breath. Almost a laugh. Not remotely happy.

SOPHIA
That is the most frightening answer you could have chosen.

Benjamin moves toward the door.

SOPHIA
Where are you going?

BENJAMIN
To give you the thing you will distrust least from me.

SOPHIA
Which is?

BENJAMIN
Space.

He stops beside her, not close enough to touch.

For one second, they share the frame: father and daughter, separated by years, death, lies, and a ship that should be gone.

BENJAMIN
Walk where you wish. Ask what you wish. Refuse what you wish.

SOPHIA
That cannot be true.

BENJAMIN
No.

He opens the door.

BENJAMIN
But it can be more true than what you have been given lately.

He leaves.

The Attendant remains.

The open door waits.

Sophia looks at the food. The clothes. The sea. The carved symbols watching from the walls.

Then she walks out.

### INT. USS CYCLOPS — PRIVATE PASSAGE — CONTINUOUS

The corridor outside Benjamin’s quarters is immaculate.

Old naval bones under new purpose.

Brass plates. Dark wood. Riveted steel polished until the ship’s age looks intentional instead of impossible. Electric light hums behind glass fixtures shaped like oil lamps.

The Attendant walks a few paces behind Sophia. Not guiding. Not guarding.

That is clearly the arrangement.

They pass two workers carrying a crate marked with careful symbols and modern inventory tags. When the workers see Sophia, their conversation dies.

One lowers his eyes.

The other puts a hand over his heart without seeming to realize he has done it.

Sophia stops.

So do they.

SOPHIA
Is there a rule about looking at me?

The workers do not answer.

The Attendant does.

ATTENDANT
No, Miss Briggs.

SOPHIA
Then why do they look like they are breaking one?

The Attendant chooses her words carefully.

ATTENDANT
Most of us were taught we might never see you.

Sophia turns slowly.

SOPHIA
Taught.

ATTENDANT
Yes.

SOPHIA
By my father?

A tiny hesitation.

ATTENDANT
By the record.

Sophia hears the capital letter in it.

She keeps walking.

Doors line the passage. Each marked with brass and clean black lettering.

CHART ROOM.

RESTORATION RECORDS.

CHRONOLOGY.

FRACTURE OBSERVATION.

One door stands open at the far end.

Not wide.

Just enough.

Sophia sees the light first. Blue-white. Moving.

She stops.

The Attendant stops behind her.

Sophia waits for a warning.

None comes.

SOPHIA
You are not going to tell me I cannot go in.

ATTENDANT
No.

SOPHIA
Because I can?

ATTENDANT
Because the door is open.

Sophia looks back at her.

SOPHIA
You all keep saying please like it changes what this is.

ATTENDANT
I was not going to say please.

SOPHIA
No. You were going to let the hallway do it for you.

The Attendant lowers her eyes again.

Not ashamed.

Trained.

Sophia turns toward the open door.

### INT. USS CYCLOPS — HISTORY ROOM — CONTINUOUS

The room beyond is not a library.

It is a war room pretending to be an archive.

Long tables hold ship logs, brittle maps, sealed folders, photographs under glass, brass instruments, and modern screens fed by cables run too neatly along century-old ribs.

The walls are covered in timelines.

Not one timeline.

Many.

Some drawn in ink. Some projected in thin light. Some interrupted by red thread, black pins, and little bone-white tags with dates written in a disciplined hand.

Sophia steps inside.

No alarm sounds.

No one stops her.

That is the threat.

She moves past a photograph of a harbor she does not know. The same harbor appears beside it, half-burned, then whole, then labeled with three different years.

A file lies open under a glass weight.

CORRECTION ATTEMPT — FAILED.

Below it, a handwritten note:

COST EXCEEDED ACCEPTABLE MEMORY LOSS.

Sophia reads it twice.

Not because she understands.

Because she nearly does.

Across another table, a ledger lists disappearances in tidy columns.

VESSEL.

LAST KNOWN POSITION.

FRACTURE PRESSURE.

RECOVERABLE.

REVISION STATUS.

Sophia’s breath catches at one entry.

MARY CELESTE.

The rest of the line is partially covered by a strip of vellum.

BRIGGS LINE — DO NOT AMEND WITHOUT DIRECT AUTHORITY.

Sophia does not touch it.

Not yet.

Her eyes move.

A model of the USS Cyclops sits at the center of one table. Not decorative. Cut open in careful section, showing decks, sealed compartments, observation rooms, and something at the core marked only with a symbol burned into the wood.

The same symbol appears on the wall behind Benjamin’s desk in the quarters.

Sophia files that away.

Workers stand at the far side of the room, speaking low over a screen. Their voices fall when they notice her.

One of them looks almost joyous.

Sophia hates him for it.

She turns from them and sees the globe.

### INT. USS CYCLOPS — HISTORY ROOM / FRACTURE GLOBE — CONTINUOUS

The globe occupies the far end of the room inside a brass ring suspended from the ceiling and floor.

Modern projection over old mechanism.

The Earth turns slowly in blue and black, overlaid with faint seams of light. Most are dim. Some pulse weakly and fade.

A few are marked with names Sophia recognizes from stories no one ever told the same way twice.

Bermuda.

Ocracoke.

A blank patch of Atlantic with no label at all.

Then the globe turns north.

One wound burns brighter than the rest.

BERING SEA — ACTIVE FRACTURE.

Sophia steps closer.

The room seems to lean with her.

No lecture. No explanation. No Benjamin at her shoulder turning mystery into lesson.

Just the image.

One door between worlds, lit and open.

Sophia studies the coordinates. The pulse interval. The small marks around it. Three dim points surrounding the active wound like anchors or failed locks.

She memorizes what she can.

A worker near the screens sees what she is doing and looks to the Attendant.

The Attendant does not move.

Sophia notices anyway.

SOPHIA
If this is history, why does it look like a map?

No one answers quickly enough.

Good.

She turns to the nearest table and looks at the open ledger again.

Her hand hovers over the vellum strip covering the Mary Celeste entry.

Still no one stops her.

That is when she understands.

The not-stopping is also part of it.

She lifts the strip.

Underneath, only three words are visible before the ink degrades into blackened scratches.

SARAH BRIGGS — UNSTABLE.

Sophia goes cold.

The globe pulses behind her.

The Bering Sea flares once, bright enough to wash blue light across her face.

She lowers the vellum exactly where it was.

SOPHIA
Who wrote this?

The worker with the joyous face looks ready to answer.

The Attendant speaks first.

ATTENDANT
That record is incomplete.

SOPHIA
That was not my question.

ATTENDANT
No.

SOPHIA
You all do that. You answer around the wound and hope I will thank you for the bandage.

The Attendant absorbs the line with a kind of reverence that makes Sophia angrier.

ATTENDANT
Your father said you would see the shape before the explanation.

Sophia turns on her.

SOPHIA
My father has had a long time to write speeches for a daughter he let believe he was dead.

Silence.

For the first time, the Attendant looks genuinely hurt.

Not for herself.

For Benjamin.

Sophia clocks that too.

SOPHIA
There it is.

ATTENDANT
Miss Briggs?

SOPHIA
You do not think I am a prisoner.

ATTENDANT
No.

SOPHIA
You think I am late.

The Attendant says nothing.

Sophia gives a small, humorless smile.

SOPHIA
That is worse.

She turns back to the globe.

Her fingers touch the brass ring around it.

The projection trembles.

Not much.

Enough.

A faint line appears beneath the Bering Sea marker, too quick for anyone else to read.

Sophia catches only fragments.

LEY DRIFT.

BLOODLINE RESPONSE.

COMPASS ABSENT.

She removes her hand.

The globe steadies.

Everyone in the room is now looking at her.

The joyous worker is no longer joyous.

Sophia tucks that away too.

SOPHIA
You are not guarding me.

The Attendant steps nearer, careful.

ATTENDANT
No.

SOPHIA
You are training me to choose the right hallway.

Before the Attendant can answer, another figure appears in the doorway.

An OFFICER-SCHOLAR. Older. Uniformed, but not military in any clean modern sense. A scholar’s ink on his fingers. A sailor’s posture in his spine.

He bows his head to Sophia.

OFFICER-SCHOLAR
Miss Briggs.

Sophia does not return it.

SOPHIA
Am I finished learning the part I was supposed to stumble into?

A flicker. He did not expect that much bite that quickly.

OFFICER-SCHOLAR
That room is not forbidden.

SOPHIA
Of course it is not.

OFFICER-SCHOLAR
It is simply not where you are meant to begin.

Sophia looks from him to the open door, then back to the globe.

SOPHIA
But it was where I was meant to arrive.

No one denies it.

The Officer-Scholar gestures down the passage.

OFFICER-SCHOLAR
Your father asks if you will join him for dinner.

SOPHIA
Asks.

OFFICER-SCHOLAR
Yes.

SOPHIA
And if I say no?

The Officer-Scholar lowers his hand.

Respectful. Almost gentle.

OFFICER-SCHOLAR
Then dinner will wait.

Sophia studies him.

The answer should feel like freedom.

It does not.

SOPHIA
How long?

OFFICER-SCHOLAR
As long as it must.

Sophia laughs once under her breath.

SOPHIA
That is not patience. That is confidence.

The Attendant looks down.

The Officer-Scholar does not.

Sophia takes one final look at the globe.

Bering Sea. Active fracture. Three dim anchor points. Compass absent.

She memorizes it again, harder this time.

Then she walks toward the door.

The workers part for her.

Not because they fear her.

Because they honor the role they think she will eventually accept.

### INT. USS CYCLOPS — PRIVATE PASSAGE — CONTINUOUS

Sophia exits the History Room.

The corridor is the same as before.

Clean. Warm. Open.

Now every door looks less like access and more like a sentence with the ending removed.

The Attendant falls in behind her.

Sophia stops without turning around.

SOPHIA
Do you have a name?

The Attendant hesitates.

ATTENDANT
I do.

Sophia waits.

The Attendant does not give it.

Sophia turns.

SOPHIA
Did he tell you not to?

ATTENDANT
No.

SOPHIA
Then why not?

ATTENDANT
Because if I give it now, you will use it kindly to make me easier to disobey.

For the first time, Sophia is caught off guard.

The Attendant meets her eyes.

Still reverent. But not stupid.

ATTENDANT
Your father asked that you not be underestimated.

Sophia considers that.

SOPHIA
He should try following his own advice.

She turns and keeps walking.

Ahead, the passage opens toward a broader deck corridor. Warmer light spills from somewhere beyond. The smell of dinner reaches her first: roasted fish, citrus, oil, bread.

Better than the Gull.

Better than captivity should smell.

At the far end, through a wide arch of carved wood and old steel, Benjamin stands beside the rail with the sea behind him.

He has not come to collect her.

He has arranged the world so she arrives.

Sophia slows.

Benjamin does not smile.

He only looks at her as if every second of her approach matters.

Sophia looks back down the corridor, toward the History Room, toward the open doors, toward the people who lowered their voices when she passed.

She has food. Clean clothes. Air. Movement. Answers close enough to touch.

None of it belongs to her.

She faces Benjamin again.

SOPHIA
That door was open because you wanted me to see it.

Benjamin’s expression changes by almost nothing.

BENJAMIN
Yes.

SOPHIA
At least that one was an answer.

She steps into the warmer light.

Not broken.

Not rescued.

Sharper than when she entered.

The ship hums around her like a cage learning the shape of its bird.

**END SCENE**


## What Changed
- Built the scene as one clean progression instead of six equal explanatory beats: care, reverence, movement, guided access, discovery, boundary.
- Put Benjamin on-page at the top to satisfy the reintroduction/father-daughter pressure, then moved him off the board so his control continues through the ship.
- Kept the USS Cyclops timeline status ambiguous by using impossible-but-not-explained details rather than a direct lore answer.
- Made the Phoenician nature of Benjamin’s quarters visible through lived-in design, not museum exposition.
- Used the attendant and officer-scholar as respectful containment instead of obvious guards.
- Made Sophia active: she interrogates the room, notices the staged freedom, memorizes the Bering Sea fracture, tests the Mary Celeste/Briggs record, and calls out the hallway design.
- Kept the Revisionist doctrine suggestive: correction attempts, incomplete records, revision status, memory-loss cost, and curated access without fully explaining the rules.
- Added the Bering Sea globe as the concrete episode hook and tied it lightly to Sophia’s bloodline/Compass pressure without making the system clean or solved.
- Ended with more access and less freedom: Sophia reaches Benjamin by choice, but understands the choice was architected.

Sophia turns toward the open door.

### INT. USS CYCLOPS — HISTORY ROOM — CONTINUOUS

The room beyond is not a library.

It is a war room pretending to be an archive.

Long tables hold ship logs, brittle maps, sealed folders, photographs under glass, brass instruments, and modern screens fed by cables run too neatly along century-old ribs.

The walls are covered in timelines.

Not one timeline.

Many.

Some drawn in ink. Some projected in thin light. Some interrupted by red thread, black pins, and little bone-white tags with dates written in a disciplined hand.

Sophia steps inside.

No alarm sounds.

No one stops her.

That is the threat.

She moves past a photograph of a harbor she does not know. The same harbor appears beside it, half-burned, then whole, then labeled with three different years.

A file lies open under a glass weight.

CORRECTION ATTEMPT — FAILED.

Below it, a handwritten note:

COST EXCEEDED ACCEPTABLE MEMORY LOSS.

Sophia reads it twice.

Not because she understands.

Because she nearly does.

Across another table, a ledger lists disappearances in tidy columns.

VESSEL.

LAST KNOWN POSITION.

FRACTURE PRESSURE.

RECOVERABLE.

REVISION STATUS.

Sophia’s breath catches at one entry.

MARY CELESTE.

The rest of the line is partially covered by a strip of vellum.

BRIGGS LINE — DO NOT AMEND WITHOUT DIRECT AUTHORITY.

Sophia does not touch it.

Not yet.

Her eyes move.

A model of the USS Cyclops sits at the center of one table. Not decorative. Cut open in careful section, showing decks, sealed compartments, observation rooms, and something at the core marked only with a symbol burned into the wood.

The same symbol appears on the wall behind Benjamin’s desk in the quarters.

Sophia files that away.

Workers stand at the far side of the room, speaking low over a screen. Their voices fall when they notice her.

One of them looks almost joyous.

Sophia hates him for it.

She turns from them and sees the globe.

### INT. USS CYCLOPS — HISTORY ROOM / FRACTURE GLOBE — CONTINUOUS

The globe occupies the far end of the room inside a brass ring suspended from the ceiling and floor.

Modern projection over old mechanism.

The Earth turns slowly in blue and black, overlaid with faint seams of light. Most are dim. Some pulse weakly and fade.

A few are marked with names Sophia recognizes from stories no one ever told the same way twice.

Bermuda.

Ocracoke.

A blank patch of Atlantic with no label at all.

Then the globe turns north.

One wound burns brighter than the rest.

BERING SEA — ACTIVE FRACTURE.

Sophia steps closer.

The room seems to lean with her.

No lecture. No explanation. No Benjamin at her shoulder turning mystery into lesson.

Just the image.

One door between worlds, lit and open.

Sophia studies the coordinates. The pulse interval. The small marks around it. Three dim points surrounding the active wound like anchors or failed locks.

She memorizes what she can.

A worker near the screens sees what she is doing and looks to the Attendant.

The Attendant does not move.

Sophia notices anyway.

SOPHIA
If this is history, why does it look like a map?

No one answers quickly enough.

Good.

She turns to the nearest table and looks at the open ledger again.

Her hand hovers over the vellum strip covering the Mary Celeste entry.

Still no one stops her.

That is when she understands.

The not-stopping is also part of it.

She lifts the strip.

Underneath, only three words are visible before the ink degrades into blackened scratches.

SARAH BRIGGS — UNSTABLE.

Sophia goes cold.

The globe pulses behind her.

The Bering Sea flares once, bright enough to wash blue light across her face.

She lowers the vellum exactly where it was.

SOPHIA
Who wrote this?

The worker with the joyous face looks ready to answer.

The Attendant speaks first.

ATTENDANT
That record is incomplete.

SOPHIA
That was not my question.

ATTENDANT
No.

SOPHIA
You all do that. You answer around the wound and hope I will thank you for the bandage.

The Attendant absorbs the line with a kind of reverence that makes Sophia angrier.

ATTENDANT
Your father said you would see the shape before the explanation.

Sophia turns on her.

SOPHIA
My father has had a long time to write speeches for a daughter he let believe he was dead.

Silence.

For the first time, the Attendant looks genuinely hurt.

Not for herself.

For Benjamin.

Sophia clocks that too.

SOPHIA
There it is.

ATTENDANT
Miss Briggs?

SOPHIA
You do not think I am a prisoner.

ATTENDANT
No.

SOPHIA
You think I am late.

The Attendant says nothing.

Sophia gives a small, humorless smile.

SOPHIA
That is worse.

She turns back to the globe.

Her fingers touch the brass ring around it.

The projection trembles.

Not much.

Enough.

A faint line appears beneath the Bering Sea marker, too quick for anyone else to read.

Sophia catches only fragments.

LEY DRIFT.

BLOODLINE RESPONSE.

COMPASS ABSENT.

She removes her hand.

The globe steadies.

Everyone in the room is now looking at her.

The joyous worker is no longer joyous.

Sophia tucks that away too.

SOPHIA
You are not guarding me.

The Attendant steps nearer, careful.

ATTENDANT
No.

SOPHIA
You are training me to choose the right hallway.

Before the Attendant can answer, another figure appears in the doorway.

An OFFICER-SCHOLAR. Older. Uniformed, but not military in any clean modern sense. A scholar’s ink on his fingers. A sailor’s posture in his spine.

He bows his head to Sophia.

OFFICER-SCHOLAR
Miss Briggs.

Sophia does not return it.

SOPHIA
Am I finished learning the part I was supposed to stumble into?

A flicker. He did not expect that much bite that quickly.

OFFICER-SCHOLAR
That room is not forbidden.

SOPHIA
Of course it is not.

OFFICER-SCHOLAR
It is simply not where you are meant to begin.

Sophia looks from him to the open door, then back to the globe.

SOPHIA
But it was where I was meant to arrive.

No one denies it.

The Officer-Scholar gestures down the passage.

OFFICER-SCHOLAR
Your father asks if you will join him for dinner.

SOPHIA
Asks.

OFFICER-SCHOLAR
Yes.

SOPHIA
And if I say no?

The Officer-Scholar lowers his hand.

Respectful. Almost gentle.

OFFICER-SCHOLAR
Then dinner will wait.

Sophia studies him.

The answer should feel like freedom.

It does not.

SOPHIA
How long?

OFFICER-SCHOLAR
As long as it must.

Sophia laughs once under her breath.

SOPHIA
That is not patience. That is confidence.

The Attendant looks down.

The Officer-Scholar does not.

Sophia takes one final look at the globe.

Bering Sea. Active fracture. Three dim anchor points. Compass absent.

She memorizes it again, harder this time.

Then she walks toward the door.

The workers part for her.

Not because they fear her.

Because they honor the role they think she will eventually accept.

### INT. USS CYCLOPS — PRIVATE PASSAGE — CONTINUOUS

Sophia exits the History Room.

The corridor is the same as before.

Clean. Warm. Open.

Now every door looks less like access and more like a sentence with the ending removed.

The Attendant falls in behind her.

Sophia stops without turning around.

SOPHIA
Do you have a name?

The Attendant hesitates.

ATTENDANT
I do.

Sophia waits.

The Attendant does not give it.

Sophia turns.

SOPHIA
Did he tell you not to?

ATTENDANT
No.

SOPHIA
Then why not?

ATTENDANT
Because if I give it now, you will use it kindly to make me easier to disobey.

For the first time, Sophia is caught off guard.

The Attendant meets her eyes.

Still reverent. But not stupid.

ATTENDANT
Your father asked that you not be underestimated.

Sophia considers that.

SOPHIA
He should try following his own advice.

She turns and keeps walking.

Ahead, the passage opens toward a broader deck corridor. Warmer light spills from somewhere beyond. The smell of dinner reaches her first: roasted fish, citrus, oil, bread.

Better than the Gull.

Better than captivity should smell.

At the far end, through a wide arch of carved wood and old steel, Benjamin stands beside the rail with the sea behind him.

He has not come to collect her.

He has arranged the world so she arrives.

Sophia slows.

Benjamin does not smile.

He only looks at her as if every second of her approach matters.

Sophia looks back down the corridor, toward the History Room, toward the open doors, toward the people who lowered their voices when she passed.

She has food. Clean clothes. Air. Movement. Answers close enough to touch.

None of it belongs to her.

She faces Benjamin again.

SOPHIA
That door was open because you wanted me to see it.

Benjamin’s expression changes by almost nothing.

BENJAMIN
Yes.

SOPHIA
At least that one was an answer.

She steps into the warmer light.

Not broken.

Not rescued.

Sharper than when she entered.

The ship hums around her like a cage learning the shape of its bird.

**END SCENE**

Do not do that.

All right.

He lets the answer stand. No correction. No command.

That restraint lands worse than anger would have.

Sophia looks around the quarters again, searching for the trick, the bars hidden under the polish.

SOPHIA
Where am I?

BENJAMIN
Aboard the Cyclops.

Sophia looks back to him.

SOPHIA
The USS Cyclops disappeared.

BENJAMIN
Yes.

SOPHIA
That was not an answer.

BENJAMIN
No. But it is the answer this room can survive for now.

A beat.

The ship creaks softly around them. Not like wreckage. Like something vast adjusting in its sleep.

Sophia hears voices outside the door. Low. Disciplined. Waiting.

SOPHIA
And you are alive.

BENJAMIN
I am here.

SOPHIA
That was also not an answer.

BENJAMIN
No.

He does not apologize. He does not defend himself. He watches her with the terrible patience of someone who has rehearsed this meeting and still cannot control it.

Sophia steps toward the table, not to eat. To put something between them.

SOPHIA
Did you pick these?

BENJAMIN
The clothes?

SOPHIA
The clothes. The food. The view. The room. The amount of distance between you and the door.

Benjamin’s eyes follow the geometry she names.

BENJAMIN
I asked that you not be made afraid if it could be helped.

SOPHIA
You failed.

BENJAMIN
Yes.

That stops her for half a breath.

He gestures toward the tray, not quite an invitation.

BENJAMIN
You are not required to eat. You are not required to forgive me. You are not required to believe anything I say today.

SOPHIA
That sounds generous until I notice all the things you left off the list.

BENJAMIN
You may walk the ship.

SOPHIA
May I leave it?

Benjamin is quiet.

There it is.

Sophia gives a small nod, almost to herself.

SOPHIA
There are the walls.

Benjamin looks pained, but not surprised.

BENJAMIN
There are dangers outside this hull that would turn kindness into negligence.

SOPHIA
And inside?

BENJAMIN
Inside, there are people who have waited a long time to meet you.

SOPHIA
That is not comforting.

BENJAMIN
No. I imagine it is not.

A knock at the door.

Not sharp. Not servant-like. A careful sound.

Benjamin does not answer immediately. He watches Sophia, letting her see that the room is waiting on him.

Then:

BENJAMIN
Come.

The door opens.

A REVERENT ATTENDANT enters with a folded shawl over one arm and a small brass key on a cord. She is composed, formal, and visibly moved by Sophia’s presence.

Not afraid.

Honored.

That is worse.

The Attendant lowers her head.

ATTENDANT
Miss Briggs. If anything is unsuitable, we can correct it.

Sophia’s eyes flick to Benjamin.

SOPHIA
Correct it.

The Attendant hears the edge and lowers her gaze further.

ATTENDANT
Yes, miss.

Sophia looks at the jacket sleeve again.

SOPHIA
How long have you known my size?

The Attendant hesitates.

Benjamin does not rescue her.

ATTENDANT
Long enough to avoid guessing.

Sophia lets out one short breath. Almost a laugh. Not remotely happy.

SOPHIA
That is the most frightening answer you could have chosen.

Benjamin moves toward the door.

SOPHIA
Where are you going?

BENJAMIN
To give you the thing you will distrust least from me.

SOPHIA
Which is?

BENJAMIN
Space.

He stops beside her, not close enough to touch.

For one second, they share the frame: father and daughter, separated by years, death, lies, and a ship that should be gone.

BENJAMIN
Walk where you wish. Ask what you wish. Refuse what you wish.

SOPHIA
That cannot be true.

BENJAMIN
No.

He opens the door.

BENJAMIN
But it can be more true than what you have been given lately.

He leaves.

The Attendant remains.

The open door waits.

Sophia looks at the food. The clothes. The sea. The carved symbols watching from the walls.

Then she walks out.

### INT. USS CYCLOPS — PRIVATE PASSAGE — CONTINUOUS

The corridor outside Benjamin’s quarters is immaculate.

Old naval bones under new purpose.

Brass plates. Dark wood. Riveted steel polished until the ship’s age looks intentional instead of impossible. Electric light hums behind glass fixtures shaped like oil lamps.

The Attendant walks a few paces behind Sophia. Not guiding. Not guarding.

That is clearly the arrangement.

They pass two workers carrying a crate marked with careful symbols and modern inventory tags. When the workers see Sophia, their conversation dies.

One lowers his eyes.

The other puts a hand over his heart without seeming to realize he has done it.

Sophia stops.

So do they.

SOPHIA
Is there a rule about looking at me?

The workers do not answer.

The Attendant does.

ATTENDANT
No, Miss Briggs.

SOPHIA
Then why do they look like they are breaking one?

The Attendant chooses her words carefully.

ATTENDANT
Most of us were taught we might never see you.

Sophia turns slowly.

SOPHIA
Taught.

ATTENDANT
Yes.

SOPHIA
By my father?

A tiny hesitation.

ATTENDANT
By the record.

Sophia hears the capital letter in it.

She keeps walking.

Doors line the passage. Each marked with brass and clean black lettering.

CHART ROOM.

RESTORATION RECORDS.

CHRONOLOGY.

FRACTURE OBSERVATION.

One door stands open at the far end.

Not wide.

Just enough.

Sophia sees the light first. Blue-white. Moving.

She stops.

The Attendant stops behind her.

Sophia waits for a warning.

None comes.

SOPHIA
You are not going to tell me I cannot go in.

ATTENDANT
No.

SOPHIA
Because I can?

ATTENDANT
Because the door is open.

Sophia looks back at her.

SOPHIA
You all keep saying please like it changes what this is.

ATTENDANT
I was not going to say please.

SOPHIA
No. You were going to let the hallway do it for you.

The Attendant lowers her eyes again.

Not ashamed.

Trained.

Sophia turns toward the open door.

### INT. USS CYCLOPS — HISTORY ROOM — CONTINUOUS

The room beyond is not a library.

It is a war room pretending to be an archive.

Long tables hold ship logs, brittle maps, sealed folders, photographs under glass, brass instruments, and modern screens fed by cables run too neatly along century-old ribs.

The walls are covered in timelines.

Not one timeline.

Many.

Some drawn in ink. Some projected in thin light. Some interrupted by red thread, black pins, and little bone-white tags with dates written in a disciplined hand.

Sophia steps inside.

No alarm sounds.

No one stops her.

That is the threat.

She moves past a photograph of a harbor she does not know. The same harbor appears beside it, half-burned, then whole, then labeled with three different years.

A file lies open under a glass weight.

CORRECTION ATTEMPT — FAILED.

Below it, a handwritten note:

COST EXCEEDED ACCEPTABLE MEMORY LOSS.

Sophia reads it twice.

Not because she understands.

Because she nearly does.

Across another table, a ledger lists disappearances in tidy columns.

VESSEL.

LAST KNOWN POSITION.

FRACTURE PRESSURE.

RECOVERABLE.

REVISION STATUS.

Sophia’s breath catches at one entry.

MARY CELESTE.

The rest of the line is partially covered by a strip of vellum.

BRIGGS LINE — DO NOT AMEND WITHOUT DIRECT AUTHORITY.

Sophia does not touch it.

Not yet.

Her eyes move.

A model of the USS Cyclops sits at the center of one table. Not decorative. Cut open in careful section, showing decks, sealed compartments, observation rooms, and something at the core marked only with a symbol burned into the wood.

The same symbol appears on the wall behind Benjamin’s desk in the quarters.

Sophia files that away.

Workers stand at the far side of the room, speaking low over a screen. Their voices fall when they notice her.

One of them looks almost joyous.

Sophia hates him for it.

She turns from them and sees the globe.

### INT. USS CYCLOPS — HISTORY ROOM / FRACTURE GLOBE — CONTINUOUS

The globe occupies the far end of the room inside a brass ring suspended from the ceiling and floor.

Modern projection over old mechanism.

The Earth turns slowly in blue and black, overlaid with faint seams of light. Most are dim. Some pulse weakly and fade.

A few are marked with names Sophia recognizes from stories no one ever told the same way twice.

Bermuda.

Ocracoke.

A blank patch of Atlantic with no label at all.

Then the globe turns north.

One wound burns brighter than the rest.

BERING SEA — ACTIVE FRACTURE.

Sophia steps closer.

The room seems to lean with her.

No lecture. No explanation. No Benjamin at her shoulder turning mystery into lesson.

Just the image.

One door between worlds, lit and open.

Sophia studies the coordinates. The pulse interval. The small marks around it. Three dim points surrounding the active wound like anchors or failed locks.

She memorizes what she can.

A worker near the screens sees what she is doing and looks to the Attendant.

The Attendant does not move.

Sophia notices anyway.

SOPHIA
If this is history, why does it look like a map?

No one answers quickly enough.

Good.

She turns to the nearest table and looks at the open ledger again.

Her hand hovers over the vellum strip covering the Mary Celeste entry.

Still no one stops her.

That is when she understands.

The not-stopping is also part of it.

She lifts the strip.

Underneath, only three words are visible before the ink degrades into blackened scratches.

SARAH BRIGGS — UNSTABLE.

Sophia goes cold.

The globe pulses behind her.

The Bering Sea flares once, bright enough to wash blue light across her face.

She lowers the vellum exactly where it was.

SOPHIA
Who wrote this?

The worker with the joyous face looks ready to answer.

The Attendant speaks first.

ATTENDANT
That record is incomplete.

SOPHIA
That was not my question.

ATTENDANT
No.

SOPHIA
You all do that. You answer around the wound and hope I will thank you for the bandage.

The Attendant absorbs the line with a kind of reverence that makes Sophia angrier.

ATTENDANT
Your father said you would see the shape before the explanation.

Sophia turns on her.

SOPHIA
My father has had a long time to write speeches for a daughter he let believe he was dead.

Silence.

For the first time, the Attendant looks genuinely hurt.

Not for herself.

For Benjamin.

Sophia clocks that too.

SOPHIA
There it is.

ATTENDANT
Miss Briggs?

SOPHIA
You do not think I am a prisoner.

ATTENDANT
No.

SOPHIA
You think I am late.

The Attendant says nothing.

Sophia gives a small, humorless smile.

SOPHIA
That is worse.

She turns back to the globe.

Her fingers touch the brass ring around it.

The projection trembles.

Not much.

Enough.

A faint line appears beneath the Bering Sea marker, too quick for anyone else to read.

Sophia catches only fragments.

LEY DRIFT.

BLOODLINE RESPONSE.

COMPASS ABSENT.

She removes her hand.

The globe steadies.

Everyone in the room is now looking at her.

The joyous worker is no longer joyous.

Sophia tucks that away too.

SOPHIA
You are not guarding me.

The Attendant steps nearer, careful.

ATTENDANT
No.

SOPHIA
You are training me to choose the right hallway.

Before the Attendant can answer, another figure appears in the doorway.

An OFFICER-SCHOLAR. Older. Uniformed, but not military in any clean modern sense. A scholar’s ink on his fingers. A sailor’s posture in his spine.

He bows his head to Sophia.

OFFICER-SCHOLAR
Miss Briggs.

Sophia does not return it.

SOPHIA
Am I finished learning the part I was supposed to stumble into?

A flicker. He did not expect that much bite that quickly.

OFFICER-SCHOLAR
That room is not forbidden.

SOPHIA
Of course it is not.

OFFICER-SCHOLAR
It is simply not where you are meant to begin.

Sophia looks from him to the open door, then back to the globe.

SOPHIA
But it was where I was meant to arrive.

No one denies it.

The Officer-Scholar gestures down the passage.

OFFICER-SCHOLAR
Your father asks if you will join him for dinner.

SOPHIA
Asks.

OFFICER-SCHOLAR
Yes.

SOPHIA
And if I say no?

The Officer-Scholar lowers his hand.

Respectful. Almost gentle.

OFFICER-SCHOLAR
Then dinner will wait.

Sophia studies him.

The answer should feel like freedom.

It does not.

SOPHIA
How long?

OFFICER-SCHOLAR
As long as it must.

Sophia laughs once under her breath.

SOPHIA
That is not patience. That is confidence.

The Attendant looks down.

The Officer-Scholar does not.

Sophia takes one final look at the globe.

Bering Sea. Active fracture. Three dim anchor points. Compass absent.

She memorizes it again, harder this time.

Then she walks toward the door.

The workers part for her.

Not because they fear her.

Because they honor the role they think she will eventually accept.

### INT. USS CYCLOPS — PRIVATE PASSAGE — CONTINUOUS

Sophia exits the History Room.

The corridor is the same as before.

Clean. Warm. Open.

Now every door looks less like access and more like a sentence with the ending removed.

The Attendant falls in behind her.

Sophia stops without turning around.

SOPHIA
Do you have a name?

The Attendant hesitates.

ATTENDANT
I do.

Sophia waits.

The Attendant does not give it.

Sophia turns.

SOPHIA
Did he tell you not to?

ATTENDANT
No.

SOPHIA
Then why not?

ATTENDANT
Because if I give it now, you will use it kindly to make me easier to disobey.

For the first time, Sophia is caught off guard.

The Attendant meets her eyes.

Still reverent. But not stupid.

ATTENDANT
Your father asked that you not be underestimated.

Sophia considers that.

SOPHIA
He should try following his own advice.

She turns and keeps walking.

Ahead, the passage opens toward a broader deck corridor. Warmer light spills from somewhere beyond. The smell of dinner reaches her first: roasted fish, citrus, oil, bread.

Better than the Gull.

Better than captivity should smell.

At the far end, through a wide arch of carved wood and old steel, Benjamin stands beside the rail with the sea behind him.

He has not come to collect her.

He has arranged the world so she arrives.

Sophia slows.

Benjamin does not smile.

He only looks at her as if every second of her approach matters.

Sophia looks back down the corridor, toward the History Room, toward the open doors, toward the people who lowered their voices when she passed.

She has food. Clean clothes. Air. Movement. Answers close enough to touch.

None of it belongs to her.

She faces Benjamin again.

SOPHIA
That door was open because you wanted me to see it.

Benjamin’s expression changes by almost nothing.

BENJAMIN
Yes.

SOPHIA
At least that one was an answer.

She steps into the warmer light.

Not broken.

Not rescued.

Sharper than when she entered.

The ship hums around her like a cage learning the shape of its bird.

**END SCENE**


## What Changed
- Built the scene as one clean progression instead of six equal explanatory beats: care, reverence, movement, guided access, discovery, boundary.
- Put Benjamin on-page at the top to satisfy the reintroduction/father-daughter pressure, then moved him off the board so his control continues through the ship.
- Kept the USS Cyclops timeline status ambiguous by using impossible-but-not-explained details rather than a direct lore answer.
- Made the Phoenician nature of Benjamin’s quarters visible through lived-in design, not museum exposition.
- Used the attendant and officer-scholar as respectful containment instead of obvious guards.
- Made Sophia active: she interrogates the room, notices the staged freedom, memorizes the Bering Sea fracture, tests the Mary Celeste/Briggs record, and calls out the hallway design.
- Kept the Revisionist doctrine suggestive: correction attempts, incomplete records, revision status, memory-loss cost, and curated access without fully explaining the rules.
- Added the Bering Sea globe as the concrete episode hook and tied it lightly to Sophia’s bloodline/Compass pressure without making the system clean or solved.
- Ended with more access and less freedom: Sophia reaches Benjamin by choice, but understands the choice was architected.
