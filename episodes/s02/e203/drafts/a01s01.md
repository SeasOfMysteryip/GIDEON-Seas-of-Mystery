INT. BLOODY GULL — NEW GALLEY — NIGHT

20 passengers sit at long  tables in the galley of the Gull with half-empty mugs, plates of food, and toy cutlasses.

JEB (O.S.)
Now, most stories about the Triangle
start the same way.
(beat)
Clear sky. Good weather. Sensible men
who wrote sensible numbers on sensible charts.

The lights are warm and electric. The brass is polished. The long tables are bolted to the deck.

The room is far too bright.

Underneath it all, the Gull creaks low.

JEB (O.S.)
They tell themselves the sea is a thing
you cross. A thing you measure. A thing
you own because your compass says north
and your engine still answers.

CARLOS VEGA sits at his monitor station in the back of the room. Each camera feed is on a monitor. House cameras, deck cams, and audio levels.

His eyes don’t leave the screens.

MARTIN KIM sits at a compact sound board next to the bar. Headphones on. One hand over the faders. A laminated cue sheet shaking in the other.

JEB (O.S.)
Then the needle turns stupid.
The horizon goes soft.
The radio starts prayin’ in somebody
else’s voice.

Martin fires a LOW THUNDER cue.

The passengers react with delighted little gasps.

Carlos glances from Jeb to the audio meter.

The meter jumps with the thunder hit. Clean. Controlled.

Carlos still narrows his eyes.

JEB BLACKWATER stands at the front of the galley. His coat is cheap and cliche. 

A painted banner behind him reads:
WELCOME ABOARD THE BLOODY GULL — BERMUDA TRIANGLE NIGHT CRUISE

JEB
(lifts his mug a beat too late)
And that, friends, is when a man learns
the ocean does not care how modern he is.

Laughter. Applause.

Near the front, a LITTLE BOY, seven or eight, watches Jeb with total belief.

The boy’s hand rests against the shiny wall panel beside him.

Under his palm, the panel gives a tiny pulse. Soft enough that only he feels it.

He looks down.

Carlos’s camera drifts toward him.

On the monitor, the polished wall glitches for two frames into dark, wet and rotted timber.

The boy presses harder.

The Gull creaks low and tense.

JEB
(his jaw sets)
Careful there lad.

The boy freezes.

The room’s focus turns to him. The audience is charmed.

Jeb moves to the boy’s table, still performing, but his eyes are locked on the wall.

LITTLE BOY (whispers)
(he looks at Jeb)
It moved.

A few of the adults “awe” at the little boy.

JEB
(crouches next to the boy)
Aye. Ships do that.
(leans closer, whispers)
Especially the old ones.

LITTLE BOY (concerned)
(he hesitates)
It felt sad.

Jeb holds the boy’s eyes a moment too long.

JEB
(places his hand on the wall next to the boy’s)
Sad?
(he nods)
Listen to the lad. He’s got the makings of a proper sailor. Already knows every ship’s got moods.

The Bloody Gull groans deep.
The lights flicker once.

At Carlos’s station, an audio level rises by itself.

Martin’s eyes snap to the sound board.

MARTIN
(under his breath)
That wasn’t me.

Carlos leans closer to a monitor.

The house speakers crackle and RADIO STATIC bleeds through the galley.

Then a distorted voice cuts in.

DISTORTED VOICE (V.O.)
(through speakers, distorted)
…cannot see land… repeat…
We cannot see land…

The passengers perk up.

One woman raises her phone.

On her screen, it plays like part of the show: Jeb, warm lights, polished brass.

TOURIST WOMAN
(looking at her phone screen)
Oh, that’s creepy.

TOURIST MAN
(tilts his head to one side and nods)
Nice.

He looks past Jeb to the speakers, then toward Martin’s board, trying to find the trick.

The woman beside him laughs and nudges his arm.

He shrugs and turns his attention back to Jeb.

Martin stares at the board. Sweat building on his brow.

None of the labeled cues are active.

His hand hovers over the master volume fader.

Carlos catches Martin’s wrist before he can pull it down.

He points two fingers at the waveform climbing on a dead channel, then at the monitor clock.

He signals to let it roll.

Martin understands and pulls his hand back, breathing shallow.

DISTORTED VOICE (V.O.)
Both compasses are out…
everything looks wrong…

Jeb makes a show of turning toward the speakers. It’s a long and dramatic motion, but his eyes are sharp.

CARLOS
(checking waveforms)
That is not playback.

MARTIN
(low, controlled panic)
Carlos…

Jeb looks at the passengers.

Twenty smiling faces waiting for the scare to get better.

JEB (growl)
(he leans in between a young married couple)
Hear that?
(pauses while the couple jumps)
The Triangle’s full of voices. Pilots.
Sailors. Men who thought the world was
built with edges they could trust.

The passengers listen raptly.

The little boy keeps his hand off the wall.

JEB (CONT'D)
(points toward the black windows)
But out there?
(shakes his head)
Out there, every road can forget where
it was meant to go.

The speakers POP.

DISTORTED VOICE (V.O.)
…we are entering white water…
nothing looks right…

Carlos looks out the nearest window and sees nothing abnormal.

But then in the glass he sees the reflection of five small aircraft shapes moving through broken light.

The passengers applaud the “sound effects.”

Jeb stands in front of them with his pewter mug in his hand. He has a blank stare on his face.

JEB
Thank ye. Drink what’s left.
Tip the crew. And if the sea starts whisperin’ your name on the way home…
(pauses before he takes a bow)
Pretend you didn’t hear it.

The crowd erupts in laughter and applause.

Martin snaps into action at the sound board.

His hands are shaking, but they go to the right places.

He pulls faders down and pushes other faders up.

Then he takes the microphone.

MARTIN
(over house speakers, bright but tight)
And now, folks, keep those hands together for The Bloody Gull Boys.

He pulls the mic volume down.

A LIVE BAND tucked against the side of the galley kicks in before the radio signal can own the room again.

THE BLOODY GULL BOYS launch into “Into the Triangle.”

The passengers turn toward the band. Phones rise again. Chairs scrape. Someone whoops. Someone starts clapping along on the wrong beat.

DISTORTED VOICE (V.O.)
(under the music, distorted)
…everything looks wrong…

The loud drunken melody and the passenger’s excitement sits above the radio signal but Carlos and Martin hear it.

Jeb doesn’t look at either of them. He keeps his fake smile aimed at the passengers.

Carlos checks playback from his camera.

Five small planes for two frames. Then gone.

The radio on his desk CRACKLES.

GUS MADDOX cuts through the static. Low. Tight.

GUS (V.O.)
Get up here, ‘Los. We got something.

Martin gives Carlos a quick nod and pushes the band in the house.

The Bloody Gull Boys get louder and the crowd follows.

Carlos grabs his camera and slips out of the galley behind the tourists.

Jeb stays where he is. He plants his palm against the wall where the boy touched it.

For one breath, the polished panel looks like the timber of a centuries old pirate ship.
