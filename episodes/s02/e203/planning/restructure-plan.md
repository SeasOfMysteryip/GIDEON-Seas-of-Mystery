## Practical Bottom Line

The restructure should cut the episode down from a presumed eighteen-scene outline to roughly thirteen scenes. The biggest structural wins are:

- Merge A1S3 and A1S6.
- Merge A2S2 into A2S1.
- Collapse Act Three setup/reveal/explanation into four consequence-driven scenes.
- Protect A1S1, A1S2, A1S4, A1S5, and A2S1 until their new neighboring cards are locked.

## What Changed

- The Act One tourist-cover material is distinct, visual, and humiliating in a useful way.
- Sophia's Benjamin track has a strong governing idea: kindness as architecture.
- Voss/pattern recognition gives the modern-world pressure a face without requiring the faction to be fully explained yet.
- The Act Three rescue/trap convergence is strong if it is played as one door, not explained three times.
- A1S1 through A1S3 currently risk repeating the same idea: the Gull is hiding badly in plain sight.
- A1S4 and A1S6 both carry watcher pressure unless A1S4 becomes active inference and A1S6 becomes physical bleed.
- Sophia's Act Two middle can become a loop of curated access unless A2S2 is absorbed and A2S3 turns her into an investigator.
- Act Three is over-separated; several scenes appear to be different angles on the same rescue/trap/breach event.
