# EP203 Act / Scene Breakdown

# EP203 — Sharpened Act Spine Act One — The Year Wound Act Two — Controlled Truths Act Three — Nassau Contact Strongest Current Cut List Scene-by-Scene Input Packet for You EP203 has a strong shape now

## Quick Read
EP203 has a strong shape now: the year jump hurts, the Gull’s humiliation is visible, Sophia’s captivity is active instead of passive, and Voss becomes useful without being cleanly trustworthy. The strongest choice is the mirrored captivity engine: Jeb is free but degraded into performance; Sophia is captive but treated like a chosen heir. Keep that contrast. Cut anything that turns this into a faction explainer or a villain-board episode.

## What's Working
- The pirate-tour cover is the right Act One engine because it makes the time jump physical, embarrassing, and playable.
- Flight 19 bleeding through the dinner performance is a clean way to fuse public humiliation with real fracture pressure.
- Sophia’s Cyclops track works because Benjamin gives her true information in a controlled environment, which is more dangerous than obvious lies.
- The Bering Sea lead is strong because nobody creates it; everyone detects it, which protects the rule that the Gull cannot open a fracture from the real world.
- Voss watching before contact makes her feel competent and slightly invasive before she ever explains anything.

## What's Soft
- Lark’s detection role needs calibration. If Voss brings the whole answer, Lark loses competence; if Lark solves too much, Voss loses purpose.
- Sarah Briggs needs one clean clue, not a cluster of clues. The newspaper clipping is strong, but it needs a precise emotional function.
- Benjamin’s dinner scene risks overexplaining his ideology too early. Let awkward fatherhood and curated evidence do more work than speeches.
- The Nassau contact sequence needs a sharper reason why Voss chooses now instead of six months ago.
- The Act Three deck ending is right, but it needs a character argument before departure so the decision costs something.

## Story Pressure To Exploit
The episode should run on desperation dressed as routine. The Gull is surviving, but survival has become humiliation. Sophia is being treated well, but that kindness is a cage. Voss offers a route, but help from surveillance is still control. Every scene should ask: who is letting someone move, and what do they expect in return?

## Three Viable Paths
1. Safer canon path: Keep Bering Sea detection mostly operational: Carlos captures Flight 19 bleed, Gus/Linda realize the ship crossed farther into the Bermuda fracture zone than intended, and Voss later confirms an active residual access window in the Bering Sea.
   Tradeoff: This protects rules and avoids overloading Lark, but the myth pulse may feel less personal unless Lark gets a small relic reaction.
2. Stronger emotional path: Make Jeb’s public performance the emotional spine of Act One and Sophia’s awkward dinner with Benjamin the emotional spine of Act Two. The mythology lands because it wounds them differently.
   Tradeoff: This means cutting or delaying some faction detail so the hour stays character-driven.
3. Wilder myth path: Let the Bering Sea signal appear through three imperfect systems: Carlos’s captured Flight 19 bleed, Sophia’s glimpse of Benjamin’s fracture map, and a brief Spyglass flare near Lark that gives only a flash of Bering Sea before going inert again.
   Tradeoff: This gives the episode stronger convergence, but it raises rule pressure around what the Spyglass can show and what ArcLight can know.

## What Sticks
- Jeb Performing His Own Ghost Story
  - Why it sticks: It turns the year jump into behavior. Jeb has to sell the myth of himself to tourists while privately knowing the real sea has shut him out.
  - Story use: Gives Act One its humiliation engine and keeps the surface-world exile from feeling abstract.
- Flight 19 as a Dinner-Show Bleed
  - Why it sticks: The tourists laughing and applauding while Carlos hears a real impossible distress call is exactly the right tonal wound: wonder misread as entertainment.
  - Story use: Creates the episode’s first real fracture proof and gives Carlos, Gus, and Linda an active discovery lane.
- Sophia Inside the Comfortable Cage
  - Why it sticks: Benjamin’s danger is not cruelty first. It is access, family, truth, and permission. That is harder for Sophia to reject cleanly.
  - Story use: Turns Act Two into investigation and seduction instead of prisoner exposition.
- Sarah Briggs as the Crack Benjamin Cannot Smooth Over
  - Why it sticks: Sarah personalizes the Revisionist archive. The altered-history room becomes more than lore when Sophia finds a family wound inside it.
  - Story use: Gives Sophia a reason to distrust Benjamin’s curated version of truth without exposing the Last Benjamin reveal yet.
- Voss as Help That Arrives Through Surveillance
  - Why it sticks: She is useful because she knows more than they do, and disturbing because she only knows that by watching them fail.
  - Story use: Creates Act Three friction and gives Gus a practical reason to oppose Jeb’s distrust.

## Working Document
### EP203 — Sharpened Act Spine
## Quick Read

EP203 should not feel like a reset. It should feel like a year-long wound finally breaking open.

The clean act progression is:

1. **Act One — The Gull has survived by becoming a performance of itself.**
2. **Act Two — Sophia learns that Benjamin’s cage is built from useful truths.**
3. **Act Three — Voss gives the crew a way forward, but not a clean one.**

The strongest choice is to keep the episode focused on mirrored captivity. Jeb is technically free but spiritually trapped. Sophia is technically captive but being treated as important, chosen, and almost royal. Voss enters as the third version of the same pressure: help that comes with an agenda.

## What Can Be Cut

- Cut any full villain summit in this episode.
- Cut any heavy ArcLight doctrine.
- Cut any big explanation of why the Bering Sea is active.
- Cut extra Sarah clues beyond one primary clue.
- Cut any version where Voss simply hands them the answer without Lark, Carlos, Gus, or Linda having already found pieces.

Keep the hour practical: performance, evidence, archive, surveillance, departure.

### Act One — The Year Wound
## Act Purpose

Act One proves the Gull has survived the year by turning itself into a tourist attraction. The crew is not idle. They are using the pirate-tour route as camouflage for fracture detection, but the work has become humiliating and spiritually corrosive.

## Scene Spine

### A1S1 — Dinner Show in the Bermuda Route

**Scene purpose:** Show Jeb performing his own legend while the crew secretly monitors for fracture behavior.

**Playable action:**
- Jeb tells an old Bloody Gull story to tourists in the galley.
- Martin runs sound.
- Carlos films the event as part of the cover.
- Beneath the dinner-show routine, the crew is monitoring strange readings.
- Flight 19 appears in a porthole reflection or camera frame.
- Flight 19 radio calls bleed through the speakers.
- The passengers think it is part of the show.
- Jeb has to keep performing through something that may be real.

**Turn:** The show accidentally catches a real fracture bleed, and only the crew understands the danger.

**Input needed from you:**
1. Does Jeb hear the Flight 19 radio bleed clearly, or only Carlos/Martin?
2. Does Jeb break rhythm for one second, or does he cover perfectly?
3. What is the old story Jeb is telling when the bleed interrupts him: sea sprites, Flight 19 folklore, Bermuda myth, or a Bloody Gull legend?
4. What does Carlos capture exactly: reflection, audio, deck light, or impossible image on playback?

### A1S2 — Carlos Slips Out

**Scene purpose:** Move the fracture discovery from public performance to crew evidence.

**Playable action:**
- Carlos gets a radio call from Gus.
- He leaves the galley while the Bloody Gull Boys begin their set to cover the transition.
- Carlos reaches the deck and finds Gus and Linda comparing anomalies.
- Linda has captured the Gull’s deck lighting up.
- Carlos has Flight 19 evidence.
- Gus realizes they are farther into the Bermuda Triangle fracture zone than they thought.

**Turn:** This is not another false signal. It is the closest they have come in nearly a year.

**Input needed from you:**
1. What does Linda’s footage show that Carlos’s does not?
2. Does Gus want to turn back because of tourists aboard, ship risk, or fear of a public incident?
3. Does Linda want to push on for proof, for science, for Sophia, or because she cannot stand another lost chance?
4. Does anything physically happen to the sea, or is the evidence mostly electronic/reflective?

### A1S3 — Safety Versus Proof

**Scene purpose:** Let Gus and Linda embody the Act One argument.

**Playable action:**
- Gus argues for turning back.
- Linda argues they may never get this close again.
- Carlos is caught between the evidence and the danger.
- Below deck, the music and tourist noise continue, making the real argument feel hidden and urgent.
- The fracture behavior fades or collapses before they can act.

**Turn:** They lose the moment. They have proof of proximity, but no access.

**Input needed from you:**
1. Who makes the final call to turn back: Gus, Jeb by radio, or the fracture collapsing before anyone can decide?
2. Does Linda resent Gus in this moment, or does she know he is right and hate that he is right?
3. Does Carlos keep the evidence secret from passengers, or does a tourist almost see too much?

### A1S4 — Lark in Nassau

**Scene purpose:** Show Lark’s year wound separately from the ship’s public humiliation.

**Playable action:**
- Lark is in a room overlooking Nassau harbor.
- She is manic, sleep-deprived, and angry.
- She rereads the Memory of Kings, trying to force it to show something new.
- It does not respond.
- The Spyglass of Storms rests in the Gull’s navigation room or nearby in its cradle, inert.
- Her frustration is not just research failure; it is abandonment by a relic that once mattered.

**Optional small myth beat:** The Spyglass gives one brief, almost dismissible flare: ice, black water, a crab pot line, a coordinate, or the phrase Bering Sea. Then nothing.

**Turn:** Lark has not given up, but obsession has stopped producing answers.

**Input needed from you:**
1. Do you want the Spyglass flare here, or save all Bering Sea confirmation for Voss and Cyclops?
2. If it flares, what is the image: words, map point, ice, crab gear, Flight 19 echo, or Sophia’s perspective?
3. Is Lark alone, or is someone worried enough to check on her?
4. Does Lark hide how bad she has gotten from the others?

### A1S5 — Return to Nassau / Watched from the Shadows

**Scene purpose:** Bring the public cover back into port and seed Voss before contact.

**Playable action:**
- The Gull returns to Nassau.
- Passengers disembark happy, amused, and unaware.
- The crew performs customer-service normalcy while privately shaken.
- Jeb has to accept thanks or applause for the fake version of the real thing that just hurt him.
- Someone watches from the shadows: Voss.

**Turn:** The audience learns the Gull has not only been searching; it has been observed.

**Input needed from you:**
1. Does Voss watch from dockside, a nearby vessel, a customs office, or a hotel/balcony?
2. Does she recognize the Flight 19 anomaly immediately, or has she been waiting for this pattern?
3. Does anyone on the crew notice they are being watched?
4. What is Jeb’s final Act One image: forced smile, private rage, silent retreat, or confrontation with Gus/Lark?

### Act Two — Controlled Truths
## Act Purpose

Act Two proves Sophia is not in a normal prison. Benjamin is giving her access, comfort, history, and family. The danger is that everything true has been arranged to guide her toward his conclusion.

## Scene Spine

### A2S1 — Dinner with Benjamin

**Scene purpose:** Stage the fucked-up family reunion: Sophia wants a father, but something in the room is wrong.

**Playable action:**
- Sophia and Benjamin dine together aboard the USS Cyclops.
- Benjamin tries to tell her what happened in 1872.
- He is awkward and unsure how to perform fatherhood.
- Sophia should be excited, but she senses the shape of a lie or omission.
- Benjamin avoids Sarah Briggs until Sophia brings her up.
- He performs sadness, but the performance does not fully convince her.
- A digital fracture-zone map is visible in the room.
- The audience sees that every zone is inactive except the Bering Sea.
- Sophia may not fully register the map yet.

**Turn:** Sophia realizes Benjamin is not simply sharing family history. He is managing it.

**Input needed from you:**
1. Does Sophia call him father, Benjamin, Captain, or something unstable between them?
2. What does Benjamin say happened in 1872?
3. What detail makes Sophia feel the Sarah omission: his wording, his face, the room, or a missing object?
4. Does Sophia notice the Bering Sea map at all, or is it audience-only in this scene?

### A2S2 — Free Aboard the Cyclops

**Scene purpose:** Show the comfortable cage and the Revisionist social pressure around Sophia.

**Playable action:**
- Sophia moves freely through parts of the Cyclops.
- Crew and staff are overly helpful.
- They anticipate needs, lower their voices, open paths, avoid touching her, or treat her belongings carefully.
- The behavior is reverent but not openly explained.
- Sophia tests the boundaries: doors, corridors, questions, access points.

**Turn:** Freedom aboard Cyclops is real enough to be tempting, but shaped enough to be suspicious.

**Input needed from you:**
1. What is one concrete thing a crew member does that feels reverent but unsettling?
2. Does Sophia enjoy any of the attention before distrusting it?
3. Is Worley present in this social environment, or saved for the later overheard scene?
4. Are there visible guards, or is the control mostly architectural and social?

### A2S3 — The Revisionist Archive

**Scene purpose:** Let Sophia find real evidence that history can be changed.

**Playable action:**
- Sophia passes closed doors and finds one that opens.
- Inside is a library/archive of altered historical records.
- The room contains records of events the audience recognizes as wrong or changed.
- Examples could include Titanic, JFK, Mary Celeste, or another familiar historical event.
- Sophia does not fully understand the system, but the audience understands that history is being revised or preserved across altered versions.
- The room feels too available to be pure accident.

**Turn:** Benjamin’s world has evidence. He is not only lying.

**Input needed from you:**
1. Which familiar altered-history examples do you want on screen? Pick two or three max.
2. Is the archive digital, physical, or a hybrid of old naval records and modern interfaces?
3. Does the room open because Benjamin allowed it, because Sophia is recognized, or because someone made a mistake?
4. What does Sophia take, photograph, memorize, or hide?

### A2S4 — Sarah Briggs Clue

**Scene purpose:** Make the archive personal.

**Playable action:**
- Sophia finds the Sarah Briggs clue inside the archive.
- Current strongest version: an old newspaper clipping saying Sarah Briggs’s body has been found.
- The date, place, wording, or archive category should make the clipping feel wrong.
- Sophia realizes Benjamin omitted more than grief.

**Turn:** The lesson about altered history becomes a family wound.

**Input needed from you:**
1. Do you want to keep the newspaper clipping as the primary clue?
2. What exactly is wrong about the clipping: date, location, cause of death, duplicate record, or the fact that Sarah should not have a recovered body?
3. Does the clipping imply Sarah is dead, displaced, duplicated, or revised?
4. Does Sophia hide the clue, confront Benjamin immediately, or go looking for more?

### A2S5 — Sophia Overhears Benjamin and Worley

**Scene purpose:** Give Sophia future-useful enemy intel without staging a villain meeting.

**Playable action:**
- Sophia heads to confront Benjamin.
- She stops when she hears Benjamin speaking with Captain Worley.
- They discuss the Phoenician pirates: Blackbeard, Ching Shih, Murakami.
- The line should imply alliance without trust.
- Benjamin sees them as useful but limited, or as tools that will outlive their usefulness.
- Sophia learns there is friction between enemy factions.

**Turn:** Sophia gains leverage: Benjamin’s alliances are not stable.

**Input needed from you:**
1. Which line is sharper: “useful until they outlive their usefulness” or “they do not see the big picture”?
2. Does Worley agree with Benjamin, challenge him, or warn him not to underestimate the pirates?
3. Does Sophia hear enough to name Blackbeard, Ching Shih, and Murakami, or only enough to know there is mistrust?
4. Does Benjamin know she overheard, or is this genuinely outside his control?

### Act Three — Nassau Contact
## Act Purpose

Act Three turns the episode from humiliation and captivity into motion. Voss should not feel like rescue. She should feel like the first useful person the Gull has every reason not to trust.

## Scene Spine

### A3S1 — Voss Approaches Gus and Linda First

**Scene purpose:** Let Voss enter through the practical crew lane instead of immediately through Jeb.

**Playable action:**
- Voss approaches or corners Gus and Linda in Nassau.
- She already knows they crossed into a stronger fracture proximity than usual.
- She references enough shared Season One history to prove she is not random.
- She does not mention ArcLight by name if you want to hold that back.
- She speaks operationally: tracking, windows, residual access, risk.
- Gus is more open to her than Jeb will be.
- Linda wants the data because proof matters.

**Turn:** Voss has information that matches what they just experienced.

**Input needed from you:**
1. Where does this conversation happen: dock, tavern, back office, pier, or aboard the Gull?
2. Does Voss seek out Gus because she trusts him, because he is practical, or because Jeb would reject her first?
3. What exact proof does Voss show: Bering Sea readings, their own route data, Flight 19 correlation, or a map of active/inactive fracture zones?
4. Does Linda challenge Voss harder than Gus does?

### A3S2 — Jeb Finds Out / The Crew Argument

**Scene purpose:** Force the Bering Sea choice through character conflict.

**Playable action:**
- Jeb learns Voss has contacted Gus and Linda.
- He is angry: not only because of distrust, but because she has been watching his ship.
- Gus argues that distrust does not change the data.
- Linda argues they cannot waste the first real lead in a year.
- Lark either confirms she has seen something small or admits she has nothing and hates needing Voss.
- Voss refuses to explain the full group behind her.

**Turn:** Jeb cannot trust Voss, but he also cannot ignore the Bering Sea.

**Input needed from you:**
1. What is Jeb angriest about: surveillance, outsiders near his crew, dependence, or the possibility that Voss is right?
2. What does Gus say that actually lands with Jeb?
3. Does Lark defend Voss’s data, attack it, or quietly confirm part of it with the Spyglass flare?
4. What does Voss refuse to answer directly?

### A3S3 — The Bering Sea Lead

**Scene purpose:** State the operational problem cleanly without explaining the full mythology.

**Playable action:**
- Voss reframes the crew’s mistake: they are not opening fractures; they are chasing residual access.
- Bermuda gave them bleed, not entry.
- Bering Sea is the only currently active window her people are watching.
- Nothing has entered or exited there recently, which makes it strange rather than safe.
- The window is unstable and may close.
- The cause is unknown in EP203.

**Turn:** The Bering Sea is not a rescue route. It is a bad opportunity.

**Input needed from you:**
1. Does Voss say “residual access,” or do you want a more natural phrase like “a door already cracked open”?
2. How much hard data does she give: coordinates, timing, weather, anomaly type, or only destination?
3. Does she warn them not to go, or tell them they have to go now?
4. Does she offer resources, escort, a contact, or only information?

### A3S4 — Lark, Jeb, Gus, and Linda on Deck

**Scene purpose:** End the episode with the crew choosing motion under pressure.

**Playable action:**
- Lark, Jeb, Gus, and Linda stand together on deck.
- Nassau and the tourist cover are behind them.
- The Bering Sea route is ahead.
- No one feels victorious.
- Jeb accepts the course because staying means returning to performance and failure.
- Gus has to carry the practical burden of the choice.
- Linda carries proof hunger.
- Lark carries obsession and maybe shame that Voss had the missing piece.

**Turn:** The Gull leaves the tourist lane because it has to, not because it has won.

**Input needed from you:**
1. Who gives the order to depart?
2. What is Jeb’s final line or final silent action?
3. Does Voss come with them, stay in Nassau, or direct them from a distance?
4. Does the episode end on the Gull departing, Sophia looking at the Sarah clue, or a crosscut between both?

  - Canon Compatible - This gives the opening a strong crew mechanics lane, but Jeb’s private reaction needs a later beat so he is not only the humiliated performer.

  - Canon Candidate - This keeps Lark active without making her solve the episode. The risk is relic-rule pressure if the Spyglass has not been defined for this behavior.

  - Sophia finds an old clipping: Sarah Briggs’s body has been found. The wording, date, or source feels wrong enough to crack Benjamin’s story but not enough to reveal the Last Benjamin truth.
  - Canon Candidate - This is clean and visual, but less emotionally immediate than a voice fragment.

  - Sophia finds a damaged recording or archived voice note from Sarah that contradicts Benjamin’s sadness or warns against his idea of purpose.
  - Canon Candidate - More emotional force, but higher convenience risk unless the archive logic is very controlled.

  - Canon Compatible - This makes Voss’s timing practical and sharp, but it makes her first impression colder.
