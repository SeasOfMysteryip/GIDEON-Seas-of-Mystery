###### ***A1S1 \- The Compass Turns Without Her***

EXT. QUEEN ANNE’S REVENGE — PRIVATE CHAMBER — NIGHT

The fog outside the warped glass panels looks bruised.

Violet and amber streaking through the static flash of the sky like the Spectra Sea is remembering an old wound.

Inside the Queen Anne’s Revenge, stillness.

Sophia Briggs stands at the edge of the chamber \- backlit by leylight creeping through the timber wall behind her.

Her arms hang loose, but her posture is rigid. Her eyes shimmer, but they don’t shake.

Her compass, the Compass of the Drowned, rests on the table besides her.

It’s not still.

The needle quivers, stutters \- then turns.

Not magnetic. Not logical.

ZZZZZZ.

It decides where it goes.

And Sophia’s right index flicks.

The Compass of Ley Currents stops.

TICK.

From the opposite side of the room, a voice cuts the silence like a fond insult \-
                    BLACKBEARD (low, easy)
            (chuckles)
        You know what’s funny, girl?
        The war never ended.

His ring, the Phoenician made Ember Crown, flares on his right hand.

For a moment, the air fights to keep its own oxygen.

BLACKBEARD (cont’d)
Same god damn war.
Just different clothes.
    (beat)
And different names.

He’s framed in silhouette \- lounging like a ghost who’s made peace with his afterlife.

Cheerful. Loose. A man who commands armies and still talks like he’s just leaning on a bar.

                    BLACKBEARD
        Jeb always thought he could sail through a storm
        just by smilin’ at it.
            (beat)
        I love him like he’s my own blood.
        A brother.
            (voice cracks, briefly)
I hope he’s smilin’ when I have to put him under.

Sophia doesn’t flinch.

But her veins \- just beneath her skin \- begin to glow.

Faint at first. Then they flare.
                    SOPHIA (steeled, calm)
        He’s more than you shall ever be, Captain.

She flicks her finger again. The Compass spins.

ZZZZZZ.

A low echo beneath the spin. Not words — just the shape of
    a name.

                    SOPHIA (cont’d)
        You’re too calm for a man with a storm chasing
        him.

                    BLACKBEARD
        Jeb? That storm is mine. He always has been.
            (beat)
        And I’ve named every wave.

He pushes off the wall with deliberate ease, drifting toward the table.

Never threatening.

That’s the threat.

He glances at the Compass \- its needle spinning, then locking dead north.

Only… the Sea doesn’t have a north.

                    BLACKBEARD (cont’d)
        Haven’t seen it flare like that since…
            (smirks)
        Well… since your father opened the first breach.
        Guess the blood runs hot both ways.
Sophia’s jaw tightens. But her breath stays slow.

She steps closer. One hand near the Compass, but not reaching.

It turns again \- without her.

It wants her.

Or maybe it wants what’s in her blood.

                    BLACKBEARD (cont’d)
        The Phoenicians knew how to burn the world open.
            (beat)
        My people just kept the match lit.
        And you?

He shakes his head. His eyes close.

                    BLACKBEARD (cont’d)
            (he slowly looks up and opens his eyes)
        You’re the daughter of both fires.

Sophia studies him \- no fear, but a ripple of uncertainty.

He’s not lying. He’s not manipulating.

He’s reliving.

                    SOPHIA (low)
        You were there.

                    BLACKBEARD
            (shrugs, amused)
        More than once.
            (beat)
        We didn’t sail time back then. We broke it into
        pieces and sold it like rum.

The ring on his right ring finger simmers. The air around it looks like it’s melting.

BLACKBEARD (cont’d)
        The Atlanteans built locks.
        We built keys.

A long silence.

The Compass pulses once \- sharp and red.

Sophia winces \- but doesn’t pull back.

                    BLACKBEARD (cont’d)
        You’re feeling it, aren’t you.
        The pull.
        That little hum in your blood.
        That’s not magic, love.
        That’s your soul remembering.
        Trying to wake up.

Sophia closes her eyes.

Just for a second.

There’s a storm building inside her.

The Shell of Memory is gone. But she doesn’t need it.

The Spectral Sea itself dwells in her. The heartbeat of the sea is her own.

She opens her eyes again \- calm. Testing him.

                    SOPHIA (cold, near whisper)
        You think you’ve got this whole thing charted.
        But you missed something.

Blackbeard tilts his head. Curious.

                    SOPHIA (cont’d)
            (beat)
        He’ll come for me.

That lands.

Not like a threat.

Like truth with gravity.

The Compass of the Drowned flares \- red, violent, personal.

The tremor rumbles the floor beneath them.

Blackbeard’s smile doesn’t falter \- but his eyes narrow.

Not fear.

He recognizes.

                    BLACKBEARD (quiet)
            (almost… proud)
        Aye.
        That he will.

CUT TO BLACK.

###### ***A1S2 \- The Armada Waits***

EXT. FRACTURE BLEED — THE CRIMSON LOTUS — COMMAND DECK — DAY

The water is like onyx. Black. Still. Death.

But beneath the surface \- a hum.

THUMP.

Ships. Hundreds. Some flicker in and out of phase \- spectral outlines trying to remember what era they belonged to.

THUMP.

The Crimson Lotus holds center \- her hull obsidian red, her sails whispering in no wind.

A massive U.S. Navy collier floats just off the starboard line, immaculate. Too immaculate.

There’s towers and discs that collect the static in the sky.

She has an amber shimmer just beneath her gunmetal gray hull.

Ching Shih’s crew \- as hauntingly silent as they are still \- like the deck of the Crimson Lotus.

Ley charts hover in slow rotation. Holographic pulses drift like constellations seen through the fog of time.

Ching Shih stands alone at the command helm.
No orders spoken. Her crew doesn’t need them.

The Crown of the Abyss, twisted and forged black coral, breathes. Salt and seawater run from it.

Yet it captures every bit of leylight around it.

Almost like a beacon.

Her presence is gravitational.

She’s the master of her crew, her ship \- the Sea itself.

Behind her, a reflection moves. A man \- only seen in shadow, never in focus.

                    BENJAMIN BRIGGS (O.S.)
        They always said Atlantis fell.
        Broken by pride. Drowned by wrath.

                    CHING SHIH (cold, venom)
        They have always been wrong.
        It wasn’t pride.
        It was cowardice.
            (beat)
        And the lack of strength to wield true power.

She doesn’t turn around.

She watches as two of the ships shimmer \- timelines fighting for dominance in their hull shapes.

A Roman trireme with radar towers. A modern Chinese missile frigate bearing sails.

                    BENJAMIN BRIGGS (O.S.)
        And now?

                    CHING SHIH
        Now we have the girl.
            (beat)
        Just as you promised…
        The storm…
            (listening)
        …listens to her.

She steps closer to the helm. A faint compass needle \- embedded in the glass itself \- begins to twitch.

It’s not hers.

But it knows her command.

                    BENJAMIN BRIGGS (O.S.)
        Is she ready?

A flicker of emotion passes across Ching’s face.

Regret? Admiration? Calculation?

It’s a three.

                    CHING SHIH
        That’s not the question.

She finally turns, finally faces the silhouette. His form glows faintly \- leylight warping the edges like he’s not entirely here.

                    CHING SHIH (cont’d)
        The question is whether we are.
            (a long beat)
        Whether you are.

A longer silence.

The sound of the Sea \- even here \- grows louder. Like breath building toward scream.

THUMP.

Ching lifts her hand \- not in gesture. She’s embracing all that’s around her.

THUMP.

Every line in her palm carries the weight of a war unspoken.

                    CHING SHIH (cont’d)
        Everything we’ve built, Benjamin…
        She will either sanctify…
            (beat)
        Or burn it.

The armada of ships flickers again.

From the deck of the massive Proteus Navy vessel, a sigil flares.

Red. Old. Phoenician.

Very much alive.

THUMP.

###### ***A1S3 \- The Compass Within***

INT. QUEEN ANNE’S REVENGE — LOWER CORRIDOR — DAY

The Queen Anne’s Revenge groans.

Not because she’s straining. She’s breathing.

Something in the ship is alive. Watching.

Sophia Briggs slips into shadow, her breath held.

She’s not feeling the shudder of the Queen Anne’s Revenge.

No Shell. No Chronometer. No map.

Just her.

And her blood.

She crouches behind rusted rigging cables, the metal warped and veined with ley scorch.

Her fingers tremble midair. She’s not afraid.

She’s hearing the reverb of the Spectral Sea.

Tiny specks of blue flames stutter between her knuckles \- not lightning, not magic.

Something older.

Biological. Cosmic. Inherited.

                    SOPHIA (whisper)
        Come on…
        Not because of them.
        Because of me.

She exhales \- and the Sea exhales back.

A seam splits the air six feet in front of her \- like a crack in glass \- mid-freeze.

The world splits like a veil being pulled \- slowly, deliberately \- a crimson-tinted rift like a scar made patient.

Sophia’s eyes go wide.

She lowers her hands.

The rift holds.

No relic. No chant. No compass at her hip.

Just her.

Her inheritance.

Her decision.

She smiles.

Her eyes glow amber gold and Atlantean blue.

From the other end of the corridor \- a hand grazes the railing.

Blackbeard.

He emerges from mist like a man walking out of a history book.

No posture of threat. No weapon in hand.

Just his presence. And his curiosity.

His Phoenician ring glows faintly \- not alight, not forcing \- just… resonating.

Sophia glances down.

The flicker between her fingers begins to echo the ring’s pulse \- like two tides syncing across a trench.
She doesn’t back down.

She smiles again.

Faint. Controlled. Proud.

The kind of smile that means, “I know I belong here now.”

Blackbeard stops a few feet from the rift seam \- admiring it like an old sailor recognizing a new current.

                    BLACKBEARD
        You really are worth the hassle.
            (beat)
        Fascinating.

Sophia turns her eyes on him \- steady.

                    SOPHIA
        If Jeb doesn’t find me, Lark will.

Blackbeard grins \- not wide, not wicked.

Like a man who finally saw something he stopped believing in.
                    BLACKBEARD
        Oh Sophia. That’s the idea.

The rift beats once \- then stabilizes.

The hull doesn’t crack.

The Sea doesn’t rage.

It listens.

###### ***A2S1 — The Bones We Carry***

EXT. BLOODY GULL — MAINDECK — DRIFTING

The Bloody Gull limps through fog like a dying thing.

Her hull leaks light \- leyfire bleeding slow from fresh seams.

From the record player down below, Desperado crackles with warm vinyl tone.

        “Desperado, why don’t you come to your senses…”

Sailcloth hangs torn and prayer-wrapped. The water laps against her ribs like a refreshing breath. Like an apology.

The crew works in silence. Gus grips a line with bloodied knuckles. Kip is stitching sail like a medic threading torn flesh.

Every man moves like a ghost still pretending to be alive.

Above them, on the upper deck, Jeb Blackwater bellows.

                    JEB (tight-throated)
        Run that line again and run it tight.
I want that mainsheet trussed like a noose.

His voice cracks. Not from volume \- from strain.

                    JEB (cont’d)
        And if that stern rail ain’t bolted by nightfall,
        we lose it.

He’s not commanding. He’s bleeding orders out of muscle memory.

Barking to stay upright.

        “Your pain and your hunger, they’re drivin’ you
        home…”
“And freedom, oh, freedom, well, that’s just some people talkin.”

The Cutlass of Surge glows faint on his hip. Like it’s listening.

Lark Hawthorne watches from the stairwell. Still. Silent.
Cold fog curling around her shoulders.

She sees the deck. She sees the crew.

The cracks.

But her eyes stay locked on the Captain.

The ship groans \- a sound like teeth grinding underwater.

Jeb grips the starboard rail.

Knuckles white. Eyes burning on nothing.

                    JEB (low)
            (to himself)
        Charles didn’t plan it.
He didn’t need to. He was just following orders.
    (beat)
        Just like Blackbeard taught us.

The Cutlass pulses once \- a quiet flicker.

                    JEB (cont’d)
        Lysara died because someone followed orders.
        Red burned because someone followed orders.
        Sophia vanished because I went chasing ‘em.

Behind him, the fog shivers. The horizon doesn’t move. It just waits.

                    LARK (quiet)
        Is that why you’re angry?

Jeb turns. Doesn’t answer.
                    LARK
            (steps forward, steady)
        Or is it because you think it should’ve been you?

The crew keeps working. They don’t look. They don’t stop. This is a sacred silence.

Jeb exhales like it’s dragging salt from his lungs.

                    JEB (quiet)
You ever look at someone… and you know…
you know that they’re gonna end up like the rest of em?
    (beat)
Lysara. Red.


He looks at the Shell of Memory sitting on the stairs where
    Lark was sitting.

                    LARK
        She’s a lot stronger than you give her credit
            for.

                    JEB (grim)
        That’s what’s got me worried, Lark.
        For all of us.

He doesn’t wait for her answer.

                    JEB
    You know what we’re up against, and what we are
    involved in now. I’ve heard ye two talk about it.
            (beat)
    You two can’t escape it. It’s who ye are.
    And I promised Elias to protect ya both.
The Cutlass glows again \- then dims.

        “Desperado, why don’t you come to your senses?
        Come down from your fences, open the gate…”

Lark watches his back vanish into the fog.
She doesn’t move. She doesn’t breathe.

Below them, the Gull creaks \- not in pain, but in mourning.

###### ***A2S2 — The Relic Circuit***

INT. BLOODY GULL — CAPTAIN’S GALLEY — NIGHT

The Spectral Sea is unusually calm, but a storm resonates in the Bloody Gull’s timbers.

A single candle burns in the center of the mess table, crew men and women are in and out.

The flame leans sideways \- drawn, not by wind, but by something in the ship’s bones.

The walls creak like they’re holding their breath.

Lark sits at the table, elbows tight, spine stiff.

The Memory of Kings is open wide in front of her.

The pages are worn, weather-faded. Gold ink spiderwebs across them \- fractured, hand-drawn ley charts laced with nautical glyphs.

Not just a map.

A heartbeat written in ink.

Jeb leans in the galley doorway, arms crossed, watching with that long, salt-buried stare.

He pulls on the joint between his lips, long and slow.

He holds the smoke for a moment, then exhales it through his nose.

His Cutlass, the Cutlass of Surge, hangs at his hip \- quiet, for now.
                    LARK (soft, but edged)
        Tell me what you know about an Atlantean relic
        circuit?
What was my dad doing?

Jeb snorts and the joint falls from his lips.

                    JEB
        Sure. Right after the segment on Hollow Earth
        smugglers and that lunatic from Land Pirate
        Radio.
            (picks up the joint, takes another drag)
        “Ten relics to rule them all,” shit, right?
        Ain’t that how these tales go?

Lark sighs deep. She knows a stubborn pirate is going to be a hard sell.

                    LARK
        This one’s not from a radio host, Jeb.
            (beat)
        It’s from my father.

She turns the page. Ink curls like tidal scars. The candle sputters, then steadies.

Jeb steps forward, boots slow against the old boards. He eyes the book like it might bite.

                    JEB (skeptical)
        This supposed to be the real thing?
        I knew that there was something about you and
        Sophia being blood related and “special.”
        But this?

Lark doesn’t answer.

She traces a circuit diagram with her fingertip \- a spiral of relic names woven through compass lines, ghost ship sighting, ley fractures.

And there’s a new one. The Cutlass of Surge.

                    LARK (excited)
        Jeb. Look. The sword.
        You know it’s real, Jeb.
You can feel it as much as I can.

She looks at the spyglass in the book, the compass, the shell, the tuning core \- now the Bloody Gull \- the cutlass.

                    LARK (cont’d)
        He said they weren’t just scattered.
        They were placed.

                    LARK (cont’d)
        Ten relics. Ten anchors. All part of some…
        library.
        A map that only responds to blood.

                    JEB
        The bloodline bullshit again?

                    LARK (snarls)
        You were there when she lit up with the compass
        in her hand.
        Don’t act like it’s all fairy dust.

Jeb grunts. But he’s looking closer now. One glyph \- shaped like a stormglass \- catches his eye.

He leans in. Runs a knuckle along the edge of the page.

The ash from his joint falls but it never hits the pages. It’s brushed away by the book itself.

                    JEB (quietly)
        Blackbeard had that one.
Swore it could hear storms before they hit.
I thought he was just drunk off his own myth.

The page hums.

A soft pulse rolls form the ink \- golden lines flickering like breath caught under candlelight.

The ship creaks louder. Rigging above moans once, as if shifting in sleep.

Lark stills. Eyes wide.

                    LARK (low)
        It’s responding.
        I think he wants to say something.

Jeb steps back a half pace, his gaze narrowing.

The book brightens. The pulse evens out \- rhythmic bow. Like a heartbeat.

She reads.

                    LARK
            (reading aloud)
        “There are ten. Always ten. Bound to the sea and
blood.
Hidden in simple objects. You’ll know the first when you stop searching…”

The flame flutters.

And from behind her \- without warning \- Jeb joins her.

                    JEB (quiet, deep)
        …When the Sea names you by your scars.

Lark looks up \- stunned.

                    LARK
        You’ve heard this before?

                    JEB
        He said it. Elias. In a letter Blackbeard stole.
            (beat)

                    JEB (cont’d)
        I read it drunk, thought it was some poetic
        bullshit.
        But I remembered.
You don’t forget a line like that.

She closes the large book slowly \- reverent, like sealing a crypt.

Or a prophecy.

The hum lingers.

From a nearby shelf, the Shell of Memory begins to whisper. Just faint \- voices not yet formed.

Jeb eyes it. Frowns. His voice drops.

                    JEB
        You think your old man really mapped this?
        This relic shit?


                    LARK
        It’s all here Jeb. In this book.
        He’s in this book.
            (beat)
        Wherever you lost him, he ended up here.

She rests her hand on the Memory of Kings.

The book flickers one last time.

Outside, the fog thickens around the hull \- not choking, but waiting. Listening.

Lark stares down at the Memory of Kings again.

                    LARK (soft)
            (almost to herself)
        Mankind’s greatest treasure…
        Not gold. Not power.
        Just… lost knowledge and memories.

CUT TO BLACK.

###### ***A2S3 — The Memory Speaks***

EXT. BLOODY GULL — MAIN DECK — AFT OF THE HELM — NIGHT

The fog hangs thick, but the Gull is lit from within.

Not by lantern. By song.

The Shell of Memory hums \- a sweet, eerie chord that cuts through the hull like it’s always been part of it.
The tone is layered.

Polyphonic.

High harmonics drift into the air like the first breath of a storm-spun choir.

                    SHELL OF MEMORY (faint whispers)
“Raise up yer glass, and drink to the seas\!”
"Drink to the seas\!"

The Gull answers.

Deck boards thrum. A subtle blue pulse ripples outward from the mast seams \- like ley lines waking under the floor.

SHELL OF MEMORY (faint whispers)
To the crew and the Gull, to the current in the
    breeze\!
"Current in the breeze\!"

Jeb stands near the helm, hand resting on the hilt of the Cutlass of Surge.

It glows. Not dimly. Not idly.

A full flare \- electrical blue under the fog, before fading again, like a breath exhaled.

SHELL OF MEMORY (faint whispers)
From the bow to the stern, to the cannons that roar—
We fight, we sing, and we hunger for more\!

Lark clutches the Memory of Kings in both hands \- but she’s not reading.

The book is no longer ink.

Glyphs lift from the page \- not projected, not imagined \- hovering midair, cared from leylight and memories.

They spin. They burn. And then-

                    ELIAS (V.O.)
        (voice like cracked glass layered with warmth)
        This isn’t the first time you two have stood
        here.
        But it might be the last that matters.

Jeb stiffens. Lark doesn’t move.

The air warps. The fog shifts. For just a second, the stars above them twitch.

Constellations glitch forward, then settle. Like the sky is trying to catch up to something they’ve done before.

                    JEB (tight, rattled)
        What are ye saying, Hawthorne?

                    ELIAS (V.O.)
        You were never meant to win, Captain.
            (beat)
        You were meant to remember.

The glyphs continue to dance and the constellations in the sky take new shapes.

**⟁**    Power
**⟁⟁**    The Gates of Realms
**◬**    Ley Rupture
**⌖**    Lost Relics

The Shell’s song deepens. A low chord joins the high. The Gull moans underfoot like an organ about to shift key.

Jeb doesn’t take his eyes off the space where Elias’s voice came from.

Lark doesn’t blink. Her throat’s tight.

                    LARK (whispers)
        The visions in the Spyglass…
        The wrecks. The different futures.
The drowned versions of us.
    (lets out a sigh)
        They weren’t hallucinations.
        They were echoes.
            (she looks at the spyglass)
        Possible futures. Different ends.
            (looks at Jeb)
        Jeb. Alternate timelines. Different versions of
        reality.

The glyphs flicker. The voice sharpens.

**⧖**    Time Locked
**⟡**     Echo Star Signal Received
**⟁⟡**     Threaded Glyph Ley Navigation
**⟐**    Queen’s Command

                    ELIAS (V.O)
        They all lead to this moment, Lark.

The Cutlass of Surge pulses again \- once. Then goes dark.
The Shell of Memory shifts its song \- not a hum now, but a rising, beckoning tone. Like it’s preparing to call someone back.

SHELL OF MEMORY (melodic, rising)
Out on the sea where the stormwinds roar,
    We sail as one, ever hungerin’ for more.
    The Bloody Gull’s our home, our fire, our flame,
    Our name strikes fear in the Builder’s game\!

Jeb swallows. He looks down. Not at the book. At Lark.

And for the first time since the storm that brought her aboard, he doesn’t see a stowaway. Or a documentarian.
Or a daughter of a ghost.

He sees the one who was always meant to find this.

                    JEB (low)
        Let’s bring Sophia home.

###### ***A2S4 — The Lock***

EXT. BLOODY GULL — PROW — NIGHT, LEYFIRE STORM EDGE

The sky isn’t clear \- but it’s cracked open.

Stormlight flickers in slow pulses over the Gull’s bow, bleeding into the fog like breath drawn through fractured lungs.

Lark Hawthorne stands at the front rail. Alone.

Except for every ghost behind her.

In her right hand, the Echo of the Tides.

Her father’s spyglass. Something she’s known her whole life. But now she knows the truth.

Atlantean made. Blood-calibrated. Hers now.
She lifts it like a blade. Like a prayer.

The Gull exhales \- boards groaning.

Leyline traces glow beneath her bare feet, surging toward the bow in a rush of blue fire.

At the helm, Jeb has one hand resting on the hilt of his Cutlass \- silent.

Gus is bracing himself while white knuckling a handful of rigging \- not blinking.

Linda stands with her camera down. Just watching.

The Shell of Memory hums under the deck.

The Cutlass of Surge glows faint again.

The Memory of Kings sits open nearby \- no words spoken, but the air bends like it’s listening.

LARK (soft)
            (to herself)
        Come on… show me where she is.

She dials the Echo. Smooth. Familiar. Like a scar she stopped flinching from.

The lens flashes once \- and the Sea spits.

A line of light rips across the fog ahead \- not a beam.

A seam.

A crack in the current. A corridor between moments.

And through it-

Sophia Briggs.

Mid-stride on the Queen Anne’s Revenge, walking toward her own storm.

But she stops.

She lifts her head.

She looks straight through.

Her eyes glow. Blue-gold. Atlantean spiral over Phoenician fractal.

And she smiles.

She sees them.

She sees her.

                    LARK (whispers)
        There you are.

Jeb steps beside her, but not close.

He doesn’t speak for a long moment.

Then he does.

                    JEB (low, reverent)
        She sees you, Lark.

The Gull’s hull lights up like a ley circuit \- deck to stern, prow to mast.

The Abyssal Chronometer ticks and buzzes, unseen gears coming to life.

Old Red’s Bone Dice fuse into the helm. One glows amber, one is onix black with blue leylight as veins.

Sophia lifts her hand. Doesn’t wave \- signals. Like she’s drawing them through. Like she knows.

                    LARK    (steady)
        I’ve got you.
            (beat)
                    LARK    (cont’d)
        Hold on.

A burst of ley energy erupts from the spyglass and the Bloody Gull follows it through the fracture.

The jump begins.

CUT TO BLACK

###### ***A3S1 — RIFT RUN***

EXT. THE SPECTRAL CORRIDOR — FRACTURE VOID — UNKNOWN

The sea is no longer the sea \- it’s a wound.

Cracked open by leyline scars.

She’s not happy.

The Bloody Gull emerges off the starboard of the Queen Anne’s Revenge \- not appearing, but being born from glitched light and shadow.

Her hull snarls against reality. Her sails snap like torn film frames \- frame skips, frame burn, frame gone.

                    JEB (shouting over the ley-scream)
        Gus \- port side cannons\! Fire\!
            (beat)
        NOW\!

The Gull fires.

A searing arc of spectral blue tears out across the void \-

CRACK\!

The Queen Anne’s Revenge jerks \- hull scorched, glyphs on her side flickering gold and angry.

The blast connects.

Devil’s Lantern scratches through the strom — silhouette mimicking the Gull, outline flickering like mirrored flame.

It fires — it doesn’t miss.

But it does. The Gull phases in and out and the blast
    goes through the space where she was

Jeb snaps to the starboard rail —

JEB (shouts)
Devil’s Lantern\! Starboard side\!
