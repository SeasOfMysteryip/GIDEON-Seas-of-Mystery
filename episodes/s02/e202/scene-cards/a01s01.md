## Scene Pulse

- Scene purpose: Open EP202 on Sophia’s active captivity aboard Queen Anne’s Revenge, where Blackbeard does not threaten her by force first — he courts her through history, inheritance, and certainty.
- Entry state: Sophia is isolated in Blackbeard’s private chamber. The Compass rests near her but moves without clean obedience, and Blackbeard enters the silence already in control of the room’s emotional temperature.
- Exit state: Sophia refuses Blackbeard’s worldview by anchoring herself to Jeb’s inevitable rescue; Blackbeard recognizes the truth of that bond instead of dismissing it.
- Turn: The scene shifts from captivity interrogation into mythic seduction: Blackbeard claims the war, the breach, the keys, and Sophia’s bloodline as part of one inheritance.
- Core pressure:
  - Sophia is physically captive but emotionally resistant.
  - The Compass of Ley Currents / Compass of the Drowned behaves as if it wants something from Sophia’s blood rather than simply pointing.
  - Blackbeard’s calm is the threat; he never needs to raise his voice to dominate the room.
  - Blackbeard frames Jeb as beloved brother, doomed rival, and storm already claimed.
  - The Blackbeard’s Ring / Ember Crown flare makes the air itself feel pressurized.
  - Sophia hears more than manipulation in Blackbeard’s claims; some part of him is reliving old war-memory.
  - The Shell of Memory is absent, leaving Sophia without that tool, but the draft asserts she carries the Spectral Sea inside her.
- Emotional turn: Sophia moves from controlled captivity into tested certainty: “He’ll come for me.”
- Exit image: The Compass flares red and violent; the floor tremors; Blackbeard’s smile holds, but his eyes narrow in recognition.
