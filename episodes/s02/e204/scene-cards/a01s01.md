## Scene Pulse

- Scene purpose: Establish the villain-alliance reset for EP204: Blackbeard, Ching Shih, Murakami, and Benjamin Briggs are all using Sophia to begin the relic hunt, but they are not a unified machine.
- Episode function: Open the first full relic-hunt episode after EP203’s breach movement by making Sophia the contested instrument at the center of an unstable alliance.
- Entry state: Sophia is physically outnumbered in a fracture chamber, enemy fleet space, or controlled neutral deck. The exact setting and room controller are not yet chosen.
- Core pressure:
  - Sophia is trapped in a room of people who all speak as if they understand what she is.
  - None of them agree on what she is for.
  - Blackbeard wants relic power as a path back to flesh and mortal land.
  - Ching Shih wants command and strategic control, and is more aligned with Benjamin’s larger strategy than Blackbeard’s hunger.
  - Benjamin wants purpose, containment, control, or correction, but his full involvement should remain unrevealed to the Gull.
  - Murakami serves as Blackbeard’s second while carrying separate Revisionist / Phoenician pressure.
- Turn: Sophia is forced from listening and reading the alliance into performing for it: she must read a relic wake or circuit disturbance that points toward the next hunt site.
- Exit state: The villains have a direction for the next relic hunt, but Sophia has also seen the fracture lines inside the alliance.
- Scene-beats pass:
  1. **The Table Is Already Hostile** — Sophia is brought into or positioned within the fracture chamber / enemy fleet space / controlled neutral deck. No one needs to shout; the power geometry of the room tells her she is outnumbered.
  2. **Alliance Without Agreement** — Blackbeard, Ching Shih, Benjamin, and Murakami frame Sophia as necessary, but their language reveals different uses for her.
  3. **Blackbeard Names Hunger** — Blackbeard pushes the relic hunt toward bodily restoration: relics as the road back to flesh, mortality, and walking real land.
  4. **Ching Names Command** — Ching Shih redirects the room away from appetite and toward disciplined control, making clear she sees Sophia as a force that must be governed, not devoured.
  5. **Benjamin Names Purpose Carefully** — Benjamin offers the cleanest, most persuasive logic. He does not have to sound cruel; his danger is that his explanation may feel useful.
  6. **Murakami Reveals the Second Current** — Murakami appears to support Blackbeard, but his phrasing or attention exposes a separate Revisionist / Phoenician pressure.
  7. **Sophia Reads the Cracks** — Sophia stays intellectually alert, tracking who interrupts whom, who defers, who watches the others, and who avoids naming the whole plan.
  8. **The Relic Wake Answers** — The chamber, map, instrument, circuit mark, or fracture disturbance reacts to Sophia, forcing the room to stop arguing and watch what she can access.
  9. **The Next Hunt Site Emerges** — Sophia is made to read the relic wake or circuit disturbance. The result points toward the next hunt site, while the alliance’s disagreement remains unresolved.
  10. **Exit Image — The Table Holds, Barely** — The villains have what they need for the next move, but Sophia leaves the scene with evidence that this machine can split.
