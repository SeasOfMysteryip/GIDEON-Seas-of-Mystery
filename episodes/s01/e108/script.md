SEAS OF MYSTERY



EPISODE 108 — INTO THE ABYSS








WRITTEN BY:
ADAM BOYD











5/2/2026
ACT ONE

INT. BRIG — SEA BURIAL — NIGHT


The ley lined cell hums. Ley currents race through the timber and irons.

The chains are etched with ancient Phoenician glyphs. They glow and shift with the ley energy’s current.

Clasped and chained in the cell is Sophia Briggs. Her veins glow with a dull gold, flaring in time with her heartbeat.

Phoenician glyphs start to emerge on her skin like tattoos.

Sophia takes a breath and then she smiles

Behind her, the Compass of Ley Currents spins slowly in a containment box.

She hears two thumps above her and she looks up.

Out in the hallway there’s a muffled grunt followed by the sound of something against the wall.

Outside the cell, boots lightly cross the timber floor and then Darak al-Mhar walks into the room.

His coat is soaked and blood drips from the twin blades at his belt.


DARAK
(smiles wide and wild)
Stealing the sea from the man that thinks he owns it?
(he flips his coin)
Now that’s my kind of crime.


Sophia doesn’t move. She doesn’t blink.

The ley energy in the room starts to resemble the ley energy running through her veins.

She smiles slow, like she knows something Darak doesn’t.


SOPHIA (low)
Oh Captain al-Mhar…
(her voice is deeper and wiser, like the sea itself)
Nobody shackles the sea.


She flicks her finger once.
The Compass of Ley Currents flares and screams in its cage.
The Abyssal Chronometer rages from the cell’s bulkhead.

Ley energies cross in a mix of Phoenician amber and Atlantean blue.

The ley cell shudders. Reality knots.
The floor beneath them ripples. Wood planks become water and then wood again.

Sophia’s body shifts. The chains fall.

She grabs her compass and the chronometer and steps out of the ley shield that was holding her back.

INT. HALLWAY — SEA BURIAL — CONTINUOUS


Darak pulls Sophia out into the hallway outside the cell. It’s dimly lit by lanterns and it’s eerily quiet.

Sophia pauses and takes a deep breath. The pulsing ley energy under her skin settles.


DARAK (whisper)
(pulling Sophia forward)
Come on, my young friend.
Now isn’t the time to take in the ship’s beauty.
(he face goes serious)
Murakami will skin us both if we don’t get out of here.


Sophia’s eyes glow blue with gold streaks of ley fire.

As they make their way down the hallway, they pass a body in samurai armor slouched against the wall. His blood paints the wood above him.


SOPHIA (whispers)
The tide is with us, Captain al-Mhar.
(looks at the dead sailor as they pass)
And it looks like YOU are with me.


Darak continues to lead her towards the stairs at the end of the hallway.

EXT. ABOVE DECK — SEA BURIAL — CONTINUOUS


At the top of the stairs, a door to the deck is already open. Two more of Murakami’s men lay dead. Blood from slit throats paints the deck red.

Darak steps through the door first and makes sure there’s nobody waiting for them. Then he ushers Sophia across the threshold.

Sophia’s compass glows violet at her hip. The Abyssal Chronometer shrieks. It’s gears and chains coming to life.

Sophia looks to the starboard rail and sees the beginning of a fracture.
DARAK (whisper)
(points to the rift)
Go, Sophia!
Profits and relics mean nothing if we are both dead, my young associate!


As they run toward the rail, the Sea Burial starts to howl. The voices of Murakami’s men can be heard above the sea’s roar.

Sophia and Darak continue to run. Samurai armored men chase behind them.

The fracture begins to open off the starboard rail. Murakami’s men start to close in around them.


SOPHIA
(looks at Darak)
Do you trust me captain?


DARAK
(looks at Sophia and smiles)
I don’t think I have a choice, young lady.


They close the distance to the rail and they both leap over it.

Murakami’s men watch them disappear into the ley fracture. All that’s left is the static residue where the seam used to be.

CUT TO STATIC.


EXT. DECK — SAYF AL-BAHR — INSIDE THE BREACH — CONTINUOUS


The Sayf al-Bahr cuts through the breach. Her rigging cries and spectral winds coil around the mast.

For a moment, reality bends. The sky turns real and water from the Atlantic ocean washes the deck.

Then reality snaps back.

Sophia and Darak slam out of the fracture. Darak rolls, curses and grins. Sophia doesn’t hit the deck. She lands lightly.

She presses one hand to the soaked planks and for a moment, the Sayf al-Bahr shudders at her touch.


DARAK (laughing)
(hauls himself up to his feet)
Welcome aboard the Sayf al-Bahr, Sophia Briggs.
Captain Darak al-Mhar at your service.
(he bows deep)
For a nominal fee, obviously.


Sophia lifts her head and her eyes burn with leyfire.


SOPHIA
(her eyes meet Darak’s)
Eventually, you will need to pick a side, Jackal.


Behind them, the fracture slams shut and the Sayf al-Bahr rides the Spectral Sea.

CUT TO BLACK.


EXT. DECK — SAYF AL-BAHR — SPECTRAL SEA — NIGHT


The Sayf al-Bahr sails through spectral waters. Ley currents blow beneath her keel.

The Compass of Ley Currents sings at her hip. Its needle pulls more than it points.

Her hands are aglow with ley energy. The Spectral Sea’s ley pulse responds to her hand’s movements.

At the Sayf’s stern, two sets of sails give chase.

On the port side, the Phantom’s Tide and Charles Vane.

On the starboard, the Sea Burial and Murakami.

The Sayf’s crew prepare to deal with a fight while Darak and Sophia keep their eyes on the ley fractures ahead.

Darak grips the Sayf’s wheel with force. His jaw is clenched and he temples are beaded with sweat.


DARAK
(under his breath to himself)
That locket better be worth the effort, Jebediah.


Sophia doesn’t look at him. She doesn’t look at anyone.

She takes control of the leylines swirling beneath the waves.

She speaks them awake.



SOPHIA
(low, crackling with leyfire)
Steady now, Jackal.
You wear the teeth well,
but the sea knows what you are.


EXT. — PROW — PHANTOM’S TIDE — CONTINUOUS


The Phanom’s Tide gives chase like a rabid beast.

Charles Vane stands at the prow. One hand gripping a line and the other holding the Cutlass of Surge.

The blue energy of the blade is reflected in his dark eyes.


VANE
(he turns his head to his crew)
Bring the Sayf and al-Mhar down.
Gut the girl.
(he turns his eyes back to Sophia)
We’ll send her to Jeb in pieces.


The Cutlass of Surge hums. Its spectral flame flares across Vane’s arms. It looks as though it’s pulling him and the Phantom’s Tide towards Sophia.

He snarls and the Phantom’s Tide peels off, splitting from Murakami’s ship.

EXT. DECK — SAYF AL-BAHR — CONTINUOUS


The Sayf al-Bahr cuts through the choppy sea. Ley energy gathers around the hull.

Sophia braces herself for the oncoming ley surge. She feels it before anyone else.
The Abyssal Chronometer groans form Darak’s belt pouch. It too feels the oncoming ley surge. It wants to connect with Sohpia’s blood.

Sophia’s skin is covered in old Phoenician markings and then she lets the chronometer's rhythm sync with her own heartbeat.

A fracture blooms open ahead and then Sophia’s voice rips across the sea.

The Sea Burial fires its cannons.


SOPHIA
(her hands move with the leylines)
Hard to port!
Now!


Sophia moves her hands in an opening motion as Darak jerks the wheel to port.

The Sayf twists and Sophia's fracture swallows the water where the Sea Burial’s shots would have struck.

The sky cracks like thunder and ghost ships from forgotten wars glitch and scream.

EXT. DECK — SEA BURIAL — CONTINUOUS


The Sea Burial lurches as it runs from the glitching storm and ghost ships in the sky.

The cannons still smoke and sizzle around Murakami Hideyoshi.



MURAKAMI (growls)
(his eyes locked on the Sayf and Sohpia)
Sophia Briggs! You are mine!
You have always been mine!


He pounds the rail with his fist as leylines flare around him and the Sea Burial.

EXT. DECK — SAYF AL-BAHR — CONTINUOUS


The Sayf al-Bahr looks like it’s collecting ley energy as it runs from the Phantom’s Tide and the Sea Burial.

Sophia makes her way aft. As she passes Darak al-Mhar at the helm she grabs the Abyssal Chronometer from his belt.

Ley energy starts to ripple away from her body as she climbs the stairs up to the poop deck.

She stops at the aft rail. The Phantom’s Tide and the Sea Burial on either side of her.

She looks up and her eyes are bright with leyfire.


SOPHIA (calm)
(she looks towards the Sea Burial)
You’ll never shackle the sea.


She moves like she’s wielding swords. Instead of steel, she cuts with ley energy. Fractures rip open between the Sayf and the chasing ships.

In an instant, the Phantom’s Tide and the Sea Burial are swallowed in the rage of the sea and the memory storm.

FADE OUT.
ACT TWO

INT. HOLD — BLOODY GULL — MORNING


The Bloody Gull’s core, deep in the hold where the mainmast meets the keel, glows blue. The ley energy thrums like a beating heart.

Old Red watches Lark Hawthorne place the last piece of shattered timber into a gap in the wood. The shard glows and settles into place.


OLD RED
(smiles, rolls his dice in his hand)
There she is. The last piece. The last bit of memory.
(he places a hand Lark’s shoulder and his other on the floor)
She’s whole again. Well done, lass.


Lark nods. She looks confused for a minute, but she lets the moment happen anyway.


LARK
(her face turns serious)
Red?
(she looks up to Old Red)
Are we going to get her back?


The Shell of Memory starts to hum a familiar melody at her ribs and the Echo of the Tides shines at her hip.

Together they create a song they haven’t sung since the Compass of Ley Currents left with Sophia.
OLD RED
(smiles at Lark then stands)
It looks like the sea is saying yes, young lady.

EXT. DOCK — RED HORIZON — CONTINUOUS


The water in Red Horizon is wrong. It’s not calm but it’s not storming either. It’s heavy.

The horizon is what gives the place its name. 

The Bloody Gull sits tied to the pier. Her spectral glow is back. Her crew go about finishing repairs.

Two crewmen finish strapping down Wendy, the submersible submarine.


CREWMAN 1
(looks to the shore)
Red Horizon?
(looks at the other crew member)
What is this place?


CREWMAN 2
(looks up from the straps in his hands)
This is an old smugglers hideout.
Stumpy Pete told us a shit ton of stories about this place.
(he looks to the horizon)
The horizon never changes. Always blood red.
No sun. Ever.


CREWMAN 1
(continues to ratchet strap the sub down)
This is a mirror harbor, right?
What’s on the other side?
CREWMAN 2
(finishes securing the sub)
Barataria Bay. Louisiana.
I’m sure capt…
(he looks up and sees a ship phase through the red horizon)
Get the captain.
We got company.


The first crewman heads to the captain’s quarters while the second crewman goes to find Gus.

EXT. QUARTERDECK — BLOODY GULL — CONTINUOUS


Gus Maddox supervises the finishing work on the shattered quarterdeck rail. Crewmembers drill and wrench new railing into place.


CREWMAN 2 (yelling, O.S.)
SAILS!
Gus! Sails!


Gus looks to the crewman running up to him.


CREWMAN 2
(stops and points to the horizon)
There!
(points again)
One set!


Gus turns to the open water. He squints and tries to recognize the oncoming ship.
GUS (growls)
(doesn’t turn but speaks to the crewman)
Get Jeb. Now.
(turns his attention to the crew working on the rail)
Get ready for a fight.
Just in case.


Jeb steps up the quarterdeck stairs behind him, the first crewman in tow.


JEB
(he removes his tricorn hat)
No fight be happening here.
(looks at the crewmen on the rail)
Finish your work, lads.


Jeb steps up next to Gus and smiles.


JEB
(places a hand on Gus’s shoulder)
It’s her.
It’s Sophia.


Linda, Carlos and Martin make their way onto the deck with the others. Linda and Carlos both with cameras in hand.

Martin picks up the sound of the Shell of Memory’s melody before the others hear it. He gets Carlos’s attention and directs him to film Lark and Old Red stepping onto the deck.

Linda films Jeb’s reaction to the sound.

He turns, looks at Lark and nods.
EXT. DECKS — GULL AND SAYF — DAY

The Sayf al-Bahr slides in beside the Bloody Gull.

The Gull MOANS.

Blue energy pulses through the tide.

Sophia stands alone at the Sayf’s rail.

Barefoot.

Her old sailcloth clothes are gone.

She wears a sea-dark tunic over fitted pirate trousers. A long, tide-worn mantle hangs from her shoulders.

Faint Phoenician angles and Atlantean spirals run through the seams.

The Compass of Ley Currents hangs at her chest.

It spins and sings in harmony with the Shell.

Red light pulses from it.

On the deck of the Bloody Gull, Lark watches Sophia approach.

Lark places a hand over the singing Shell.

LARK
(whisper)
There’s something different about her.

Sophia’s eyes no longer reflect ley currents.

They burn with Phoenician gold and Atlantean blue.

She is calm. Changed.
Darak al-Mhar lingers behind her, leaning against the rail.

Casual posture.

Careful eyes.

He watches Jeb.

The Abyssal Chronometer at Darak’s belt ticks and hums.

Sophia lifts one finger.

Flicks it once.

The Chronometer goes silent.

Across the gap, Lysara’s Locket glows against Jeb’s chest.

EXT. DOCKS — RED HORIZON — DAY

Planks drop between decks and dock.

Boots hit damp wood.

Jeb steps onto the floating dock first.

Slow at first.

Then faster.

Sophia steps down from the Sayf.

Bare feet on wet wood.

She faces him.

Jeb stops.

For a moment, neither of them moves.

Jeb flinches first. He breathes a rattled and shaky breath.
JEB (low)
(he’s near breaking)
I…I thought I might have lost ya for good.


Sophia tilts her head slightly to the right and the Compass of Ley Currents at her chest flares again.

The lid opens and it’s pointing true north. 

Her voice is calmer and more mature than the last time Jeb heard it.


SOPHIA (soft)
(her voice is like the tide)
As one of your old songs says, captain…
You can never shackle the sea.


Jeb nods. Slow.

He swallows hard.

Behind Sophia, Darak shifts his weight. He moves in an easy and casual manner.

He doesn’t look at Sophia. He doesn’t look at Jeb.

He looks at the locket hanging just above Jeb’s heart.

Darak doesn’t speak. His eyes reflect the locket’s shine.

He flips his coin. Catches it.

Jeb’s hand covers the locket and it hums warm against his chest.



JEB (low)
(mutters to Sophia)
I reckon he ain’t done here until he gets what he wants.


Sophia meets Jeb’s stare. Her eyes reflect Jeb’s sadness.


SOPHIA
(she turns and looks at the chronometer at Darak’s belt)
Neither am I.


She flicks her finger one more time and the Abyssal Chronometer ticks in response.

CUT TO BLACK.

EXT. BLOODY GULL - MIDDECK - RED HORIZON - DUSK


Lark and Sophia lean against the midship rail, quiet.
Old Red crouches between them with one hand pressed to the deck.

The relics, Spyglass, Shell and Compass, hum low. Threads of leylight weave between them.

Old Red drums his fingers against the deck. The rhythm is slow and steady.

The Bloody Gull answers with a pulse and a groan through the wood.

Sophia’s bare feet feel it.

Lark watches the relics as the light between them grows brighter.

SOPHIA
(smiles)
She feels…different.


LARK
(she looks at Sophia)
We all do Sophia.
(she studies Sophia’s skin)
We all do.



EXT. BLOODY GULL - MIDDECK - CONTINUOUS


The Shell of Memory glows and starts to sing.

The Compass of Ley Currents at Sophia’s neck flares and hums a soft harmony.

Memory images flash across the deck.

GRAPHIC NOVEL INSERT — FOUR PANEL

— Jeb pulls young Sohpia from the phantom decks of the Mary Celeste.
— Jeb wraps his coat around her shoulders.
— Jeb stands at the wheel of the Gull during a storm.
— A younger Sophia and Jeb laugh together on a dock beneath broken lantern light.

EXT. BLOODY GULL — MIDDECK — CONTINUOUS

Jeb steps onto the middeck from the quarterdeck shadows.

Darak is close behind him, his eyes watching the girls and Old Red.

Lark, Sophia and Old Red turn to see the locket in Jeb’s fist start to glow.
Darak’s eyes move to the locket as well.


DARAK
(his eyes don’t leave Jeb’s fist)
Don’t forget our deal, Jeb Blackwater. The girl for your chain.
(he grips the hilt of the sword at his hip)
Don’t make me rethink the terms.


Jeb’s grip on the locket tightens.

The memories on the deck shift.

GRAPHIC NOVEL INSERT — 4 PANEL

— Lysara stands on the deck of the Bloody Gull beside Jeb.
— The locket is in her hand.
— Young Jeb watches her.
— Lysara places the locket against his chest and kisses his forehead.


LYSARA (whisper)
(into Jeb’s ear)
Anchor.


The Shell of Memory finishes its melody and the memory fades back into the present deck.

Jeb looks at Sophia, then at the place where Lysara’s memory stood.


JEB
Aye.
(to Darak)
A deal’s a deal.

He looks off the portside and sees Lysara swimming beside the Gull.

Jeb smiles and a tear runs down his cheek.

He kisses the locket once, turns to Darak and places it in his hand.

The locket brands Darak’s hand, a sigil spiraling across his palm.

He yelps but grins through the pain.

EXT. BLOODY GULL - MIDDECK - CONTINUOUS


The Bloody Gull lets out a sad groan beneath their feet.

Jeb turns away from Darak and doesn’t see the smuggler tuck Lysara’s locket against his chest.

Instead, Jeb makes his way to Sophia. He stops in front of her. A tear forms in his eye.

Sophia’s relics, the compass and shell hum together.


SOPHIA (whisper)
Jeb…


JEB (hoarse)
(he nods and manages a smile)
You’re home, girl.


EXT. BLOODY GULL - MIDDECK - CONTINUOUS


Old Red’s bone dice roll across the deck by themselves.
Click. Clack. Click.

The compass, the shell and the spyglass sing together.

The Gull’s pulse spreads through the middeck as the relics stabilize and connect.

Lark watches Sophia and Jeb stand in the center of it.

The Bloody Gull feels alive again.

CUT TO BLACK.
ACT THREE

EXT. SAYF AL-BAHR - DECK - RED HORIZON - FRACTURE-HEAVY DUSK


The Sayf al-Bahr and the Bloody Gull drift together under the red horizon.

Jeb, Sophia and Darak stand on the deck facing each other. Jeb looks tense, Sophia is calm and Darak is giddy.


DARAK
(his arms sweep in a wide gesture)
Oh, well now…
The rumors are true. You are an honorable man, Jebediah Blackwater.
(beat)
Maybe, my new friends… 
(he brushes the locket under his shirt)
There’s hope for future dealings yet.


JEB
        Don’t make me regret it, al-Mhar.


Sophia places a hand on Jeb’s arm. It calms him down and keeps him from speaking further.


SOPHIA (soft)
(she leans into Darak and whispers in his ear)
You helped me, you helped us… when you didn’t 
have to.
This makes us even.



DARAK (whisper)
Even?
(he smiles)
For now, my dear girl.
For now.


Sophia’s hand moves to the Abyssal Chronometer at his belt.
Its gears start to whine.

She removes it easily.


DARAK (whisper)
(in Sophia’s ear)
It likes you better anyway.


EXT. SAYF AL-BAHR - DECK - CONTINUOUS


Red mist fractures across the horizon.

The Spectral Sea howls as a seam rips open in the sky.

Vane’s ships tear through: the Phantom’s tide, Broken Promise, and Mercy’s Price.

The Sea Burial and Mirror of Ash follow.

On the prow of the Phantom’s Tide, Charles Vane watches Murakami bear down on the Sayf al-Bahr. The Cutlass of Surge burns bright blue.

From the Sayf, Jeb sees the trap starting to close.


JEB
Shit.


EXT. SAYF AL-BAHR - DECK - CONTINUOUS

The Sea Burial’s opening volley cuts through the mist. The shots come in a staggered rhythm, each arriving a little out of sync.

The Sayf al-Bahr starts evading, skipping seams and slips between fracture-lanes.

Sophia feels the leyline tear beneath the Sayf but she can tell something is wrong. It’s too clean. Too quiet.

Her trio of relics, the compass, the shell and the chronometer work together to hold the tear open.

Then she realizes what Murakami is trying to do. He’s measuring where the Sayf will jump.

The Mirror of Ash phases in through the mist, blacker than night, igniting ley mines around the Sayf.

Sophia rushes to open a rift in the Spectral Sea. Just big enough for a person to jump through.

Darak recognizes what she’s doing. He grins and makes a run for it. Before he leaps, Lysara’s locket flashes at his chest.


DARAK
I knew I liked you, Sophia Briggs!
(beat)
Legacy burns hotter when you steal it!


He vanishes through the rift. It snaps shut behind him but its wake doesn’t vanish. The Abyssal Chronometer ticks out of time.

Sophia realizes her mistake. Murakami knew she’d commit to the rift.
EXT. SAYF AL-BAHR / BLOODY GULL - DECKS - CONTINUOUS


The Mirror of Ash fires a pulse inversion.

The invisible net inverts. Darak’s rift pulls one way. The ley tear under the Sayf pulls another.

The Sayf is hit between both pressures. Leyfire tears through her port side but the real damage is done when the ship’s seam-skip misfires and folds back into itself.

Jeb spots the Bloody Gull fighting through the mist with the Phantom’s Tide silently closing in from her aft.

Sophia starts to open another fracture. 

As she grabs Jeb, the Compass of Ley Currents burns bright. The Shell of Memory sings a song to the Sea. The Chronometer shudders as it fights against the Mirror of Ash’s net.


SOPHIA
(strain showing on her face)
Looks like it’s my turn to save you, captain.


Jeb hesitates when he sees Sophia’s veins glow amber. But he jumps with her anyway.

The second rift opens inside the remaining wake from the first.

The Sayf takes the full inversion. Her port side blooms with leyfire. Her keel screams. Her ley energy is split in two as she phases out of existence.

CUT TO STATIC GLITCH.


EXT. BLOODY GULL - MAIN DECK - RED HORIZON - FRACTURE-STORM


Linda, Carlos and Martin scramble across the Bloody Gull’s deck. 

Linda captures Sophia and Jeb crashing to the deck from Sophia’s fracture rift.

Old Red pulls Sophia to her feet, then notices the burn crawling up her arm.

From the mist, Vane’s voice cuts through the storm.


VANE (O.S.)
Bring me the old man!


Carlos has his camera locked on the Phantom’s Tide as it turns on the Bloody Gull.

Old Red sets his dice on the deck and rolls them once.

The Tuning Core hums in his chest and the Gull answers.

Jeb and his crew scramble into fighting positions.


JEB (shouting)
(raises his cutlass into the air)
They're gonna board!


Charles Vane leaps onto the Gull’s deck, the Cutlass of Surge already burning blue in his hand. 

He heads straight for Old Red.

Lark, Linda and her crew are surrounded at middeck with Sophia and Gus.
Jeb charges and tries to get to Vane first but he’s blocked by Vane’s men.

Old Red stands alone near the wheel, his old cutlass drawn.


OLD RED
(grins and points at his dice on the deck)
The dice have already been rolled, boy.
(his lifts his arms wide)
The Sea, oh the Sea, she’ll decide the rest.


EXT. BLOODY GULL - MAIN DECK - CONTINUOUS


Vane stalks his way up the quarterdeck stairs. When he reaches the top, Old Red starts to glow. 

Leyfire dances up his arm. His old cutlass glows in front of him.

The deck beneath Old Red starts to breathe with him.

Vane prowls slowly, getting closer and grinning wider.


CHARLES VANE (mocking)
You think you can save what’s already broken?
(he laughs and spits)
I’ll take it all, old man.
Everything he loves.
(he looks at Lark and Sophia and growls)
Everyone he loves.


Vane lunges with the Cutlass of Surge.

Old Red meets him with his old and battered cutlass. When the blades clash, relic fire and relic song ignite.
Old Red looks at his friends and crewmen trying to fight their way to him. He smiles. Tears forming in his eyes.

He lifts his cutlass one more time.

Blood is already dripping from the burns covering his fingers.

The Cutlass of Surge pulls toward the Tuning Core at the heart of Old Red and Vane drives it through.

Every relic aboard the Gull falls silent.

Vane staggers back, burned by his blue flamed cutlass.

Old Red falls to his knees and smiles.


OLD RED (sings)
(drops his cutlass to the deck)
The sea takes who she will, they say.
She don’t bargain. She don’t plead.
Step too close and lose your way,
Drift into the endless sea.


EXT. BLOODY GULL - MAIN DECK - RED HORIZON - NIGHT


The Bloody Gull shudders as Old Red breathes his last breath.

GRAPHIC NOVEL INSERT — 4 PANEL INSERT

— Sophia falls to her knees.
— Lark screams.
— Jeb freezes.
— The bone dice scatter across the deck.

Charles Vane raises the Cutlass of Surge and it feeds on the Gull’s broken ley pulse.
Sophia’s grief triggers a fracture backlash. The compass, the shell and the chronometer fail to stabilize it.

The Gull’s deck begins to split. The mast rigging tears loose. The ship starts folding between worlds.

The Spectral Sea opens beneath the Bloody Gull.

The crew loses all footing as the ship drops.

Charles Vane’s laugh can be heard from the wreckage as the strom consumes the deck.

The Spectral Sea folds around them and the Bloody Gull sinks into the abyss.

CUT TO BLACK.
