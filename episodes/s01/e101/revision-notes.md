# Episode 101 Revision Notes

## Late-Pass Source Read

The `FULL VERSION POST READER NOTES` branch shows a real three-layer revision process:

1. AFF-style external reader feedback and diagnosis
2. a triage / action-plan pass separating obsolete notes from still-live notes
3. scene-by-scene Tier Six final-pass reports that compare old vs updated scene versions

Useful source buckets:

- `Notes/Reader Notes 3 column action plan`
- `SCRIPT/UPDATED SEAS OF MYSTERY EPISODE 101 - THE GIRL IN THE FOG 3/17/2026`
- `TIER SIX FINAL PASS - ACTS/SCENES`

## What The Reader Notes Actually Said

The external feedback focused on:

- plot feeling busy or scattered
- world-building clarity not always landing cleanly
- action lines reading too novelistically
- the Briggs family needing more presence and emotional grounding
- ensemble sprawl making roles and voices blur
- Jeb needing clearer agency and centrality
- occasional on-the-nose dialogue

## What The Action Plan Shows

The `Reader Notes 3 column action plan` is useful because it already sorts the feedback into:

- obsolete notes tied to the old version
- notes already handled by `THE GIRL IN THE FOG`
- still-live craft notes worth carrying forward

That triage is important. It confirms that the rewrite already addressed several large structural issues instead of merely line-polishing the old draft.

## Confirmed Revision Priorities In The Final-Pass Docs

Across the Tier Six reports, the recurring late-pass focus is:

- cut overly novelistic action lines into more shootable beats
- preserve character voice while trimming prose density
- keep myth pressure tactile instead of over-explained
- maintain ensemble clarity by simplifying handoffs and traffic
- collapse duplicate sound / glitch / image beats into one strong hit
- preserve silence and implication over explanation

## What Appears Already Resolved

The post-reader-notes material strongly suggests these reader concerns were already handled in the updated pilot:

- the old pilot architecture built around a larger Mary Celeste prologue and delayed rescue is gone
- Jeb now has a much clearer mythic role and decision-making presence
- Sophia now functions as a real engine, not just rescued background history
- doc crew vs Bloody Gull crew separation is clearer by POV, function, and behavior
- the world rules are more explicit through crossings, fractures, loops, and ship behavior
- the specific on-the-nose line the reader flagged is gone

## What Stayed Live Through The Final Pass

The scene-by-scene reports show that these stayed active craft concerns even after the larger rewrite:

- action-line density
- occasional lyric / prose flourish that wants sanding
- making crowded scenes easier to visualize on first read
- choosing one strongest image rather than layering several
- maintaining teleplay clarity without sterilizing voice

This is the main late-pass personality of `EP101`: not story reinvention, but disciplined compression.

## Representative Scene-Level Patterns

### `A1S1 — Where Truth Begins`

The final-pass report treats this scene as mostly successful on:

- ensemble clarity
- tone balance
- dialogue sharpness

But it still flags:

- dense metaphor stacks
- prose-forward action blocks
- paragraph-level pace clogs

So the pattern here is:
strong voice, light trim pressure.

### `A2S1 — The Girl in the Fog`

This report shows a very clear before/after logic:

- updated version cuts back equipment-failure redundancy
- trims duplicate metaphor buttons
- reduces purple phrasing around Lark's internal state
- keeps the relic discovery tactile and grounded

The report repeatedly favors:

- one strong failure beat per device
- one strong ship-breath image
- one clean THUMP cadence instead of overcycling it

### `A3S2 — Dragon's Maw / Okinawa Harbor`

This pass is especially useful because it shows the late-pass style at its clearest:

- keep the harbor grounded
- make the glitch motif tactile (`file doesn't save`, `non-echo`)
- cut duplicate harbor bustle indicators
- keep one strong modern sound sting
- correct minor continuity and copy errors

This scene demonstrates that the final pass was not trying to make everything prettier. It was trying to make it cleaner and easier to film in the reader's head.

## Repo-Native Takeaway

For repo purposes, the most valuable thing to preserve from this branch is:

- which reader-note concerns were already solved
- which concerns remained active into the final pass
- what kinds of trims the late pass consistently preferred

The most valuable recurring rule from this folder is:

`preserve the myth pulse, but reduce duplicate flourish`

## Revision Posture For Future Episodes

Based on this folder, the house late-pass logic for future episode revision should be:

- keep voiceprint and myth law intact
- favor image clarity over stacked flourish
- compress repeated glitch / sound / metaphor hits
- keep ensemble traffic trackable
- let silence carry meaning before dialogue explains it

## Preserve In Repo-Native Form

- reader-note triage logic
- scene-level trim philosophy
- what issues were already resolved versus still live
- any continuity-sensitive correction that affects canon meaning
