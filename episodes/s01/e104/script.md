SEAS OF MYSTERY






S1EP104 - THE KINGS WAKE



Written by:
ADAM BOYD


















FINAL V1 5/2/2006
ACT ONE

C.O.S1

INT. — Found Footage — UNKNOWN


The monitor screen crackles to life and then goes black.

Glitched pirates clash with the British navy off the coast of Nassau.

Spectral Sea cannon fire arcs in leyfire trails.
Ghost rigged ships battle each other under a blood moon.

Charles Vane boards a merchant ship with a snarl and fire in his eyes.


JEB (V.O.)
(low, whispered, secret)
Some legends never die… and some refuse to stay buried.


The monitor screen flares with static.

In a storm lit cove, Benjamin Hornigold trains a younger Blackbeard. Blades clash and reflect storm light.

Hornigold grins through broken teeth — then hands the blade over. A faint glyph is visible on the hilt.

Blackbeard doesn’t blink.


JEB (V.O.)
(builds like a rising tide)
There’s an old tale about Blackbeard’s skull.
One most folk don’t dare speak of…

Spectral map rooms glitch across different timelines — leylines surge and rupture across glowing maps.

Crews crawl over fragments of maritime relics.

Their eyes flare, burn and gloss over with madness.

The monitor goes dead.

C.O.S2

EXT. — BLOODY GULL DECK — NIGHT

Jeb hauls the wheel of the Bloody Gull with a storm raging behind him.


JEB (V.O.)
After they took his head, they say it was turned into a drinking goblet… cursed to the bone.


Cannon smoke curls in the air above the water. Ley lines travel up the ship like veins.

Old Red stands still in the crow’s nest. His mouth barely moves but his chant resonates through the ship’s rigging.
The Sea speaks through him now.


OLD RED
(electric and marrow deep)
Where’s my head… where’s my head…

The sky cracks open and the sea parts to reveal Blackbeard in the mist.

He drags his cutlass through the air and the storm rips open. Mist glows in the blades path and seals the wound it carved.

JEB (V.O.)
Wars, they never end here. THey just scatter, like bones across the sea floor.
(beat)
Every storm here be someone’s unfinished business.


In the sky, a young Blackbeard and a younger Jeb raise tankards in the Salty Siren.

The metal overlaps as they clash. Two mugs become four. Ley cracks move across Jeb’s knuckles.


JEB (V.O.)
Some want the power, some just want peace and others want to burn it all down.


Charles Vane rips through the storm overhead.
He’s already screaming.


VANE (YELL)
(snarls and screams)
I’ll tear it all down to the waterline!


The storm behind him retreats in fear.


JEB (V.O.)
(graveled and worn)
But the Kings?
(chuckle)
The Kings… well, they play a different game.


Blue Atlantean symbols blaze across the sky.

El Caleuche sails across the heart of the abyss.

Amber Phoenician glyphs race below the waves.


JEB
(growl, worn thin, tide going out)
Best we settle this.
(takes a big rip from his blunt)
Before the sea swallows us all.

CUT TO GLITCH.

A1S1

EXT. THE SPECTRAL SEA — BLOODY GULL — NIGHT


The Bloody Gull sails over Phoenician fracture grounds.
The sky above her is torn between the void and stars.

Black nothingness swallows the horizon while stars streak across the sky like arteries.

The sea below looks like stained glass and living ink.

The Gull creaks and hums below the swell of ley current.
It syncs with the Shell in Sophia’s hand Sophia herself.

She’s surrounded by other ships heading towards the jagged silhouette on the horizon.

Ancient Phoenician glyphs are eched into their hauls. Some fly no flags while some fly too many.

Crew faces flicker in ghost light and bioluminescent glow.
Eyes dart between sky and sea.

A deckhand speaks.


CREWMAN
(muttered)
I’ve been to Jades Maw a hundred times.
It’s still some creepy ass shit.


Two passengers brace against the rail near the mid-deck.

LT. ALINA VOSS watches leylines with a tactical eye

Gus angles to get a look at her.


GUS
(muttering to himself)
I’ve seen her kind before.
(he eyes her gear, her stance and her calm)
Trained. Smart. She doesn’t flinch when the fighting starts.


TOMAS REYES clutches a battered rucksack. His eyes are raw and weathered.

Sophia stands at the bow with the Shell of Memory cradled in her hands and The Compass of Ley Currents around her neck.

Glyphs crawl beneath her skin like veins.

For a heartbeat, the glyphs stutter and the shell flickers.

Her pulse starts to sync with the shell and the Bloody Gull.

Jeb grips the rail hard, old scars glowing across his knuckles.



JEB (low)
(eyes squint as he looks at the storm)
She be pulling at the tides like the moon’s fuckin’ drunk.


His lip curls into a bitter grin.

Leylines flare brighter and the Bloody Gull hums low while the Spectral Sea’s static hum rises.

The Gull, and the relics on deck start to sync.


GUS (low)
(slides closer to midship, eyes still on Voss)
It doesn’t rattle her. Not at all. She’s definitely not a civvie.


LT. VOSS (low)
(into her field recorder)
Field signature beyond prior mappings. Recording an anomaly vector.


Tomas clutches his rucksack behind her.


TOMAS
(cracked whisper)
There ain’t no safe harbor. Just doors that don’t close once you step through.


Sophia looks up at the stone wall ahead.
Her pulse, the Shell’s melody and the Bloody Gull’s hum align.

The storm parts and reveals Jade’s Maw.
CUT TO GLITCH.

A1S2

INT. BLOODY GULL — LARK’S QUARTERS — NIGHT


The storm beats the hull of the Bloody Gull.

Lark sits alone in her cabin. She looks over the Spyglass in her hands.

Her breath catches as she lifts the Echo of the Tides. Her hands are shaking with exhaustion.

The glass ripples and the sea can be seen in her eye.
The spyglass pulls at her.

She sees ships caught in time, shredded and stitched over. Sails burn.
Ghost fleets trapped in loops, crashing, splintering and reforming in mid-air.

Ley energy from the Spyglass sketch a map across her face.

The inside of the Echo of the Tides flashes.

Lark sees a Chornometer. She sees the ancient circuitry inside of it.

She lets out a shallow breath.

The timber of the Bloody Gull creaks in time with the Spyglass’s hum.

One of the ticks of the Chronometer plays backwards and Lark tightens her grip on the spyglass.

Her heat’s rhythm matches the rhythm of the Spyglass, the Gull and the Spectral sea.

LARK (low)
(in breathless awe)
What are you trying to show me?


Inside the Spyglass cascades into the storm’s howl until it turns into incoming sails.

Lark’s breath stutters.

She looks out the window, her eyes widen as ley flares burn bright in the distance.

For a moment, the storm flashes in her eyes.

CUT TO LIGHTNING FLASH.

A1S3

EXT. THE SPECTRAL SEA — FRACTURE STORM — UNKOWN


The void and the star bleed together. It’s not night and it’s not dawn.

The sea is like a black mirror until leylines tear it open.

Storm clouds coil around them and the thunder glitches.

The crew of the Bloody Gull stiffens.

Gus crosses himself while Pete spits over the side.


STUMPY PETE
(lights a cigarette but doesn’t smoke it)
Yonagumi’s bones and Jade Maw’s throat.
No man sails out the same.


Linda’s camera trembles in her hand as her monitor fills with static.

Near mid-deck, Lt. Voss adjusts her recorder and notes a reading.

Tomas mutters under his breath.

Their eyes meet.

Lark grips the rail.

The Gull groans as leylines flare.

CUT TO STATIC.

A1S4

EXT. THE SPECTRAL SEA — JADE’S MAW — NIGHT


The Sayf al-Bahr cuts through the mist.

Her hull is black, bow like a scimitar and crimson sails.

Mist parts at the prow.

Darak al-Mhar strides the deck with a swagger.


DARAK AL-MHAR
(playful smile)
Chronometers crack, fortunes fray — but the Jackal always finds a hand to play.


The Gull’s deck stiffens.

Darak steps to the prow of the Sayf.


DARAK AL-MHAR
(calling out across the water)
Hope you kept a seat warm, Jebediah Blackwater!
Council without the Jackal isn’t worth the ink!


Gus crosses himself again.


GUS (whisper)
(to himself)
Of course he’s here.
That bastard sails through hell like it’s happy hour.


STUMPY PETE
(spits over the rail in disgust)
He’s here. And he’s more alive than I’d like…


Linda’s camera shudders in her grip as she locks tight on Darak’s grin.

At mid-deck, Lt. Voss adjusts her jacket and steps closer to a visibly shaken Tomas.

More ghost banners emerge through the mist and fog.

Murakami stands at the prow of the Sea Burial.


MURAKAMI (low)
(inhales slow)
Watchers. Builders.
Atlantis. Phoenicia.
One ran away while the other was left with all of this.
(his gaze hardens, voice is calm)
Tonight, we bring them back together again.

The Bloody Gull lists slightly at the sight of Murakami and the Sea Burial.

The Crimson Lotus rises from the mist and the tide surges beneath her.

Lanterns cast crimson light across her hooded crew.

Ching Shih stands at the prow.
Wind tears at the banners and sails around her.


LARK
(walks past Lt. Voss and Tomas)
I thought she was just a story.


Pete stands beside her, his eyes locked on Ching Shih.


STUMPY PETE
(lights a cigarette but doesn’t smoke it)
She is.


Ching Shih looks across the sea and the storm quiets.

Waves bend away from the Queen Anne’s Revenge.

Phoenician leytech glows under her hull.

Blackbeard’s boots are planted wide at the hull.
There’s a flare of old memories in his eyes.



BLACKBEARD
(deep, carved from stone)
The kings are awake.
    (he looks to the Bloody Gull)
And there’s Jebediah.
(beat)
Good.


Thunder rolls beneath the sea and the Gull groans.

Linda, Carlos and Martin stand frozen.

Carlos fights to film Blackbeard’s arrival with his drone.

Leylines surge in time with the Sea’s breath as the four ships settle into the harbor.

CUT TO BLACK.
ACT TWO

A2S1

EXT. JADE’S MAW — DOCKS — NIGHT


In Jade’s Maw, emerald towers rise above silent tides.
Mist rolls low across cracked stone wharves.
Ley fissures chart maps across the cobble stone streets.

Lines fly and gangplanks thud.
Dock workers, street vendors, smugglers and sailors, all blur into the harbor chaos.

The Bloody Gull leans into her slip.

Sophia tucks the Shell of Memory into her coat. The compass of Ley Currents hangs from around her neck.

Jeb crosses the deck and tucks Lysara’s locket under his shirt. He eyes the docks with suspicion.

Lark scans her surroundings. The Echo of the Tides slung over her shoulder like a rifle. 

At the end of the gangplank, three of Blackbeard’s men wait.


JEB
(to Gus and Pete)
Stay with the Gull. Offload. Restock.
The usual.


GUS
(nods)
Aye, captain.
(looks to Lt. Voss and Tomas)
What about those two?
JEB
(sucks air in through his teeth)
Tell ‘em this be the end of the line.
(looks over Gus’s shoulder)
Make sure they both get passage back through the gate and to the mainland.


GUS
(he hesitates)
And you? You, Red and the girls.
You sure you don’t need backup?


Jeb chuckles.


JEB
(shakes his head)
Teach didn’t call us here to fight.
Not yet.


In the distance and through the mist, a bell tolls three times.


JEB
(looks at Red, Lark and Sophia)
Time to go.


The four of them approach the men at the end of the gangplank.


JEB
(to the men)
Let’s get this over with then.
Lark, Sophia and Old Red follow Jeb and the emissaries into the mist.

Gus and Pete stop Lt. Voss and Tomas halfway down the ramp.


LT. VOSS
Where are they heading?


GUS
Ma’am, you wouldn’t believe me if I told ya.


Lt. Voss laughs and reaches into her coats inner pocket.


LT. VOSS
I’m assuming this is the end of our trip.
(she hands Gus a card)
Take this, Maddox.
Former Lt. Gus Maddox, SEAL TEAM 5, correct?


GUS (curious)
(nods)
Aye. That was a long time ago.
(beat)
What’s it to you?


Voss smiles.


LT. VOSS
(chuckles)
Just know you still have brothers and sisters on the outside if you need them. All of you.
(she motions to the Gull and her crew, looks to Tomas)
I’ll take care of him and sure he gets home.
She grabs Tomas by the elbow and walks away.


LT. VOSS
(to Gus over her shoulder)
Don’t loose that fucking card, Frogman!
It just might save your ass one day.


Gus looks up and nods then he looks at her card.


LT. ALINA VOSS
U.S. NAVAL RESEARCH LABORATORY.

CUT TO STATIC.

A2S2

INT. TIDAL COUNCIL CHAMBER — NIGHT


Lanterns cast long shadows across the tavern stronghold.

The pirate council sits around a table made from a wrecked keel.

Old Red chants as he moves around the table and through the Drowned Lords seated at it.


OLD RED (low)
(his voice rolls like the tide)
Salt for blood… bone for breath… fire to bind the storm…


Jeb Blackwater stands dead center, his hands braced on the table.

He takes a brief and concerned look at Lark and Sophia.
JEB
(voice cold edged)
Fuck the storm changs, Red.
(he turns to Blackbeard in the corner)
Fuck your council.


He angrily makes his way through the rest of the council.


JEB (CONT’D)
(he turns on Murakami)
Fuck your damn Samurai codes.
(to Darak)
Fuck your greedy hands.


He pauses when he gets to Ching Shih. His attention goes to the Crown of the Abyss on her head.


JEB
And you. The Queen of the fucking Abyss. If you’re here that means you must be on the hunt for more of those.


He points at the angry crown on her head. Salt flakes and falls off of it, coral tendrils recoil as it breathes in the ley energy of the room.

Silence crosses the room.


JEB
Come ye fucking cowards.
Say it.
(he slowly looks across the room)
Say it now before I end each of you.
Blackbeard steps out of the corner. Leylines pulse under his feet.


BLACKBEARD
(calm, dangerous)
There he is.
(he looks at Ching Shih and chuckles)
There’s the cold blooded killer I remember.
Before he went and fell in love with a mermaid.


Ching Shih nods then looks at Lark and Sophia.

Lark grabs Sophia by the elbow and pulls her close.

The Echo of the Tides, the Shell of Memory and the Compass of Ley Currents glow once.

Every eye in the room looks at the two girls now.

Darak prowls the room.


DARAK AL-MHAR (sly)
(grins sharp)
I will take both of them…
(beat)
For a fair price, of course.


He catches Blackbeard’s eye.


DARAK AL-MHAR (CONT’D)
(he shrugs)
Is this not an auction, old friend?


Blackbeard’s gaze puts Darak back into his seat.
Sophia’s grip tightens on the shell and glyphs crawl under her skin.


MURAKAMI (soft)
(watches Sophia closely)
It is her.
The tales of two tides bleeding into one.


His gaze flicks to the Shell, the Compass and the Spyglass.


MURAKAMI (CONT’D)
(to the group)
He was right.
She’s the key. If we have her, we control the tides.


Ching Shih sits in silence.
The coral in her crown breathes.


CHING SHIH
(ice-cold)
No, Captain Hideyoshi.
If we claim her…
(beat)
We control the whole storm.


Sophia looks down at the Shell in her hands.


SOPHIA
(with deep wisdom)
The Shell…
JEB (sharp)
(cuts Sophia off)
Keep yer damn mouth shut, girl.


The room freezes.

An arc of ley energy flashes at Lark’s right temple. She speaks before anyone else gets a chance.


LARK
(cold in her voice)
Try and take her. I fucking dare you.


The table looks at her.


JEB
(he looks at Blackbeard)
Same to you, Lark.
Hold your fucking tongue.


Blackbeard watches the room.


BLACKBEARD
(mischievous calm)
Now, now. Let’s all calm down.
(to Jeb, Old Red and the girls)
Go, brother. Take these girls and Old Red with you.
(beat)
You will not have any trouble from us… tonight.


Jeb laughs and looks at everyone in the room.
JEB
I’m going to hold you to that… brother.
(his gaze lingers on Blackbeard)
Don’t think I haven’t noticed there’s one of ye missing.
(eyes look harder at Blackbeard)
Where’s Charles? Where’s your dog?


BLACKBEARD
(his ring flares red)
I told our brother to stay away tonight, Jebediah.
(beat)
No one here will be hurting you or yours.


JEB
(smirks)
I find that fucking hard to believe.
(he quickly turns to the girls and Old Red)
Time to go. Red, take the girls back to the Gull and get her ready to leave.


CUT TO LIGHTNING GLITCH.

A2S3

EXT. STREETS OUTSIDE STRONGHOLD — NIGHT


Ching Shih watches the group walk out into the storm form the doorway of the tavern ruins.
The Crown of the Abyss breathes softly atop her head.


CHING SHIH
(eyes follow Sophia, Lark and Old Red)
The storm always crowns the young…
But it is the old who must drown first.
Her gaze lingers on Lark’s spyglass for a moment before she walks away.

Sophia cradles the Shell of Memory as it flickers softly.
With each step, trails of leyfire ignite beneath her bare feet.

Lark walks beside her. The Echo of the Tides reflecting storm light at her side.

Old Red walks to an unheard rhythm behind them.


OLD RED
(muttering, half laughter)
Slat for blood… bone for breath… fire to bind the storm…


His eyes track the girls. His eyes are filled with storm clouds and leylight coils at his heels.

Jeb Blackwater and Edward Teach, Blackbeard, stand alone at the water's edge.

The storm rages over the ruins behind them.

They stand in silence as Jeb watches the Gull in the distance. Blackbeard’s gaze is locked onto the back of Jeb’s head.


JEB
(breaking the silence)
So what’s your play, Teach?
These relics. The girls.
(he turns to face Blackbeard)
Do I need to be looking over my shoulder for you and your dogs?


Blackbeard’s eyes are locked onto Sophia and Lark off in the distance.


BLACKBEARD
(he waits to answer)
The tide brings all things home, Jebediah.
Even blood.
(beat)
Even blood.


Jeb’s jaw flexes. He’s heard this before.


JEB
(his voice grinds rougher)
Charles. He’s still your leashed beast?
(he let’s it sit)
Where the fuck is he?


Blackbeard’s eyes flicker at the mention of Charles Vane. His hand brushes the runes carved into the hilt of his blade. His ring simmers.


BLACKBEARD
(he laughs)
Storms run wild sometimes.
(beat)
I told Charles and Hideyoshi to hold the storm tonight.
If you or yours bleed, it won’t be by my hand.


JEB
(stare sharpens like steel)
Don’t speak of bleeding when you ain’t bled yourself.
Blackbeard turns his eyes to Jeb.


BLACKBEARD
We’ve all bled it, brother.
(breathes heavy)
Some just hide the wounds better. That’s all.


Behind Jeb’s eyes is a memory of Charles Vane, Lysara and the end of the blood oath between them.

Blackbeard looks past Jeb to the Gull, Sophia and Lark.


BLACKBEARD
(nods slightly)
Tide’s coming in.


He turns and strides towards the Queen Anne’s Revenge.

Jeb watches him go, spits salt into the wind and turns back to the Bloody Gull.

FADE TO BLACK.

A2S4

The storm sits low over the docks.
The tide pulls at the piers.

The horizon is broken up by the silhouettes of ships as seagulls glitch in and out of the sky.

The harbor bustles. Lesser captains bark orders. Dockhands haul cargo while smugglers offload goods from the real world.
Bits of old shanties drift between ships.

The Crimson Lotus gleams dark scarlet in the ley lit mist.
Her crew waits at a silent attention.
Ching Shih ascends the gangplank without a word.
Chained relic fragments chime as she passes.

Her crew bows their heads in silence as she passes.

Lt. Alina Voss watches from another gangplank further down the docks.

She keeps her eyes locked on the Crimson Lotus as the ship she’s on slips into the harbor.

Murakami stands alone, oblivious to the dock commotion around him.


MURAKAMI
(tracing ley lines with his finger)
The sea counts its debts…
(beat)
And I remember when it’s time to collect.


He turns without hesitation and steps into the fog.

A3S1

EXT. THE BLOODY GULL — GANGWAY — NIGHT


Darak al-Mhar waits at the gangplank of the Bloody Gull.
He grins and flips an old coin between his fingers.
Before he pockets it, he rolls it across his knuckles.

The Gull’s crew works the deck, hauling rigging and locking down crates.

As Darak approaches, the crew's rhythm stutters.


CARLOS
(adjusts his camera)
This storm is fucking bad, man.
Gear isn’t doing shit. Cameras are fucked.


MARTIN (mutters)
This shit ain’t worth shooting anyway, Carlos.


They continue getting their camera gear sorted and try to avoid eye contact with Darak.


Darak flips the coin high into the air, catches it and then rolls it across his knuckles again.


DARAK
(grins wide and sharp)
Yes yes, my friends. This storm is counting souls tonight.
(beat)
Ye best not be last up the line.
He makes his way past the crew as he heads up the gangplank.

Across the deck, Jeb Blackwater watches him.
In his mind, he sees Lysara’s hand with a coin similar to Darak’s.


JEB
(rough)
The devil always keeps count…


He lets Darak pass.

As Darak slips by, his gaze locks on the locket strung around Jeb’s neck.


DARAK
(low and easy)
Aye. But the devil can’t count what he can’t catch.


He vanishes on the deck, blending in with the crew, equipment and shadows.

Thunder rumbles and lightning cracks.
The Gull shudders.

FADE TO BLACK.

A3S2

INT. THE GULL — MIDSHIP — NIGHT


Lark Hawtorne moves between stacks of rope, canvas and cargo.
Her fingers hover near the Echo of the Tides at her hip.
The Spyglass lens catches a flare of ley shimmer.

Darak steps from the shadows, carrying something in his hand.

The coin appears and he flips it once before it vanishes.

As he gets closer to Lark, the object in his hand ticks louder.

Suddenly he thrusts the Abyssal Chronometer out to Lark.
It ticks and buzzes as Atlantean sigils meet Phoenician glyphs.

Lark hesitates, but she reaches out and grabs it anyway.

As soon as the Chronometer touches her skin, it screams. The symbols on it ignite.

Lark stumbles and blood beads at her fingertips.
She holds back a scream of pain.


DARAK
(matter of fact)
These types of things, some will call them relics, take blood to wake, young lady.
(he looks at the blood covered chronometer and smiles)
And it looks like your blood works just fine enough.


Glyphs scatter light across her knuckles and move up her wrist.

The Spyglass lens jolts and casts a fractured map across her chest.
Then it’s gone.


DARAK
(low and sharp)
The tide always pays the clever.


Lark’s knees try to buckle as her heartbeat syncs with the ticking of the chronometer.


LARK
(through gritted teeth)
And what are you selling?
If you think it’s me, you are very much mistaken, Jackal.


DARAK
(he grins)
Tonight is is much more than silly girls and lost and forgotten relics.


The room shakes as the storm outside rages.

Lark’s eyes are filed with leyfire as she stares at Darak.


DARAK (CONT’D)
The tide is coming, child, and it’s time for me to say goodbye.

CUT TO BLACK.

A3S3

EXT. THE BLOODY GULL — DECK — NIGHT


A heartbeat rises from below the Bloody Gull.

Ley fractures arc across the horizon as the storm tightens around a phantom fleet.

Jeb stands on the Gull’s upper deck.


JEB
(voice roars)
Brace the fucking Gull!


The crew moves as one.

Gus hauls a line while Stumpy Pete scrambles to lock down crakes and kegs.


CARLOS
(jams the camera under his jacket)
Can’t film if we’re dead!


MARTIN
(securing gear)
I’m just trying to stay on my feet, man.
Let the pirates deal with the ship.


Boots hammer the deck around them. Rigging snaps and barrels roll across the deck.

Ghostlight flickers across the Gull.

Darak al-Mhar lurks in the shadows while the storm rages around them.
The old coin catches storm light as he flips it in the air.

His eyes are locked on the surge of ley energy running between all ships in the harbor.

Around him, the Gull’s crew continue to fight the tempest.
A smile crosses Darak’s face.


DARAK
(quiet laugh)
Sometimes, the storm is just a really loud auction.
(beat)
And I always win.


The old coin vanishes into his palm.
He smiles once more and fades into the shadows.

Lark emerges from below and staggers her way to the middeck rail.

Blood streaks her palm and the Spyglass at her hip flickers.

She stands next to Sophia, who is clutching the Shell of Memory against her chest.

Glyphs moves across the surface of the shell.

The Compass of Ley Currents glows against her chest.

Lark grips the Abyssal Chronometer tight.
The blood at her fingertips spiral with the glyphs under her skin.

The Echo of the Tides flares bright.
Glyphs crawl from her blood and tangle with ley threads on the ship’s deck.

Linda’s camera catches the flare of the girl’s relics before the feed flares and guts to static.

CUT TO STATIC.
ACT FOUR

A4S1

EXT. STORM WALL — NIGHT


The stormwall rips open and allows three ships to pass through.

On the right is the Broken Promise, an old and rotted Spanish Galleon. On the left is the Mercy’s Price, sailing in reverse.
In the center is Charles Vane and the Phantom’s Tide.

Vane’s eyes are black as night, his skin is cracked like he’s been struck by lightning.


JEB
(low, growl)
God damnit.


Jeb watches the three ghost ships prowl.


JEB (shouting)
Pull the fucking lines!
(he grabs the ships wheel)
It’s time to go!


His crew doesn’t hesitate. The Gull is already pulling away from the slip.

In the middle of the crew’s hustle, something begins to happen.

The Shell of Memory in Sophia’s hands pulses with leyfire and starts to whisper in a old, unknown language.
