SEAS OF MYSTERY





EPISODE 105 — Tides of Blood and Bone




Written by:
Adam Boyd













3/17/2026

Redline
COLD OPEN

INT. THE BLOODY GULL — RADIO BAY — UNKNOWN


A monitor glitches on. Static creeps in from the edges.
It flickers then freezes.

The radio room is tight. The walls slick with saltwater. Wires run up and down the walls and dials glare.

A storm rages outside and the Gull groans under the pressure.

Jeb Backwater sits at the radio station.


JEB
(low and raspy)
Ain’t no captain sails the Spectral Sea twice…
(his fingers twist dials)
But the storm don’t remember the safe captains…
(he turns to the camera and smiles)
Now does it?


The monitor screen flares and cuts to static. Then it cuts to Sophia’s hands clutching the Shell of Memory.

Then to the Echo of the Tides before stopping on Charles Vane.


JEB (V.O.)
(half snarl, half invitation)
Let me spin ye a yarn to cut through the dark…


The Bloody Gull creaks and Old Red, the Tuning Core, and his bone dice hum low through the ship.


JEB (V.O.)
(graveled and ghostly)
Picture this.
A chest o’ glitterin’ gold, buried in a cavern where the air’s think as a deathbed promise.
The first hand to clutch it, aye… finds gold… and the gallows not far behind.
(he pauses as the Gull creaks)
Accidents that ain’t accidents. Whispers clawing through the dark.
One by one, crew vanish — snatched clean off the deck by hands unseen.


The rigging groans with ley energy.


JEB
(growling)
The sea — she gives with one hand and takes with the other.
Always has.
(he pauses for the storm outside)
She always will.


The monitor frame glitches with ley energy.


JEB
(voice can split iron)
Every coin’s a blood tithe. Every relic… carries a curse waitin’ for its turn.
(his fingers mess with the dials)
Makes a man wonder…
It it the treasure that’s cursed…
(the radio goes static)
Or the hearts of them that chase it?


The monitor’s glitch syncs to the Bloody Gull’s heartbeat.


JEB (V.O.)
(his voice goes to a sharp whisper)
So think long and hard, mates…
(the radio signal flares)
What would ye give?
What would ye lose for a chance at fortune?


The radio signal clears and the Gull breathes.


JEB
(smiles into camera)
The race is on you bloody fucking bastards.



ACT ONE

EXT. GRAVEYARD OF KINGS — THE BLOODY GULL — NIGHT


The Bloody Gull is surrounded by the ribs of dead leviathans. They arc high about the ship and shimmer in ley light.

Her rigging screams and her hull groans, but she presses on.

The Echo of the Tides at Lark’s hip thrums. Scorch marks spiral from her palm to her wrist. A reminder of the Abyssal Chronometer’s bite.

The Shell of Memory in Sophia’s grip hums and the Compass of Ley Currents spins wildly against her chest.


LARK
(to Jeb, at the helm)
Captain? Where are we?


JEB (dry)
(deep chuckle)
This be the Graveyard of Kings.
(he lets the words land)
The Bones of Atlantis.
Ain’t no man sails these waters by maps.


The storm growls around the Gull. Lightning etches ancient runes into the sky.

Old Red leans at the rail, his face lit by lightning and eyes glowing bright.



OLD RED (low)
(hands on the rail like he’s talking to the ship)
Salt in the blood… breath in the bones…
Salt in the blood…
Breath in the bones!


Across the deck, Sophia grips the her shell tighter. Her breath is steady, eyes like fathoms and leyfire bright under her skin.

As Old Red chants, the Shell of Memory hums louder. The compass needle at her chest locks.

The Bloody Gull shudders in response. 


SOPHIA (low)
(voice like silk-wrapped steel)
The Sea calls us forward.
(ley arcs dance in her eyes)
It cares not if we want to follow.


A crack splits dimensions and the Phantom’s Tide cuts through the abyss, followed by her two phantom shadows.


GUS
(yells to no one and everyone)
Those fuckers move like Voldemort and his deatheaters!


STUMPY PETE
(laughs at his best friend)
You fucking nerd!
(grasps Gus’s shoulder)
You ain’t wrong though, brother.
Fuck me.
Lark’s spyglass flares and constellations ignite across her torso.


LARK
(eyes narrowed and focused)
Gus isn’t wrong, captain.
Why’s he so twisted on you?


JEB (dry)
(eyes locked on the Phantom’s Tide)
He was my brother once.
Now he’s my ghost. A phantom from my past.
(his eyes flare with memories)
He didn’t need a reason.
(beat)
But I gave him one anyway.


LARK
(she grabs the fire controls for a ley cannon)
Well, care if we give him another one?


She pulls. Cannon fire roars across the abyss.

Ghost wakes ripple from the Gull’s hull and ley light races her rigging.

CUT TO LEY FLASH.

EXT. GRAVEYARD OF KINGS — BLOODY GULL — CONTINUOUS


The crew of the Bloody Gull scrambles.

Gus hauls the lines. Pete lashes kegs and Carlos braces himself next to Jeb.

Sophia stands mid-deck, untouched by the storm.
The Shell of Memory’s hum threads Atlantean glyphs into the ley energy in her veins.

Her lips barely move, but her voice carries the weight of the sea.


SOPHIA (deep)
(the Spectral Sea speaks through her)
The tide remembers him.
(the Shell whispers against her ribs)
It remembers us all.


Jeb takes over the helm.


JEB (snarl)
Hold course!
(eyes lock on the Phantom’s Tide)
Let him fire, lads.
A good death of a wild run — either way, we carve our names in the tide.


Carlos looks back at him with terror in his eyes.

Old Red grips a line and leans over the rail.


OLD RED (sing-song)
(calls to the crew)
From cursed fog came ships of dread…


CREW (shouts)
(response)
Sails torn and gray with death!


OLD RED (sing-song)
(to the crew)
Their ghostly crew with ember eyes…


CREW (shouts)
Hunting mortal breath!


The crew readies for battle, revved up by the call and response.

Then —
BOOM.

The Phantom’s Tide fires its broadside ley-cannons.
The storm and sea start to fracture under the blasts.


The Gull heaves and howls, but she holds.


OLD RED AND CREW (sing-song)
(singing together)
Oh, row, lads row! Through the shadows and the fire!
(Sophia’s eyes flare with stormlight)
Fight for your lives, for the seas desire!
(the shell pulses with the tides)
With a ghostly fleet and a captain’s roar,
(Vane’s silhouette looms closer now)
We’ll sail to hell and back once more!


CUT TO BLACK.

EXT. GRAVEYARD OF KINGS — BLOODY GULL — CONTINUOUS


The storm and the sea fold inward around the Bloody Gull.
It rides low through ley fractures.
Lark claws her way to the prow. She stands against the wind and raises the Echo of the Tides to her eye.

The spyglass ignites and flares light down her charred arm.


LARK
(fights the pain from the chronometer burn)
There’s a ley map through the storm! I see the fractures!
(she turns to Jeb and shouts over the storm)
I see it Jeb! I see the way!


She shifts the spyglass and the path tightens.


JEB (under his breath)
Atta girl.
(he smiles and yells back)
Show us fast, Hawthorne!


The Echo of the Tide flares.
Larks fingers work fast despite the pain.

Glyphs across her skin pulse in rhythm with the spyglass and the ley paths snap into place.

The storm peels open and the Bloody Gull dives into light beyond.

Charles Vane’s roar causes Lark to turn around, but she grips the spyglass tighter.

Her eyes burn bright and the Echo of the Tides locks the rest of the path forward.



LARK (quiet)
(to herself, the Gull and the sea itself)
Thread the needle.


Lark and the Bloody Gull disappear into the fracture.

CUT TO FRACTURE STATIC.
ACT TWO

EXT. ABYSSAL TRENCH — FIELD OF WRECKS — NIGHT


The Bloody Gull emerges from the fracture jump in an abyssal trench. A boneyard of broken ships drifting like ghosts.

Whale song and ghostly laments fill the air between the Gull and the wrecks.

The crew of the Bloody Gull hushes. Their eyes are on the Phantom’s Tide prowling close behind them.

It fires a warning shot but Jeb doesn’t flinch.

Lark looks through the spyglass.

GRAPHIC NOVEL INSERT — 4 PANELS

— A skeleton's hand clutching a leatherbound book.
— Phantom sailors man spectral rigging.
— Leviathan bones turn into constellations.
— Ancient warships drifting alongside the Bloody Gull.


LARK (low)
(whispers to only herself)
Holy fuck, dad.
What the fuck did you leave me?


Sophia runs to the rail.


SOPHIA (shouts)
That’s his book!
(she looks at Lark)
There’s Elias’s book!

LARK
(runs up next to Sophia)
What book?
(she follows Sophia’s stare)
Wait. I just saw that…
(she looks at Sophia)
How… That’s… my dad’s?


Sophia nods.


JEB (commands)
(he yells from the helm)
Grapples! Fast!
Now!


Gus and Stumpy Pete don’t hesitate. Lines fly over the rail and hooks lock home.

The corpse clutching the tome crumbles to dust and the Memory of Kings floats free.

As the book glows as it crosses the rail, the shell, the spyglass doing the same.


SOPHIA
(hands the leather tome to Lark)
This is for you, Lark. I think your father left it for you to find here.


The Memory of Kings hums in Lark’s grip.

EXT. THE PHANTOM’S TIDE — CONTINUOUS

The Phantom’s Tide prowls closer at the Gull’s aft.
Charles Vane leans over the bowsprit. 
VANE (growl)
(to himself)
Fools.
(he spits)
No book will save you.


CUT TO BLACK.

EXT. THE BLOODY GULL — CONTINUOUS


The Echo of the Tides catches flickers of a shadow fleet trailing the Phantom’s Tide.
Ghost ships and dead kings glitch between wrecks.


LARK (breath ragged)
(shouts to Jeb)
They’re closing fast!


JEB
(smirks)
So are we.


Jeb throws the Gull into reverse and creates a reverse wake spiral. It looks like the Bloody Gull is being sucked into the storm.

The old wrecks become teeth. Ley surges warp across the field.

The Phantom’s Tide follows anyway.

Leviathan monarchs flicker across the wreck field.

The Gull dives into the abyss as the storm collapses around her.
EXT. ABYSSAL TRENCH — BLOODY GULL — CONTINUOUS


The Bloody Gull fights the storm’s grip.
Rigging is torn. Sails fight off lightning.
It’s like the Gull is being dragged across the tide.

Ahead is a black spire rising high in the air.
Ley energy surges from the top like a lighthouse lamp.
It sings its own version of a siren’s song.

Everything is being pulled to it.
All ships, all relics, all blood.

GRAPHIC NOVEL INSERT — 4 PANEL

— The Shell of Memory throbs wild in Sophia’s hands.
— The Echo of the Tides at Lark’s side burns. Glyphs flaring across her chest.
— The Memory of Kings, lashed to the deck, crackles with ley fire, pages flicking in the storm.
— The Compass of Ley Currents at Sophia’s belt spasms. Its needle locked to the Beacon.

Sophia’s hands glow. Glyphs crawl up her arms.
She turns toward the Spectral Beacon.

Jeb doesn’t see her. He’s leaning over the portside rail, eyes looking at something glinting below the surface.

He steadies himself to dive in.


JEB
(breathes deep)
I’m comin’ then.


In inhales.
Then he dives off the Gull.
EXT. UNDERWATER — ABYSSAL TRENCH — CONTINUOUS


Stormlight churns above Jeb as he dives.

Below him, chained to the wrecked hull of a warship is a cutlass. Its blade crawls with lightning etched script.

Jeb kicks hard.

A shadow moves to his right. He doesn’t see it.
He keeps kicking.

A leviathan eye opens in the dark. It watches him kick his way to the wreck. To the Cutlass of Surge.

Jeb reaches the blade and grabs the hilt.
Leyfire races up his arm. He grits his teeth and tightens his grip.

Then he turns his head to the left. He makes eye contact with the leviathan.

It blinks at him.

Suddenly the chains holding the cutlass crumble.

The Cutlass of Surge erupts in ley energy. The blue and white light wraps Jeb’s body.

The leviathan watches. Jeb looks at it and blinks. The leviathan blinks back.

Jeb starts to rise. His body arcs toward the storming surface. The Cutlass of Surge in his grip.

EXT. WATER SURFACE — WRECK FIELD — CONTINUOUS


Jeb erupts to the surface. He gasps for air.

The Cutlass of Surge howls in his grip. The locket around his neck glows once.

He looks to where the Bloody Gull should be, but it's gone.
He sees it being pulled closer to the Spectral Beacon.

Jeb feels Vane’s presence before he hears him.
Jeb grabs the nearest piece of wreckage and pulls himself up.


VANE (low)
(smiles, standing on the wreckage)
Didn’t feel like sharing, Blackwater.


JEB (snarl)
(turns to face Vane)
Good, ye fuckin’ bastard.
(the Cutlass of Surge flares)
Let's keep the blood between us.


Their blades clash. Steel screams and energy explodes.

GRAPHIC NOVEL INSERT — 1 PANEL
— Younger Jeb and Vane, brothers and kobun to Blackbeard, reciting their blood oath.

Every strike of the Cutlass of Surge flares white and blue leyfire.

Vane’s blade shreds coral to dust.

Jeb’s cutlass electrifies the air with every swing.

They clash across leviathan bones, slipping across the surface.

Their blades clash.
GRAPHIC NOVEL INSERT — 1 PANEL

— Lysara, laughing under a sunlit sky, hand in Jeb’s, the locket around her throat.

Jeb stumbles. His boots skid over old bones.

Vane lunges, his blade coming in fast. Jeb parries.

Their blades clash again.

GRAPHIC NOVEL PANEL — 1 PANEL

— Jeb and Vane finishing their oath to Blackbeard.
“Family first, till blood runs dry.”

They both roll, locked together. Fists fly. Blades scrape across rotten decks. Both spill blood across the wet wood.

Vane’s fist connects to Jeb’s jaw. Blood flies into the storm.

Jeb swings blind and the Cutlass of Surge finds Vane’s blade.

GRAPHIC NOVEL INSERT — 1 PANEL

— Lysara, dying under a broken mast. Vane’s blade buried in her chest. Her eyes full of sorrow.

Jeb spits blood into the wind. His lip split.


VANE
(spits)
You’re too old for this, Blackwater!


JEB
(bloody grin)
Still too fast for you.
In the distance, the Bloody Gull moans.
Jeb turns to look.

Vane swings high. Jeb gets his blade up at the last second.

GRAPHIC NOVEL INSERT — 1 PANEL

— Jeb sliding Lysara’s locket from her cold throat.
“I’ll carry you.”

The locket at his chest flares twice.

CUT TO LEY GLITCH.

EXT. ABYSS TRENCH — THE BLOODY GULL — DECK — CONTINUOUS


Sophia hides behind a group of crates. The Shell of Memory held against her chest.
Glyphs crawl her collarbones.

Lark hauls herself to the edge of the quarterdeck. The cracked lens of the spyglass casts constellations across her chest and torso.

Gus runs past Lark and slides to the portside rail.


GUS
(slams into the rail)
Brace the sails! Hold the line!


The crew scrambles. Lines are pulled. Sails rolled.

Sophia staggers to the mast. The compass at her belt spasms. It’s needle locks on the Spectral Beacon.


SOPHIA
(she looks at the compass face)
It’s… it’s calling.


Old Red steps to the prow.


OLD RED
(his eyes are on the beacon)
On a lonely rock where the fractures cry, three keepers stood ‘neath a storm-torn sky.
Their lamp burned bright with Phoenecian flame, but the sea spares none… no blood no name.
