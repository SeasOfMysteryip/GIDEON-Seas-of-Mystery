A1S0 — COLD OPEN
FOUND DOCUMENTARY FOOTAGE


The Echo of the Tides flickers.


LINDA (V.O.)
(sharp inhale, voice tight, frayed)
Stay with me.
Stay with me…


Ley energy ripples across the lens.


LARK
(out of breath, slight obsession)
Third day since crossing the fracture tide.


The Echo of the Tides shudders.


LARK (CONT’D)
(looks frazzled and crazy)
Running low on power. Lower on reason.


The Bloody Gull is now behind her.



LARK (CONT’D)
(talking faster)
I can’t turn back. I don’t know what it is…
(looks back to the Bloody Gull)
But my spyglass…
(raises the Echo of the Tides)
I think it’s going to help me find my dad.
(focuses the spyglass)
And Sophia. Old Red.
(breath)
Something happened at that chamber.


The brass of the Spyglass glows blue.


LARK
(rubs the initials with her thumb)
To these…things.


Through the Spyglass Lark sees British phantom ships.


LARK (V.O.)
They call ‘em Saltwater Phantoms.
Ghost fleets.
Darak said they don’t drift by accident.
(in a Darak al-Mhar imitation)
He said, “Not even I bargain with the Phantoms.
(beat)
And I love a good bargain more than most, Lady 
Hawthorne.”


Lark sees the Bloody Gull being devoured by British ships.

She sees Jeb with a blue cutlass held to Jeb’s throat.



CHARLES VANE
(smiles evilly)
Brother.


She sees Sophia on the Queen Anne’s Revenge.

Lark screams in pain.

The Bloody Gull glitches through the horizon.


OLD RED (O.S.)
(rhythmic, steady, like a slow tide)
Don’t go down, don’t look in…
Turn yer back or lose yer skin…


The Gull phases out of the sea once then returns.


OLD RED (O.S.)
(deeper, prophetic in weight)
The sea’s got doors no man should pass…
Step inside, ye won’t come back…


The Bloody Gull and the Sayf al-Bahr are surrounded by British ships.

ACT ONE

A1S1 — Old Memories that aren’t Your Own
EXT. THE GULL — LEY DEAD ZONE — DAY


The record player needle touches down on the vinyl.

“Well, I don’t know, but I’ve been told…”

The Bloody Gull heads towards a storm wall on the horizon.

“You never slow down, you never grow old…”

Jeb Blackwater takes a drag from a joint as he mans the wheel at the helm.


JEB (LOW)
(prophetic poetry)
Everyone wants a map that leads to treasure.
But the cost of getting there, aye…
They don’t ever think about that, do they?


He spits.

The Shell of Memory hums a melody against Sophia’s ribs.

Jeb watches blue and gold energy streaks up Sophia’s arm, forming symbols and glyphs.


JEB (UNDER BREATH)
(grips the helm tighter)
God damnit.
(beat)
What did you bring to my boat, Elias?



Sophia flinches and her hand reaches for the shell.

She sees a sword.

She blinks.

Jeb sees visions in his head.
A sword thrust through scaled skin.

Charles Vane’s sigil carved into knuckles.

Blood and splatter on deck timber.


OLD RED (WITH MUSIC)
(drifting in and out of fog)
“The sea takes who she will,” they say,
“She don’t bargain, she don’t plead.”


The Bloody Gull’s instruments go haywire.


OLD RED (CONT’D)
“Step to close and lose yer way…
Drift into the endless sea…”


Jeb’s jaw tightens.

Sophia’s shell vibrates and hums harder.

The Gull creaks.

“Last dance with Mary Jane, one more time to kill 
the pain…”

Sophia locks her eyes on the Spyglass near Lark’s hand.

Lark flips through her father’s journals.
LARK (LOW)
(stops turning on a page with the spyglass)
“See through the tide, Echo through time…”
(closes her eyes in frustration)
I don’t know what any of this means.
(beat)
Dad, what the fuck is all this?


“She grew up tall and she grew up right, with them 
Indiana boys on them Indiana nights…”

Linda’s eyes snap between Sophia and Lark.


LINDA HAN (SERIOUS)
(bordering on anger)
How can you all act like this makes any logical 
fucking sense?
(slams a battery into camera)
I’ve seen some fucked up shit, man…


She raises her handled camera and points to the storm on the horizon.


LINDA HAN (CONT’D)
(turns and laughs)
But ghost ships and…whatever the hell these…
(points to the Spyglass, Shell, Compass)
Things are…
(goes back to filming)
You’re all acting like it’s just another normal fucking day.


Jeb stares at her and he shakes his head.

“Oh my my, oh hell yes…”
OLD RED (O.S.)
(same melody as Last Dance with Mary Jane)
Don’t go down, don’t look in…
Turn yer back or loose yer skin…


The Gull groans, prow cutting through the current.

Old Red finds his way to the tip of the prow.


OLD RED
(he stands atop the mermaid carving)
The sea’s got doors no man should pass…
Step inside, ye won’t come back…

A1S2 — The Tale of Elias Hawthorne
EXT. THE GULL — ON DECK — CONTINUOUS


The Gull sails through black water while a spectral storm glitches on the horizon.

Jeb drops the needle on the record and “Son of a Preacher Man” starts to play.

Jeb lights up a blunt, inhales and then blows the smoke.


OLD RED (INVITING)
(sways to the rhythm of the music)
Gather close, and heed me well,
Night’s got tales too dark to tell.


Sophia walks barefoot across the deck.

The ley fire tattoos look familiar now.

She hums with Dusty Springfield.

“The only boy who could ever reach me, was the 
son of a preacher man…”


JEB (LOW, NARRATION)
(eyes far—off in the distance)
Aye, I heard tell o’ this map once…
(takes a big rip on his blunt, eyes glow)
…from a man who weren’t much fer telling tall 
tales.


LARK (WHISPERED)
(her head snaps up, eyes wide)
Elias…


Old Red’s dice click like bones.


OLD RED
(looks around to the gathered crew)
Night the stars when dark, me boys…


JEB
(to the night sky)
Carried compasses like priests carry crosses.
If it weren’t on a chart, he didn’t believe it.


LARK
(through Linda’s camera)
That’s what he used to say.
(her voice trembles)
My dad has told me this story before.


The Spyglass, the Compass and the Shell of Memory all glow at the same time.

Lark’s right eye starts to glow like Sophia’s arm.

Sophia’s arm glows brighter.


OLD RED
(moving along the rail)
Wind it ceased, the waves stood still…


JEB
(steps to the rail behind Old Red)
It wasn’t the ship that caused him to get lost.
(looks up to the sky)
It was the stars.


The constellations twist and change shape in the sky.


LARK (SOFT)
(through Linda’s camera)
Orion stretched his bow where he never had before…


JEB
(turns toward Lark and the crew)
North Star slid south like a trick o’ the eye.


OLD RED
(looks at the Shell in Sophia’s hand)
No moon to mark the endless void…



JEB
(watches the sky change shapes)
Heavens redrew themselves like an invisible hand 
had hold of the quill.


The three relics glow brighter and start to hum.

The sky plays the memory of Jeb’s story.


LARK
(watching the sky turn into a map)
A new constellation…
(she traces the stars with her finger)
Seven stars.
An arrow pointing where no man dared to mark.


OLD RED
(rolls his bone dice across the deck)
Mast did groan, the hull did moan,
A wretched sound like splittin’ bone.


The arrow in the sky flares and its tip pulses.


JEB
And at the tip?
A star that pulsed…not like a twinkle, not like 
a shimmer of heat…


LARK (EMOTIONAL)
(finishes Jeb’s sentence)
…but like a heartbeat.


Sophia’s Shell glows.
JEB
Three days passed. Always in threes.
Sea turned a color they couldn’t name.


LARK
(eyes to the sky)
That color — my dad drew it.
He saw it.


JEB
(watches the map in the sky move)
Birds gone.
Fish gone.
Constellations never leavin’ the sky.


OLD RED
Some were gone, and none knew who.


The dice stop.

JEB
(steps into the light cast by the map above)
We followed the stars…


LARK
…but they were not stars at all.


JEB
They were eyes.


The crew sits in silence.

Old Red watches Sophia hum the ending of the song.

Jeb lifts the needle of the record and pulls from his blunt.

JEB
(exhales smoke)
Now, I ain’t sayin’ it’s true, mind ye…
(takes in the Spectral Sea)
But if ye ever find yerself starin’ up at the 
sky, and the stars start moving in ways they 
ought not to…


He pauses to take one more drag before flicking the butt into the ocean below.


JEB
…best ye ask yeself one question, mates.
(looks directly at Lark)
Are ye followin’ a map…
(last bit of smoke exhaled)
…or is something leadin’ you?


The Compass and Shell ignite as Sophia’s arm and Lark’s eye do the same.

A1S3 — Truths and Consequences
EXT. THE GULL — DECK — CONTINUOUS


The Shell of Memory convulses in Sophia’s hands.

She clenches down on it but it doesn’t stop.

Her eyes slam shut.

In her mind she sees a man with a little girl on his shoulders.
SOPHIA (RAGGED)
No…
(voice catches in her throat)
That’s not mine…

Lark screams and she almost drops the spyglass.

Visions flash in her mind.
The Bloody Gull engulfed in flame then Sophia dragged under black tides.
She sees herself, alone, holding the Spyglass as the stars fall dead.


LARK (DESPERATE)
(tears spilling)
This…this isn’t mine—
Sophia! No!
(she gasps at a hand that isn’t there)
No!


Symbols burn across her skin.

Gus, Pete and Linda stop what they are doing and watch the two girls.

Sophia’s tattoos ignite.


LARK (RAGGED)
(tears spilling)
Make it stop!


Sophia collapses.

Lark drops to her knees beside her.
Old Red stands over both of them.


OLD RED (SHAMANIC)
(bending down behind the girls)
The storm’s got teeth, she does…
(places his hands on their shoulders)
But she hums sweet, too.
Just gotta know when to lean in extra close, see?


The Shell of Memory starts to calm down in Sophia’s lap.

The Echo of the Tides vibrates softly in Lark’s shaking hands.


Ley lines streak the Bloody Gull’s rigging.


OLD RED (SOFT)
(to the sea)
There we are, girl…
Show us what’s in yer bones.


The Shell and the Spyglass flare together.

Storm clouds part under Old Red’s words.

Leyfire threads the sky.

The star map steadies.

⟁⟁  Twin Pillars Gate of Realms
⧖    Chrono Seal Time Locked
⟁⟡  Threaded Glyph Ley Navigation

The spectral storm holds off as the map moves and flares one more time.

A2S1 — Pirates of Nassau
EXT. THE GULL — NIGHT


The Gull sails under a dark sky.


STUMPY PETE
(leaning on some crates, lights a cigarette)
Storm’s passed, Jeb. But Nassau’s still a week 
out.


GUS
(coiling rope)
A week, and a thousand ghosts behind us.


JEB
(says with a chuckle)
And many more ahead.
(points to the load Pete is leaning on)
Load’s intact. Let’s not tempt the Sea’s memory.

Ghost ships flicker at the front of the storm.


STUMPY PETE
(wipes layer of salt off a crate)

GUS
(eyes the ghost ships with appreciation)
Spectral fleet. Third this month.

STUMPY PETE
(smiles and shakes his head)
Aye. Never gets old though.

Lark’s grip tightens on her spyglass as she lifts it up to her eye.


OLD RED (PROPHETIC)
(rolls his dice in his hand)
Oh, the morning broke with cannon smoke,
The British fleet was near…


Sophia flinches as the Shell of Memory starts to hum at her ribcage.

Through the Spyglass Lark sees a dead fleet.


OLD RED (CONT’D)
Five great ships with guns and grit,
But we showed no bloody fear!


The Gull’s veins glow ley—blue.

The Echo of the Tide is streaked with blue.

The Shell of Memory glows a blue and green burst of ley energy.

The Compass of the Drowned flares red at Sophia’s side.


JEB (DARK)
(ashes the ember on his blunt)
Nassau burned the map once.
The Spyglass shows the shadows become a fleet.


JEB (CONT’D)
Thought the sea’d forget.


The horizon glitches images of old sea battles.


GUS

Cap?


JEB

(he smiles at the memory)
Grog—soaked and gun—mad.
Calico Jack Rackham. Anne Bonny. Mary Read.
Charles Vane ridin’ hell behind us.

JEB (CONT’D)
Divide and conquer, I told ‘em.
Split their fleet and make a run for Nassau.
(hits his blunt)
And it fucking worked, lads!
(watches the memory glitch into the storm)
We led those Redcoat fucks on a merry chase 
through the squall of their lives!


The deck boards of the Bloody Gull swell.



JEB (CONT’D)
But the sea wasn’t just a battlefield…
(ash falls from his blunt)
It was our ally.


Old Red meets Jeb’s stare.

The storm cracks open.

OLD RED & JEB
(sing the rest of the song together)
So raise the black and trim the sail,
We’ll send the Redcoats straight to hell!
Through storms and fire, we never tire,
We’ll die, but we’ll live free…


Old Red’s eyes glow blue and gold.

Vernon.

(tightens grip on rigging)

Old Red’s dice still, mid rattle.

(watches ley energy crawl up Sophia’s collarbone)
Christ…


SOPHIA
(controlling the ley energy and the storm)
We are her fury returned.

Linda catches the moment the Gull punches through the fracture wall.
