ACT ONE

EXT. WRECKFIELD — BLOODY GULL — UNKNOWN


The Bloody Gull sails away from the Spectral Beacon. She’s scared and her riggings are tangled.

She’s surrounded by shattered hulls like gravestones.
Beneath the water, leviathan ribs form a reef of bones.

At the helm, Old Red bends low over his dice.


OLD RED (low song)
(through cracked lips)
Oh where have ye gone, beyond the tide’s pull?
Lost to the deep, where the dead men mull.
(he rolls the dice once)
No graves, no stone, just the sea's call, and the lighthouse stands with no men at all.


Out beyond the rail, an armada of broken vessels sail in with dawn.

The Flying Dutchman, the Lady Lovibond, the Octavious, the Jenny and the Ourang Medan.
They move in formation, like a parade of lost ships.

Linda’s camera fights her at the rail. The image cuts in and out and then it holds.

Across the deck, Gus, Carlos, and Martin stand rooted in place. Silent. Exhausted.

Old Red watches the ships drift past from behind the wheel.



OLD RED (whisper)
(he bows his head to the dead armada)
No tides forget.
No ship ever outruns her debts.


The Bloody Gull creaks, low and deep.

INT. MURAKAMI’S SHIP — LEY HOLD — UNKNOWN TIME


Sophia Briggs hangs from rusted iron chains. They whisper, ley energy flowing through them.

Sweat runs down Sophia’s back. Her shoulders are slack but her eyes are locked on Murakami Hideyoshi.

He stands across the cell, his eyes never leaving her.

He stands above the Abyssal Chronometer, a hand resting on its dial.

The ticking stutters beneath his fingers.


MURAKAMI (sinister)
(his voice is sharp and cold)
How does it feel to have your life force pulled from you, one beat of your heart at a time?


The chronometer needle spins counterclockwise, each tick pulls at Sophia like a wire.

Her eyes flicker.

The ley energy beneath her skin shifts.



MURAKAMI
(raises his eyebrow in respect)
Oh, you are a fighter, Sophia Briggs.
Indeed.
I’ll give you that.
(he steps forward)
Your friends will come for you, I have no doubt.
Dragging chains. Screaming names.
But in the end, I will cut each one loose.


Sophia’s jaw tightens. Her eyes meet his.

Her eyes glow with ley fire.


MURAKAMI
Let them come. Let them drown.
History loves to repeat itself.
(he leans in close)
But I’m going to rewrite it.


The chronometer needle shivers.
Sophia doesn’t flinch. She still hangs, unbroken.

She looks down at the Abyssal Chronometer as it spasms and slows.

It speeds up and then it slows again.
The chains in the room rattle.

Sophia looks up and her eyes look like the Spectral Sea.
The ley light beneath her skin moves and a single strand of hair lifts on its own.

The chronometer stalls.



MURAKAMI
(he smiles)
There it is.
(he steps forward)
Just enough to burn a path right to us.


Chains pulse with ley current.
Sophia takes a deep breath and stares into Murakami’s eyes.


MURAKAMI (loathing)
(he mocks her)
Stronger than you should be. Yes.
But I still don’t see what makes you special.


The Abyssal Chronometer hums and starts to glow amber gold.
A bead of sweat rolls down Sophia’s cheek and leaves a trail the same color as the chronometer’s glow.

Sophia breaths more steady and even.
The chains in the room stop vibrating.
Sophia closes her eyes.


MURAKAMI
(speaks to a shadow outside the door)
Perfect.
I don’t need her to swim.
She just needs to move the water.


In the shadows, Darak al-Mhar watches. He doesn’t blink.
He watches Sophia’s skin glow with both blue and amber ley light.

He sees Sophia notice it too.



DARAK
(he chuckles)
Funny thing about baiting a sea beast…
(he smiles at Sophia)
Sometimes more than the hook gets eaten.


CUT TO BLACK.

INT. THE BLOODY GULL — NAVIGATION ROOM


Cracked maps cover the chart table. Old. Frayed. Salt crusted.

The Gull groans through her battle scars.

Lark hunches over the Memory of Kings. Its pages twitch and try to turn on their own. But she flips them faster.

Her burned hand traces the pages.
She doesn’t notice the Echo of the Tides flares once in its cradle beside her.

The page she is turning rewrites itself. She doesn’t see her dad’s handwriting.

“The Echo of the Tides…”

Her pupils shimmer.


LARK (quiet)
(to the room)
It’s totally normal for my dad to talk to me through a book that is both ancient AND from the future. Right?


The exhausted crew of the Bloody Gull moves through the room with no purpose.
Old Red is swaying in a corner, wide eyed and whispering to himself. He bends low and lets his bone dice roll.

He starts to sing a low song.


OLD RED (low)
Sailors claim on moonless tides, where the scaffold shadows stand…
A wraith will sway in phantom ropes, by the dead man’s marrow hand.


Linda, crouched next to Gus, kisses him gently on his split lips.
She goes back to wrapping his bleeding arm.
Her blood smeared camera lays next to her.

Carlos sits down on the ground and turns the camera on.


CARLOS
(turns the camera to Linda)
How you holding up, boss?


LINDA
(takes a breath)
Better than most.
(looks into the lens)
Not my first warzone.


She turns back to Gus and wipes some blood from his face.



OLD RED (O.S.)
(song melody)
Listen close as our anger swells, when the tides begin to roar…
Pete’s voice will howl upon the breeze, as our anger strikes once more. 


Carlos shifts the camera’s focus to Gus.


CARLOS
Gus?
What the fuck is happening?


GUS
(grits his teeth)
Brother, I’ve seen a lot of shit.
But this?
This is a serious shit in the tides.
Even for this fucked up place.


Old Red picks the dice up and lets them roll again.


OLD RED (singing)
So raise yer mugs to the ghosts of the gallows, and all our brothers gone.
They sail with us through every squall, their rebel blood strong.
For every rope and every noose, their fight will always be —
A pirates cry, a rebel’s song, “Ye’ll never shackle the sea!


The bone dice stop at the foot of the door.
Jeb’s hand hits the door frame and he leans heavy on the wall.

He steps forward with a grimace of pain, picks up the dice and hands them back to Old Red.

He stands at the table and sees Lark’s hand. Her fingers leaving ley light trails across the pages of the Memory of Kings.


JEB
(to Lark)
Your dad chased truths no one else wanted.
And he found one, I think.
(beat)
Didn’t mean it was good.
(he studies Lark, then the book)
He worshipped that thing like it bled truth.
(beat)
Let’s see if it’s still got a few secrets left.


Larks looks up at him and slams the book shut.


LARK
Well, he isn’t helping.
(she opens the book again)
It’s either etching new symbols or staying dead quiet.


She lets the silence hang.
She turns a page and new symbols scrawl.

Words in her father’s handwriting start to appear.

“To look beyond time is to forget how to see.”

She flips the page again. Harder.
INT. MURAKAMI’S SHIP — LEY HOLD — CONTINUOUS


Sophia is led through the halls of the Sea Burial.
Her wrists are in ley-lined shackles. Sweat stains her back.

She stares past Murakami in front of her.
Something glitches in her gaze. A horizon only she can see.


SOPHIA
(strong and commanding)
My friends. They WILL come for me.
(her chains try to tighten)
And it’s not going to end the way you think it will.


Murakami Hideyoshi stops.
His head turns slightly.

The Abyssal Chronometer in his hand ticks loud.

Outside, the sea calms.

Below the surface, ley currents shimmer. Each pulse is like a heartbeat. But the rhythm is off.

Then it sings a hymn it should have forgotten.

BLACKBEARD
Ah, Old Grog.
(deep rumble of a laugh)
That fool Vernon is still fighting for a king the world has long since forgotten.
(his ring flares)
This should be fun.


The Queen Anne’s Revenge’s wheel twitches and starts to turn on its own.

The four fleets prepare for battle. Somewhere below the tide, the Spectral Sea prepares as well.

Lark Hawthorne sits in silence. She stares at the maps laid out on the table in front of her.

Her burnt hand twitches once, then stills.

The Echo of the Tides is silent, but it angles itself towards her.

The Memory of Kings lays open on the table and the Shell of Memory pulses once.

Old Red sways in from the shadows. His mouth is moving but his voice is low. 


OLD RED
(a bit off, almost reverent)
Some ships pick their captains.
(he smiles at Lark)
Some storms do too…


Lark puts a hand on the spyglass and it flares.

Through the spyglass she sees Sophia. Her spine long, her limbs slack. Her eyes still.

Ley light bleeds beneath her skin.

Sophia looks through the lens, right at Lark.

Lark tries to back away but the Gull lurches first.

The chart room bends. Walls stretch, light smears and time flinches.

Lark slams into the table and has the wind knocked out of her.


LARK
(gasps for air)
Soph-

The Gull rips out of the seam, her hull steams and her sails are shredded.

Behind her is burned dimensional residue, still smoking.

Lark stumbles onto the deck, Gus Maddox almost runs her over as he sprints to the port side rail.

SAILS!
(he turns to the rest of the crew)

JEB!

Vernon’s three ships are moving to trap the pirate ships in a triangle.

The HMS Valor’s Mercy covers the HMS Thunderwake with a highband leyshield.

JEB (shouts)
He’s gonna try and trap us all!
(he’s already turning wheel)
Hold on!

The Bloody Gull reappears right in front of Charles Vane and his phantom fleet.

Martin is at the rail, camera zoomed in close on the spiral speeding towards them.


MARTIN (O.S.)
(yells at anyone who will listen)
Gus! Hey guys?

The three ships whipping in a wild spiral, the crews laughing and screaming.


MARTIN (scream)
GUS!
(he turns to Gus)

(to the cannon crews)

(flips Darak off)

EXT. ABYSS — THE BLOODY GULL — UNKNOWN


The Bloody Gull floats in a space that has water in it.

She doesn’t drift but her hull creaks anyway.

Half of the crew looks down to the water like shadows below the keel. The other half looks up wondering where half the sky went.

From the shadows rises something massive. It defies gravity.

The megalodon teeth the size of towers. Its body curves as it rises in silence.

Below it tentacles rise up to grab it but they don’t.

The kraken rises slowly. Tentacles entwined and searching.

Its eyes are white and pupil-less. It is blind.
As both beasts rise, they twist and circle each other.

Old Red stands still under the main mast.

His eyes are cloudy. His mouth is half open.


OLD RED (low)
(chanting)
From fractures deep, a shadow grew,
The Deep Maw Kraken, its vengeance due…

(chanting louder)

(finish the chorus together)

CREW (repeat)
The Kraken’s lament!

OLD RED (soft)
(he looks at the kraken)

LARK (hoarse)
(she wipes blood from her lip)

LARK
(spyglass lifted to her eye again)
Come on Sophia. Where are you?

ELIAS (V.O.)
(from the Memory of Kings in her bag)
The way forward is between the tides.

I’m gonna haunt you forever, brother.

JEB

Boarders!

GUS
You want the Gull?

Hold the fucking line, ye dogs!

EXT. BATTLEFIELD — CRIMSON LOTUS — CONTINUOUS


A ship emerges from the fog. It creates no ripple. It leaves no wake. It’s silent and black winged.

Ching Shih stands at the prow of the Crimson Lotus. Her arms are folded. The Crown of the Abyss sits atop her head.

Her crew lines the deck behind her. They do not move.

Ching Shih stares at the Bloody Gull.
She watches the Gull fire ley cannons at the British fleet.

And then she sees Lark Hawthorne with the Echo of the Tides.


CHING SHIH
(she nods curiously)
She is the daughter of Elias Hawthorne.
She must be. The Echo of the Tides confirms it.
(beat)
Interesting.


EXT. BATTLEFIELD — HMS RELENTLESS — CONTINUOUS


The HMS Relentless’ path to the Bloody Gull is now blocked.


VERNON (stuttering)
…the abyss… has come for me.
(his face goes from awe, wonder to terror)
I thought it would be thunder and glory.
Not her.

Vernon watches Ching Shih raise her left hand.


CHING SHIH
Now.

Cannons blast leyfire at Vernon’s fleet. The Crown of the Abyss glows atop Ching Shih’s head.

INT. THE BLOODY GULL — CHART ROOM — NIGHT


The Shell of Memory sits on the chart table. It glows a faint violet and hums a melody that sounds like whale song.

The Memory of Kings lies open next to it. Symbols move across the pages.

The Echo of the Tides rests in its cradle.


ELIAS (V.O.)
(from the Memory of Kings)
Lark. I could never force you to do anything you didn’t want to.
And I won’t start now.
But this isn’t a choice. It’s who you are.


Lark stands over the spyglass.


LARK
(she looks to the Memory of Kings)
Dad? I don’t even know what I’m doing here.
Your old spyglass? It’s more than a spyglass but I have no idea what to do with it.


The Echo of the Tides starts to glow.
The Memory of Kings turns another page. It stops on a page with an image of the spyglass.
LARK
This has been here the whole time?


ELIAS (V.O.)
It’s been written for centuries.
It’s part of our family’s story. Our legacy.
It’s your blood rite, Birdie.


Lark breathes deep and tries to steady her hand.

Old Red quietly walks through the doorway.
One eye gleams. His feet are bare.


OLD RED (quiet)
She didn’t find the seam.
The seam found her.
(he circles the table)
Maps ain’t always made of ink, ye see.
Sometimes, they are weaved into our blood.


The relics on the table react.
The shell’s song is louder. The book turns another page. The spyglass flares once.

Lark tries to speak but she can’t.

She looks at Old Red.


OLD RED
(he smiles)
Pick it up, girl. It’s yours.


Lark lifts the Echo of the Tides to her eye. Leyfire spreads to her temple.
Through the spyglass she sees Sophia. And Sophia looks back at her.


SOPHIA (whispers)
I can feel you Lark. Come find me.


EXT. SPECTRAL SEA — THE BLOODY GULL — NIGHT


Gus stands on the deck. Bloody and beaten. But he stands.

Linda stands beside him, her camera dead in one hand, Gus’s hand in the other.

Lark and Old Red step onto the deck.
Old Red rolls his bone dice in his hand.
Lark carries the Echo of the Tides and the Memory of Kings.


LARK (excited)
Guys! I think I fig-

OLD RED (whispers)
Four old knives in a velvet sheath, watching close but drawin’ no teeth.

Not here to cut — just to smell the bait…
(he rolls the dice across the deck)

What the fuck?

These two don’t get dirty unless they have to.

LARK
(eyes looking back and Ching Shih)
Jeb? Why is she looking at me?

JEB
I reckon it be the spyglass your old man left ye.

Now ain’t the time, girl.
