SEAS OF MYSTERY



EPISODE 107 — THE FALL OF NASSAU








WRITTEN BY:
ADAM BOYD











3/20/2026
ACT ONE

EXT. BLOODY GULL — MAIN DECK — PRE-DAWN


The Bloody Gull sails through thick fog. Lanterns on the deck flicker.

Hollow Bastion, Cartagena’s mirror harbor fades away as she makes her way to Saltcrow Haven. Nassau.

The deck is still and quiet. Old Red walks barefoot across the damp planks.

He wears the coat he wore 300 years ago, sleeves still torn, salt crusted and stiff.

His right hand is in his coat pocket, rolling his bone dice out of site.


OLD RED (low)
(he stops at the wheel and whispers to it)
Aye, girl… yer ribs still hum.
The tide might be quiet, but we ain’t done.


The Bloody Gull groans low in response.

Old Red crouches low and places his hands on a rusted nail at the base of the helm.


OLD RED
Each nail’s a name I owed a tune…
Each creak a ghost I once knew.


The rigging shifts above him.
The sails don’t catch the breeze. They breathe the air.
OLD RED
(he adjusts a lantern)
They call you driftwood. A haunted hull.
But I know you better, Bloody Gull.


He heads aft. His footfalls are soft and gentle.
He stops middeck and rolls the dice in his palm.


OLD RED
You breathe like thunder. Sleep like bone.
I’ve tuned you close…
But we’re not alone.
Hold it fast. We ain’t done with war.


The wood tenses beneath his hand and the deck hums.
Then the timbers relax.

The fog on the deck splits just enough for Old Red to step through it.

EXT. BLOODY GULL — STARBOARD RAIL — CONTINUOUS


The Bloody Gull creaks. A low fog covers the sea below.

The ember of Jeb’s blunt flares in the fog. For a moment, his shadow looms across the deck.

He leans into the rail. He looks like a statue.


JEB (low)
She was just sittin’ there. Cross-legged.
Her hands folded like she was praying.
The deck behind her was painted red.
(his jaw clenches)
The Celeste’d been gone ten days.
No drift scars. No claw marks.
Just her.
Waitin’, like the sea had left her there on purpose.


A seagull lands beside him. It doesn’t squawk. It just watches him.
Jeb pays it no attention.


JEB
At first, Red called her flotsam.
But flotsam don’t hold a compass like it’s beatin’ with your own heart.
(scratches a scar under his jaw)
She didn’t speak. Not for two days.
Slept with one eye half-cracked.
Clutched that compass like it was a lifevest.


His fingers tap the rail.


JEB
We never asked her what happened.
Some stories… rot worse when aired.
(sad chuckle)
Red said she was cursed.
I said she’s just a kid.
Truth is somewhere in the middle.


The Gull shifts under him.


JEB
She finally said her name was Sophia.
Wasn’t until later we learned the name of the ship.
She’d been in that damn loop for ten years by the Spectral Sea’s time.
Over a hundred and fifty in the real world.
(he wipes a tear from his eye)
She didn’t ask to belong. She didn’t need to.
She just stayed.


Off in the distance, Saltcrow Haven’s silhouette starts to emerge. Its spectral glow casting shadows in the fog.


JEB
She’s ours. She’s mine.
(he pushes off the rail and the gull flies away)
And I ain’t laying anchor until she’s back on this ship.


He walks towards the bow and doesn’t look back.

CUT TO BLACK.

EXT. THE BLOODY GULL — POOP DECK — EARLY SUNRISE


The sun is creeping its way over the horizon.

It silhouettes Lark Hawthorne on the poop deck.
One of her boots is locked under a crossbeam, her hood is up and her hair is tangled underneath.

The Echo of the Tides rests at her hip.
She looks at it before removing it and holding it in both hands.

She opens it with a click that cuts the quiet.

It doesn’t hum. It doesn’t glow.


LARK
(sarcastic smile)
Ok, “Old Eye.”
(she raises the spyglass to her eye)
Let’s not fuck up this time, cool?


At first all she sees is black.

And then the visions flicker.

GRAPHIC NOVEL INSERT — 4 PANEL

— A red flag blackened by smoke and backlit by flames.
— Ben Hornigold, the pirate lord. Hollow, silent and skeletal.
— Woodes Rogers walking down the gangplank of a Crown ship like death in leather boots.
— Fort Nassau itself. Smoke rising.

She hears a girl scream. And then nothing.

Lark dials the notched ring on the lens.
It pinches her thumb.


LARK (mutters)
No more fucking ghosts!
Show me, “now.”


Nothing but black.

She anchors herself one more time. She takes a deep breath and furrows her brow.

Through the lens she sees inside Fort Nassau.

She sees a chained figure with bloody knees. Motionless.

It’s Sophia Briggs. Lark can’t see her face, but she knows it’s her.
The image quivers and tries to go away.


LARK
(she snickers at the spyglass)
There you are.


Lark takes a sharp breath.

She bends down and lets her fingers touch the Memory of Kings.

The Echo of the Tides flickers one more time.

Through the spyglass she sees the pirate republic banner. It’s split and ripped.

She lowers the spyglass but her eyes don’t leave the horizon.

CUT TO BLACK.

INT. FORT NASSAU — PRISON CELL — PRE DAWN


An old fort looms over the bay at Saltcrow Haven. It’s walls are outlined in green and blue ley energy.

The cells in the brig curve inward.
There are no corners.
Phoenician alloy chains spiral above, glowing amber.

Sophia Briggs stands inside one of the stone cells. Her wrists are bound and there’s blood caked to her neck and collarbone.

Her right index finger flicks. The Abyssal Chronometer behind her ticks in time with her finger.

Murakami Hideyoshi appears from the shadows.
He doesn’t look at Sophia. He admires the ley enforced prison cell.

He walks behind Sophia.


MURAKAMI
(adjusting the chronometer)
Your heartbeat is no longer yours, Sophia.
It’s just a ley pattern now.
A map etched by old gods and broken promises.


SOPHIA (calm)
My heart and my blood, Captain Hideyoshi, belong to the sea.


Nothing in the cell moves. The chronometer’s ticking misses beats.

Murakami brushes the cell’s central control node. The chains tighten in silence. They constrict.


MURAKAMI
You still don’t get it, girl.
You are the sea. Child of two bloodlines.
The Spectral Sea made human.


Sophia closes her eyes.
Her veins ripple. Just a little bit of light under her skin.


SOPHIA
If that’s true…
(she stares straight into his eyes)
You’ve already lost.
The Compass of Ley Currents glows once.
Her right index finger flicks.
The Abyssal Chronometer ticks.

Murakami doesn't look away.
ACT TWO

EXT. SPECTRAL SEA — OFF SALTCROW HAVEN — DAY


Saltcrow Haven is busy. Ships come and go between the Caribbean and the Spectral Sea.

Calico Jack Rackham captains the William past a modern day pleasure yacht with curious onlookers aboard.

Admiral Vernon’s fleet looms on the horizon.
The HMS Relentless is flanked by the HMS Thunderwake and the HMS Valor’s Mercy.

Even battered and bruised, they sail in tight formation.

They cut through thick fog with full sails and cannons loaded.

EXT. THE BLOODY GULL — PROW — CONTINUOUS


Jebediah Blackwater stands at the prow. Unmoving.
An unlit blunt hangs from his lips.

His eyes are locked on the horizon. On Vernon.
He watches Vernon’s ships move to take a blockade position below the fort.


JEB
Some things never change.
Got his ass handed to him but here he is.
Back for another round.


He gives Calico Jack a nod as they pass each other. Rackham throws a look towards Vernon’s fleet.

Jeb scans the rest of the bay.
The Queen Anne’s Revenge cuts through the fog.
Black, still and silent.


JEB
(spits)
I’m starting to get really tired of this shit.
(lights his blunt)
Fuckin’ hell.


He turns away from the prow and heads aft.

EXT. THE BLOODY GULL — FORE — CONTINUOUS


The deck of the Bloody Gull is alive. Deckhands secure crates, haul lines and repair damage taken in the last fight.

They’re nervous. They avoid making eye contact with Jeb as he moves through them.

They sky above the Gull goes from day to night. The light folds in on itself and turns into digital auroras across the black.

Deck rivets twitch with ley energy. Timbers flex. The Bloody Gull groans.


LINDA
(standing on the deck)
Why does the ship feel twitchy?


No one moves.
Then a rope snaps over head.

Everyone looks up except Old Red.

EXT. THE BLOODY GULL — MAIN DECK — CONTINUOUS


Old Red stands below the main mast. He raises both hands to the sky.

He rolls his bone dice in his hand, creating a rhythm for his chant.


OLD RED (whispering)
(his roll back with a shimmer of leyfire)
Bound to the sea, forever free…
The pull of the tides beats in me.


He rolls his bone dice across the soaked deck.

They stop at Jeb’s feet.

He looks down at them for a moment and then keeps moving.

Old Red kneels and puts both his palms on the deck.


OLD RED
(his eyes glow and look at the sky)
She ain’t just wood and iron, boys.
She’s bones and blood too.
(his hands brush the Gull’s deck)
She remembers all those who bled for her too.


EXT. — THE BLOODY GULL — HELM — CONTINUOUS


The Bloody Gull cuts through the breakers and makes her way into the bay. She rises and falls with the waves.

Jeb places a hand on the helm and it hums beneath his touch.
JEB (soft)
(to the ship)
Hang in there girl. We ain’t out of this yet.


The Gull bites leylines.
Her whole hull shudders.

The mermaid figurehead glows red.
Lightning veins through the prow and lanterns flare.

The Shell of Memory, the Echo of the Tides and the Memory of Kings hum with the ship.

The deck boards shift like the Bloody Gull is taking a deep breath.

EXT. THE QUEEN ANNE’S REVENGE — DECK — CONTINUOUS


On the far side of the bay The Queen Anne’s Revenge halts.
Fog slowly wraps around her. 

Blackbeard stands at the maindeck rail. Arms folded in front of him. His officers lined up behind him, hands clasped behind their backs.


BLACKBEARD (O.S.)
(delighted)
There she is.
Took you long enough, Jeb.


He turns and walks through the space between the rows of men behind him. They turn to face him as he passes.

The Queen Anne’s Revenge disappears into the fog.


INT. THE BLOODY GULL — CHART ROOM — DAY


Behind the wheel, behind Jeb is the Bloody Gull’s chartroom. He turns and looks through the open door.

Lark is inside, pacing in front of the table.

The Echo of the Tides casts fractured light.
On the table, the Memory of Kings rests with pages drifting like the book is breathing.

Ink shifts with each page flick. Gold filigree and black ink.

The book settles on a page and glyphs crawl onto the parchment.

𐤋 (guidance) 
𐤉𐤔𐤂 (dark-thread)
𐤇𐤉𐤕 (called, not found)


JEB
(walking into the room)
Will any of this gonna help us get past Vernon’s blockade?
And what about Blackbeard?
(he looks out the door)
He’s out there, just waiting for us to make a move.


Lark steps forward, the spyglass and the book begin to hum.

From water pooled on the floor, a thread rises.
Silver energy suspended in air.


JEB (rasp)
(eyes on the ley-thread)
What in the seven hells is that?

LARK (unsure)
(she watches the thread)
I think it’s a fracture stitch.
The book, my dad, said it’s an old “hacker trail?”
Tuned by blood and not mapped.


The Echo of the Tides hums louder.
The Memory of Kings turns a page.


JEB (cautious)
(looks at Lark)
And where exactly does it run?


LARK
(points to the Memory of Kings)
The mouth of the cove. Right below the fort.
Through Old Grog’s forward line.
(beat)
Jeb, if Sophia is still alive, I think this is her.


The glyphs shift and burn brighter.

𐤃 Passage.
𐤄 Revelation.
𐤅 Connection.

The Memory of Kings begins to vibrate.


ELIAS (V.O.)
Remember your promise, Jebediah.
Keep them safe.
(the book begins to close)
And trust them.


The Memory of Kings closes.

The Echo of the Tides glows green.
LARK
(reaches out to the thread)
Sophia. I’m here.
I’m coming.
(she looks to Jeb)
I can do it. I can get us there.



EXT. — THE BLOODY GULL — MID-DECK — CONTINUOUS


The bay of Saltcrow Haven settles in for a fight.
Ships that shouldn’t be there either tie up or sail out.

The Bloody Gull holds just out of the British fleet’s cannons.

The crew share glances as they prepare for a fight.

Sparks bleed from a cracked cannon charge. A deckhand is hunched over it, trying to solder it closed.

The documentary crew’s drone sputters overhead.


CARLOS
(stressed)
Fuck.



Its rotors stop and it starts to fall.

Gus catches it mid-drop with one hand a grunt.


GUS
            (half a laugh)
Tell your toy to quit dying on us, ‘Los.
(hands the drone over)
We’ve got enough ghosts on this damn deck already.


Linda checks her lens.
The camera is still rolling but her hand is trembling.
LINDA
(deadpan, sarcastic humor)
The drone had my wide shot, Carlos. Thanks for killing the drama.


GUS
(grinning, takes the drone back)
Baby, you want drama?
Give it ten minutes. Something always happens.


The Gull lurches. Her bow dips deep and then rises again.
The deck tilts with a wet groan.
Everybody stumbles.

Gus catches Linda at her waist.


GUS
(pulls Linda close)
I said we don’t need more ghosts around here.


Linda looks at him. She breathes in like she might say something else. She doesn’t.
She shifts the camera, still on him.


                    LINDA
        If we die here, I just want to say-
    
    
She stops short. Breathes. The moment hangs.

Carlos cuts in, his voice panicked but fighting to stay 
practical.


                    CARLOS
            (trying to get the drone back up)
Save it Linda.
(he pauses, then quieter)
I’m not dying here. Martin ain’t dying here. And you’re not fucking dying here neither.
GUS
(snorts a half-choked laugh)
That’s the fucking spirit, Carlos.
(turns back to Linda)
But if we do die here…
(sly smile)
I’d want you to be the one to film it.


He winks.

The deckhand working on the cannon charge curses.

CHIME.


DECKHAND
(laughs nervously)
There. It’s holding.
It’s actually holding.


The Bloody Gull drives her bow into the waves.
She moans.

No one speaks.
ACT THREE

INT. SALTCROW HAVEN FORT — DAY


Murakami men stand atop the fort. Below them, Vernon’s fleet blockades the cove between the fort and the bay.

Inside the fort, more of Murakami’s crew are packing up gear and preparing to haul it to the docks.

Three of Murakami Hideyoshi’s men lead Sophia through the fort.
She’s shackled at her wrists and her ankles.

The hallway they are heading down is long and dark. Crates, barrels and piles of supplies line the walls.


SOPHIA
My crew is here, aren’t they?
(she tries getting a glance out of a window)
I tried to warn your captain.


The guard behind her pulls her by her hair and she stumbles backwards.


GUARD (snarl)
Shut your mouth, witch.
(he hauls her roughly to her feet)
Or I’ll shut it permanently for you.


SOPHIA (gritty)
(she laughs)
You can’t do that. Hideyoshi needs me.
(her voice lowers)
He needs me more than he needs any of you.


The rear guard nods to the two on Sophia’s sides and they send extra ley current into the chains.
Sophia winces. Then she continues to walk.

A distant cannon blast is followed by a loud crash and shaking walls.

The guards brush the falling dirt and dust out of their faces and exchange worried looks.


SOPHIA
(continues walking)
This fort isn’t going to stop them…
(the compass at her belt flares)
and you won’t be able to hold me forever.


The guards lead her to an inner courtyard where Murakami Hideyoshi is getting the rest of his men ready to leave.

EXT. FORT — COURTYARD — CONTINUOUS


Murakami’s crew are packing up weapons and hauling crates out of the fort. Cannon blasts in the distance are followed by slams into the fort’s ley shield. 

The walls and the ground rumble and shake with each blast.


MURAKAMI (commanding)
(barks orders)
Leave everything except your weapons.
(a blast hits the outer walls and he turns)
It’s time to go.


The guards bring Sophia to Murakami.


MURAKAMI
(looks Sophia over)
Take her to the Sea Burial. Use the tunnels. The rest of the men are going to the Mirror of Ash in the harbor.
(he brushes dust off his shoulder)
We will leave from the hidden cove.

Sophia’s head lifts when Murakami mentions the word cove.
She smiles.


MURAKAMI
(he pulls out the Abyssal Chronometer)
You won’t find this amusing once we get to Windless Arch.


Before he can adjust the chronometer, Sophia flicks her index finger and the chronometer ticks.

Sophia smiles one more time.

EXT. BAY — THE BLOODY GULL — HELM — DAY


The Bloody Gull’s cannons still smoke and sizzle after bombarding the fort.

Jeb is at the wheel and Lark is braced the starboard deck to his right.


JEB (growl)
(looks at Lark)
If there’s a time to try your thread idea, it’d be now, kid.
(he points to the front of the ship)
Go! Now!


Lark doesn’t hesitate. She races down the quarterdeck stairs.

EXT. HMS RELENTLESS — DECK — CONTINUOUS


The HMS Relentless is anchored and getting repairs made to her battered hull.
Admiral Vernon Harrow watches the Bloody Gull change its heading. Toward his blockade.

He sees Lark race down the Gull’s maindeck, past Wendy the submersible and towards the foredeck.

The spyglass glows green and blue in her hand.

He looks to his portside at the HMS Thunderwake.


VERNON (stern)
(he slides his hands into the gauntlets at the helm and looks at a deckhand)
Signal the Thunderwake. She’s going to use the spyglass.
(his gauntlets come to life)
That will not be happening today.


The deckhand turns and walks away.

EXT. THE BLOODY GULL — FOREDECK — CONTINUOUS


The Gull pushes through the waves toward the British fleet. 

Deckhands get extra cannon charges ready for quick reload. Gus checks armor on his crewmen, tightening straps on a frightened greenhorn and patting him on the shoulder.

Lark hurdles over crates and slides past barrels.

She looks at the blockade in front of her and the cove just beyond them.

She climbs down the foredeck and lands on the bow.

She runs to the rail and braces herself at the bowspit.

The Memory of Kings in her bag starts to hum and the Shell of Memory slung against her ribs starts to sing.

The Echo of the Tides finds its way to her right eye.


LARK
(she twists the spyglass trying to focus)
Fuck you, Old Grog.
(she focuses through the spyglass)
There you are.


Through the spyglass she sees Sophia being moved in the fort. The Compass of Ley Currents glowing at her hip. 

Sophia smiles. 

Lark smiles back. She starts to turn the dial on the side of the Echo of the Tides.

A blast rips across the sea. The lights in the sky go dark. Ley energy dims.

The wake from the HMS Thunderwake’s Disrupter Charge hits hard and heavy.

The three relics with Lark shriek and then go silent.
The Echo of the Tides blasts Lark into the wall behind her. 

The Bloody Gull and Old Red groan at the same time.
Old Red falls to his knees.

Lark lays motionless at the rail.

EXT. THE BLOODY GULL — QUARTER DECK — CONTINUOUS


The Bloody Gull is at a dead stop. She bobs up and down with the tide. Her ley glow is gone. She looks like an ancient, old and rotted pirate ship.

Jeb lifts himself up and braces himself at the helm.
The wheel drifts on its own.

His crew is scrambling in front of him.
Gus is loading cannon charges alongside deckhands.

Old Red walks up and down the main deck checking on crewmembers.

He looks to the empty bow.


JEB (shouts)
(he grabs the wheel and starts to turn)
Lark! Where’s Lark?


He looks to the three ships in front of him.
Vernon’s ships. The HMS Relentless isn’t moving.
The Valor’s Mercy is coming around the Gull’s portside. The Thunderwake is off the starboard.

Old Red makes his way down the main deck towards the quarter deck. He lock eyes with Jeb and nods.

Jeb nods back and starts to spin the ship’s wheel faster.

EXT. THE BLOODY GULL — MAIN DECK — CONTINUOUS


Martin and Carlos are sprinting up the main deck towards the bow when the Bloody Gull starts to spin. Carlos follows Martin with his camera up and shooting.

Martin dodges deckhands, crates and spools of rigging line.

Carlos’s camera is shaky and unstable.

The spinning throws Marin to the rail.


MARTIN (screams)
(picks himself up and keeps running)
Lark! Lark!


When they reach the foredeck they’re both thrown to the rail. They don’t let go.


CARLOS
(grunts)
Fuck.
(looks at Martin)
You alive?


MARTIN
(winces and grabs his ribs)
I think so.
They use the posts and the rail to crawl to the bow.

EXT. HMS VALOR’S MERCY — CONTINUOUS


The crew of the Valor’s Mercy waits for their order to attack.

Gun crews are at the ready. Boarders grip lines and hooks.
A greenhorn shakes nervously.

The Valor’s Mercy’s captain stands at the helm, his eyes locked on the HMS Relentless.

EXT. HMS THUNDERWAKE — CONTINUOUS


Sailors load charges into cannons.

The ley disruptor and gathering another charge.

The captain walks up and down the lines of men, giving last words of encouragement.


THUNDERWAKE CAPTAIN
(pats his crew on the shoulder)
For king and country.
(gathers a group around him)
We give them no quarter. The admiral wants that spyglass.
And we will give it to him.
 

EXT. HMS RELENTLESS — HELM — CONTINUOUS


Admiral Vernon looks over the scene in front of him.
The Valor’s Mercy to his right, the Thunderwake to his left and the Bloody Gull between them.

He clasps his hands behind his back and straightens his spine.

He turns to look at the repairs being made to his ship and smiles.
VERNON
(nods and smiles)
Almost finished.
Good.
(turns back to the ships in front of him)
It is time.
(he signals his first mate)
Tell the Thunderwake to open fire and the Mercy to move to board.


Vernon leaves the rail and heads to the helm.


VERNON
(slides his hands into the gauntlets)
Bring her around.
(he starts to turn the ship)
It’s time to end this once and for all.


EXT. THE BLOODY GULL — POOP DECK — CONTINUOUS


The deck of the Bloody Gull is eerily calm. The crew brace themselves to keep from flying across the deck. Gus grips a line and barks orders at the men.

Old Red stands below the Gull’s flag on the poop deck.

He rolls his bone dice in his hand. They start to glow blue and amber gold.

The Thunderwake unleashes its first round of cannon fire.


OLD RED (whisper)
(closes his hands around the dice)
Come back to us, girl. Come back to us.


He slams the dice onto the deck of the Gull.
His eyes go lightning blue as the deck of the Bloody Gull glows the same color.

The ship’s spin reaches its peak and the Bloody Gull phases out of space and time.

The Thunderwake’s cannon blasts crash into the Valor’s Mercy.

EXT. THE BLOODY GULL — BOW — DAY


Lark’s eyes open slowly. She’s still at the bow rail, laying on the deck.

Carlos and Martin finally make it to her.


LARK (raspy)
(eyes blink as she looks up at Martin and Carlos)
The spyglass. What happened?
(grabs hands and rises to her feet)
Where’s Sophia?
Did we make it?


Carlos and Martin’s faces go blank.


LARK
What happened? I tried to get us through the thread and past the blockade.
(she looks out over the rail)
Oh no.


The Bloody Gull is right in front of the HMS Relentless with the Valor’s Mercy and Thunderwake flanking them.

Three ships emerge from a fracture rift. The Sayf al-bahr, the Maiden of Dust and the Lantern Thief.


LARK (excited)
(points to Darak’s ships)
Guys! It’s Darak!
(grabs a line and leans over the rail)
What are they doing?


The boys try and get her back on the deck.



CARLOS (desperate)
(holding the back of Lark’s shirt)
Hey, boss lady!
Why don’t you get down from there.


MARTIN (freaked)
(grips a line)
God damnit. Lark. Get down!


Lark doesn’t listen. Darak’s ships are working in unison.

GRAPHIC NOVEL INSERT — 3 PANEL

— The Sayf unloads on the Valor’s Mercy before it even knows she’s there.
— The Maiden of Dust launches itself in reverse through its own wake and hits the Relentless.
— The Lantern Thief distracts the Thunderwake.

The Bloody Gull unloads its cannons.

EXT. THE BLOODY GULL — GUN DECK — CONTINUOUS


GUS
Fire! Fire! Fire!
They forgot about us! Make them pay!


Deckhands manning the cannons fire in unison. As soon as they can, they are reloading.

They fire again.


GUS
Atta boys! Give ‘em fucking hell!


He pats a crewman on the shoulder and runs up to the main deck.


EXT. THE HMS RELENTLESS — HELM — DAY


Vernon wears his anger on his face.
He pounds the helm before he mounts his hands in the gauntlets.


VERNON (screams)
FUCK!
(his go cold and he looks at the Sayf)
The bloody fucking Jackal. I hate that man.
(beat)
Mister Jones, signal to the others.
It’s time to leave.


MISTER JONES (sheepish)
Yes Admiral… are you sure?


VERNON (pissed)
Yes, Mister Jones. I am bloody fucking sure. 


He cranks the gauntlets and the ship starts to turn.

EXT. THE BLOOD GULL — QUARTER DECK — CONTINUOUS


DECKHAND (relieved)
(runs to the rail)
They’re running!


JEB
I’ll be damned.
(to the crew in general)
Slow us down! We hold here!


His crew hears him and jump into action. Lines pulled. Sails rolled. 
For a moment, there’s a bit of calm.

Gus makes his way up the stairs to stand with Jeb.

He looks concerned.


GUS
(shakes Jeb’s hand)
We made it out of that one but Murakami got away.
And I bet he took Sophia with him.


Jeb lights a blunt and takes a big rip.


JEB
Where’s Lark? Where’s Red?


GUS
(points and nods towards the poop deck)
Red is behind you sir. Up top.
(turns and looks to the front of the ship)
I haven’t seen Lark since she ran past me towards the bow.


LARK (hoarse)
(walks up the stairs with Carlos and Martin)
I’m here. We’re fine.
We lost Sophia. Again.


Linda makes her way up the opposite stairway while Old Red makes his way down the stairs from the poop deck.

The Bloody Gull lets out a deep groan.


OLD RED
(rolls his dice in his hand)
Aye, Jeb. The old girl is telling us she ain’t ready. Might need to give her a bit o’ love.


JEB (snaps)
(pounds the helm)
We ain’t got the time for that!

Hello friends! It is I. The Jackal!

DARAK (playful)
You lot do look busy.
Maybe I can help lighten the load?


Lark and Jeb approach the rail to get closer to Darak.
Darak’s eyes are locked on Jeb. At the locket around his neck.


JEB
What do you mean? And what do you want?
(flicks his joint into the sea)
Ye don’t offer help unless you can profit from it. What’s your offer and what’s your price?

LARK
(whispers to Jeb)
I know I shouldn’t trust him…

DARAK (joyful)
(raises his arms in welcome)
Friends, friends, friends!

Come aboard and let us make a deal?
Jeb and Lark agree. Deckhands run a plank between the two ships.

CUT TO BLACK.

INT. THE SAYF AL-BAHR — CAPTAIN’S QUARTERS — NIGHT


Jeb and Lark sit in front of Darak’s desk. Darak sits on top of it.

Nautical related items scatter the room. Spyglasses, compasses, astrolabs, chronometers and a piece of coral encased in glass.

Lark looks around the room with suspicion. 


LARK
(interested in the items)
Are you a smuggler or a collector?
Why do you have all these things?


DARAK
(smiles and embraces the room with open arms)
Yes and yes, young lady. I’m what you might call a hunter. 
A hunter of rare artifacts.


LARK
(her eyes narrow)
Artifacts like this?
(she lifts the Echo of the Tides)
Why do you and every other psycho here want this, Sophia’s compass, the singing conch shell and my dad’s book?


Darak looks at Jeb and then he strokes his long beard.
Jeb nods at Darak.
DARAK
(he shrugs)
You see… there’s a tale of items left behind by the people of Atlantis. A way to access their forgotten knowledge.
(he picks up a compass from his desk)
And who better to profit than the Jackal?


He looks at Jeb again.

Jeb shifts in his chair. He’s visibly uncomfortable.


LARK
(her eyes are burning into Jeb)
So all of this is about lost artifacts and a magic “library?”
(she looks around the room)
What about my dad? Is this tale what he was chasing?


Darak looks at Lark with curious eyes.


DARAK
(he chuckles)
Would his name be Elias? Hawthorne?
(he looks at Jeb)
You are either a lucky man or a very unlucky man, Jebediah Blackwater.

JEB
She’s mine to protect, Darak. Elias knew she’d show up and I promised him I’d keep her safe.

But fates and destiny sometimes have a mind of their own.

Ah yes. The Briggs girl.

(he talks with his hands)
I, Darak al-Mhar, will get you back young Sophia Briggs in trade for that.

LARK
Jeb. You can’t.

You have a deal, Jackal.

Excellent! Excellent!

Wait for me at Red Horizon, the old smuggler’s cove. You will be able to make repairs to your ship there without anyone finding you.

Sometimes you have to gamble and pick the side that doesn’t look like they will win.

The Bloody Gull sails through calm seas.

Lantern light illuminates the deck while the crew batten down crates, pull lines and mend sails.

Lark and Jeb stand alone on the fore deck of the Gull.

Old Red’s bone dice roll on the deck behind them.

JEB (almost soft)

LARK

So heed my tale, ye greedy lot, and steer ye clear, I pray…
