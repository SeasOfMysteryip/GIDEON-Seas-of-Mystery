# Ghost Ships and Phantom Crews

## Use Rule

Use as a folklore engine for abandoned vessels, sightings that cannot be logged cleanly, and Jeb-as-radio-host texture.

This dossier preserves the usable episode material as source lore, not a full transcript. Mine it for folklore, caller texture, guest formats, recurring sponsor brands, and story seeds. Do not treat host banter, caller claims, or commercial copy as locked canon unless another canon file promotes it.

## Segment Breakdown

| Segment | Material | Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Opening | Captain Jeb frames ghost ships as warnings, memories, and stories that refuse to stay sunk. | Good tonal model for Jeb turning dread into showmanship. |
| Mary Celeste / abandoned ships | The show uses the Mary Celeste as the clean entry point into vessels found without crews. | Useful for mystery structure: intact evidence, missing motive, no satisfying proof. |
| Listener call-ins | Callers describe lobster traps destroyed in calm water, underwater singing, and spectral figures near working boats. | Seed minor witness accounts, civilian rumors, and modern coastal fear. |
| Flying Dutchman expert exchange | Professor Smythe challenges Jeb's supernatural read while preserving the legend's emotional force. | Good model for Gideon's balance between lore and skepticism. |
| Reader stories | Maine, Florida, and Boston accounts widen the ghost-ship network. | Mine for regional one-off hauntings or tavern tales. |
