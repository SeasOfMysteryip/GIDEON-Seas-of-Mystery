# Cursed Treasures of the Deep

## Use Rule

Use for relic-side folklore, especially objects that demand a cost before the crew understands the rule.

This dossier preserves the usable episode material as source lore, not a full transcript. Mine it for folklore, caller texture, guest formats, recurring sponsor brands, and story seeds. Do not treat host banter, caller claims, or commercial copy as locked canon unless another canon file promotes it.

## Segment Breakdown

| Segment | Material | Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Treasure curse overview | Oak Island, Aztec gold, pirate treasure, and recovered objects are treated as a continuum of greed and consequence. | Useful for relic behavior that punishes extraction. |
| Caller block | A black idol, drowned necklace, jade amulet, silver cutlass, Spanish coins, and pearl necklace all cause obsession or repeated dreams. | Mine for cursed-object mechanics and minor props. |
| Historical artifact cases | The Hope Diamond and other famous curse examples provide public-world parallels. | Good for skeptical exposition before a true relic event. |
| Letters and second call-in | Repeated pattern: possession, warning signs, attempted disposal, consequence persists. | Strong template for relics that do not stop affecting the owner after abandonment. |

- A cursed object should change behavior first, then sleep, then memory, before it creates visible spectacle.
- The silver cutlass caller can inform haunted-weapon rules without becoming the Cutlass of Surge.
- Blackwater's Ghost Repellent can be used as joke merchandise sold in ports where people half-believe the stories.
