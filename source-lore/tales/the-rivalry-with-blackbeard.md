# The Rivalry with Blackbeard

## Use Rule

Use this as origin-history source lore for Captain Jeb, the Bloody Gull, and the crew's oral tradition. The tale can feed Jeb monologues, deckside storytelling, flashback seeds, character DNA, scenario structure, and ship-history texture.

This tale is not automatically literal canon. Treat it as a remembered, performed, and adapted version of Bloody Gull history unless a canon file, episode card, or approved scene locks a specific event.

## Origin / Adaptation Importance

Major Jeb voice source. Establishes the deck-tale frame, Jeb's preference for wit over brute force, the Bloody Gull's speed and shallows advantage, and Blackbeard as rival pressure rather than simple monster.

## Tale Summary

Jeb tells the crew about an early rivalry with Blackbeard, built around a treasure map, a race for Spanish gold, shallow-water cunning, and mutual respect between dangerous captains.

## Full Deck Tale

Captain Jeb's Tale: The Rivalry with Blackbeard
Narrator:
The Caribbean, 1718. The golden age of piracy was in full swing, and legends roamed the seas. Among them, none were as notorious - or as cunning - as Edward Teach, better known as Blackbeard, and Captain Jebediah Blackwater, the Land Pirate of the *Bloody Gull*. Their paths rarely crossed, but when they did, the sea itself seemed to hold its breath.
This is the tale of one such encounter, told by Captain Jeb himself as his crew gathered in the dimly lit galley of the *Bloody Gull*.
The Tale Begins
[Sound: The gentle creak of ship timbers, the clink of mugs, and distant waves.]
Captain Jeb:
"Gather 'round, lads, and I'll tell ye about the time I crossed swords - metaphorically - with none other than Blackbeard himself. Now, don't get yer hopes up. We weren't enemies, but we weren't swappin' rum recipes, either. Let's just say we had... a healthy rivalry."
[Crew cheers lightly, raising mugs.]
Captain Jeb:
"It all started when we took a Spanish galleon off Havana. A fine haul, but the real prize was this."
[He pulls a tattered map from his pocket and waves it.]
"A map leadin' to the treasure of the Spanish governor of Santo Domingo. Gold, jewels, and enough silver to buy an island - if ye were so inclined to settle, which I ain't."
A Shadow on the Horizon
Captain Jeb:
"We set sail for a little Bahamian island marked on the map. Spirits were high, the *Bloody Gull* was flyin' fast, and the wind was with us. But as the island came into view, so did another ship.
"And what a ship she was - *Queen Anne's Revenge*. Blackbeard's pride and joy. Cannons bristlin', sails black as a moonless night. And there he was, on the quarterdeck, his beard smokin' like he'd just walked out of hell."
Narrator:
Blackbeard's reputation preceded him. Known for his theatrics and sheer brutality, he had a knack for getting what he wanted without so much as firing a shot. But this time, he faced a captain as wily as he was.
A Challenge is Made
Blackbeard:
"Jebediah Blackwater!"
[His voice booms over the waves.]
"I hear ye've got somethin' that belongs to me - a map worth more than yer scrawny hide. Hand it over, and I might spare yer ship."
Captain Jeb:
"Belongs to ye? Last I checked, Teach, maps don't carry names. But if ye want it, let's settle this like men of the sea."
Blackbeard:
"And how's that?"
Captain Jeb:
"A race. First to the treasure keeps it, no bloodshed... unless ye can't resist."
Narrator:
Blackbeard, ever the showman, couldn't resist the drama of a race. He agreed, with one condition.
Blackbeard:
"If ye cheat, Jeb, I'll see yer bones decorate my bowsprit."
Captain Jeb:
[Grinning.]
"Fair enough, Teach. May the best pirate win."
The Hunt Begins
Captain Jeb:
"That island was a devil's maze, lads - mangroves thicker than a miser's purse and swamps deep enough to swallow a horse. We split the crew, searchin' high and low. Blackbeard's men did the same, though not without a bit o' sabotage."
[Sound: Shovels striking dirt, distant curses, and the buzz of jungle insects.]
Captain Jeb:
"At one point, they tried to block us off with fallen trees. But we're the *Bloody Gull*, aren't we? Ain't nothin' stoppin' us. And soon enough, we struck gold - literally. Buried under a rock shaped like a devil's horn."
The Escape
Captain Jeb:
"Now, here's where the real fun begins. With the treasure aboard, we made for the ship, but Blackbeard's crew weren't far behind. We hadn't even raised the anchor before the *Queen Anne's Revenge* was bearing down on us."
Narrator:
Blackbeard's ship was a behemoth, her cannons primed and her crew baying for blood. But Jeb had a plan.
Captain Jeb:
"Ye see, lads, speed's our greatest weapon. I knew the waters better than Teach, and there was a narrow channel nearby - shallow enough to give his great beast of a ship trouble. So, we headed straight for it."
[Sound: Cannons firing, sails flapping, and the rush of water.]
Captain Jeb:
"With the *Revenge* strugglin' in the shallows, we slipped away. And by the time he was free, we were well outta sight, headin' for open water with our prize."
The Aftermath
Captain Jeb:
"A few months later, I ran into Teach in Nassau. Thought he'd be breathin' fire, but instead, he raised a mug to me."
Blackbeard:
"Yer slippery as an eel, Jeb. But next time, I won't be so kind."
Captain Jeb:
[Chuckling.]
"Kindness don't suit ye, Teach. But I'll drink to the chase any day."
Narrator:
And so, the two captains parted, rivals bound by mutual respect, their names forever etched into the lore of the high seas.
The Crew Reacts
[Sound: Mugs clinking, laughter, and cheers.]
Crewmate:
"Cap'n, do ye think Teach'll ever catch ye?"
Captain Jeb:
"Catch me? Not unless he grows wings and learns to fly. Now, drink up, lads. The sea waits for no man!"
[Sound: The sounds of the galley fade into the roar of the open sea.]
The Tale of Captain Jeb and Blackbeard: A Pirate's Duel of Wits
Narrator (deep and resonant):
The wind howled over the churning waves, and the scent of salt hung thick in the air as Captain Jebediah Blackwater and his crew aboard the *Bloody Gull* sailed through the Caribbean waters. It was a time when legends roamed the sea, and among them stood Blackbeard himself, Edward Teach - a pirate whose name alone sent shivers down the spines of even the bravest sailors. Jeb and Teach weren't enemies, nor were they allies. They shared a bond forged in the crucible of the high seas: mutual respect and a touch of competitive fire.
This tale begins with a treasure map, a stormy night, and the fateful crossing of two legendary captains.
[Scene: The Galley of the Bloody Gull]
[Sound: Mugs clinking, fire crackling, and the low murmur of the crew.]
Captain Jeb (leaning back in his chair, mug in hand):
"Ah, lads, gather round! Let me tell ye of the time I crossed swords - figuratively, mind ye - with none other than Blackbeard himself. It weren't a battle of cannon or cutlass, but a contest of cunning, wit, and a bit o' luck. Aye, ol' Teach and I had a dance, and it be a tale worth the telling."
[Sound: A low murmur of interest from the crew, chairs scraping as they lean closer.]
Narrator:
The *Bloody Gull* had just come into possession of a treasure map - parchment worn and brittle, marked with the promise of Spanish gold buried on a small island hidden in the Bahamas. The crew was jubilant, but Jeb's instincts told him they weren't the only ones who'd caught wind of the prize.
As they approached the island under the cover of night, a familiar silhouette loomed on the horizon. It was the *Queen Anne's Revenge*, Blackbeard's notorious flagship, her lanterns glowing like the eyes of a beast in the dark.
Captain Jeb:
"There she was, lads - the *Queen Anne's Revenge*, as fearsome as the stories tell. Her cannons were ready, her crew watchful. But ol' Teach weren't one to fire first unless the stakes were worth it, and I reckoned he knew I wasn't bringin' a fight. Not yet, at least."
Narrator:
The two ships anchored a short distance apart, their captains meeting on neutral ground - a sandy spit of beach lit by torches, the crashing waves the only witness to their parley.
[Sound: Waves crashing, crackling torches, and the faint sound of wind.]
Blackbeard (gruff and commanding):
"Well, if it ain't Captain Jebediah Blackwater. Ye've got a knack for sniffin' out treasure, don't ye? But this here map be known to me as well. What say we settle this like gentlemen - pirate gentlemen, that is?"
Captain Jeb (grinning):
"Teach, ye old scoundrel, always a pleasure. Ye've got the firepower, but I've got the speed. We could waste time tradin' cannon fire, or we could make a wager, one captain to another. What's yer fancy?"
Blackbeard (stroking his beard, embers from his fuses glowing):
"A wager, ye say? I like yer style, Jeb. Let's have a race - a proper pirate's duel. First to dig up the treasure and return to their ship claims it all. Loser buys the rum for the next year."
Captain Jeb (laughing):
"Done and done! But mind ye, Teach, I've got tricks up me sleeve. Don't say I didn't warn ye."
Narrator:
And so, the race was on. Blackbeard's crew and the *Bloody Gull*'s pirates stormed the island, digging, searching, and occasionally sabotaging each other.
[Scene: The Jungle Interior of the Island]
[Sound: Shovels hitting dirt, shouts, and the rustling of leaves.]
Crewman:
"Cap'n! We've got somethin' here - feels like a chest!"
[Sound: The sound of a shovel striking wood.]
Captain Jeb:
"Dig faster, lads! Teach'll be on us quicker than a shark smells blood."
Narrator:
But just as Jeb's crew unearthed the chest, a loud cry erupted from the other side of the island. Blackbeard's men had struck gold as well. Both captains now raced to their ships, each laden with a chest of glittering treasure.
[Scene: The Bloody Gull's Deck]
[Sound: Waves crashing and orders being shouted.]
Captain Jeb (calling to his crew):
"Hoist the sails, ye dogs! We've got to outrun the *Queen Anne's Revenge*, or this treasure'll be nothin' but a memory."
Narrator:
The *Queen Anne's Revenge* gave chase, her cannons roaring, but the *Bloody Gull*'s speed outmatched her sheer might. As dawn broke, Jeb's ship disappeared into the horizon, leaving Blackbeard to curse and laugh in equal measure.
[Scene: Back on the Bloody Gull]
[Sound: Laughter and clinking mugs.]
Captain Jeb (grinning):
"So there it was, lads - a race, a treasure, and a bit o' friendly rivalry. Teach and I went our separate ways, both richer for it. But the best part? That scoundrel still owes me a bottle o' rum for makin' it back to port first!"
[Sound: The crew erupts in laughter.]
Narrator:
And so, the tale of Jeb and Blackbeard became one of legend - a meeting of two pirate titans, not as enemies, but as rivals, each chasing glory and gold across the vast and unpredictable seas.
