# The Leviathan's Wake

## Use Rule

Use this as origin-history source lore for Captain Jeb, the Bloody Gull, and the crew's oral tradition. The tale can feed Jeb monologues, deckside storytelling, flashback seeds, character DNA, scenario structure, and ship-history texture.

This tale is not automatically literal canon. Treat it as a remembered, performed, and adapted version of Bloody Gull history unless a canon file, episode card, or approved scene locks a specific event.

## Origin / Adaptation Importance

Good source for old-water language, northern sea dread, and Jeb's awe when the ocean outranks him.

## Tale Summary

Jeb tells an Icelandic-seas Leviathan tale that emphasizes humanity's smallness, the age of deep water, and the kind of mystery that deserves a toast instead of an answer.

## Full Deck Tale

Narrator:
In the galley of the Bloody Gull, Captain Jeb sits at the table, his mug of rum now nearly empty. He taps the rim with his finger, gathering the crew's attention. The lanterns flicker as the sea roars outside, the ship gently rocking on the waves. The crew settles in, knowing they're in for another of Jeb's legendary tales.
There's no sailor alive who hasn't whispered of the Leviathan - great creatures of the deep, as old as the ocean itself. Some call them legends, others call them nightmares. But Captain Jeb, with the sea's salt running thick in his veins, knows that sometimes, the stories are true. And when he speaks of the Leviathan's Wake, it's not just a tale - it's a warning.
When he's ready to begin, Jeb leans forward, a gleam in his eye*.*
Captain Jeb: *(grinning as he pours himself another mug of rum)*
"Alright, lads and lasses, gather 'round for another one. This tale... well, it might chill yer bones, but I reckon it's a good one. It happened years ago when I was sailing the waters off the coast of Iceland. It was a dark and stormy night, the kind of night where the sea feels like it's tryin' to swallow the world whole."
Narrator:
Jeb leans back in his chair, his voice taking on the tone of a man who's seen things, things most men would never dare to speak of.
Captain Jeb:
"It was my first time in those waters. The crew was uneasy, feelin' the weight of the storm, but we pressed on - 'cause that's what we do. We sail through the storm, always. That's how ye make yer fortune, aye? But the sea... the sea had somethin' else in mind that night."
Narrator:
The captain pauses, eyes distant, as if reliving the event. The crew listens closely, hanging on every word. The wind howls outside, but inside the galley, it's a different kind of storm.
Captain Jeb:
"We were makin' good time, but the weather turned bad, quick-like. Wind howled, waves crashed against the hull, and the rain - it came down like needles. We had no choice but to drop anchor and wait it out. That's when it happened. The first mate, Cooper, he shouted something about the water changin'. I thought he'd lost his mind in the storm, but then I saw it too."
Narrator:
Jeb leans forward, speaking in a low, almost reverent tone.
Captain Jeb:
"Out in the distance, the sea began to boil. Not from the storm, no - this was different. The water churned, like something beneath it was movin'. Something... big."
Narrator:
The crew exchanged uneasy glances, each one feeling the weight of Jeb's words. The storm raged on outside, but inside the Bloody Gull, it was as if the very air had thickened with tension.
Captain Jeb:
"We thought it was just the storm - maybe a whale or a school of sharks, something big movin' through the waves. But the noise... the noise was somethin' else entirely. It wasn't a rumble or a growl, no. It was a deep, resonating *thrum*, like the heartbeat of the ocean itself."
Narrator:
The room fell silent. The crew could almost hear it - the pounding rhythm of the sea's heart, beating beneath them, alive and ancient.
Captain Jeb:
"Then the water broke, just a hundred yards off the starboard side. And out of the depths... came somethin' that made even the bravest men on board tremble. It rose outta the sea like a mountain - black, slick, and massive. It towered above us, like a shadow cast by a beast from the darkest depths. Its body stretched so far we couldn't see the end of it. I've heard tales of the Kraken, but this... this was somethin' different."
Narrator:
Jeb pauses, his eyes narrowing as he remembers the feeling of that moment.
Captain Jeb:
"That thing, whatever it was, had tentacles as thick as trees, each one slappin' the water with a force that nearly shattered the ship. Its eyes - two great, dark orbs - glowed like twin lanterns in the blackness. We couldn't see its full body, but the wake it left was enough to tell me: This was no natural creature. This... was something ancient. Something that had lived longer than mankind, deep in the heart of the ocean."
Narrator:
The crew was spellbound, caught between disbelief and wonder. Jeb's voice had taken on the cadence of someone who'd seen the impossible and lived to tell the tale. But the real terror lay not in the sight of the beast, but in the way the sea seemed to respond to it.
Captain Jeb:
"As quickly as it came, it disappeared. Like it'd never existed. One moment, the sea was calm - then, *bam*, this great beast reared up outta the water, and before we could make sense of it, it was gone. Just the wake left behind - a swirling vortex, pullin' everything in, even the stars above us seemed to get sucked into it."
Narrator:
The Bloody Gull creaked with the sound of memory, and the air seemed to thicken with the weight of Jeb's story. The captain looked around at the crew, his grin slowly returning, but it was the grin of a man who'd stared into the abyss and come back unscathed.
Captain Jeb:
"We pulled anchor and made sail right quick, for fear that whatever had risen from the depths might take an interest in us. And while we made it back safe, I'll tell ya this, lads and lasses - there's somethin' down there, something older than the sea itself. And we, we're just visitors in its world."
Narrator:
As the tale settled over the crew, Jeb's words hung in the air like the last traces of a storm's fury. The Leviathan's Wake - a reminder that the sea, in all its beauty and mystery, still holds secrets too great for human comprehension. Secrets that, if we're lucky, might never rise again.
Captain Jeb: *(smiling, raising his mug)*
"To the deep, me hearties, and the mysteries it keeps. May we sail on, always curious and always bold."
Narrator:
*The crew raise their mugs in unison, a toast to the unknown. And somewhere below, in the abyss, the Leviathan waits.*
And so the Bloody Gull sailed on, *cutting through the dark waters, ever moving, ever searching*, with a crew that would never forget the night they glimpsed the true face of the deep. A face that might just be the stuff of legends - or nightmares. Only the sea knows for sure.
