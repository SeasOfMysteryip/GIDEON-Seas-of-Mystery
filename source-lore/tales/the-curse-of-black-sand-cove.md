## Full Deck Tale

The Curse of Black Sand Cove: Captain Jebediah Blackwater's Tale
Narrator:
On a stormy night aboard the Bloody Gull, the sea raged as if it, too, were eager to hear the tales of Captain Jebediah Blackwater. The crew huddled in the galley, the lantern light casting eerie shadows that danced with the swaying of the ship. Captain Jeb, his tricorn hat pulled low, sat at the head of the table, a mug of rum in one hand and the other resting on the hilt of his cutlass. His voice, low and gravelly, cut through the storm's howling.
Captain Jeb:
"Listen close, ye lot, for what I'm about to tell ye ain't a tale for the faint o' heart. It's a story 'bout greed, curses, and the heavy price a man pays when he meddles with things better left alone. This be the story o' the cursed treasure of Black Sand Cove."
Narrator:
The crew exchanged uneasy glances. Tales of Black Sand Cove were whispered in the darkest corners of portside taverns, stories of riches beyond measure guarded by vengeful spirits.
Captain Jeb:
"It began in Tortuga, that den of sin and drink. I were sittin' in a tavern called The Gilded Gull, nursin' a bottle o' rum older than the barmaid servin' it, when a scrawny wretch came stumblin' in. His clothes were tattered, his eyes wild with fear, and he clutched a leather-bound journal like it were his very soul."
Narrator:
The captain took a long pull from his mug, his gaze distant as he continued.
Captain Jeb:
"He sat at me table without so much as an 'if ye please' and slapped the journal down. 'Black Sand Cove,' he said, his voice shakin'. 'Riches enough to make a man king - but ye'll not want 'em, for they're cursed.'"
Narrator:
Jeb chuckled darkly, his fingers drumming against the table.
Captain Jeb:
"I thought the man mad, but curiosity's a devil that whispers sweet in a pirate's ear. I took the journal, paid the man a few coins - he refused to touch 'em, said they'd bring him ruin - and sent him on his way. The map inside was like nothin' I'd ever seen, its landmarks shiftin' as if the very paper were alive."
Narrator:
The room grew tense as Jeb described the journey.
Captain Jeb:
"The Bloody Gull set sail under skies black as tar. The sea were restless, churnin' like it knew where we were headin'. Strange things started happenin' almost as soon as we hit open water. The compass spun like a drunkard, the winds howled words we couldn't understand, and some o' the crew swore they saw figures movin' in the waves."
Narrator:
Jeb leaned forward, his voice dropping to a whisper.
Captain Jeb:
"By the time we reached Black Sand Cove, even the bravest among us were shakin'. The beach were somethin' to behold - black sand glitterin' under the moonlight, like crushed obsidian and starlight. And at the center, half-buried in the sand, was the chest. It gleamed like it were callin' to us, promisin' riches beyond our wildest dreams."
Narrator:
He slammed his mug on the table, causing some of the crew to jump.
Captain Jeb:
"But there ain't no such thing as free gold. We opened the chest, and it were full - coins, jewels, and a strange black mirror that seemed to drink the light. The moment we touched it, the sea screamed, and the spirits came."
Narrator:
The crew leaned in as Jeb described the horrors that followed.
Captain Jeb:
"They rose from the waves, pale as moonlight and with eyes that burned like the devil's own. These weren't men - they were somethin' worse, bound to the treasure they'd stolen in life. Their voices were a cacophony o' curses, their touch cold enough to freeze a man's soul."
Narrator:
Jeb's voice grew thick with emotion.
Captain Jeb:
"Me quartermaster, Silas, grabbed the black mirror, thinkin' it might be the key to stoppin' them. But the moment he touched it, he fell dead, his eyes black as pitch. The spirits closed in, and we fought like hellions. But how do ye kill what's already dead?"
Narrator:
He paused, his knuckles white as he gripped the table's edge.
Captain Jeb:
"I ordered the chest closed and buried deep in the black sand, hopin' it'd appease the spirits. But they weren't lettin' us go so easy. One by one, me crew fell, their screams resonating through the cove. By the time we reached the Bloody Gull, there were only five of us left."
Narrator:
The crew shuddered as Jeb's voice softened, his eyes dark with the weight of his choices.
Captain Jeb:
"We sailed out o' that cursed place with the spirits wailin' in our wake. Even now, I hear their cries in the dead o' night, remindin' me o' the lives lost to me greed."
Narrator:
The room fell silent, the only sound the distant rumble of thunder and the creak of the ship.
Captain Jeb:
"So mark me words, lads and lasses: if ever ye come across a map leadin' to Black Sand Cove, burn it. Let the treasure rot with the souls o' the damned. Riches ain't worth the weight o' yer soul."
Narrator:
With that, Captain Jeb leaned back, his tale complete. The crew sat in uneasy silence, the flickering lantern light casting shadows that seemed just a bit darker than before.
