# The Sea Sprites

## Use Rule

Use this as origin-history source lore for Captain Jeb, the Bloody Gull, and the crew's oral tradition. The tale can feed Jeb monologues, deckside storytelling, flashback seeds, character DNA, scenario structure, and ship-history texture.

This tale is not automatically literal canon. Treat it as a remembered, performed, and adapted version of Bloody Gull history unless a canon file, episode card, or approved scene locks a specific event.

## Origin / Adaptation Importance

Important for the show's seductive-water logic and for the line between wonder and threat. Also feeds Lysara-adjacent mood without replacing Lysara canon.

## Tale Summary

Jeb tells a fuller sea-sprite tale where beauty, play, warning, and danger blur around the crew and Lysara-adjacent ocean magic.

## Full Deck Tale

The Tale of Captain Jeb and the Sea Sprites (Expanded)
(*Scene: The crew of the Bloody Gull sits gathered around the flickering fire, the shadows from the hearth dancing against the wooden walls of the galley. The salt of the sea hangs thick in the air, and the creaking of the ship is a familiar song to those aboard. Outside, the waves of the Southern Seas rise and fall, their rhythm deep and ancient. Captain Jeb, ever the master of the tale, settles in with a knowing smile as he prepares to continue his story.*)
Narrator:
*The ship's timbers groan gently with the rocking of the sea. The evening is calm, a stillness hanging in the air that seems to lean in, eager for the next words to come. The crew listens intently, the firelight casting strange shadows on their faces, as if the ship itself were listening for something more than just the crackle of the logs. And then, Captain Jeb speaks again, his voice a low rumble in the stillness, a familiar lilt in his tone, drawing the crew deeper into the mystery.*
Captain Jeb: (*His voice rich and dark, like the depth of the sea itself*)
"Lads, I told you I've seen strange things in the water. But that night, with those sea sprites - those glowing creatures, pulsing in the deep - it changed me. Changed the way I look at the sea, and perhaps the world itself."
Narrator:
*The captain pauses, his eyes narrowing as he reflects on the tale.*
Captain Jeb:
"You see, as we sailed away from Tidesreach that night, I couldn't shake the feeling that those little sprites weren't just some oddity of the sea. No, there was something more to them. Their movements - they weren't random. They weren't just playing in the waves. There was a purpose behind them. And that purpose was something I couldn't quite grasp."
*The fire flickers in the background, and a slight chill runs through the galley as Jeb's tone shifts, growing more serious.*
Captain Jeb:
"We sailed on, my crew growing uneasy under the weight of what we'd seen. But then, just as we passed the edge of the Southern Sea, the strange light came again. Only this time, it wasn't in the distance - it was right beneath us. I felt it before I saw it. A sudden drop in temperature, a deep vibration in the ship's hull, like the water beneath us had become... *alive*."
Narrator:
*The crew leans forward, hanging on Jeb's every word. The captain smiles darkly, savoring the tension.*
Captain Jeb:
"We were sailing at night, the sky clouded over, no stars to guide us, just the echo of the wind and the creaking of the ship. Then, as I looked down into the water, I saw it - the glow. This time, it was different. It wasn't just a single light, but a swarm of them, weaving through the depths like fireflies, lighting up the whole sea beneath us. It was as if the water itself had become alive, pulsing in time with the rhythm of the creatures."
(*The fire pops, sending a shower of embers into the air, as if to punctuate Jeb's words. The crew shifts uneasily in their seats, each wondering what this could mean.*)
Captain Jeb:
"I ordered the crew to stay silent, to watch, to not make a single sound. I didn't want to risk disturbing whatever was going on below. We watched as the sprites moved beneath us - each one leaving a streak of light behind them, like a trail of stardust. But they weren't like fish, or any creature of the deep we know. No, these sprites were intelligent. They moved with purpose, as if they were... aware of us. As if they knew we were watching, testing us, perhaps."
Narrator:
*A hushed murmur rises from the crew. They all know Captain Jeb well enough to understand when he's holding something back, but even so, the tension is thick in the room now. Jeb's voice drops lower, as if recalling the true essence of the encounter.*
Captain Jeb:
"We lowered a lantern over the side, and one of the sprites, the brightest of them all, came up to the surface. It was as if it wanted to be seen, like it was choosing to make contact. I swear to you, lads, it wasn't like any fish or creature I've ever seen. Its body seemed to flicker, its form shifting between solid and ethereal. The light around it grew brighter, more intense. And in that light, I could see... *eyes*. But they weren't like our eyes. They were deep, almost infinite, as though they contained the whole of the sea within them."
(*The crew falls silent, the atmosphere heavy with anticipation. The flickering fire casts strange, elongated shadows across the walls as the mystery unfolds.*)
Narrator:
*The crew remains still, caught in the captain's web of words. In the flickering firelight, their faces reflect a mix of awe and uncertainty, as if the sea itself has whispered secrets into their ears. Captain Jeb's tale grows deeper, darker, as he continues, his voice like a soft thunder in the quiet room.*
Captain Jeb:
"I reached over the rail, hand trembling, almost *feeling* the power of the sprite beneath me. But before I could touch it, it vanished - *gone*, as if it had never been there. The sea calmed again, the glow fading into the depths. But there was something in that moment, something I can't put into words. Something... alien. These sea sprites weren't just creatures of the sea. No. They were beings of the *water* - of the very element itself, as if they were from some place beyond our world. Perhaps they weren't just 'from the sea,' but *of it* - born from some mysterious, hidden part of the ocean, or perhaps even from somewhere far beyond this earth."
Narrator:
*He leans back, looking at his crew with a knowing glint in his eye, as if the weight of the encounter still lingers.*
Captain Jeb:
"Lads, they moved like nothing I've ever seen. Not like fish, not like mermaids, but like... like something from another world. And that's what haunts me. I've sailed the seas, and I know the waters. I've met creatures of the deep - creatures that roam the land and the sea. But these... these sea sprites - they felt like they were from somewhere *else*. Like underwater aliens, only we don't have the words for what they are. We don't have the words for what they *could* be."
(*He pauses, his gaze drifting toward the horizon, as if seeing something beyond the waves - something he's not quite ready to share.*)
Captain Jeb:
"There are places in this world - places that even the bravest of men dare not venture. And sometimes, the sea shows us things we were never meant to see. Things that blur the line between what's real and what's beyond our understanding."
Narrator:
*The room is quiet, the crew deep in thought. They've heard stories from Jeb before, but there's something different in this one. Something unspoken lingers in the air, thick with mystery and wonder. Finally, one of the sailors speaks up, breaking the silence.*
Crewmember:
"Captain, do you think they'll come back? Those sprites?"
Narrator:
*Jeb looks up at the crew, his eyes soft with experience, a knowing smile curling at the corner of his lips.*
Captain Jeb:
"They'll come back when they're ready, lad. When the sea decides it's time. But don't go looking for them. Some things, some creatures, are meant to be left alone. The sea isn't a place for chasing ghosts or looking for answers to questions you don't know how to ask. Those sprites? They were a reminder. A reminder that there's much more to the world than we can ever understand."
Narrator:
*Captain Jeb stands up, raising his mug in a quiet toast.*
Captain Jeb:
"To the sea, lads. Mysterious, vast, and full of secrets. May she always keep a few for herself."
Narrator:
*The crew raises their mugs in unison, their eyes reflecting the firelight, deep in thought. Outside, the waves crash against the hull, their song now a bit more mysterious, a little more alien than it was before.*
*And so, the night goes on, the crew of the Bloody Gull holding their secrets and their questions close. The sea stretches endlessly before them, an ocean of mysteries waiting to be uncovered. But some mysteries, like the sea sprites, may forever remain just beyond their reach - alien, otherworldly, and as eternal as the waters themselves.*
STORY 1:
The Tale of Captain Jeb and the Mermaids of Mistral Bay
(Scene: The creaking of the ship's timbers in the quiet evening air, the scent of saltwater mixed with freshly baked bread, and the murmur of the crew filling the galley. It's warm inside, and the flickering light of an oil lamp casts shadows across the faces of the sailors gathered around. Captain Jeb leans back in his chair, his hands folded behind his head, a sly smile on his lips. The crew grows quiet, sensing a story is about to unfold.)
Narrator:
The Bloody Gull sways gently at anchor in Mistral Bay, a bay as mysterious as the fog that creeps in at twilight. The crew are gathered in the galley, listening to their captain as he takes a deep breath, preparing to share a tale that will leave them wondering about the sea's deepest secrets. Captain Jeb, with a glint in his eye, begins his story, weaving a memory from long ago.
Captain Jeb: (Chuckling, his voice deep and rich, with just a hint of nostalgia)
"Well, lads, you've heard the stories - mermaids, those sirens of the sea who lead sailors astray with their songs. But I'll tell you, they're not the wicked creatures the old legends make them out to be. No, no, they've got a whole other side to 'em, if you're willing to listen."
(He leans forward, his voice lowering to a conspiratorial whisper.)
"Let me take you back... to a summer long ago, when the wind was warm and the stars above us felt a little closer. We were anchored off Mistral Bay - right near the edge of the Shifting Tides, a stretch of water that's said to be more trouble than it's worth. But I knew the waters there like the back of my hand, and I had no fear of what lay beneath."
(The crew leans in, the clinking of mugs and the crackling of the fire seem to fade as Jeb speaks.)
Narrator:
The bay is dark and quiet now, but in Captain Jeb's tale, the waters are alive with an energy that promises adventure. The air is thick with the promise of the unknown as the crew listens, spellbound.
Captain Jeb:
"Well, we dropped anchor, and I went about me business - catching fish, setting traps, all the usual. But as the sun set, something strange happened. I was out on deck when I heard it - a soft, lilting tune carried by the wind. It wasn't the wind, though, and it wasn't the gulls, either. It was a song, like a melody from a dream. Now, I'm no fool, I've sailed these waters long enough to know when the sea's calling to me. But this? This was different."
(Jeb's eyes narrow, remembering the moment, and he pauses to let the tension build.)
Narrator:
The crew can feel the hairs on the back of their necks stand up as Jeb continues. The low hum of the ocean outside seems to echo in the background, and the flames in the galley flicker just a little more brightly, as if they, too, are caught in the tale.
Captain Jeb:
"I followed the sound, out to the edge of the ship, where the water gleamed silver under the moon. And there, in the shimmering waves, I saw them - mermaids, with long, flowing hair like kelp, eyes as deep as the sea itself, and tails that glittered in the moonlight like the finest emeralds. Now, these weren't the dangerous creatures you hear about in old sailor's tales. They weren't singing to lure me into the deep - they were simply singing for the joy of it."
(He chuckles softly.)
"I remember one of 'em, the leader of the group, looked up at me, and smiled. I swear to you, boys, she didn't have a care in the world. She gestured for me to come closer. Now, I've had my fair share of run-ins with creatures of the deep, but there was something about this moment that felt... peaceful. Almost like an invitation."
(Jeb pauses, his fingers tapping the edge of the table as if recalling the sensation of the moment.)
Narrator:
The crew exchanges uncertain glances, their minds racing to picture such an encounter. Was it a dream? Was it magic? Whatever it was, it's clear that Jeb's tale is about to take a turn they weren't expecting.
Captain Jeb: (With a grin, his voice full of mischief)
"I couldn't resist, so I climbed down into one of the smaller boats we had hanging off the side, and rowed out toward them. The closer I got, the more I could hear their song, not in words, but in notes that filled the air and vibrated through the water like a pulse."
(Jeb leans in, his voice softer now, as though he's sharing a secret with his crew.)
Captain Jeb:
"They didn't speak in our tongue, but they didn't need to. One of the mermaids - her name was Lysara, though I'm not sure how I knew that - reached out, gently brushing the side of my boat with her tail. I've never felt anything like it. It was like the whole sea was reaching out to me through her. She smiled, and I saw something in her eyes... a sort of understanding. She knew I wasn't there to harm her or her kin. She knew that I, too, had a love for the sea."
(The crew is silent now, caught up in the tale as Jeb leans back, lost in the memory.)
Narrator:
The warmth of the fire contrasts with the chill of the water in the story, as Jeb's voice becomes more wistful. His words are the very rhythm of the sea itself, ebbing and flowing with each moment he relives.
Captain Jeb:
"Lysara and I spent hours together, speaking without words, just sharing the same love for the ocean. She showed me places I'd only heard of in whispers - sunken ships, hidden caves, and ancient coral reefs that glowed with an otherworldly light. But what struck me most was the peace they carried. They weren't the dangerous temptresses the stories made 'em out to be. No, these were creatures of the sea, as old as the tides themselves, guardians of its secrets."
(A long pause, and then Jeb's voice lowers, as if drawing the crew in closer still.)
Captain Jeb:
"As the first light of dawn broke across the horizon, Lysara gave me one last smile before slipping beneath the waves. 'We'll always be here,' she said without words, but the meaning was clear. I was part of the sea now, just as they were."
(The room is quiet for a long moment, each sailor lost in their thoughts, perhaps imagining the mermaids beneath the waves, unseen yet ever-present.)
Narrator:
And as the tale winds down, the crew of the Bloody Gull realizes that the sea is much more than a vast expanse of water. It's a living thing, filled with mysteries that even the bravest cannot fully understand. The mermaids, guardians of the deep, remind us that sometimes the greatest treasures of the sea are not gold or riches, but the moments of peace and wonder we find in the most unexpected places.
Captain Jeb: (With a knowing smile)
"So, next time you hear a song on the wind, boys... don't be too quick to dismiss it. The sea has a way of calling us all, in one form or another. And who knows? Maybe, just maybe, you'll find yourselves in the company of a mermaid - or a merman - who has a story of their own to tell."
Narrator:
As Jeb raise his mug, the crew follows suit. With the fire crackling, the waves whispering in the distance, the crew, for just a brief moment, feel as though they're not just sailors. They're part of something much older, something timeless.
Captain Jeb: (With a grin)
"Fair winds, lads. Here's to more tales - both real and fanciful - of the sea."
(The crew cheers, their voices blending with the sound of the waves outside, as the story settles into the memory of the night.)
