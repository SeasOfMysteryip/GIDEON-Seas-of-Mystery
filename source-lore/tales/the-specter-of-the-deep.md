# The Specter of the Deep

## Use Rule

Use this as origin-history source lore for Captain Jeb, the Bloody Gull, and the crew's oral tradition. The tale can feed Jeb monologues, deckside storytelling, flashback seeds, character DNA, scenario structure, and ship-history texture.

This tale is not automatically literal canon. Treat it as a remembered, performed, and adapted version of Bloody Gull history unless a canon file, episode card, or approved scene locks a specific event.

## Origin / Adaptation Importance

Useful for dread pacing, entity restraint, and the rule that the sea can become wrong before anything appears.

## Tale Summary

Jeb recalls an unnerving encounter from younger days, where a silent sea, missing wildlife, and a presence below the ship become more frightening than open attack.

## Full Deck Tale

Narrator:
In the galley of the Bloody Gull with lantern lights flickering off the walls, the crew gathers around Captain Jeb. He sits at the head of the long wooden table, nursing a mug of spiced rum. The salty air of the open sea seeps into the room as the ship creaks with each rolling wave.
The savvy crew knew better than to ignore Captain Jeb when he was in the mood to spin a yarn. His stories carried the weight of a thousand sails and the thrill of a tempest. Tonight, as the sea whispered its secrets outside, the captain's voice rose above the clink of mugs and the crackle of the galley stove.
Jeb grins as he surveys the room.
Captain Jeb:
"Gather 'round, lads and lasses. Let me tell ye of the time I came face to face with the *Specter of the Deep*. A beast so strange, it'd make yer hair stand on end and yer knees knock like an unmoored rowboat in a storm."
Narrator:
The crew murmurs in anticipation, leaning closer as Jeb's voice drops to a conspiratorial whisper.
Captain Jeb:
"It was a night much like this one - quiet seas, a soft breeze, and a full moon hangin' low, its silver light spillin' over the waves. I was a younger man then, captainin' a smaller vessel called *The Silver Marlin*. We were chartin' a course through the waters off the Azores, huntin' for new trade routes. The sea was calm, but it held a strange stillness, as if it were holdin' its breath."
Narrator:
Jeb's voice grew softer, the weight of the memory pressing against his usual bravado.
Captain Jeb:
"Now, I've sailed through calm seas before, but this was... different. The water was like black glass, so still it felt unnatural. No fish, no gulls - just silence. My first mate, Briggs, leaned over the side and said, 'Cap'n, there's somethin' wrong with this place.' I told him to keep his wits about him, but truth be told, I felt it too. The air had a chill that sank straight into yer bones."
*(He pauses, taking a long sip from his mug as the crew listens in rapt silence.)*
Captain Jeb:
"Then it started. A low hum, like the sound of a church organ played beneath the waves. It wasn't loud, but it cut through the silence like a blade. We all heard it, clear as day. The men were on edge, mutterin' about spirits and sea witches, when suddenly, the water began to ripple. At first, it was just small circles, like raindrops in a pond. But then..."
*(He slams his hand on the table, causing a few crew members to jump.)*
Captain Jeb:
"WHOOSH! A great column of water shot straight into the air, fifty feet high if it was an inch! And out of it came the beast."
Narrator:
The crew gasped, their imaginations running wild as Jeb leaned back in his chair, the flickering lantern casting shadows across his face.
Captain Jeb:
"It was like nothin' I'd ever seen before. Long and serpentine, with scales that shimmered like polished metal in the moonlight. Its eyes glowed like twin lanterns, and its body pulsed with a strange green light that seemed to come from within. It didn't roar or thrash - no, it just hovered there above the water, starin' down at us like we were the ones intrudin' in its world."
Narrator:
The room fell deathly quiet as the captain's voice dropped to a near whisper.
Captain Jeb:
"Briggs shouted, 'It's not natural, Cap'n! We need to get outta here!' But I couldn't take my eyes off it. There was somethin' in the way it moved - almost... deliberate. Like it was studyin' us, tryin' to decide if we were worth its time. And then, just as suddenly as it appeared, it dove back into the depths. The water erupted in a whirlpool, rockin' the ship so hard we nearly capsized. By the time we got our bearings, the sea was calm again, and the hum was gone."
Narrator:
Jeb looks around the table, his expression serious.
Captain Jeb:
"We made it back to port with a hold full of goods, but the men were never the same. Briggs took to the bottle, sayin' he could still hear the hum in his dreams. And as for me, well..." *(He taps the side of his head, smirking.)* "I've spent many a night wonderin' if what we saw was some ancient sea creature... or somethin' from beyond the stars."
Narrator:
The crew exchanged uneasy glances, the weight of the story settling over them like a thick fog. Jeb, sensing the tension, broke into a grin.
Captain Jeb: *(chuckling and raising his mug)*
"But don't let it keep ye up at night, me hearties. Whatever it was, it didn't seem too keen on stickin' around. Maybe it just wanted to remind us that there's more to this world than we can see. So here's to the mysteries of the deep - may they keep our hearts curious and our sails full!"
*(The crew cheers, their nerves easing as the captain's infectious spirit takes hold. The camera lingers on Jeb's face, his eyes twinkling with the kind of wonder only a lifelong sailor could know. )*
Narrator:
And so, the Bloody Gull sailed on, its crew warmed by the captain's tale but ever watchful of the dark waters below. *Outside, the waves continue their eternal dance, hiding secrets that only the bravest - or most foolish - might dare to uncover.*
