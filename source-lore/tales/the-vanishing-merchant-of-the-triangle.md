# The Vanishing Merchant of the Triangle

## Use Rule

Use this as origin-history source lore for Captain Jeb, the Bloody Gull, and the crew's oral tradition. The tale can feed Jeb monologues, deckside storytelling, flashback seeds, character DNA, scenario structure, and ship-history texture.

This tale is not automatically literal canon. Treat it as a remembered, performed, and adapted version of Bloody Gull history unless a canon file, episode card, or approved scene locks a specific event.

## Origin / Adaptation Importance

Useful for Bermuda/Triangle public myth, real-world disappearance logic, and Jeb's ability to make a route feel like a character.

## Tale Summary

Jeb recounts a vanished merchant ship tied to the Triangle, using the galley tale frame to turn a maritime disappearance into a warning about routes that change while men are watching.

## Full Deck Tale

Narrator:
"In the warm glow of the galley, Captain Jeb sat at the head of the table, his tankard in hand and a mischievous glint in his eye. The crew leaned in, eager for another of their captain's legendary tales. This one, he assured them, was of victory, treasure, and a mystery that left even him scratching his head."
Captain Jeb:
"Right, mates, settle in! This be a tale of gold, glory, and the cursed waters o' the Bermuda Triangle. Aye, it's about the time the Bloody Gull chased down a merchant ship with a belly full o' riches and came out on top - or so we thought."
[Sound: A distant rumble of thunder and the faint crash of waves, setting the mood.]
Captain Jeb:
"It began on a day as fair as any. We'd been patrolin' the Triangle, lookin' for fat merchantmen foolhardy enough to take a shortcut through those infamous waters. By mid-afternoon, the lookout cried out from the crow's nest - 'Sail ho!' A merchant ship, ridin' low in the water, meanin' she was heavy with cargo. Gold, spices, maybe even silks - enough to keep us livin' like kings for years."
Crewmate (interrupting):
"Was she armed, Captain?"
Captain Jeb:
"Aye, but lightly. Just a few cannon, more for show than fightin'. We closed the distance fast, the Bloody Gull cuttin' through the waves like a blade through butter. As we drew near, I raised the flag - ye know the one, black as night with a skull grinnin' wide. I gave 'em a chance to surrender. They didn't take it."
Narrator:
"Jeb paused, letting the tension build. The crew shifted in their seats, hanging on his every word."
Captain Jeb:
"Instead, they fired their cannons - an act of bravery or foolishness, I'll leave ye to decide. We answered back, our broadside sendin' wood splinters flyin'. It wasn't long before we closed the gap and boarded. Their crew fought hard, I'll give 'em that, but the Bloody Gull's men fought harder. Steel clashed, muskets roared, and the deck ran slick with sea spray and sweat."
[Sound: The clang of swords, shouts of battle, and the roar of cannon fire.]
Captain Jeb:
"In the end, we took the prize. The hold was stuffed with treasure - gold coins, jewels, and even barrels o' fine rum. We loaded it onto the Gull, celebratin' our victory with cheers and a few swigs of the spoils."
Crewmate:
"So where's the mystery, Captain? Sounds like a right good day!"
Captain Jeb (grimly):
"Patience, lad. Here's where it gets strange. As we were sailin' away, leavin' the merchant ship to drift, a fog rolled in - not from the sea, but from above. Thick as soup, it was, and cold enough to make yer bones ache. When it cleared, the merchant ship was gone."
[Sound: An eerie wind whistling, fading into silence.]
Captain Jeb:
"Gone, I tell ye! Not a mast, not a splinter, not even a ripple on the water to show she'd ever been there. It was as if the Triangle itself had swallowed her whole. I sent a few men to the crow's nest to look for wreckage, but there was nothin' to be seen. Just an empty horizon."
Narrator:"The crew's murmurs grew louder. Even the most hardened among them looked uneasy."
Captain Jeb:
"I thought maybe she'd sunk fast, takin' her secrets to the depths. But here's the thing - when we reached port weeks later, word came from another ship that had passed through the Triangle. They claimed to've seen a ghostly vessel, matchin' the merchant ship's description, sailin' silently under a full moon. No crew, no lights - just a shadow glidin' across the water."
Crewmate (nervously):
"Ye think it was cursed, Captain?"
Captain Jeb:
"Cursed? Perhaps. Or maybe the Triangle has its own way o' claimin' what sails through its waters. Either way, I'll never forget the sight o' that fog rollin' in and the ship vanishing without so much as a whisper."
Narrator:
"Jeb leaned back in his chair, his eyes glinting in the firelight, as if daring his crew to challenge the story. None did. Instead, they sat in stunned silence, the weight of the tale settling over them like a shroud."
Captain Jeb:
"So, lads, remember this: the Triangle's not just a place o' storms and tales. It's alive, watchin', waitin'. And if ye take too much, it might just take ye back. Now, who's got the next round?"
[Sound: The crew bursts into nervous laughter, tankards clinking as the haunting sea shanty swells and the scene fades.]
