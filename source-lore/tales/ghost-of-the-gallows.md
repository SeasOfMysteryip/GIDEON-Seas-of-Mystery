# Ghost of the Gallows

## Use Rule

Use this as origin-history source lore for Captain Jeb, the Bloody Gull, and the crew's oral tradition. The tale can feed Jeb monologues, deckside storytelling, flashback seeds, character DNA, scenario structure, and ship-history texture.

This tale is not automatically literal canon. Treat it as a remembered, performed, and adapted version of Bloody Gull history unless a canon file, episode card, or approved scene locks a specific event.

## Origin / Adaptation Importance

Strong source for Jeb's grief-voice and the show's interest in justice, haunting, and rebel memory.

## Tale Summary

Jeb tells a somber tale of a condemned pirate named Tom, gallows injustice, ghostly return, and defiance that survives execution.

## Character DNA

- Jeb somber monologue
- gallows injustice
- ghost as unresolved wrong
- Old Red-style fatalism
- public legend from private grief

## Full Deck Tale

Captain Jeb *(voice low, somber):*
"Gather 'round, mates. Tonight, I've got a tale to tell - one that ain't full of gold or glory, but of somethin' far darker. A tale of men I knew, pirates like me and you, who met their fate not on the seas, but at the end of a hangman's noose. They called it 'justice.' I call it the 'Ghost of the Gallows.'"
(The fiddle fades, replaced by the hollow echo of a creaking gallows. A faint wind whistles, as though carrying ghostly whispers.)
Narrator *(deep, reflective tone):*
"It was the year 1721, and the tides of the Golden Age of Piracy were changin'. What was once a life of freedom on the open seas had become a desperate game of survival. The kings and queens of Europe, sick of pirates makin' fools of 'em, decided it was time to take back their oceans. They declared war on us - a war fought with ropes and iron cages."
Captain Jeb:
"I remember the day like it was yesterday. I was docked near Port Royal, keepin' a low profile after a bit o'... let's call it 'redistribution' of Spanish treasure. Word spread like wildfire that Black Sam Bellamy's crew, what was left of 'em after *The Whydah* went down, were set to hang in Boston. A bloody spectacle, they said, to show the world what happens to men who dared defy the crown."
(The sound of bustling crowds grows, murmurs of an angry mob and heavy boots on cobblestone. A distant bell tolls slowly.)
Narrator:
"They called it the 'Great Pirate Purge.' Dozens of pirates, some guilty of nothin' more than sailin' under the wrong flag, were marched to the gallows. Among them was a man named Tom - a fine sailor and an even finer friend to Jeb."
Captain Jeb *(voice growing thick with emotion):*
"Tom and I had run together a time or two, back in the days when the seas were ours to claim. He had a laugh that could drown out a storm and a knack for turnin' the dullest rum into a grand feast. But there he was, hands bound, standin' tall as they dragged him toward the noose. His crime? Bein' loyal to the wrong captain and refusin' to rat on his mates. A better man I never knew."
(A rope creaks ominously, and the crowd's murmurs swell into shouts of anger and triumph. The sound of a drumroll begins, steady and foreboding.)
Narrator:
"They say Tom stood proud, even with the rope around his neck. He didn't beg. He didn't curse. Instead, he looked out over the crowd and spoke words that still echo through history."
Captain Jeb *(solemnly, as if quoting):*
"'Ye can take my life,' he said, 'but ye'll never shackle the seas. For every man ye hang, ten more'll rise with fire in their hearts and freedom in their souls.'"
(The drumroll stops. Silence, save for the creaking rope and a gust of wind.)
Captain Jeb *(lower, heavy):*
"And then... it was done. The trapdoor fell, and so did Tom. They left him there, swingin' in the breeze, as a warnin' to the rest of us. But what they didn't know was that they'd done more than take his life - they'd made him a legend."
(A faint, ghostly moan drifts through the air, followed by the sound of chains rattling. The atmosphere turns eerie.)
Narrator:
"They say the gallows at Boston Harbor are haunted now. On moonless nights, sailors swear they've seen shadowy figures swingin' from invisible ropes, their faces frozen in defiance. And in the dead of night, a voice can be heard whisperin' through the mist..."
Captain Jeb *(haunting tone, as if mimicking a ghost):*
"'Ye'll never shackle the seas... never shackle the seas...'"
(The ghostly whisper resonances and fades, replaced by the soft lapping of waves.)
Captain Jeb *(voice soft, reflective):*
"Tom's spirit, and those of all the others who met their end on the gallows, ain't gone. They're out there, watchin' over us, remindin' us of what it means to be free. The *Ghost of the Gallows* ain't just a story - it's a warnin'. The crown may've won that day, but they'll never win the soul of the sea."
(The fiddle tune from earlier returns, this time slower and sadder. Jeb's voice grows lighter, though still respectful.)
Captain Jeb:
"So next time ye see a noose swayin' in the breeze, give a nod to ol' Tom and his crew. They may be gone, but their fight lives on in every pirate's heart. Now, raise yer mugs, mates - to freedom, to the sea, and to the ghosts who keep us honest."
(The sound of clinking mugs and hearty cheers fills the air as the fiddle swells into a hopeful, lively tune.)
Captain Jeb *(cheeky again):*
"And fer the love of Neptune, don't go gettin' yerselves caught! I'd hate to have to tell yer tale on this here show!"
(The music transitions into the upbeat, cheerful outro, and the sounds of the sea fade into the distance.)
