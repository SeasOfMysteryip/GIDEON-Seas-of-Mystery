# The Ballad of the Mermaids of Mistral Bay

## Aliases

- `Sirens of the Silv'ry Star`
- `Mistral Bay Wailers`
- `The Bloody Gull's Doom Chant`

## Performed By

- The Bloody Gull Boys

## Current Source Function

This song reads as a Navigator cautionary ballad and shipboard warning chant tied to siren peril, fracture water, and marrow-bound doom.

## Primary Signals

- warning refrain around tide and song
- Shell pulse and Bone Dice in the sound bed
- mermaid / siren peril as attraction plus doom
- Jeb and crew framed as already inside oral-history memory

## Strong Refrains / Hooks

- `Beware the tide, beware the song`
- `Where the mermaids weep and wail`
- `Were lost in the ocean's tale`
- `They're coming, me lads`

## Adaptation Value

This is especially useful for:

- Old Red warning lines
- Mermaid / Lysara / siren-adjacent atmosphere
- Navigator training or cautionary language
- Bloody Gull folklore told as ship lesson rather than exposition
