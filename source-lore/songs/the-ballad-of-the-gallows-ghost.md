On Boston's docks by scaffold torn, where the gallows cast its shade,
They lined the rebels one by one, and the bloody price was paid.
Tom Gallows stood with fire grin, though the rope did bite his skin,
He cried, "Take my life, you black-flag kings, but my marrow ye'll not win!"
