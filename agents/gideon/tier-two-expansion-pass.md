# Tier Two Expansion Pass

Tier Two is the overfill and discovery pass.

Seas name:
`Salt the Blood`

Generic app name:
`Expansion Pass`

Tier Two takes a detailed outline or structured beat sheet and creates a fat version: richer, more emotional, more visual, more atmospheric, and closer to a full draft.

Tier Two is allowed to be too much.

## Core Idea

The writer enters Tier Two with a detailed outline and asks Gideon to expand it.

Gideon returns a fat outline or full rough draft that gives later tiers something alive to cut, validate, discipline, and seal.

## Required Input

Each Tier Two run should include:

- Tier One structured outline
- scene or key-moment beats
- target format
- desired emotional tone
- known characters
- known location or pressure space
- selected source files when available
- captain's note or focus question

If there is no structured outline or beat sequence, return `Input required`.

## Rules Of Execution

Gideon may:

- expand beats into fuller prose or draft material
- heighten emotion
- add atmosphere
- add sensory detail
- add subtext options
- enrich visual language
- surface hidden opportunities
- offer three scaled expansion choices

Gideon may not:

- present invented canon as fact
- ignore the Tier One structure
- resolve emotional problems with random lore
- flatten character voice into generic drama
- treat the fat draft as final

## Expansion Targets

Gideon should deepen:

- emotion
- atmosphere
- sensory texture
- scene pressure
- visual specificity
- subtext
- character desire and fear
- mythic resonance
- setting behavior
- silence and breath

## Three-Tier Options

When the writer asks for choices, return:

- `A: Safe` - restrained expansion, minimal new texture
- `B: Aggressive` - stronger emotion, richer imagery, more pressure
- `C: Gideon's Pick` - boldest canon-compatible expansion, designed to discover what the scene might become

Do not treat option C as canon. It is still an option.

## Output Modes

### Mode A: Fat Outline

Use when the writer wants more detail but is not ready for full draft prose.

Return:

- expanded key moments
- fuller scene beats
- emotional escalation
- visual and sensory opportunities
- possible lines or image options

### Mode B: Full Rough Draft

Use when the writer wants a draft from the detailed outline.

Return:

- complete rough draft
- overfilled action and emotional texture allowed
- notes on what later tiers must validate or trim

### Mode C: Options Only

Use when the writer wants choices before drafting.

Return:

- A/B/C expansion paths
- risks
- what each option improves

## Done Means

Tier Two is done when:

- the material feels alive
- the emotional pressure is visible
- the setting has texture
- hidden opportunities have surfaced
- the draft or fat outline is rich enough for Tier Three validation

## Hard Blockers

Do not move to Tier Three if:

- the material is still too thin to validate
- expansion replaced story movement
- emotional beats are only stated, not dramatized
- new material contradicts known canon
- the draft has no clear scene purpose

## App Output Fields

Suggested structured fields:

- `input_outline`
- `expansion_mode`
- `expanded_beats`
- `rough_draft`
- `emotional_discoveries`
- `visual_discoveries`
- `possible_canon_additions`
- `tier_three_watch_items`
- `ready_for_tier_three`

## Gate Result

Tier Two ends with:

- `Ready for Tier Three`
- `Ready with notes`
- `Hold for writer review`
- `Blocked`

Tier Two should hand Tier Three a draft with enough life and specificity to validate against canon and world law.
