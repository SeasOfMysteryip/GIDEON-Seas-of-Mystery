# Tier Three Canon Validation

Tier Three is the world-bible and canon validation pass.

Seas name:
`Gulp It Deep`

Generic app name:
`Canon Validation Pass`

Tier Three proves that the draft belongs to its world. It tests relics, myth, supernatural systems, factions, fleets, tactics, setting behavior, benchmark compliance, and forbidden drift before the scene moves into deeper character consequence work.

Tier Three may tighten, trim, and mark exact redlines inside approved margins. It may not invent, rewrite freely, or inject new myth.

## Source Note

This protocol is informed by the Tier Three `Gulp It Deep Audit Sheet`.

The reusable product insight is that Tier Three should not merely ask whether lore is "cool." It should enforce world identity against source law and benchmark scenes.

## Required Input

Each Tier Three scene should include:

- episode title and number
- act and scene ID
- scene title
- source path or app source location
- full scene draft or selected scene section
- relevant world-bible files
- relevant relic, myth, faction, fleet, or tactic files
- emotional pulse, maximum three beats
- dialogue redlines, exact text only, when applicable
- no-touch zones
- optional captain's note
- optional benchmark references

If input is missing or unclear, return `Input required`.

## Benchmark Compliance

Tier Three may use benchmark scenes as quality and identity references when the writer selects them.

Benchmark references are not permission to copy lines, events, or lore.

They are used to check:

- intensity
- specificity
- scene pressure
- sea behavior
- relic behavior
- dialogue force
- emotional load

For a generic app, this becomes `Benchmark Compliance`: selected reference scenes or passages that define the project's standard for world behavior and scene force.

## Tier Three Cut Sheet

The app should collect or derive these fields per scene.

### Emotional Pulse

Maximum three beats.

Tier Three uses emotional pulse as a guardrail so world validation does not become detached lore inspection.

If worldbuilding is correct but no emotional pulse is visible, flag `World valid, pulse weak`.

### Dialogue Redlines

Exact text only.

Use this when a line needs cadence tightening, fat trimming, or approved line shuffling.

Required fields:

- character
- original line
- redline change
- reason

Do not add new dialogue.

Do not rewrite freely.

### No-Touch Zones

Exact lines, moments, beats, images, or transitions that Gideon must not alter.

Use for:

- sacred lines
- final image before a cut
- visual-only beats
- relic behavior that is already locked
- scene rhythm that must remain intact
- intentional silence

If a no-touch zone appears to conflict with canon, flag it for captain review instead of changing it.

### Editing Margin

Tier Three approved actions:

- cadence tightening
- trim fat only
- line shuffling only if marked or requested

Tier Three prohibited actions:

- no additions
- no rewrites
- no myth injection
- no unsupported lore
- no new relic behavior
- no emotional reinterpretation

### Captain's Note

Optional directive or concern from the writer.

In the source sheet, this includes watchwords that may be overused:

- pulse
- glow
- flicker
- rift
- fracture

Treat these as word-drift checks, not automatic bans.

## Word Drift Checks

Tier Three should watch repeated world-language that can become decorative or imprecise.

### Pulse

Keep when tied to:

- breath
- heart
- Sea memory
- rhythm
- inherited mechanism

Flag when used as ambient filler.

Possible alternatives by function:

- rhythmic or organic: throb, beat, tremor, surge, shudder
- mythic or sea-tied: current, undertow, breath, resonance
- mechanical or inherited: tick, hum, sync, echo, threadline

### Glow

Keep for relic ignition, bloodline response, or emotionally tethered light.

Flag when decorative.

Possible alternatives by function:

- mythic or relic: flare, ignite, seethe, flare back
- emotional or lineage: burn low, ember, smolder, bleed light
- subtle or environmental: glint, gleam, hold light, shade-lit

### Flicker

Keep when it serves instability, breath, interruption, or Sea memory.

Flag when it weakens myth by becoming generic visual filler.

Possible alternatives by function:

- weak light instability: waver, stagger, ghost, twitch
- emotional or visual drift: glitch, stutter, quiver, tremble, hiccup
- mythic or sea-tied: crackle, flash, breath-skip, seam tug

### Rift

Canon-safe in many contexts, but should not overpopulate a scene.

Use alternatives when the moment is physical, temporal, emotional, or sea-memory specific.

Possible alternatives by function:

- sea tear or portal: seam, tear, breach, divide
- mythic or emotional divide: split, corridor, gap, crack in the tide
- legacy or Sea memory: old wound, forgotten passage, memory scar

### Fracture

Use sparingly unless it reflects internal breakage or timeline logic.

Possible alternatives by function:

- literal or structural: break, split, shatter, snap, faultline
- mythic or emotional: scar, rupture, collapse, unraveling, cleave
- time or memory: driftline, seamline tear, echo break, ley split

## Required Source Checks

Use the appropriate source material:

- `canon/core/` for series identity, promise, tone, and world-story contract
- `canon/relics/` for relic behavior, cost, bearer law, symbiosis, and circuit logic
- `canon/deep-myth/` for Spectral Sea rules, fractures, mirror harbors, system architecture, and myth law
- `canon/factions/` for faction motive and power behavior
- `canon/fleets/` for ship identity and vessel behavior
- `canon/tactics/` for maneuver and combat doctrine
- `episodes/` for continuity, selected benchmark scenes, and already-locked scene behavior
- `season-planning/` when active season direction matters

## Validation Checks

### World Identity Check

Ask:

- Could this scene belong to this world only?
- Are the details specific or generic?
- Does the setting behave according to source law?
- Is the scene leaning on source truth or genre fog?

### Relic And Myth Check

Ask:

- Does relic behavior obey canon?
- Does myth behavior obey system law?
- Are costs, limits, and consequences visible?
- Is relic or myth activity decorative?
- Has new lore been presented as locked fact?

### Benchmark Check

Ask:

- Does the scene meet the selected benchmark standard?
- Is the gap intensity, clarity, emotional force, world behavior, or dialogue?
- Is the scene borrowing benchmark energy without copying benchmark content?

### Dialogue Redline Check

Ask:

- Is the redline exact?
- Does the change preserve meaning?
- Does the change preserve voice?
- Is this cadence tightening or a rewrite in disguise?

### No-Touch Check

Ask:

- Are all no-touch zones preserved?
- Does any no-touch zone create canon risk?
- If so, has it been flagged for captain review instead of changed?

### Word Drift Check

Ask:

- Are world-language words becoming repetitive?
- Are they emotionally tethered?
- Are they serving specific function?
- Would a more precise alternative improve clarity?

## Gate Result

Tier Three must end with one clear gate result:

- `Ready for Tier Four`
- `Ready with notes`
- `Hold for captain review`
- `Blocked`

`Ready for Tier Four` means world law and canon validation are strong enough to enter `Trial the Fire`.

`Ready with notes` means non-blocking concerns should carry forward.

`Hold for captain review` means the writer must answer a question or approve a redline.

`Blocked` means canon, world identity, relic behavior, myth system behavior, benchmark compliance, or no-touch conflict fails.

## Hard Blockers

Do not send a scene to Tier Four if:

- a locked canon fact is contradicted
- relic behavior violates canon
- myth behavior violates system doctrine
- unsupported lore is presented as fact
- no-touch zones conflict with canon and need writer decision
- benchmark compliance fails in a scene meant to match a named benchmark
- dialogue redline changes meaning or voice
- word drift creates repeated generic world texture

## Relationship To Tier Four

Tier Three proves the world is legal and specific.

Tier Four then proves that the legal world behavior creates character consequence.
