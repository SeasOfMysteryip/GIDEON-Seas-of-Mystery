# Gideon Voice Check

Use this mode when the user only wants dialogue reviewed.

## Checks

### Silas Merrin

- Controlled, analytical, and quietly dread-haunted.
- Prefers measurement to emotion.
- Data-first without becoming robotic.
- No swagger; pressure hides behind restraint.

### Beau Halvorsen

- Performative, branded, danger-blind confidence.
- Sees spectacle before consequence.
- Influencer energy without becoming pure parody.

### Marin Halvorsen

- Quieter, more sensitive, more receptive to atmosphere.
- Should register unease before others do.
- Not mystical by default, but porous to the Sea.

### Lt. Alina Voss

- Clipped, tactical, clinical.
- No mystical language.
- Operational metrics over emotion.
- Calm analysis under anomaly pressure.

### Tomas Reyes

- Low, halting, fragmented speech.
- Memory phrases instead of explanations.
- Haunted and time-worn.
- Feels like survival turned into warning.

### Lysara

- Otherworldly, lyrical, and eternal.
- Speaks like a song half-remembered.
- Slow-rising cadence; silences carry weight.
- Never explains; she remembers.
- Distinct from Sophia's prophecy and Elias's guidance.

### Admiral Vernon Harrow

- Clipped imperial command voice.
- Aged brass, disappointment, and formal decay.
- Gives orders even in defeat.
- Haunting sense of command structure surviving past reality.
- No pirate swagger; his menace is uniform, ritual, and delusion.

### Charles Vane

- Iron-and-rum rage.
- Rough rhythmic war-sermon speech.
- Explosive, but the whisper can be worse.
- Violence and regret fused together.
- No calm strategic polish.

### Murakami Hideyoshi

- Precision draped in frost.
- Every syllable permissioned.
- Methodical and absolute.
- States fate instead of threatening it.
- No warmth, no wasted language, no chaos.

### Edward "Blackbeard" Thatch

- Silken menace and monarchic control.
- Slow-burn cadence that turns surgical.
- Never raises his voice.
- Myth used as strategy, not worship.
- Theatrical dominance without wasted noise.

### Ching Shih

- Arctic calm with razored intent.
- Minimalist, precise, and strategically silent.
- Every word counts.
- Control replaces flair.
- Sounds like command already decided the outcome.

### Darak al-Mhar

- Silver-tongued charm with hidden threat.
- Every line feels like a deal, dodge, or pressure test.
- Velvet hustle cadence.
- Amused under pressure.
- Never noble on purpose, even when he does something unexpectedly human.

### Elias Hawthorne

- Speaks like memory waking up.
- Quiet authority with no ego.
- No pep talks, overt prophecy, fear, or exposition dumps.
- Gentle, restrained, and intentional.
- Sounds like someone who has already grieved the moment and still chooses care.

### Linda Han

- Fast, precise, hard-edged speech.
- Warzone-hardened realism.
- No indulgence in mysticism.
- Under pressure, voice drops lower and tighter.
- Protects through command, focus, and brutal clarity.

### Drew Mitchell

- Visual thinker.
- Awe filtered through camera language and light-chasing instinct.
- Not mystical in speech, but open to image-driven wonder.
- Works best in concise observational lines.

### Jordan Reyes

- Laid-back on the surface, sensitive underneath.
- Hears patterns before others do.
- Audio-oriented observations and static/hum language fit.
- Avoid making him frantic too early.

### Martin Kim

- Curious, enthusiastic, and in over his head.
- Youthful energy without becoming goofy.
- Fear and courage should coexist.
- Reads as the crew's innocent eyes, not as a fool.

### Carlos Vega

- Pattern-reader and anomaly decoder.
- Dry, focused, visual-logic voice.
- Sees structure in glitches.
- Avoid flowery mysticism; keep him analytical.

### Old Red

- Rhythmic, half-sung, pulsewave speech.
- Sounds like prophecy, curse, joke, or sailor's hymn.
- Never gives a straight answer.
- Speaks from harmony, warning, and sea-memory.
- Avoid plain explanatory lore dumps.

### Gus Maddox

- Direct, pressure-valve humor.
- Action language over poetic language.
- Calm under fire.
- Dry swagger masking loyalty and trauma.
- No mystical speech pattern.

### Stumpy Pete

- Gravel voice, blunt old-salt shorthand.
- No poetry; every word earned.
- Slow unless pissed.
- Nostalgic flashes inside gruffness.
- Crew-first seamanship over mythic abstraction.

### Jeb Blackwater

- Low volume, high intent.
- Sparse lines unless confessing or monologuing.
- Silence carries weight.
- Sailor metaphor lands like scripture.
- No longwinded exposition.
- No performative yelling.

### Lark Hawthorne

- Clipped, efficient, sharp.
- Concludes rather than wonders aloud.
- Intelligent dry humor.
- High-register analysis under pressure.
- Swears when furious.
- Quiet when scared.

### Sophia Briggs

- Quiet prophecy.
- Reluctant certainty.
- Speaks as if listening to the Sea.
- Relics recognize her; she does not command them like tools.
- Avoid generic mysticism.
- Avoid technical exposition.
